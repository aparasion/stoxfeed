Strava, the leading digital community for fitness enthusiasts, has successfully implemented a rapid globalization strategy, enabling its newly acquired app, Runna, to go global in just six weeks. This achievement stems from the strategic leadership of Eduardo D’Antonio, Strava's Globalization Director, who built a comprehensive tech stack centered around Crowdin, facilitating seamless integrations with over 25 content repositories.

The localization journey highlights the importance of speed, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness in today’s fast-paced market. Strava's decision to utilize Crowdin was driven by the need for quick implementation and robust support, allowing them to localize Runna into multiple languages, including Dutch, French, and Japanese. This approach not only enhances Strava's market reach but also underscores the growing significance of automation and advanced language technologies in localization workflows.
