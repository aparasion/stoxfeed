Motorsport Games Inc. (MSGM) reported a significant financial turnaround, achieving a 95% increase in quarterly revenue to $3.8 million, primarily driven by the success of its flagship title, Le Mans Ultimate. The company also posted a quarterly net income of $800,000, a substantial improvement from a net loss of $2.9 million in the prior year, and an annual revenue of $11.3 million, marking a 30% increase overall. The positive momentum is attributed to strategic shifts, including the sale of its NASCAR license and a focus on enhancing player engagement through its Race Control platform.

This financial rebound highlights the growing popularity of Le Mans Ultimate, which accounted for 78% of annual revenues, alongside a burgeoning subscription model through Race Control, which now boasts over 26,000 active subscribers. The company’s cash position has also improved, rising to $6 million as of February 2026, supporting ongoing development and expansion plans.

As Motorsport Games prepares to launch Le Mans Ultimate on consoles and continues to innovate its gaming experience, the company is well-positioned for sustained growth.
