Lokalise has introduced a streamlined workflow designed to automate context management in localization, helping teams reclaim valuable time lost to fragmented information. The process begins with auditing existing context for a specific user journey, identifying where context currently resides and who is responsible for clarifications. This initial step sets the stage for a more effective localization process.

By standardizing what constitutes "good context" and centralizing it within a Translation Management System (TMS), teams can ensure that translators have access to comprehensive information, including visual and structural context. Automating the flow of context through integrations with design and development tools further enhances efficiency, allowing for smoother collaboration and reducing the need for manual uploads and clarifications.

For localization professionals, this approach not only minimizes rework and developer time but also provides measurable KPIs to track improvements. I highly recommend checking out the full article for a deeper dive into implementing these strategies effectively.
