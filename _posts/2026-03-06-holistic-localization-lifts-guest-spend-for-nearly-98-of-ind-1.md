The London Book Fair 2026 will feature a diverse array of literary works, showcasing both established and emerging authors. Among the highlighted titles is "The 36 Watchers: Book I: Fall" by Dan Bar Hava, which blends historical conspiracy with supernatural elements. This book will be presented by The Maple Staple at Booth 6A100 from March 10-12.

Additionally, the fair will spotlight "A Narrative Rewritten" by Michaia Walker, exploring themes of pain and healing, and "A Trail to Love" by Joanne Kiersarsky. Other notable presentations include "Council Ford Insurrection: The Second Amendment 20432063" by J. Thomas Bowne, JD, and children's book "A Time to Dream" by C. A. Houp. The event will also feature Dr. Ronald Citrons' "When Universities Are Destroyed," which examines the resilience of educational institutions post-disaster.
