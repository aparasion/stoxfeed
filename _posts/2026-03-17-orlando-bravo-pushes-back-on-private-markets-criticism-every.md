Orlando Bravo, founder of Thoma Bravo, defended the private equity sector amid rising scrutiny, emphasizing that deep sector expertise is crucial as artificial intelligence reshapes the software industry. In a recent CNBC interview, Bravo highlighted the firm’s focus on detailed company analysis over broad market trends, asserting that their specialized approach positions them well against the backdrop of increasing markdowns in private markets. He noted that while some firms face challenges, Thoma Bravo's investor base remains confident due to the firm's transparency and strong historical performance.

The financial landscape is shifting, with Morgan Stanley forecasting direct-lending default rates nearing 8%, while Apollo's John Zito criticized the valuation practices of private equity firms. Bravo acknowledged a misstep in overpaying for Medallia but maintained that the majority of Thoma Bravo's portfolio is thriving, particularly in the AI sector. He pointed out that many publicly traded software firms are more vulnerable to disruption, making their declining valuations justifiable.
