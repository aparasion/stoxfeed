In a recent webinar featuring experts Kathleen Pierce from Forrester and Morana Perić from DeepL, the discussion centered on the critical balance between leveraging Language AI and maintaining brand integrity. They highlighted the risks and rewards of AI in translation, emphasizing the importance of trust in customer relationships and the need for organizations to redefine quality as "fit for purpose" rather than a one-size-fits-all standard.

The insights shared underscore a significant shift in the localization landscape: as AI technology advances, localization teams must evolve from mere executors to strategic advisors. This transition allows organizations to tailor their translation processes based on content type, ensuring that human expertise is applied where it matters most, such as in cultural nuance and context.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is the necessity of robust control mechanisms in AI translation systems. To delve deeper into these insights and discover how to effectively manage AI-driven translations, I recommend checking out the full webinar.
