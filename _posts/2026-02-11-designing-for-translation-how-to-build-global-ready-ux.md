Szymon Metkowski, a Language and UX Consultant, emphasizes the critical need for integrating localization into the design process rather than treating it as a post-launch task. In a recent episode of The Agile Localization Podcast, he highlights how neglecting language considerations can lead to confusing interfaces and erode user trust. By including linguists early in the design phase, teams can avoid costly localization errors and create a more seamless user experience.

This approach is particularly vital as poor localization can detract from user confidence, especially with microcopy—the small yet impactful text that guides users. Metkowski argues that effective microcopy must be clear, culturally relevant, and emotionally resonant, as it plays a pivotal role in user interaction. Additionally, he discusses the role of AI in localization, stressing the importance of human oversight to prevent culturally inappropriate or misleading content.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is to prioritize language in the design process to enhance user experience and foster trust. I highly recommend listening to the full podcast episode for deeper insights into this transformative approach to localization.
