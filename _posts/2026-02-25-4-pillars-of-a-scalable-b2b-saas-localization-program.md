B2B SaaS companies often approach localization as a straightforward translation process, but this method falters when scaling. In a recent episode of The Agile Localization Podcast, host Stefan Huyghe speaks with Nicola Calabrese, Founder of Undertow, about building structured, scalable localization programs that leverage AI effectively. Calabrese emphasizes that AI alone cannot resolve chaos; rather, it can amplify existing issues if not paired with a solid strategy, skilled personnel, modern technology, and well-defined processes.

For localization professionals, the discussion highlights the importance of aligning localization efforts with business goals and ensuring that teams are equipped with the right tools and support. Calabrese outlines a "Minimum Viable Experience" approach to prioritize essential content and emphasizes the need for a centralized translation management system (TMS) to streamline workflows. 

The key takeaway is that successful localization requires more than just technology; it demands a comprehensive framework that integrates strategy, people, and processes. For a deeper dive into these insights, I highly recommend listening to the full podcast episode.
