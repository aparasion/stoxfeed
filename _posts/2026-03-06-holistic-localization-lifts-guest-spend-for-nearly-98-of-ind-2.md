Seattle-based author David O. Otebele's contemporary novel, "From March 10-12, 2026," will be prominently featured at the London Book Fair, alongside several other notable works. The Maple Staple Bookstore will showcase Otebele's book at Booth 6A100, highlighting its socially charged themes. 

Additionally, Dan Bar Hava's historical conspiracy novel, "The 36 Watchers: Book I: Fall," will also be presented at the same venue, promising an engaging blend of suspense and supernatural elements. Other featured titles include Michaia Walker's "A Narrative Rewritten," which explores themes of pain and healing, and Joanne Kiersarsky's "A Trail to Love," reflecting resilience and courage.

The fair will also present works by C. A. Houp and Patricia Viles-Antonellis, among others, emphasizing a diverse range of genres and narratives. This event serves as a significant platform for authors to reach new audiences and engage with the literary community.
