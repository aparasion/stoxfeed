The Ethereum Foundation has released a new 38-page mandate that clarifies its role as a neutral steward of the Ethereum ecosystem, emphasizing decentralization and public goods over product development. This document has ignited a heated debate within the Ethereum community, with supporters viewing it as a reaffirmation of core principles, while critics argue it reflects a lack of proactive leadership at a time when institutional interest in blockchain technology is surging.

The implications of this mandate are significant for Ethereum's market position. Critics express concern that the foundation's hands-off approach may hinder Ethereum's ability to compete with rival networks, especially as institutional players increasingly seek robust blockchain solutions. Supporters, however, believe that the focus on foundational values will help maintain Ethereum’s integrity and resilience as a decentralized platform.
