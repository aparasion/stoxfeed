Metals prices plummeted on Thursday amid rising oil concerns linked to the U.S.-Iran conflict, with gold dropping nearly 6% and silver falling 8%. The broader sell-off impacted industrial metals as well, with copper and palladium declining by 2% and 5.5%, respectively. Investors are increasingly worried that surging oil prices will reignite inflation, potentially leading to sustained higher interest rates, which diminish the appeal of non-yielding assets like gold.

The market is now grappling with recession risks, as declining copper prices are often interpreted as a signal of slowing economic growth. Analysts warn that prolonged elevated oil prices could alter consumer and business spending, pushing the economy toward a stagflation scenario. However, some experts argue that historical precedents suggest such outcomes may not be inevitable, citing past oil shocks that did not lead to recessions.
