Ryanair has announced it expects to receive certification for the Boeing 737 MAX 10 by Q3, with deliveries slated to begin early next year. This timely aircraft delivery is crucial for the airline's capacity expansion and network growth, as it aims to increase passenger numbers from 207 million to approximately 215 million in the upcoming financial year while implementing modest fare increases.

The airline's decision to reduce operations at Brussels Charleroi Airport by about 10% due to a new passenger tax highlights its sensitivity to regulatory changes. Despite challenges such as rising jet fuel prices driven by geopolitical tensions, Ryanair maintains a strong financial position with around €1 billion in net cash. Analysts have responded positively, with Evercore ISI upgrading the stock to “outperform” and Bernstein raising its target, indicating confidence in Ryanair's operational resilience.
