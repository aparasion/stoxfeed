MyHeritage has transformed from a genealogy-focused platform into a billion-dollar AI and DNA powerhouse, with localization at its core. Valentina Wainstein, who has been with the localization team for nearly a decade, emphasizes the importance of overcoming language barriers to ensure that life-changing discoveries in genetic health and ancestry are accessible to all users, regardless of their language proficiency.

The company’s recent growth has necessitated a robust localization strategy, leading to a shift to Crowdin Enterprise for centralized workflow management. This transition has allowed MyHeritage to efficiently handle millions of words across 50 languages, leveraging advanced machine translation engines and a passionate community of volunteers to maintain quality and cultural relevance in translations.

As MyHeritage continues to expand its language offerings, localization professionals can glean valuable insights from their multi-step translation process and the integration of AI tools. For a deeper dive into their innovative approach, I encourage you to read the full article.
