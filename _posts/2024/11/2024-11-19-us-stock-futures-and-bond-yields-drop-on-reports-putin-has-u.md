U.S. stock futures and bond yields declined sharply after President Vladimir Putin approved a significant update to Russia's nuclear doctrine, allowing for a nuclear response to conventional military actions. This decision follows the U.S. greenlighting the use of weapons by Ukraine against Russian targets and the involvement of North Korean troops in the conflict, escalating geopolitical tensions. The S&P 500 futures (ES00) fell, while the yield on the 10-year Treasury bond dropped by 5 basis points, reflecting increased market anxiety.

This development is critical for financial markets as it heightens geopolitical risks, potentially impacting investor sentiment and market stability. The shift in nuclear policy could lead to heightened volatility in equities and fixed income, as traders reassess risk premiums in light of escalating military engagements.

Market professionals should closely monitor these geopolitical dynamics, as further escalations could influence sector performance, particularly in defense and energy, while also impacting overall market liquidity and risk appetite.
