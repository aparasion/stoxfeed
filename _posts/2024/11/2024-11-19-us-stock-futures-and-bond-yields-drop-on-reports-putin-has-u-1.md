U.S. stock futures and bond yields declined sharply following reports that Russian President Vladimir Putin has revised the country's nuclear doctrine, allowing for a nuclear response to conventional military actions. This development comes amid heightened tensions, particularly after the U.S. greenlit the use of its weapons by Ukraine against Russian forces and North Korean troops' involvement in the conflict. The S&P 500 futures contract (ES00) dropped, reflecting investor concerns, while the yield on the 10-year Treasury fell by 5 basis points.

This geopolitical shift is significant for financial markets, as it raises fears of escalation in the Ukraine conflict, potentially impacting global stability and investor sentiment. The bond market's reaction suggests a flight to safety, indicating that traders are seeking refuge in government securities amid rising geopolitical risks.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor developments closely, as further escalations could lead to increased volatility in equities and shifts in bond yields, influencing portfolio strategies and risk management.
