**Market Brief for March 18, 2026**

**What Happened Today**  
The financial markets experienced heightened volatility today, driven primarily by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and disappointing macroeconomic data. The Federal Reserve maintained its interest rate at 3.5% to 3.75%, a decision influenced by the ongoing conflict in Iran, which has led to a spike in oil prices, with Brent crude exceeding $108 per barrel. The Producer Price Index (PPI) rose 3.4% year-over-year, surpassing expectations, further complicating the inflation narrative. This backdrop contributed to a sell-off on Wall Street, with the Dow Jones dropping nearly 1%. Notably, energy stocks were in focus, with significant gains in companies like NextEra Energy as investors reacted to rising oil prices. Additionally, Micron Technology's stock continued its upward trajectory, reflecting strong demand for memory chips.

**Expectations for the Next Session**  
Market participants are bracing for continued volatility as they assess the implications of the Fed's decision and the ongoing geopolitical situation. Investors will be closely monitoring oil prices and any developments in the Iran conflict, which could further impact market sentiment. The focus will also shift to upcoming earnings reports, particularly from key players in the semiconductor and energy sectors. Expectations remain uncertain, as the combination of high inflation and geopolitical risks may lead to a cautious trading environment.
