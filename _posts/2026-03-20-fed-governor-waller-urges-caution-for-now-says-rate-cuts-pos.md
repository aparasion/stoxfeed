Federal Reserve Governor Christopher Waller signaled a cautious stance on interest rate cuts during a CNBC interview, highlighting the need for a careful assessment of economic conditions, particularly in light of recent labor market trends and geopolitical uncertainties. While Waller had previously supported rate cuts, he now emphasizes the importance of monitoring job growth and inflation before making any decisions, especially with the labor market showing signs of weakness.

This shift in Waller's outlook reflects broader market sentiment, where expectations for rate reductions have diminished significantly due to rising oil prices and the ongoing conflict in Iran. Market participants had anticipated multiple cuts earlier this year, but now the consensus leans towards maintaining current rates through 2026, complicating the Fed's policy trajectory amid fluctuating economic indicators.
