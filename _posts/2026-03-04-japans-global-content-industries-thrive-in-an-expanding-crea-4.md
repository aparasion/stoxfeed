Stanford University's Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) is a key institution focused on interdisciplinary research and policy dialogue concerning contemporary Asia. The center aims to educate future Asia experts and facilitate connections between research and practical applications through publications and international collaborations.

APARC engages with U.S. and Asian decision-makers by providing policy-relevant research that enhances public understanding of Asian affairs. Recent publications from its scholars include works on America's trade policy in the Asia-Pacific, China's rise as a global power, and the dynamics of U.S.-Southeast Asia relations.

In a notable recent event, former diplomat Rahm Emanuel discussed the current political crisis in America and its implications for U.S. alliances in the Indo-Pacific during an APARC briefing. This conversation highlighted the importance of maintaining strong partnerships in the region amidst shifting political landscapes.
