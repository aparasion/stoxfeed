Pop Mart, the collectible toy maker, is collaborating with Sony Pictures to develop a live-action and CGI hybrid film centered around its popular Labubu doll. Directed by Paul King, known for "Paddington" and "Wonka," this project aims to expand Pop Mart's "The Monsters" franchise beyond toys, potentially revitalizing interest in the brand after a decline in resale prices.

This partnership is significant as it highlights Pop Mart's strategy to leverage storytelling to enhance consumer engagement and drive sales. The company's revenue from "The Monsters" series constituted 34.7% of its total in early 2025, but analysts at HSBC have warned of potential earnings normalization in 2026, anticipating an 11% to 13% cut in forecasts. By venturing into film, Pop Mart seeks to create a deeper connection with its audience and generate new product ideas.
