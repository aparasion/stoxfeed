Small-cap stocks have struggled to gain traction in recent years, lagging behind large-cap tech stocks that have thrived on the AI boom. The Vanguard Russell 2000 Index ETF (VTWO) exemplifies this trend, as mid-cap stocks have outperformed small caps, raising questions about their long-term viability. Investors face the challenge of distinguishing between genuine value opportunities and potential value traps, particularly in a market environment where small-cap stocks are sensitive to interest rates and economic growth.

The current landscape is particularly challenging for small caps, with rising oil prices due to geopolitical tensions and signs of weakness in the labor market raising recession fears. Small-cap companies, often more leveraged and with weaker balance sheets, could struggle in a downturn, making the case for caution. However, with over 30% of the Russell 2000 invested in financials and healthcare—sectors poised for AI benefits—there remains a potential for long-term gains.

For those with a long-term investment horizon, a measured allocation to small caps may be prudent. This could provide a hedge against overconcentration in large-cap tech and position investors to capitalize on future growth.
