Gold Royalty Corporation (GROY) saw its shares plummet 9.1% on Thursday, primarily driven by a significant downturn in the gold sector, which experienced nearly a 6% drop in gold prices. This decline comes amid rising inflationary fears, which typically bolster gold's value but are currently causing a paradoxical decrease as investors react to the Federal Reserve's stance on interest rates. Despite reporting a 33.5% increase in fourth-quarter revenue to $4.5 million, GROY's earnings results likely had minimal impact on its stock performance.

The broader implications for the financial markets are noteworthy. With the Federal Reserve maintaining interest rates and long-term Treasury yields rising, expectations for fewer rate cuts this year are growing. This environment suggests a tightening of monetary conditions that could hinder gold's appeal as an inflation hedge, especially as its recent rally may have already priced in geopolitical risks.
