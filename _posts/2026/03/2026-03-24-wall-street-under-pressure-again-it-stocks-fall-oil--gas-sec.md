US stock market sentiment is wavering as investors grapple with escalating tensions in the Middle East and mixed diplomatic signals from Washington. S&P 500 futures fell by 0.8%, reflecting ongoing uncertainty, particularly in the technology sector, where significant selling pressure has emerged. The lack of de-escalation among the US, Israel, and Iran raises recession risks, while a swift resolution could spark a relief rally.

The geopolitical turmoil is impacting market dynamics, with oil prices climbing back above $90 per barrel amid fears of supply disruptions. US Treasury yields have risen, indicating limited demand for safe-haven assets despite the heightened tensions. UBS warns that volatility will likely persist due to potential supply chain disruptions and the need for oil inventory replenishment, prompting a defensive stance in European markets.

Investors should remain vigilant as the situation develops, particularly regarding energy stocks and broader market volatility, which could influence portfolio strategies in the near term.
