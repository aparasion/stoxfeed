The Strait of Hormuz remains a focal point for global economic stability, with Bloomberg estimating that a full two-month closure could lead to significant disruptions. However, market sentiment suggests a potential reopening could occur sooner, alleviating immediate concerns. The Persian Gulf is crucial, accounting for approximately 20% of the global oil and gas supply, with developed economies particularly reliant on this flow.

As oil prices show signs of cooling, a bearish divergence could emerge if prices dip below $100 per barrel, with key support identified at $96. This price action is critical for traders as it reflects broader market dynamics influenced by geopolitical tensions, especially regarding Iran's diminished military capabilities following extensive strikes by the US and Israel, which may deter further conflict escalation.
