Oklo (OKLO +1.08%) is making strides toward commercializing its innovative small nuclear reactor, the Aurora powerhouse, which could cater to data centers and remote industrial sites. This shift in nuclear technology represents a significant market opportunity, especially as demand for on-site power surges with the rise of AI data centers. However, Oklo's path to success hinges on obtaining a license from the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC), as the company currently operates at a loss and is essentially pre-revenue.

For investors, the stakes are high. While Oklo has made progress in its licensing efforts and has a backlog of projects, translating these into firm sales remains a challenge. The company must demonstrate not just operational viability but also profitability through scaling its reactor deployments to justify its current $15 billion market capitalization.
