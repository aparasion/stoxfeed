The Chicago Municipal Employees’ Annuity & Benefit Fund has become the latest pension fund to sever ties with a small-cap equity manager, citing performance issues. This decision follows similar actions taken by two other pension funds in recent months, signaling a growing scrutiny on smaller asset managers as institutional investors prioritize returns in a challenging market environment.

This trend comes amid a backdrop of shifting regulatory dynamics, particularly with the Department of Labor reinstating the five-part test for fiduciary investment advice. Experts warn that the evolving regulatory landscape, especially regarding rollover advice, could complicate compliance for asset managers, potentially impacting their operational strategies and client relationships. Additionally, the continued decline in average fees for mutual funds and ETFs is intensifying competition, particularly for smaller issuers struggling to maintain market share.

Market professionals should closely monitor how these developments may influence asset allocation strategies and the competitive positioning of small-cap managers as performance pressures mount.
