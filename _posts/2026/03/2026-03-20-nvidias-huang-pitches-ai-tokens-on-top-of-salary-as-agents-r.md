Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang has introduced a groundbreaking compensation model for engineers that includes artificial intelligence tokens, which are designed to enhance productivity. During the annual GPU Technology Conference, Huang proposed that engineers could receive a token budget in addition to their base salaries, incentivizing them to deploy AI agents that automate tasks. This innovative approach signals a shift in workforce dynamics, where engineers may soon oversee a multitude of AI agents capable of executing complex tasks autonomously.

This development has significant implications for the tech sector and the broader labor market. Goldman Sachs estimates that AI could automate 25% of U.S. work hours, potentially displacing 6% to 7% of jobs. While concerns about job loss are prevalent, Huang argues that the demand for software will increase as AI agents become more prevalent, creating a paradox where talent scarcity persists even amid fears of workforce reduction.
