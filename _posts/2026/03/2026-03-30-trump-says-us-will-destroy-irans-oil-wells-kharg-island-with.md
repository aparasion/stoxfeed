U.S. President Donald Trump has issued a stark warning regarding Iran, threatening to "completely" destroy the country's electric plants and oil infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz is not reopened and a peace deal is not reached soon. His comments come amid ongoing military operations and escalating tensions, as the U.S. considers deploying ground forces to seize Kharg Island, a critical hub for Iran’s oil exports.

The implications for financial markets are significant. The Strait of Hormuz is a vital shipping lane for global oil supplies, with about 90% of Iran's crude exports passing through it. As shipping traffic has dwindled due to recent military actions, oil prices have surged, with Brent crude poised for its largest monthly increase on record. This volatility underscores the potential for further disruptions in supply, which could ripple through energy markets and impact broader economic conditions.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in this geopolitical landscape, as any escalation could lead to sustained fluctuations in oil prices and broader market reactions.
