Energy Transfer LP (ET) is positioning itself as a key player in the rapidly expanding data center market, driven by surging demand for electricity from AI workloads. With the global data center market projected to grow from approximately $300 billion in 2026 to nearly $699 billion by 2034, Energy Transfer's strategic agreements with major tech firms like Oracle and CloudBurst Data Centers highlight its potential to capitalize on this trend. The company’s diversified operations across crude oil, natural gas, and NGLs further mitigate exposure to commodity price volatility.

While Energy Transfer's stock has gained over 15% in 2026 and offers a compelling dividend yield of nearly 7%, investors should remain cautious. The company carries significant long-term debt exceeding $70 billion and faces challenges such as regulatory hurdles and the need for capital-intensive infrastructure. As such, potential investors should monitor upcoming earnings reports closely for any developments that could impact cash flow and dividend sustainability.

In summary, Energy Transfer presents a balanced investment opportunity, combining growth potential in the data center sector with the stability of long-term contracts, but investors must weigh the associated risks carefully.
