Microsoft has announced a significant price increase for its Microsoft 365 E7 enterprise plan, raising the monthly fee to $99 starting May 1, a 65% jump from its previous rate. This move is part of the company's strategy to enhance revenue from its AI offerings, particularly following a hefty $72 billion investment in AI data center infrastructure over the last two quarters. As Microsoft aims to leverage its Copilot AI to attract more high-paying enterprise customers, the company currently has only 15 million paying Copilot accounts out of 450 million Microsoft 365 commercial users.

This price hike underscores the competitive landscape in AI, where Microsoft faces pressure from rivals like ChatGPT, which boasts over 50 million subscribers. Investors should monitor how effectively Microsoft integrates its AI tools into existing systems to retain users and prevent them from switching to other platforms. A close watch on growth in both sales and subscriber numbers within the Microsoft 365 commercial segment will be crucial for assessing the success of this new pricing strategy.
