Phantom, a prominent Solana wallet provider, has received a significant boost from the U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC), allowing it to access regulated derivatives markets without the need for broker registration. This “first-of-its-kind” no-action letter enables Phantom to serve as a non-custodial interface, connecting users directly to CFTC-registered entities, such as futures commission merchants, while ensuring compliance with specific conditions.

This development is crucial for the financial markets as it paves the way for enhanced integration of crypto wallets into regulated environments, potentially increasing user confidence in digital asset trading. By facilitating access to regulated derivatives and event contracts directly within its app, Phantom could attract a broader user base and set a precedent for other crypto wallet providers looking to navigate regulatory landscapes.
