Arizona Attorney General Kris Mayes has filed 20 criminal counts against prediction market operator Kalshi, accusing it of running an unlicensed gambling business and illegally accepting bets on elections. This legal action intensifies Kalshi's ongoing battles with state regulators, particularly as the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) moves towards a more favorable regulatory framework for prediction markets, asserting its jurisdiction over event contracts.

The implications for financial markets are significant. If Kalshi is classified as a gambling operator rather than a derivatives venue, it could face substantial operational hurdles and legal challenges across various states. This case highlights the tension between state regulations and emerging federal guidelines, which could impact investor confidence and market dynamics in the prediction market sector.
