On March 13, 2026, Tom Thomas, VP and Corporate Controller of Integer Holdings Corporation (ITGR), sold 825 shares of the company’s stock at $85.00 each, as reported in an SEC Form 4 filing. This sale represents 15.85% of his direct holdings and is part of a pattern of insider selling, with Thomas having executed three sales since 2018, including a larger sale of 1,500 shares last April.

The sale comes amid challenging market conditions for Integer, whose shares have declined approximately 28% over the past year due to softer-than-expected projections. Despite this, the company reported a solid 8% year-over-year sales increase for 2025, driven primarily by its cardio and vascular segment. The transaction appears to be a routine liquidity measure rather than a signal of declining confidence, as Thomas has consistently managed his holdings.

For investors, the key takeaway is that while insider selling can raise red flags, this particular transaction seems more about personal liquidity than a shift in the company’s fundamentals. The focus should remain on Integer's ability to convert revenue growth into improved margins and a stronger balance sheet moving forward.
