The ongoing conflict in Iran has sent shockwaves through global markets, with oil prices surging and insurance costs for shipping skyrocketing due to heightened risks in the Strait of Hormuz. While the stock market initially appeared resilient, the reality is that the structural damage to supply chains and the economic implications of the war are significant and compounding. The $1.5 trillion commitment by major tech firms like Meta and Amazon to AI infrastructure is now under threat, as rising costs for components, shipping, and energy could undermine projected returns.

As these pressures mount, analysts warn that the market has not fully accounted for the friction introduced by the conflict, especially given the interconnected nature of semiconductor supply chains. With rising costs affecting everything from fertilizer to energy bills, the anticipated productivity gains from AI may be delayed, leading to a potential correction in stock valuations tied to these ambitious investments.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the situation in Iran could have far-reaching effects on the AI buildout and overall economic stability.
