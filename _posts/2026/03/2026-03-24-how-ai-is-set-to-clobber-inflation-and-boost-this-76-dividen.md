With futures markets signaling fewer expected rate cuts and inflation forecasts potentially indicating stagflation, investors are reassessing their strategies. The surge in oil prices has particularly affected the tax-free 7.6% dividend on municipal bonds, creating a buying opportunity for long-term investors who recognize the deflationary trends driven by AI advancements.

As Powell highlighted stagnant job growth in the private sector, the implications for corporate profitability and cost structures are becoming clearer. Many businesses are leveraging AI to reduce expenses and improve efficiency, which could lead to a gradual decline in inflation. This backdrop positions municipal bonds, like the Nuveen Municipal Credit Income Fund (NZF), as attractive investments, especially as their prices have dipped below earlier levels, erasing previous premiums.

Investors should consider taking advantage of the current pricing of NZF, as the anticipated shift in market sentiment towards AI's impact on inflation could soon close the window on this opportunity. The fund's tax-free status and strong yield make it a compelling choice for those looking to balance income with growth in a changing economic landscape.
