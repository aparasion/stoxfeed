European stocks are poised to open lower on Thursday as traders respond to escalating tensions in U.S.-Iran relations, following Iran's rejection of a U.S. ceasefire proposal. The Iranian Foreign Minister dismissed American plans, asserting that negotiations are not currently taking place and emphasizing Iran's control over the Strait of Hormuz, a critical shipping route. Iran's five conditions for a ceasefire include demands for U.S. and Israeli military action cessation and recognition of Iranian sovereignty.

This geopolitical uncertainty is impacting market sentiment, with U.S. equity futures slipping amid concerns that ongoing hostilities could disrupt global oil supply chains. Brent crude prices surged 1.5% after a previous decline, indicating that traders are reacting to the potential for further conflict in the region. Meanwhile, Asian markets also showed declines, reflecting the pervasive anxiety surrounding the situation.

Market professionals should closely monitor the developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as any escalation could significantly affect oil prices and broader market stability, influencing trading strategies in the coming days.
