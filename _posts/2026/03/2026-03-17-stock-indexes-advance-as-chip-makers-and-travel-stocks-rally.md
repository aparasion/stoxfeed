The S&P 500 Index closed up 0.25% on Tuesday, buoyed by gains in chipmakers and travel stocks, while lower bond yields provided additional support. The 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.20% following a weaker-than-expected ADP employment report, indicating a slowdown in hiring. Meanwhile, an unexpected rise in pending home sales added to the positive sentiment in the market.

However, the backdrop of rising crude oil prices, driven by renewed tensions in the Middle East, looms large over the markets. WTI crude surged over 2% as attacks on energy infrastructure raised concerns about supply disruptions, with Goldman Sachs warning that prices could exceed 2008 highs if the situation escalates. This volatility in oil prices could impact inflation expectations and Fed policy, especially with the FOMC meeting underway.
