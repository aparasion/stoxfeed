A recent report by Human Security reveals a significant shift in internet traffic dynamics, with automated traffic from AI and bots now surpassing human users. According to the State of AI Traffic report, automated traffic grew nearly eight times faster than human activity in 2025, driven by the widespread adoption of AI chatbots and large language models like ChatGPT and Google's Gemini. This shift indicates a fundamental change in how online interactions are structured, as machine-generated traffic is projected to dominate.

The implications for financial markets are profound, particularly for sectors reliant on digital engagement and advertising. As AI-driven interactions increase, companies that leverage this technology may see enhanced operational efficiencies and improved customer engagement. Conversely, businesses slow to adapt could face declining relevance and market share. The surge in automated traffic, which rose 187% within a year, underscores the urgency for firms to integrate AI solutions into their strategies.

Market professionals should consider the growing dominance of AI traffic as a critical factor influencing digital marketing strategies and investment decisions. Understanding this trend will be essential for navigating the evolving landscape of internet commerce and technology.
