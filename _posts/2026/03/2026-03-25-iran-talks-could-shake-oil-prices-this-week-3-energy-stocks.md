Tensions surrounding Iran have escalated, with President Trump issuing an ultimatum for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face U.S. military action against its power plants. Following a weekend of constructive dialogue, the deadline was extended by five days, raising hopes for a peaceful resolution. However, if talks collapse, oil prices are likely to surge, creating volatility in the energy market.

In this context, three energy stocks stand out as resilient investments. Energy Transfer (ET) is well-positioned with its diversified infrastructure and stable fee-based earnings, while Clearway Energy (CWEN) benefits from long-term power purchase agreements and a robust growth pipeline. Chevron (CVX) is also strategically positioned to capitalize on rising crude prices, with significant free cash flow projections bolstered by recent expansions and a strong balance sheet.

Investors should consider these stocks as potential safe havens amid geopolitical uncertainty, as their growth trajectories remain intact regardless of oil price fluctuations.
