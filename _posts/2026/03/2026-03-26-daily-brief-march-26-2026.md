**Market Brief for March 26, 2026**

**1. What Happened Today**  
The U.S. stock market experienced a notable decline, with major indexes like the Nasdaq and S&P 500 falling over 2%. This downturn was primarily driven by escalating geopolitical tensions, particularly regarding Iran, which has rejected U.S. ceasefire proposals, leading to a surge in oil prices above $100 per barrel. The energy sector saw mixed reactions, with traditional oil stocks benefiting while some renewable energy companies faced headwinds. Notably, memory chip stocks, including Micron and Samsung, were pressured following Google's announcement of its new AI model, TurboQuant, which negatively impacted market sentiment in the semiconductor sector. Bitcoin also slipped below $70,000 amid broader market uncertainty.

Looking ahead, market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, particularly any new negotiations or military actions involving Iran, which could further influence oil prices and market stability. Additionally, upcoming economic data, including U.S. jobless claims, may provide insights into the resilience of the economy amidst rising inflation concerns. Sentiment remains bearish as investors grapple with the implications of ongoing conflicts and their potential economic fallout, leading to cautious trading strategies and a shift towards safer assets.
