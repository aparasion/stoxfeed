The Russell 2000 has officially entered correction territory, dropping over 10% from its recent peak, marking it as the first major U.S. benchmark to do so in 2026. Initially, small caps showed resilience, buoyed by hopes of easier monetary policy, but have since faced significant pressure due to geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing war in Iran, which has driven Brent crude oil prices up by more than 50%.

This decline highlights the vulnerability of small-cap stocks to external shocks, especially given their higher exposure to cyclical sectors. The Russell 2000 has fallen more than 6% this month alone, while other indices like the Dow Jones and Nasdaq Composite hover just below correction thresholds, indicating a broader market sensitivity to economic headwinds.
