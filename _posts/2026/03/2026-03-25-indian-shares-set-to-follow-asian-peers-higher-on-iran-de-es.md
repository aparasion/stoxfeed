Indian shares are poised for a positive opening as optimism grows over a potential de-escalation in the Iran conflict. The U.S. has reportedly proposed a 15-point plan to Iran aimed at ending hostilities in the Middle East, which includes discussions for a one-month ceasefire. This news follows a volatile trading session where the Sensex and Nifty indexes rebounded, gaining 1.9% and 1.8%, respectively, after a previous decline.

The implications for financial markets are significant. A resolution to the Iran conflict could ease geopolitical tensions, leading to a drop in crude oil prices, which have recently pressured the rupee and contributed to foreign fund outflows. In early Asian trading, Brent crude futures fell 6% to $94 a barrel, while Asian markets surged, indicating a broader market response to the potential for peace talks.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that developments in U.S.-Iran negotiations could influence oil prices and currency stability, impacting trading strategies and portfolio allocations in the coming days.
