European stocks are poised for a flat opening on Tuesday amid ongoing volatility in global markets and fluctuating oil prices. The U.K.'s FTSE 100 is expected to rise marginally by 0.1%, while major indices like Germany's DAX and France's CAC 40 are projected to remain unchanged. Investors are weighing the implications of unrest in the Middle East, with oil prices dipping to just below $95 a barrel after a brief spike above $100, influenced by reports of a U.S. coalition to secure shipping routes.

This environment of uncertainty is heightened as traders anticipate the U.S. Federal Reserve's policy meeting, where expectations lean toward maintaining interest rates due to geopolitical tensions. The Fed's decision on Wednesday will be closely monitored, especially with pressure from political figures to lower rates. Meanwhile, earnings reports from Prudential and Poste Italiane, along with EU economic sentiment data, could provide further insights into market direction.
