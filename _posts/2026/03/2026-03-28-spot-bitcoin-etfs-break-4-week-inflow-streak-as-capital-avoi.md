Spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds (ETFs) experienced a significant shift, reporting $296.18 million in net outflows for the week ending Friday, effectively breaking a four-week inflow streak that had seen over $2.2 billion in new investments. This downturn follows a notable trend of daily withdrawals, with Thursday and Friday alone accounting for over $396 million, marking the largest single-day redemption since early March. Cumulative net inflows into Bitcoin ETFs now stand at $55.93 billion, but total net assets have decreased to $84.77 billion from over $90 billion just a week prior.

The decline in inflows and trading volume, which fell to $14.26 billion from $25.87 billion, highlights a cautious market environment. An analyst from Bitunix noted that while macro conditions appear stable, underlying geopolitical risks persist, leading Bitcoin to behave more as a reflection of liquidity rather than a breakout asset. This indicates that the market may remain range-bound, with limited directional risk appetite.

Market professionals should note that the recent outflows from both Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs suggest a potential shift in investor sentiment, warranting close monitoring of liquidity trends and macroeconomic developments for future trading strategies.
