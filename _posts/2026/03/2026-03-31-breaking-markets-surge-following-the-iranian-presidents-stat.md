Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian's recent statement signaling a willingness to de-escalate tensions with the U.S. and Israel has sparked a significant rally in global financial markets. Pezeshkian emphasized that Iran seeks peace only with formal security guarantees against future attacks, framing the military actions of the U.S. and Israel as violations of international law. This diplomatic overture is seen as a pivotal moment, leading to a sharp decline in geopolitical risk premiums.

The immediate market response was robust, with the S&P 500 climbing 2.55%, the Nasdaq rising 3.27%, and the Dow Jones gaining over 1,000 points. Additionally, U.S. Treasury yields fell, reflecting a shift in investor sentiment towards reduced risk. Notably, WTI crude oil prices dipped below $101, indicating easing concerns over supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz.

However, caution remains essential as Iran simultaneously threatens military action against the UAE, complicating the diplomatic landscape. The sustainability of this market optimism hinges on the U.S. response to Iran's conditions for peace.
