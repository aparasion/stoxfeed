Gold prices continued their downward trajectory on Thursday, hitting a one-month low as hawkish comments from the Federal Reserve and geopolitical tensions weighed heavily on the market. Spot gold fell 2.2% to $4,712.50 an ounce, while U.S. gold futures dropped 3.7%. The dollar's strength, bolstered by Fed Chair Jerome Powell's remarks on inflation and rising short-term Treasury yields, further pressured bullion prices.

The Fed's decision to maintain interest rates, coupled with unexpected wholesale inflation data, has heightened market volatility. Meanwhile, escalating conflicts in the Middle East, particularly attacks on oil and gas infrastructure, have sent Brent crude prices soaring nearly 10% to $118 a barrel. The uncertainty surrounding energy supply routes and geopolitical stability adds another layer of complexity for investors, impacting both commodity and equity markets.
