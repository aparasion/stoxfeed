President Trump has issued a stark warning to the U.K. and France, stating that the U.S. will no longer support its allies in military actions against Iran. His comments, made via Truth Social, criticized both nations for their refusal to participate in military operations and highlighted France's denial of airspace for U.S. military flights headed to Israel. This rhetoric underscores a growing rift between the U.S. and its European allies regarding Iran's aggressive posture in the Strait of Hormuz.

The implications for financial markets are significant, particularly in the energy sector. The ongoing conflict has already disrupted oil and gas shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical maritime route, leading to increased volatility in oil prices. Trump's threats to escalate military actions could further destabilize the region, potentially driving oil prices higher and impacting global supply chains.

Market professionals should monitor developments closely, as any escalation could lead to substantial shifts in energy markets and broader economic implications, especially for countries reliant on Gulf oil.
