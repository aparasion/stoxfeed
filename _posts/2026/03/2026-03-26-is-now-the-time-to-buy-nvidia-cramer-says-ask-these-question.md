The ongoing conflict in Iran is reshaping investment strategies, pushing investors to adopt a military-like approach rather than traditional stock-picking, according to CNBC's Jim Cramer. With uncertainty surrounding the war's resolution, Cramer emphasizes the importance of assessing how geopolitical events impact specific stocks, particularly in the tech sector. He highlights Nvidia as a key example, noting its recent 3% decline since the conflict began, while urging investors to consider whether this drop is linked to the war or other market dynamics.

Cramer points out that while Nvidia's direct exposure to the conflict is hard to quantify, broader factors like rising interest rates and memory prices could influence its performance. Despite potential short-term volatility, he remains optimistic about Nvidia's strong demand, especially following insights from the recent Nvidia GTC conference. 

Ultimately, Cramer advises investors to view this as an opportunity to acquire high-quality stocks at lower prices, suggesting that a proactive approach may yield benefits as the situation evolves.
