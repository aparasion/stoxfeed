Bitcoin's recent rally is encountering significant resistance as traders brace for the Federal Reserve's meeting on Wednesday. After briefly reaching $76,000, Bitcoin retreated to around $74,000, with analysts warning that hawkish remarks from Fed Chair Jerome Powell could dampen sentiment across risk assets, including cryptocurrencies. The focus is on how the Fed will address rising inflation, particularly in light of the Producer Price Index (PPI) data set to be released the same day.

The implications for the market are substantial. A combination of strong PPI data and a hawkish Fed stance could lead to a stronger dollar and increased pressure on equities and crypto. Currently, expectations are shifting towards a prolonged period of unchanged rates, with market probabilities for cuts pushed further into 2026. Analysts suggest that Bitcoin's price may remain capped between $74,000 and $76,000 in the near term.
