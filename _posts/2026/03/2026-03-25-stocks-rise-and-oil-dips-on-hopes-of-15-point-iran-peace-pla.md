A US court has ordered Meta Platforms Inc. to pay $375 million following a jury's ruling that the company facilitated harm, including child sexual exploitation, on its social media platforms, Facebook and Instagram. This landmark case highlights the increasing legal scrutiny tech giants face regarding user safety and content moderation practices.

The financial implications for Meta are significant, as this fine adds to ongoing regulatory pressures that could impact the company's stock performance. Investors may be concerned about the potential for further legal challenges and the associated costs, which could affect earnings and overall market sentiment toward the tech sector. The ruling also underscores a broader trend of heightened accountability for digital platforms, which could lead to stricter regulations across the industry.
