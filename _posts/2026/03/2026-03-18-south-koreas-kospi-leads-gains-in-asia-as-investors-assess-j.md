South Korea's Kospi index surged over 3% on Wednesday, leading gains across Asia as investors reacted to Japan's robust trade data and awaited the U.S. Federal Reserve's interest rate decision. The Fed is widely expected to maintain rates between 3.5% and 3.75%, which has contributed to a positive sentiment in the markets. Japan's Nikkei 225 and Topix also saw significant increases, buoyed by a 4.2% rise in exports for February, outperforming expectations.

This uptick in Asian markets comes amid ongoing geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East, where escalating conflicts have raised concerns about energy supply disruptions. U.S. oil prices edged up to $96.27, reflecting these supply worries. Meanwhile, U.S. stock futures showed little movement ahead of the Fed's announcement, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite both closing higher in the previous session.
