Markets are rapidly recalibrating expectations for Federal Reserve monetary policy, shifting from anticipated rate cuts to potential hikes amid rising inflation concerns fueled by escalating Middle East tensions. The CME FedWatch Tool now indicates nearly a 30% chance that the fed funds rate will exceed its current range of 3.50%-3.75% by year-end, a stark reversal from earlier predictions of cuts. This change is largely driven by surging oil prices, which have jumped from about $70 to $111 per barrel, pushing long-term Treasury yields higher.

The implications for various asset classes are significant. While oil prices remain elevated, traditional safe havens like gold have faltered, dropping around 20% since the onset of geopolitical tensions. In contrast, Bitcoin has shown short-term resilience, holding steady in the $65,000-$70,000 range, although it continues to lag behind equities and gold over longer time frames. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for sustained inflationary pressures, which could influence investment strategies across sectors, particularly in energy and defense, as military spending is likely to increase in response to geopolitical developments.
