Palantir Technologies (PLTR +1.70%) has seen its stock surge 1,500% since its direct listing in September 2020, driven by impressive sales growth and profitability. Analysts project robust future growth, with revenue and EPS expected to increase at CAGRs of 49% and 54% from 2025 to 2028, positioning Palantir among the fastest-growing tech stocks. However, the company faces significant challenges that could impact its trajectory.

Palantir's reliance on government contracts, which accounted for over half of its revenue in 2025, raises concerns about sustainability, especially as geopolitical tensions ease and spending contracts. Additionally, its controversial reputation, particularly regarding its surveillance tools used by agencies like ICE, may hinder expansion in privacy-conscious markets. Coupled with a high valuation—trading at 142 times earnings—these factors could limit upside potential, despite strong operational performance.

Investors should approach Palantir with caution, weighing its growth prospects against the risks associated with its business model and market perception before making investment decisions.
