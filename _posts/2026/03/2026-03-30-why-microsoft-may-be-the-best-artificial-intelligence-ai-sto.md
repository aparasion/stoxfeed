Microsoft (MSFT) is emerging as a compelling investment option for retirees seeking exposure to artificial intelligence (AI) stocks without excessive risk. Despite a 25% decline in its stock this year, the tech giant's diversified business model—spanning gaming, cloud computing, and office software—positions it well to leverage AI across various segments. Notably, Microsoft reported double-digit growth in its service revenue, which constitutes 80% of its total income, indicating robust demand for its offerings.

The company's strong financials, with over $119 billion in profit over the last year, and an attractive price-to-earnings ratio of 22, align with the S&P 500 average, making it a safer bet in the tech sector. For risk-averse investors, Microsoft's consistent performance and modest dividend yield of about 1% further enhance its appeal as a long-term investment.

In summary, Microsoft presents a balanced opportunity for those looking to invest in AI while minimizing exposure to volatility, making it a strategic choice for conservative portfolios.
