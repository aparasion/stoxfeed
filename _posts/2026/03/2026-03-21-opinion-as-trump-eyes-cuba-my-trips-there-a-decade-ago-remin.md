The White House has intensified its stance against Cuba by cutting off its oil supply and hinting at a potential "friendly takeover" amid ongoing military operations in Venezuela and Iran. President Trump has suggested that Cuba could be his next target, stating, "They're a very weakened nation right now," which raises significant geopolitical concerns for the region and beyond.

This development could have profound implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors tied to energy and commodities. The oil shortage is already straining Cuba's economy, which may lead to increased volatility in related markets. Investors should be aware that any escalation in U.S.-Cuba relations could impact regional trade dynamics and energy prices, especially if further sanctions are imposed or if military action is considered.
