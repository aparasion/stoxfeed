European stocks are poised to extend their recent gains as optimism grows around potential peace negotiations between the U.S. and Iran. Oil prices dropped over 4% in Asian trading following President Trump's assertion that discussions are ongoing, despite Iranian officials disputing this. The U.S. has reportedly proposed a 15-point plan to end the conflict, while Israel's military actions in the region continue, adding complexity to the situation.

This diplomatic momentum is impacting market sentiment, with Asian markets rising and U.S. equity futures showing strength. However, the backdrop remains volatile, as tensions persist with Iran's military actions and the potential for U.S. troop deployments in the region. Notably, gold prices surged over 2%, reflecting investor caution amid geopolitical uncertainties.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the delicate balance between optimism for de-escalation and the reality of ongoing military tensions, which could lead to further volatility in energy prices and broader market movements.
