Despite a broader market pullback from 2026 highs, two tech stocks—Palantir Technologies and Sandisk—are defying the trend and trading near their peaks. Palantir (PLTR), heavily involved in U.S. defense and intelligence, has seen its stock surge, bolstered by its AI Platform (AIP) which is gaining traction in the commercial sector. The company reported a remarkable 70% revenue growth last quarter, although its forward price-to-sales multiple sits at a steep 51, raising questions about valuation sustainability.

Meanwhile, Sandisk (SNDK), recently spun off from Western Digital, capitalizes on the booming NAND flash memory market, driven by supply constraints and soaring demand for high-performance solid-state drives. With a forward P/E of just 8, Sandisk appears more attractively valued, although its cyclical nature and dependence on emerging technologies like high-bandwidth flash could introduce volatility.

For market professionals, these stocks highlight the divergence within the tech sector amid economic uncertainty, with Palantir representing a high-growth narrative and Sandisk offering potential value amidst speculative risks.
