Escalating tensions in the Middle East are driving significant market reactions as the Houthis launched missile and drone attacks against Israel, prompting Israeli strikes in Tehran. The U.S. military presence in the region is also intensifying, with the deployment of special forces and Marines, while Pakistan prepares to host U.S.-Iran talks that could influence oil infrastructure discussions. The Israeli military has confirmed it will not participate in any potential U.S. ground operations in Iran.

The geopolitical climate is impacting commodity markets, with Brent crude oil prices opening higher at approximately $107.7 per barrel, though they retreated amid uncertainty over the Strait of Hormuz. Gold and silver prices continue to rise as investors seek safe-haven assets, reflecting concerns over inflation and potential conflict. Meanwhile, the Bank of Japan is signaling further monetary tightening, which has strengthened the yen against the dollar.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving geopolitical situation and its implications for oil prices and safe-haven assets, as well as upcoming economic indicators such as the U.S. NFP report, which could influence Federal Reserve policy amid these tensions.
