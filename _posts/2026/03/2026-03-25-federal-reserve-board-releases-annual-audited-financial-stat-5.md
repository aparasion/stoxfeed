The Federal Reserve Board has released its annual audited financial statements for 2025, confirming the integrity of its financial reporting with unqualified opinions from an independent accounting firm. The audit, which encompasses the financials of the 12 Federal Reserve Banks and the Board itself, found no material misstatements, aligning with applicable auditing standards. This comprehensive review also included assessments of internal controls over financial reporting.

These audited statements provide crucial insights into the Fed's assets, liabilities, and operations as of December 31, 2025, reflecting its ongoing response to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. Such transparency is essential for market participants, as it enhances trust in the Fed's financial health and operational decisions, which can influence monetary policy and market stability.

Market professionals should note that the Fed's commitment to regular audits and transparency may bolster confidence in its financial management, potentially impacting interest rates and liquidity conditions in the broader economy.
