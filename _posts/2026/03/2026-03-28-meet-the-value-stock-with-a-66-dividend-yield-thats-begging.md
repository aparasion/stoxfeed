General Mills (GIS) has seen its stock plummet 36.7% over the past year and 40% over the last decade, significantly underperforming the S&P 500's 222% gain. The decline has pushed its dividend yield to an attractive 6.6%, well above competitors like Coca-Cola and PepsiCo. However, the company is facing a challenging outlook, forecasting a 16% to 20% drop in adjusted earnings per share for fiscal 2026, following a 7% decline in fiscal 2025, largely due to inflationary pressures and shifting consumer sentiment.

Despite these headwinds, General Mills is adapting its product lineup to align with health trends, focusing on protein and fiber innovation. The company is seeing success in its Nature Valley and Cheerios Protein brands, which could help stabilize its performance. With a low valuation and a commitment to maintaining its dividend, General Mills presents a compelling opportunity for income-focused investors.

For market professionals, General Mills’ current valuation and dividend yield make it a noteworthy candidate for portfolios seeking high-yield stocks, especially as the company pivots to meet evolving consumer preferences.
