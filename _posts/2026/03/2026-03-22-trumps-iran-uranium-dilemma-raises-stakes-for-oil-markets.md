President Trump is faced with a critical decision regarding U.S. military involvement in Iran, particularly concerning the country's stockpile of enriched uranium. With airstrikes proving ineffective, the prospect of deploying ground troops is being considered, a move that could escalate tensions in an already volatile region. As the situation unfolds, global energy markets are reacting, with Brent crude prices recently retreating from peaks above $118 per barrel, now trading around $112.02, while WTI crude is at $98.32.

The implications for financial markets are significant. Analysts warn that continued instability in the Middle East could lead to oil prices soaring to unprecedented levels, potentially reaching $200 per barrel if the Strait of Hormuz remains compromised. This situation is exacerbated by a reported cut in global oil supply by up to 8.2 million barrels per day due to the conflict, creating a substantial deficit in the market.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in U.S. military strategy and its potential impact on oil supply and prices, as any escalation could lead to heightened volatility in energy markets and broader economic repercussions.
