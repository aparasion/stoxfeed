The Bank of Japan (BOJ) is facing a challenging economic landscape as it navigates persistent inflation, which has exceeded its 2% target for 45 consecutive months. The recent escalation of tensions in the Middle East threatens to exacerbate this situation, with rising oil prices potentially leading to "cost-push" inflation—an undesirable scenario for the BOJ, which prefers inflation driven by wage growth. The central bank's decision to maintain steady rates reflects its caution amid these external pressures.

This backdrop complicates the BOJ's policy strategy, as higher global energy prices could significantly impact consumer prices in Japan, a nation heavily reliant on oil imports. Analysts suggest that a 10% rise in energy prices could translate to a 0.7% increase in overall inflation, further straining real wages that have already been declining. The BOJ may find itself in a bind, balancing the need to control inflation while supporting economic growth.
