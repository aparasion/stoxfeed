Recent discussions around Social Security have highlighted three prevalent myths that could significantly impact retirees' financial decisions. Contrary to popular belief, claiming benefits early due to fears of the program's insolvency could lead to a lifelong reduction in monthly payments. Specifically, individuals born in 1960 or later may face a 30% decrease if they claim at age 62 instead of waiting until the full retirement age of 67.

Additionally, many assume they must stop working upon claiming benefits, but once full retirement age is reached, there are no earnings restrictions. Prior to that age, understanding the earnings test limits is crucial to avoid benefit reductions. Lastly, there’s a misconception about delaying spousal benefits; unlike individual retirement benefits, spousal benefits do not increase if claimed after full retirement age.

For financial professionals, these insights underscore the importance of educating clients on Social Security strategies to optimize their retirement income and avoid costly mistakes.
