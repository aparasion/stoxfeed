The UK investment firm’s platform has generated 2,916 surveillance alerts over the past five months due to its Contracts for Difference (CFD) activity, raising concerns among regulators about market integrity. This surge in alerts highlights the ongoing scrutiny of trading practices as firms adapt to a post-pandemic environment, where remote work and home schooling have posed operational challenges.

The investment banking sector, previously characterized by rapid growth, is now facing increased pressure as regulatory changes reshape compensation structures. Notably, Wall Street and UK banks are moving away from fixed allowances, which could impact talent retention and recruitment strategies. Meanwhile, the hedge fund industry saw a notable uptick in activity, with 562 new fund launches last year, the highest since 2021, indicating a potential shift in investor sentiment.

Market professionals should closely monitor these developments, as the evolving regulatory landscape and changes in trading practices could significantly influence market dynamics and investment strategies moving forward.
