Gold prices experienced a significant drop on Monday, with spot gold falling 4.7% to $4,279.27 an ounce, while U.S. gold futures plummeted 6.4% to $4,315.54. This decline is largely attributed to rising inflation and concerns over potential interest rate hikes by major central banks, driven by escalating energy prices amid the ongoing conflict in Iran. The market is reacting to signals from central banks, including the Federal Reserve, indicating a readiness to raise rates to combat inflationary pressures.

The implications for the financial markets are notable. As energy costs rise due to geopolitical tensions, risk-off sentiment is dominating, pushing the dollar higher and Treasury yields near an eight-month peak. Investors are increasingly wary of how these dynamics will affect inflation and global economic growth, especially in light of aggressive rhetoric from U.S. and Iranian leaders regarding military actions.

Market professionals should closely monitor central bank communications and geopolitical developments, as these factors are likely to influence not only gold prices but broader market trends in the coming weeks.
