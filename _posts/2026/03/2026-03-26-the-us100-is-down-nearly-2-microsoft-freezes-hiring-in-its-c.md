Wall Street is experiencing a notable downturn, with the Nasdaq dropping over 2% and S&P 500 futures hitting new session lows. This decline is largely attributed to Microsoft’s announcement of a hiring freeze in its cloud computing and sales divisions, which raises concerns about the overall health of the tech sector and the sustainability of the current AI-driven market enthusiasm.

Additionally, geopolitical tensions are resurfacing, particularly following the death of Iran's leader Ali Khamenei. The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) is increasingly vocal about pursuing nuclear capabilities, adding a layer of uncertainty that markets are reacting to. This development could impact energy prices and overall market sentiment, as investors weigh the implications of potential conflict in the region.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the dual pressure from both corporate earnings concerns and geopolitical risks, suggesting a cautious approach to tech investments and broader market exposure in the current climate.
