Iran's recent launch of two intermediate-range ballistic missiles targeting Diego Garcia, a key U.S.-U.K. military base, marks a significant escalation in regional tensions. Although neither missile struck the base—one failed mid-flight and the other was intercepted by a U.S. warship—the incident underscores Iran's growing capabilities and willingness to project power beyond its borders, as reported by the Wall Street Journal.

This development could have far-reaching implications for global markets, particularly in defense and energy sectors. Increased military tensions in the Middle East often lead to volatility in oil prices and could impact defense stocks as investors assess the potential for heightened conflict and increased military spending. Additionally, the geopolitical ramifications of the U.K.'s planned sovereignty transfer of the Chagos Islands to Mauritius could further complicate the strategic landscape.
