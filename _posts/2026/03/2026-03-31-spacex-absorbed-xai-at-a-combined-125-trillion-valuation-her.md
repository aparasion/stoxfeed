Elon Musk's empire is gearing up for a monumental year, highlighted by SpaceX's merger with xAI, which has set the stage for what could be the largest IPO in history. The merger valued SpaceX at approximately $1 trillion and xAI at $250 billion, bringing their combined valuation to $1.25 trillion. As SpaceX aims for a staggering IPO valuation of $1.75 trillion, the financial community is keenly watching how this will impact investor sentiment and market dynamics.

This ambitious IPO could raise between $40 billion and $80 billion, making it a significant event in capital markets. However, the projected valuation raises concerns about the stock's potential performance post-IPO. With SpaceX's reported revenue of $16 billion in 2025, and possibly $20 billion when including xAI, the anticipated market cap would suggest an extreme valuation, potentially leading to disappointing returns for early investors.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach the SpaceX IPO with caution. Despite the excitement, a steep valuation may warrant a wait-and-see strategy rather than immediate investment.
