Warren Buffett may have stepped back from his role as chief stock-picker at Berkshire Hathaway, but his investment philosophy continues to influence the conglomerate's portfolio. Three notable stocks that reflect his enduring legacy include Coca-Cola, Visa, and Berkshire Hathaway itself, each offering unique strengths for long-term investors.

Coca-Cola remains a cornerstone of Berkshire’s holdings, with a history of consistent dividend growth and a robust market presence, despite increasing competition and changing consumer preferences. Visa, while a smaller position, showcases resilience and adaptability, reporting revenue growth even amid economic fluctuations. Meanwhile, investing in Berkshire Hathaway provides a straightforward way to align with Buffett’s investment strategy, granting exposure to a diverse array of companies, including many private entities.
