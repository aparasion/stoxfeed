Berkshire Hathaway has significantly increased its stake in Chevron, acquiring over 8 million shares in Q4 2025, which now represents 7.24% of its equity portfolio. This move comes as WTI crude prices approach $100 per barrel, positioning Berkshire's investment as strategically timed. The company has also bolstered its energy exposure by closing the acquisition of OxyChem from Occidental Petroleum, indicating a clear commitment to the sector.

Chevron's robust performance in 2025, marked by record production and cash flow despite a challenging pricing environment, underscores the strength of its operational scale and shareholder returns. The company returned $27.1 billion to shareholders and raised its dividend for the 39th consecutive year, demonstrating resilience that may enhance its attractiveness as oil prices rise.
