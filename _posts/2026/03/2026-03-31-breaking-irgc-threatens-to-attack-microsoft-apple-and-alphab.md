The Iranian Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has escalated tensions by labeling 18 major U.S. tech firms, including Microsoft, Apple, Google, Intel, and Boeing, as "legitimate targets" for potential attacks starting April 1. This unprecedented warning ties the companies to U.S. and Israeli operations against Iran, marking a significant shift in rhetoric towards the civilian tech sector. 

The immediate market impact was palpable, with shares of the targeted companies reversing early gains as investors reacted to the heightened geopolitical risk. Concurrently, oil prices surged, reflecting market sensitivity to any threats of supply disruptions in the region. Historically, while the IRGC has issued similar threats without follow-through, the specific April 1 deadline adds urgency that could influence market sentiment more profoundly this time.

Market participants should closely monitor developments, particularly Washington's response and any statements from the affected companies regarding their operations in the Middle East, as these could shape both tech and commodities market dynamics in the coming days.
