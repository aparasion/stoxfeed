**End-of-Day Market Brief - March 27, 2026**

**1. What Happened Today**  
Today's market activity was heavily influenced by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly concerning Iran, which led to significant volatility in oil prices. WTI crude surged over 5% to nearly $100 per barrel, while Brent crude approached $110 amid fears of supply disruptions. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq indices fell by approximately 1% and 1.5%, respectively, marking one of the worst trading days since the onset of the Iran conflict. Notably, technology stocks suffered, with major players like Microsoft and Meta down significantly, reflecting broader investor concerns about the sustainability of growth in the tech sector. Bitcoin also faced pressure, dropping below $68,000 as long positions were liquidated.

**2. Expectations for the Next Session**  
As market participants look ahead, attention will focus on the potential for further escalation in the Middle East and its implications for oil supply and inflation. Investors are also awaiting the final University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index, which could provide insights into domestic economic health. With rising Treasury yields and persistent inflation concerns, sentiment remains bearish, particularly in the tech sector, as analysts debate whether the current downturn signals a prolonged correction or a buying opportunity. The ongoing geopolitical situation adds an additional layer of uncertainty, leading to cautious trading strategies.
