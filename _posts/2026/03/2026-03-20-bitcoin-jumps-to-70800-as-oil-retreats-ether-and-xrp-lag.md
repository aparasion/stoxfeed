Bitcoin surged to $70,800, gaining over 1% as oil prices retreated, following a coordinated effort by major economies to stabilize energy markets. While Bitcoin led the crypto recovery, other cryptocurrencies like ether and XRP lagged, posting smaller gains of less than 1%. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude fell nearly 2% to $93.80, influenced by geopolitical tensions and announcements from countries like the U.K. and Japan regarding energy market stability.

The decline in oil prices is significant for the financial markets, particularly as it coincides with increased uncertainty surrounding inflation and growth, which has led traders to recalibrate expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts. The S&P 500's recent dip below its 200-day moving average signals a bearish trend, raising concerns that risk aversion in equities could impact cryptocurrencies and other risk assets.
