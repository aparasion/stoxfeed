Appaloosa Management, led by David Tepper, has significantly reduced its stake in Whirlpool, selling 1.59 million shares valued at approximately $117.38 million in Q4 2026. This sale reflects a broader decline in the fund's position value, which fell by $150.23 million due to both the sale and adverse market conditions. Following this transaction, Appaloosa's ownership in Whirlpool dropped from 5.85% to 4.07% of its reportable assets under management.

This divestment comes as Whirlpool’s stock struggles, down 42% over the past year and trailing the S&P 500 by 59 percentage points. The company faces challenges including tariff uncertainties, sluggish consumer spending, and a substantial debt burden. Despite management's optimistic guidance for 2026, the recent dilution from a stock offering raises concerns about the company’s financial stability and future growth potential.
