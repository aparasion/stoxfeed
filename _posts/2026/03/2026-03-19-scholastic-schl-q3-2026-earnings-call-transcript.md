Scholastic Corporation has made significant strides in its fiscal 2026 third quarter, highlighted by a successful $400 million sale-leaseback transaction that has bolstered its balance sheet. This move has enabled the company to initiate a $300 million share repurchase program, including a $200 million modified Dutch auction tender offer priced between $36 and $40 per share, potentially reducing outstanding shares by 25% if fully executed.

Despite a slight revenue decline to $329.1 million, Scholastic's strategic focus on capital allocation and shareholder returns is evident, with over $650 million returned to shareholders since fiscal 2022. The company reaffirmed its adjusted EBITDA guidance of $146 million to $156 million, while also addressing challenges in its Education and Trade segments due to timing and market conditions.

For market professionals, Scholastic's proactive measures in capital management and its growth initiatives in children's publishing and Book Fairs signal a commitment to long-term value creation.
