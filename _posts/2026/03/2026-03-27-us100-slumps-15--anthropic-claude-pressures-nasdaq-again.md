Wall Street is grappling with negative sentiment as the Nasdaq 100 futures contract drops over 1.5% amid escalating tensions involving Iran, the U.S., and Israel. Concurrently, U.S. 10-year Treasury yields are climbing above 4.45%, and the dollar index is strengthening, reflecting investor anxiety about rising oil prices and their potential implications for economic activity and Federal Reserve policy. 

The market is particularly concerned about the impact of a slowdown on companies heavily invested in AI infrastructure, which had previously bolstered semiconductor stocks. Notably, firms like Microsoft are already feeling pressure, signaling a broader vulnerability in the tech sector. Additionally, a leaked draft from AI startup Anthropic regarding its new model, Claude "Mythos," raises competitive concerns for cybersecurity firms, potentially affecting their margins and client retention.

As the Nasdaq 100 dips more than 10% from its all-time high, this correction may signal a shift in market dynamics, prompting investors to reassess their positions in technology and related sectors.
