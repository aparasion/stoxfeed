President Donald Trump is set to meet with Chinese President Xi Jinping in Beijing on May 14 and 15, marking a significant diplomatic engagement that has been postponed from its original late March date. This meeting comes amid heightened geopolitical tensions due to the ongoing conflict involving Iran, which the Trump administration anticipates will last around five weeks. The timing of the summit raises questions about the U.S. expectations regarding the war's resolution and its implications for U.S.-China relations.

This meeting could have substantial implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to trade policies and geopolitical stability. Investors will be closely monitoring any discussions related to tariffs, trade agreements, and economic cooperation, as these factors can influence stock performance across various sectors, including technology, manufacturing, and commodities.

A key takeaway for market professionals is to prepare for potential volatility leading up to and following the summit, as any announcements or shifts in tone from either leader could significantly impact market sentiment and trading strategies.
