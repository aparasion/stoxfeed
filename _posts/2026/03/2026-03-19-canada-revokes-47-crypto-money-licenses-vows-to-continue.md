Canada's Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre (FINTRAC) has revoked the registrations of 50 money services businesses (MSBs) this year, with 47 of those linked to the cryptocurrency sector. The latest action involved the cancellation of 23 registrations, reflecting a significant acceleration in regulatory enforcement aimed at combating money laundering. Finance Minister François-Philippe Champagne emphasized that the government is committed to increasing transparency and compliance within the cryptocurrency landscape.

This crackdown comes at a time when the Financial Action Task Force estimates that 2 to 5% of global GDP is laundered through traditional financial systems, contrasting with Chainalysis's finding that less than 1% of crypto transactions are tied to illicit activities. The heightened scrutiny on MSBs, particularly those dealing with cryptocurrencies, could influence investor sentiment and operational strategies within the sector, as firms face increased regulatory hurdles.
