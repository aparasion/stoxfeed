Nasdaq 100 (US100) futures have risen nearly 1% today, buoyed by expectations of a potential reduction in US military involvement in the Iran conflict. Donald Trump’s recent comments suggest a willingness to withdraw troops even before the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz is addressed. Meanwhile, Senator Marco Rubio's operational objectives indicate a focus on neutralizing Iran's military capabilities rather than reopening critical shipping routes.

This development is significant for the financial markets, as easing geopolitical tensions could stabilize investor sentiment and support a rebound in tech stocks, which heavily influence the Nasdaq. Historical patterns show that similar price movements have often led to short-term recoveries, with US100 potentially targeting the 24,000 level if bullish momentum builds. However, elevated volatility and ongoing military operations in the region pose risks to sustained gains.

Market professionals should monitor these geopolitical developments closely, as they may significantly impact market dynamics and trading strategies in the coming days.
