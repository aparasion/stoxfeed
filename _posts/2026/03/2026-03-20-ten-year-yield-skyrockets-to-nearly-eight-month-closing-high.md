Treasuries experienced a significant sell-off on Friday, with the yield on the benchmark ten-year note climbing 11.0 basis points to 4.391 percent—its highest closing level since late July 2025. This decline in bond prices reflects ongoing volatility in crude oil markets, which have been heavily influenced by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The price of crude oil surged over 3 percent amid reports of new attacks on energy infrastructure, although it later fluctuated due to speculation about potential U.S. sanctions relief on Iranian oil.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial, as rising yields typically signal concerns over inflation and interest rate stability. With the CME Group's FedWatch Tool indicating that the Federal Reserve is unlikely to cut rates this year, the trajectory of interest rates remains a critical focus for investors.
