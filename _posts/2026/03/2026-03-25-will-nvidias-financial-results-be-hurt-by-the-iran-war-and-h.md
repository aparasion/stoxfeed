Nvidia (NVDA) is facing potential impacts from rising supply chain costs linked to the ongoing Iran war, which has disrupted oil transportation through the Strait of Hormuz. As crude oil prices surge, transportation costs for Nvidia's components, particularly those sourced from Taiwan, are likely to increase. This could pressure the company's gross and profit margins, as higher freight expenses are passed on to customers.

Despite these challenges, Nvidia's strong demand for its AI-enabled GPUs may allow it to mitigate some of the cost increases. The company's size and market position provide leverage in negotiations with transportation firms, and its ability to adjust pricing with customers could help maintain profitability. Analysts remain optimistic about Nvidia's upcoming fiscal Q1 results, anticipating they will exceed Wall Street expectations, bolstered by CEO Jensen Huang's bullish outlook.

In summary, while the Iran conflict presents short-term headwinds, Nvidia's robust financials and market demand position it well for sustained growth, making it a stock to watch in the coming months.
