Abbott Laboratories (ABT) experienced a significant drop of 22% from its 52-week high following a disappointing quarterly report that fell short of revenue expectations, with Q4 revenue rising only 4.4% year-over-year to $11.5 billion. The decline was primarily driven by underperformance in its diagnostic and nutrition segments, raising concerns among investors about the company's growth trajectory.

Despite these challenges, Abbott's medical device division showed resilience, with a 12.3% year-over-year revenue increase to $5.7 billion. The company’s acquisition of Exact Sciences for $21 billion is poised to enhance its diagnostic capabilities, particularly in colorectal cancer screening, addressing a substantial market opportunity. Additionally, Abbott's strong dividend yield of over 2.3% positions it favorably against the S&P 500 average.
