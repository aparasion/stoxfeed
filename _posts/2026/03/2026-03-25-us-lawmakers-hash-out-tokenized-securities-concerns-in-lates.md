Executives from the crypto industry testified before the US House of Representatives Committee on Financial Services, advocating for the application of existing investor protections and financial regulations to tokenized securities. This hearing coincides with discussions surrounding the Capital Markets Technology Modernization Act of 2026, which aims to address the implications of asset tokenization on capital markets while balancing innovation with investor safety and market integrity.

The panel emphasized that tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) could significantly reduce transaction costs and settlement times, enhancing capital market efficiency. Summer Mersinger, CEO of the Blockchain Association, highlighted that tokenization modernizes financial processes by improving transparency and lowering costs without altering existing securities laws. Lawmakers raised concerns about compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) regulations, with industry representatives suggesting that technological solutions can embed compliance measures directly into token transactions.

A key takeaway for market professionals is the potential for tokenized assets to streamline operations in capital markets, but the ongoing regulatory dialogue underscores the need for robust compliance frameworks to address potential risks associated with this innovation.
