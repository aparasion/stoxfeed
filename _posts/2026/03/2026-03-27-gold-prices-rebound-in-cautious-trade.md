Gold prices climbed 1.2% on Friday, reaching $4,432 an ounce, as investors reacted to easing tensions in the Middle East. This uptick comes after a nearly 3% drop in the previous session, but gold still faces a weekly loss driven by inflation concerns and interest rate hike anxieties. The U.S. dollar has gained traction amid ongoing risk aversion, particularly as military actions escalate between Israel and Iran, complicating the geopolitical landscape.

The backdrop of rising U.S. Treasury yields, now around 4.42%, adds to the uncertainty in financial markets, influencing both gold's appeal as a safe haven and broader market sentiment. The potential for increased military presence in the region and conflicting reports on diplomatic negotiations further complicate the outlook for commodities and equities alike.

Market professionals should closely monitor these developments, as shifts in geopolitical stability and U.S. monetary policy could significantly impact asset valuations and investment strategies in the coming weeks.
