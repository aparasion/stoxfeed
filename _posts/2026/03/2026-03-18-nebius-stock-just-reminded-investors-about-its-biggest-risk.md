Nebius Group (NBIS) has emerged as a standout performer in the tech sector, with its stock skyrocketing approximately 370% over the past year, fueled by the surge in artificial intelligence (AI) investments. The company specializes in providing AI cloud infrastructure, positioning itself to capitalize on the growing demand from tech giants. However, its recent announcement to raise $3.75 billion through convertible bonds has raised alarms among investors, leading to a more than 10% drop in share price.

The bond offering underscores a critical challenge for Nebius: its cash flow is insufficient to support its ambitious expansion plans. Despite generating $385 million in positive cash flow last year, the company’s capital expenditures soared to $4.1 billion, highlighting a significant cash shortfall. As Nebius continues to scale operations, the reliance on external funding raises the risk of dilution, which could adversely affect stock performance.
