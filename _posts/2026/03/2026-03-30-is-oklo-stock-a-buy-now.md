Oklo (OKLO) is experiencing a tumultuous phase, with its stock price plunging to around $52 after a peak of $193 last year, despite a recent surge in interest. The company is diversifying its operations beyond small modular reactors (SMRs) by acquiring Atomic Alchemy for $25 million, enhancing its nuclear fuel recycling and radioisotope production capabilities. Notably, Oklo has partnered with Meta Platforms to develop a 1.2 GW energy campus in Ohio, marking its first significant commercial deal.

This development is crucial for investors as Oklo remains pre-revenue and speculative, with its stock performance not closely tied to fundamentals. The company’s future hinges on obtaining a commercial operating license from the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC). If successful, it could lead to significant stock appreciation; however, failure to secure this license would exacerbate cash burn and hinder revenue generation.

For market professionals, Oklo represents both an opportunity and a risk. Investors should weigh their risk tolerance carefully, as the stock's volatility and speculative nature necessitate a long-term investment horizon.
