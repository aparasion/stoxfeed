Micron Technology (NASDAQ: MU) is facing a notable sell-off, with shares plummeting nearly 19.5% over the past week, marking a 23.5% decline from its recent high of $471.34. This downturn follows a strong earnings report for Q2 FY2026, raising concerns among investors about the sustainability of memory demand amidst rising capacity investments and macroeconomic uncertainties. 

Despite these fears, Micron's management highlighted ongoing supply constraints, with customers receiving only 50-66% of their medium-term memory needs. The company plans to significantly increase capital expenditures to over $25 billion this fiscal year, primarily for memory capacity expansion. Additionally, the anticipated tight supply-demand conditions, driven by AI-related demand, may counteract bearish sentiments if future earnings confirm these trends.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the current sell-off could present a strategic entry point for long-term investors, particularly if Micron's fundamentals continue to reflect robust demand and supply constraints in the memory market.
