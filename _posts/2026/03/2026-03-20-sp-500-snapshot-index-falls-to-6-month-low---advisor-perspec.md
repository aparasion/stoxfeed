The S&P 500 has plunged to a six-month low, reflecting heightened market volatility and investor concerns over economic growth. This decline comes amid rising interest rates and ongoing inflation pressures, which have led to a reassessment of corporate earnings projections across multiple sectors.

The drop in the index is particularly impactful for technology and consumer discretionary stocks, which have been among the hardest hit. Analysts are now revising their forecasts, anticipating that weaker consumer spending could further dampen earnings reports in these areas.
