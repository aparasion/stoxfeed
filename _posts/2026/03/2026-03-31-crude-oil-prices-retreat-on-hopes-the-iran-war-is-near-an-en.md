May WTI crude oil closed down 1.46% on Tuesday, while RBOB gasoline fell 1.71%, as prices retreated from earlier gains driven by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Initial optimism arose after President Trump suggested a potential end to U.S. military involvement in Iran, despite ongoing hostilities affecting the vital Strait of Hormuz. However, a shift in sentiment occurred when Iranian President Masoud Pezeskhian indicated a willingness to negotiate peace, leading to a decline in crude prices.

This volatility highlights the delicate balance between geopolitical developments and market reactions. While the International Energy Agency reported significant damage to energy infrastructure in the region, which could prolong supply disruptions, OPEC+ plans to increase production may counteract rising prices. The market is also grappling with a substantial volume of crude in floating storage, further complicating supply dynamics.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the ongoing uncertainty in Middle Eastern geopolitics, which continues to influence oil prices. Traders should monitor developments closely, as shifts in sentiment can lead to rapid price adjustments.
