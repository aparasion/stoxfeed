The Vanguard Intermediate-Term Treasury ETF (VGIT) and the iShares National Muni Bond ETF (MUB) present distinct investment profiles for bond-focused investors. VGIT targets U.S. Treasury securities, offering slightly higher yields and a lower expense ratio of 0.03%, while MUB provides broad diversification across over 6,300 municipal bonds, appealing to those seeking tax advantages and moderate income.

In terms of performance, MUB has outperformed VGIT over the past five years, with a total gain of 3.7% compared to VGIT's 0.8%. This difference is attributed to MUB’s diversified portfolio, which has helped mitigate risk during market downturns, although VGIT offers a straightforward government-backed investment with less complexity. 

For investors, the choice between these ETFs hinges on individual tax situations and risk tolerance. Those in states with no income tax may find MUB’s tax-exempt benefits particularly advantageous, while VGIT may suit those prioritizing stability and government exposure.
