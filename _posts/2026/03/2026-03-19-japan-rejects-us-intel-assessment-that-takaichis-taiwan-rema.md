Japan has firmly dismissed a U.S. intelligence assessment suggesting that Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's comments on Taiwan signify a major policy shift. Chief Cabinet Secretary Minoru Kihara emphasized that Japan's stance remains consistent, despite Takaichi's previous remarks indicating potential military intervention in the event of a Chinese attack on Taiwan. This statement comes as Takaichi engages in discussions with U.S. President Donald Trump, with the Iranian conflict expected to dominate their agenda.

The implications for financial markets are significant. Takaichi's comments had already strained Japan-China relations, leading to a decline in Chinese tourism and trade disruptions. The U.S. intelligence report highlighted the potential for Japan to engage in collective self-defense under its 2015 constitutional reinterpretation, which could alter regional security dynamics and investor sentiment towards Japanese equities and sectors exposed to China.
