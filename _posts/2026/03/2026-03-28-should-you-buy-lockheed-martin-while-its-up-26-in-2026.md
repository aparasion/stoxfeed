Lockheed Martin (LMT) has emerged as a standout performer in a turbulent market, with shares surging over 26% in 2026, significantly outpacing broader indices. The company reported a mixed financial picture, but key indicators are promising: a substantial backlog of $194 billion, a 6% year-over-year sales increase, and free cash flow that surpassed expectations. The potential for increased military spending, particularly with the Trump administration proposing a defense budget of up to $1.5 trillion for 2027, could further bolster Lockheed's growth trajectory.

However, investors should be cautious as Lockheed's valuation metrics are nearing overvaluation, with a trailing P/E ratio approaching 30 and market capitalization rising from $104 billion to $144 billion in the past year. Despite these concerns, Lockheed's guidance for 2026 suggests continued sales growth of 5% and robust segment operating growth of 25%, alongside a solid dividend yield of 2.2%.

For market professionals, Lockheed presents an intriguing opportunity for stable growth and income, but careful consideration of its valuation is essential before making investment decisions.
