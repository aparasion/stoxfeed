Indian stocks are facing downward pressure, with analysts warning that the Nifty index could decline another 10% if elevated oil prices persist. This concern arises amid ongoing geopolitical tensions, particularly related to the US-Iran situation, which has pushed crude oil prices close to $100 per barrel. The market's vulnerability is further underscored by a significant drop in mid- and small-cap indices, which have fallen over 20% in dollar terms from their 2024 peak.

The implications for investors are substantial, as rising oil prices could exacerbate inflationary pressures, impacting corporate earnings and overall market sentiment. As sectors sensitive to energy costs brace for potential headwinds, portfolio managers may need to reassess their strategies to mitigate risks associated with these macroeconomic factors.
