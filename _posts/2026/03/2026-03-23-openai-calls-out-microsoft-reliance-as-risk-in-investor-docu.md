OpenAI's recent investor document highlights significant risks associated with its close partnership with Microsoft, which has provided substantial financial backing and computing resources. The company disclosed that its operational success heavily relies on diversifying its partnerships beyond Microsoft, which currently holds a 27% stake valued at $135 billion. This disclosure comes as OpenAI prepares for a potential IPO, following a record $110 billion funding round from investors like Amazon and Nvidia.

The implications for financial markets are notable. OpenAI's valuation soared to $730 billion last month, driven by its explosive growth, particularly after the launch of ChatGPT. However, the company's reliance on Microsoft and potential supply chain disruptions—especially concerning chip supplies from Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company—could impact its future performance and stock trajectory, particularly in a competitive generative AI landscape.

Market professionals should closely monitor OpenAI's diversification efforts and the outcomes of ongoing litigation, as these factors could significantly influence investor sentiment and the company's valuation ahead of its IPO.
