Oil prices remain elevated as tensions escalate between the U.S. and Iran, with WTI crude trading at $98.61 and Brent at $111.90. President Trump's ultimatum to Iran, demanding the full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours, has heightened market volatility. Iran's threats of retaliation against U.S. military interests add to the uncertainty, raising fears of broader conflict that could disrupt oil supply chains.

This geopolitical tension is impacting market sentiment, with equity markets in Japan, South Korea, and Australia opening lower as investors adopt a risk-off approach. The potential for U.S. strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure could lead to significant disruptions in oil flows, exacerbating an already tight supply environment. Additionally, Saudi Aramco's CEO's absence from the CERAWeek energy conference underscores the seriousness of the situation.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments surrounding the Strait of Hormuz, as any escalation could lead to sharp price movements in oil and broader market implications.
