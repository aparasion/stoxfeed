The dollar index rose by 0.35% on Thursday, driven by heightened safe-haven demand amid geopolitical tensions in Iran and robust labor market data. The Pentagon's consideration of military options in Iran has intensified uncertainty, prompting a flight to the dollar. Concurrently, a decline in U.S. weekly continuing unemployment claims to a 1.75-year low signals a strong labor market, which is likely to support a hawkish stance from the Federal Reserve.

This dollar strength comes against a backdrop of rising crude oil prices, which surged 4% on Thursday, elevating inflation expectations and reinforcing the case for a restrictive monetary policy. The euro faced pressure, falling 0.29%, as German consumer confidence hit a two-year low, while the yen also weakened due to the dollar's strength and rising U.S. Treasury yields. 

Market participants should note that the current geopolitical landscape and labor market indicators could influence Fed policy, potentially leading to further dollar appreciation and impacting currency pairs and commodities like gold and silver, which have seen significant selling pressure recently.
