The generation-skipping trust (GST) is gaining attention as a strategic tool for wealthy retirees looking to transfer wealth to their grandchildren while minimizing tax liabilities. This trust structure allows grandparents to bypass certain taxation steps, effectively shielding assets from double taxation and protecting them from creditors, lawsuits, and divorce settlements.

The GST is particularly relevant given the looming generation-skipping transfer tax (GSTT), which aligns with the highest federal gift and estate tax rate, projected to reach 20% in 2026. For retirees with estates exceeding the $11.7 million exemption, careful planning becomes essential to mitigate tax implications. While GSTs can provide control over asset distribution and allow for income benefits to children, they require meticulous setup and professional guidance due to their irrevocable nature.

For financial professionals, understanding the nuances of GSTs can enhance estate planning strategies for clients, ensuring they leave a lasting financial legacy while navigating complex tax landscapes.
