The ongoing conflict involving the U.S., Israel, and Iran shows no signs of easing as it approaches the three-week mark, with the Trump administration indicating that Iran's regime remains resilient despite military pressures. This uncertainty raises concerns over the potential for extended disruptions in oil supply, particularly through the strategically critical Strait of Hormuz, where Iranian actions have already impacted tanker operations.

Financial markets are reacting to the rising oil prices, which are expected to increase consumer costs significantly. Analysts warn that every 1-cent rise in gasoline prices could reduce consumer spending by $1.5 billion annually. Additionally, domestic energy companies might see short-term gains, but prolonged conflict could lead to broader economic repercussions, including inflationary pressures that could influence Federal Reserve policy.
