Live Nation Entertainment (LYV) continues to dominate the U.S. concert industry, controlling around 80% of primary ticketing through Ticketmaster and managing nearly 460 venues globally. This substantial market share, highlighted in a recent Department of Justice antitrust complaint, underscores Live Nation's pivotal role in the live music ecosystem, capturing a significant portion of consumer spending on live events. The company's aggressive expansion strategy since 2020 has allowed it to triple its real estate footprint, enhancing its revenue streams from both ticket sales and high-margin sponsorship deals.

For investors seeking alternative exposure to the live music sector, Ryman Hospitality Properties (RHP) offers a unique opportunity. This hotel REIT, with a 5% yield, owns key venues like the Grand Ole Opry and benefits from a robust convention business that stabilizes cash flow. With both companies investing in physical assets and poised for growth, the demand for live entertainment remains strong.
