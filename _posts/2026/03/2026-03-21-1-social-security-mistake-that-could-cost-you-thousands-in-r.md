Social Security earnings records are often overlooked, but they play a crucial role in determining retirement and disability benefits. Many individuals neglect to review their earnings, which are reported by employers to the Social Security Administration (SSA). Errors in these records can significantly impact the benefits one is eligible for, as the formula is based on the highest 35 years of earnings. A simple mistake, such as misreporting an income figure, can lead to lower benefits when it matters most.

For financial professionals, ensuring that earnings records are accurate is vital for clients’ retirement planning. Discrepancies can complicate the application process for benefits and may even raise red flags with the IRS if reported income does not match SSA records. Regularly checking these records, ideally each August, can provide clarity on benefits and help in making informed retirement decisions.
