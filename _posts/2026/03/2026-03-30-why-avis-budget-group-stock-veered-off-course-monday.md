Avis Budget Group (CAR) is facing investor backlash following its announcement of a secondary share issue, leading to a nearly 9% drop in stock price on Monday. The company disclosed plans to potentially float up to 5 million shares through an equity distribution agreement, with proceeds aimed at "general corporate purposes" such as debt retirement, acquisitions, and share buybacks. The share sales will be conducted at current market prices, raising concerns about shareholder dilution given Avis's existing share count of over 35 million.

This development comes as Avis attempts to leverage recent airport chaos to promote driving alternatives, despite rising oil prices and improving airport conditions potentially undermining this strategy. The dilution fears are particularly pronounced as the market reacts to the company's aggressive financing move, which could impact future earnings and stock performance.

Investors should closely monitor how Avis deploys the new capital, as effective use could mitigate dilution concerns and support the stock's recovery in a challenging market environment.
