Wall Street is experiencing a significant sell-off, with the Nasdaq down over 2% and S&P 500 futures hitting new session lows. This decline is fueled by escalating tensions in the Middle East and renewed inflation concerns, leading to a more than 3.5% rise in oil prices. In contrast, gold has fallen by 2.8%, and silver is down over 5%, reflecting a defensive market sentiment. Additionally, Microsoft’s announcement of a hiring freeze in its cloud and sales divisions has raised alarms about the tech sector's health, particularly regarding AI investments.

The market's unease is compounded by geopolitical factors, including heightened rhetoric from the U.S. regarding Iran’s nuclear ambitions. Meta Platforms' shares plummeted by over 6.4% following landmark jury verdicts that could reshape legal risks for social media companies, while tech suppliers like SanDisk and Micron also faced declines due to concerns over reduced demand for memory chips.

Market professionals should note the potential for continued volatility as geopolitical tensions and sector-specific challenges weigh on investor sentiment, particularly in technology and commodities.
