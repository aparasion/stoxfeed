Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell has signaled a cautious stance on interest rates, highlighting concerns about potential inflation spikes that could disrupt the current stock market, particularly the bull run in AI stocks. Following the Fed's decision to maintain interest rates at 3.5%-3.75%, Powell emphasized the need for a "wait-and-see" approach, indicating that rising energy prices could threaten the central bank's inflation target of 2%.

This uncertainty around inflation and interest rates is crucial for market professionals. If inflation exceeds expectations, the Fed may be compelled to raise rates, which could dampen investor sentiment and make borrowing more expensive. Such a scenario could lead to reduced spending in sectors reliant on debt, particularly in the rapidly growing AI industry, which is currently buoyed by significant investments.

Market participants should closely monitor inflation trends and Fed communications, as any shift in interest rate policy could have immediate repercussions on stock valuations and overall market dynamics.
