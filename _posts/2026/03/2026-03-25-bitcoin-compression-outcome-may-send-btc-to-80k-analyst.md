Bitcoin (BTC) is currently testing the critical $71,500 level, a pivotal point that has seen multiple tests over the past week. Analysts suggest that a successful breakout could propel Bitcoin toward $80,000, with a bullish inverse head and shoulders pattern forming on the four-hour chart. However, the daily chart's 50-day EMA continues to act as resistance, indicating mixed sentiment among traders.

The market dynamics reveal a split between futures-driven speculation and weak spot demand, as evidenced by a significant rise in BTC open interest, which increased by $500 million to $16.5 billion. Despite recent optimism linked to geopolitical developments, Bitcoin's price action remains cautious, with a negative cumulative volume delta indicating softening demand from U.S. buyers. For a sustained rally above $71,500, stronger underlying demand and buyer support will be essential.

Market professionals should monitor the interplay between futures and spot markets, as well as the potential for increased volatility if Bitcoin can break through the $71,500 resistance with solid backing.
