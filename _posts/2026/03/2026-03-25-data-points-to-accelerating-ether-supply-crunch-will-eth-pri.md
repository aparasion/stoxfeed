Ether's (ETH) liquid supply is tightening significantly, driven by increased staking participation and declining exchange reserves. Currently, approximately 33.1% of the circulating supply, or about 38.1 million ETH, is locked in staking, marking the highest level recorded. This shift toward illiquid capital suggests a potential structural price floor for ETH, as analysts indicate that the ongoing reduction in liquid supply, coupled with sustained demand, could create a more robust price environment.

Recent data shows substantial outflows from major exchanges, with notable withdrawals from platforms like OKX and Binance, leading to ETH exchange balances hitting multi-year lows. This contraction in available tokens reduces immediate selling pressure, which may heighten price sensitivity to demand. As liquidity tightens, ETH could see increased volatility, particularly if momentum shifts back toward buying.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the current dynamics of ETH supply and demand could set the stage for significant price movements, particularly if buying interest resurfaces.
