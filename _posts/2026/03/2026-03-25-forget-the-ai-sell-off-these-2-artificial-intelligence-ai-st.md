In a turbulent month for AI stocks, Nvidia (NVDA) and Meta Platforms (META) have seen their valuations decline despite strong operational momentum. Concerns over a potential bubble and skepticism about the sustainability of infrastructure investments have led to a market reassessment of these tech giants. Nvidia, which commands a 92% market share in data center GPUs, reported record fiscal 2026 revenue of $215.9 billion, with a robust growth forecast for fiscal 2027. Meanwhile, Meta is embedding AI into its platforms to enhance user engagement and advertising efficiency, although its ambitious $135 billion AI infrastructure spending plan has pressured its stock.

The current market sentiment appears to undervalue both companies, with Nvidia trading at a forward P/E ratio of 21 and Meta at around 20, below their historical averages. This presents a potential buying opportunity, as both firms are well-positioned to capitalize on the growing demand for AI infrastructure and applications.

Investors should consider these valuation compressions as entry points, as Nvidia and Meta are likely to emerge as long-term leaders in the AI landscape, supported by their unique strengths and strategic investments.
