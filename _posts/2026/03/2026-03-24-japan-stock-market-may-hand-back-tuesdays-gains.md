The Nikkei 225 rebounded sharply on Tuesday, gaining 776.25 points or 1.97% to close at 40,083.30, effectively halting a two-day decline that saw the index drop over 970 points. This recovery was driven primarily by strong performances in financial and automotive sectors, with notable gains from major players like Mitsubishi UFJ Financial, which soared 3.28%, and Toyota Motor, which rose 1.36%. However, the outlook remains uncertain as global market sentiment turns negative amid rising interest rate concerns.

The backdrop for this volatility stems from a significant increase in U.S. treasury yields, which reached an eight-month high following positive economic data, raising fears of persistent inflation. This has led to a bleak outlook for Asian markets, with expectations that the Nikkei may face downward pressure in the coming sessions, influenced by the negative lead from U.S. markets.

Market professionals should closely monitor the upcoming household confidence index results in Japan, as this could provide insights into domestic economic sentiment amid the broader global challenges.
