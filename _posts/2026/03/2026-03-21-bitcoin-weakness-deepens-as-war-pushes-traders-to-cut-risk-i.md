Bitcoin (BTC) has experienced a nearly 5% decline this week, mirroring broader market trends as the S&P 500, DOW, Nasdaq, and Gold also face downturns. This market weakness coincides with a significant rise in crude oil prices, which have surged 7.30% amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Notably, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 ETFs have seen record outflows totaling $64 billion over the past three months, indicating a pronounced risk-off sentiment among investors.

The current market dynamics suggest that Bitcoin is struggling to maintain momentum, with recent outflows from spot Bitcoin ETFs totaling $253 million in just two days. Analysts draw parallels to previous geopolitical events, noting that sustained pressure on liquidity and rising energy costs are dampening demand for Bitcoin. Predictions indicate that a price bottom around $55,000 may be necessary before any substantial recovery can occur.
