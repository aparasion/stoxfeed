U.S. stock markets experienced a sharp sell-off on Friday, with the S&P 500 closing down 1.67%, marking a seven-month low, while the Dow and Nasdaq 100 fell 1.73% and 1.93%, respectively. The declines were fueled by escalating concerns over a prolonged conflict in Iran, which has driven WTI crude oil prices up over 5% and contributed to rising inflation expectations. The 10-year Treasury yield surged to an 8.25-month high of 4.48%, reflecting heightened risk perceptions in the market.

The geopolitical tensions are impacting not only energy prices but also broader market sentiment, as evidenced by the University of Michigan's consumer sentiment index being revised lower. Additionally, China's retaliatory trade investigations against the U.S. add another layer of uncertainty, further pressuring equities and increasing volatility in global markets.

Market professionals should closely monitor oil prices and geopolitical developments, as sustained high energy costs could force central banks to reconsider their monetary policies, potentially leading to tighter financial conditions and further market adjustments.
