OpenAI's rollout of ads on ChatGPT has drawn interest from major ad agencies like WPP, Omnicom, and Dentsu, but the process has been slower than anticipated. Advertisers committed significant budgets—between $200,000 and $250,000—yet many are frustrated by the conservative pace of the pilot program, which is designed to refine the ad experience before broader deployment. As of mid-March, ad delivery has increased by 600%, reaching about 5% of ChatGPT mobile users, but advertisers are concerned about the limited insights available from their investments.

The slow rollout highlights the challenges and potential of AI-driven advertising. Analysts predict that by 2030, OpenAI's ad revenue could exceed $30 billion, positioning it as a key player alongside traditional digital ad channels. Brands eager to leverage this new medium are urged to align their messaging with user intent to maximize relevance and impact.
