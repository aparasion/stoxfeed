Iran has escalated its military response against U.S. assets and Israel following the assassination of security chief Ali Larijani, launching missile strikes on over 100 targets in Israel and attacking the U.S. embassy in Baghdad. The Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has claimed these actions are retaliation for the recent airstrikes that killed Larijani and others, intensifying a conflict that has already disrupted regional stability.

This surge in hostilities poses significant implications for global energy markets, particularly as the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for oil transit, faces threats. With approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through this waterway, any sustained military action could exacerbate supply concerns and drive energy prices higher. The U.S. has responded with airstrikes on Iranian missile sites, indicating a potential for further military escalation.
