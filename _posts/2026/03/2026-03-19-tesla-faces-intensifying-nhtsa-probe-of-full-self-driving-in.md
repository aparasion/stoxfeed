The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) has intensified its investigation into Tesla's "Full Self-Driving" (FSD) systems, focusing on potential safety defects that could endanger drivers in low-visibility conditions. This expanded probe now encompasses approximately 3.2 million vehicles, including popular models like the Model S, X, 3, Y, and the upcoming Cybertruck, as concerns mount over the FSD's performance in adverse conditions.

This investigation is significant for the financial markets as it raises questions about Tesla's product reliability and could impact consumer confidence and sales. The NHTSA's shift to an "engineering analysis" indicates serious scrutiny, particularly following incidents where FSD was active during crashes, including one tragic case involving a pedestrian fatality. Such developments could influence Tesla's stock performance and investor sentiment, especially as the company navigates an increasingly competitive EV landscape.
