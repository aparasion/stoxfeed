U.S. President Donald Trump has issued a stark ultimatum to Iran, urging the nation to engage seriously in peace negotiations or face dire consequences. His comments, made via Truth Social, follow a period of conflicting statements from both Washington and Tehran regarding the status of ongoing discussions aimed at ending the nearly month-long conflict. Trump characterized Iranian negotiators as desperate, while Iranian officials maintain they are not directly negotiating with the U.S.

This escalation in rhetoric comes amid reports of the U.S. preparing to bolster its military presence in the region, which could signal a shift toward more aggressive actions if talks fail. Analysts suggest that potential U.S. operations could target critical oil infrastructure, such as the Kharg Island oil port, further heightening tensions in an already volatile market environment. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the increased risk of military action in the region, which could disrupt oil supply chains and lead to volatility in energy prices, impacting broader market sentiment.
