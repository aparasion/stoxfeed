President Trump intensified his rhetoric against Iran during a recent cabinet meeting, stating that Tehran is "begging for a deal" while expressing uncertainty about his willingness to negotiate. His comments come amid rising tensions, with the U.S. Maritime Administration warning of threats to shipping in the Bab el-Mandeb Strait and Trump vowing to counter Iranian drone activity. This escalation has triggered a risk-off sentiment in the markets.

The immediate market impact has been notable, with oil prices climbing as investors price in the heightened risk premium associated with potential military escalation. The uncertainty surrounding a nuclear or trade agreement with Iran is dampening appetite for riskier assets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical developments. The situation is further complicated by a reported decline in U.S. natural gas inventories, adding to the volatility in energy markets.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the increased risk premium in oil prices, which could persist as geopolitical tensions evolve. Monitoring developments in U.S.-Iran relations will be crucial for assessing future market movements.
