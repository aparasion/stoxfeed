Chewy (CHWY) has seen its stock price drop nearly 30% this year, despite the company's successful expansion and recent milestone of profitability. This decline appears to stem from shifting investor interest rather than fundamental weaknesses, as Chewy continues to grow its e-commerce business and diversify revenue streams with the addition of veterinary clinics in the U.S. and Canada.

A key driver of Chewy's sales is its Autoship service, which accounts for over 80% of total revenue from repeat customers. This strong customer retention offers visibility into future sales and indicates that Chewy's offerings resonate well with pet owners. Currently, the stock is trading at 15 times forward earnings estimates, a significant drop from over 30 times last year, suggesting a favorable valuation for potential investors.

For market professionals, Chewy's current price point may represent a compelling buying opportunity, given its robust growth trajectory and loyal customer base, positioning it well for long-term gains.
