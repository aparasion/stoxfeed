The Federal Reserve decided to keep interest rates steady at 3.5% to 3.75% amidst escalating geopolitical tensions, particularly the conflict in Iran, which has raised inflation expectations. Analysts suggest that the ongoing energy shock and rising consumer prices make a rate cut impractical, despite calls for action from political figures like President Trump. This decision comes as Americans face increased costs from gas prices and other essentials, exacerbating affordability challenges.

The Fed's pause impacts various sectors, with the 10-year Treasury yield climbing to 4.208%, influencing mortgage rates and consumer loans. The average rate for a 30-year fixed mortgage has already risen to 6.29%, reflecting market apprehension about prolonged inflation. While credit card rates remain stable, auto loan costs continue to rise, putting additional pressure on consumers already grappling with high financing costs.
