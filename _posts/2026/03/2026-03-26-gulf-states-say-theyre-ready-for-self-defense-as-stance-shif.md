Gulf states have issued a strong joint statement condemning Iran's recent attacks on their energy infrastructure, labeling them as "blatant" and "criminal." This marks a significant shift in tone, as the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) members—UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and Jordan—expressed a readiness to act in self-defense against these aggressions, particularly those emanating from Iraqi territory by Iranian proxies. The statement underscores the Gulf's frustration with ongoing Iranian drone and missile strikes, which have severely impacted oil and liquefied natural gas terminals, leading to costly repairs and undermining regional stability.

The implications for financial markets are considerable. The escalating tensions threaten to disrupt oil supply chains and could lead to increased volatility in energy prices. Gulf states have warned that continued Iranian aggression could hinder their economic development and tarnish their reputation as safe havens for business and tourism. 

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in this geopolitical landscape, as any escalation could have immediate repercussions on oil prices and broader market sentiment, particularly in energy sectors.
