The State Street SPDR Portfolio S&P 600 Small Cap ETF (SPSM) and iShares Core S&P Small-Cap ETF (IJR) are two prominent options for investors seeking exposure to the S&P SmallCap 600 Index. While both ETFs aim to deliver similar performance, they differ significantly in size, expense ratios, and yields, with SPSM offering a lower cost at 0.03% compared to IJR’s 0.06%, along with a slightly higher dividend yield.

These differences matter as they can influence investor decisions based on trading strategies and cost considerations. IJR, with $92.2 billion in assets, provides greater liquidity and smaller price gaps, making it attractive for larger trades. Conversely, SPSM’s lower fees may appeal to long-term investors focused on minimizing costs.
