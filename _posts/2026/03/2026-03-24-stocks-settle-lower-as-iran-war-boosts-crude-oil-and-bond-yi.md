The S&P 500 Index closed down 0.37% on Tuesday, alongside declines in the Dow Jones and Nasdaq 100, as escalating tensions in the Middle East continue to weigh on market sentiment. The conflict in Iran has entered its 25th day, with recent missile strikes on Israel and U.S. bases raising concerns about a broader regional escalation. This backdrop contributed to a 4% surge in WTI crude oil prices, while the 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.38%.

The heightened geopolitical risks have led to increased volatility in energy markets, significantly impacting sectors tied to oil production. Energy stocks saw gains, with major players like Marathon Petroleum and Phillips 66 rising over 4%. However, the broader market remains under pressure, particularly in technology and cybersecurity sectors, which faced steep declines following reports of Amazon Web Services' new AI initiatives.

Market professionals should monitor the evolving situation in the Middle East closely, as further escalations could lead to sustained volatility in oil prices and broader market impacts, particularly in energy-sensitive sectors.
