A recent study highlights the significant financial implications of claiming Social Security at the optimal age, with research indicating that retirees could boost their lifetime income by over $100,000 by waiting until age 70 to file. According to a 2019 report from United Income, 57% of retirees could enhance their wealth by delaying claims, while a staggering $3.4 trillion in lifetime benefits could be forfeited due to premature filings.

This finding is crucial for financial professionals advising clients on retirement strategies. With nearly 30% of retirees relying solely on Social Security and over half returning to work due to insufficient benefits, understanding the optimal claiming age can directly impact retirement planning and portfolio management. The average monthly benefit increases by about $850 when claiming at age 70 compared to age 62, making it a compelling consideration for those whose financial security hinges on these benefits.

Ultimately, while age 70 may emerge as the financially advantageous choice, individual circumstances and priorities must guide the decision. Financial advisors should tailor their recommendations based on clients' health, lifestyle goals, and income needs to ensure a balanced approach to retirement planning.
