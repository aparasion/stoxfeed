Bitcoin has dropped below $71,000, reflecting broader market concerns as Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell signaled rising energy prices are impacting inflation forecasts. Following the Fed's decision to hold interest rates steady, Powell raised the 2026 inflation outlook to 2.7% from 2.4%, highlighting the potential for prolonged price pressures. This news contributed to a decline in major indices, with the Nasdaq closing down 1.5% and the S&P 500 down 1.4%.

The market's reaction underscores a growing anxiety among investors regarding the Fed's tightening stance amid rising oil prices. Digital asset stocks, including major players like MicroStrategy and Galaxy Digital, fell significantly, with some losing up to 15%. The sentiment has shifted, particularly among Bitcoin holders, as over $100 million worth of BTC was sold following the Fed's hawkish signals.
