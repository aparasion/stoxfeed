The EURUSD currency pair is experiencing downward pressure, trading between 1.157 and 1.1580 USD per euro. This movement is influenced by a combination of monetary policy decisions from the Federal Reserve and European Central Bank, alongside escalating geopolitical tensions and macroeconomic data. The Fed's decision to maintain interest rates in the 3.50–3.75% range suggests a prolonged period of elevated rates, while the ECB's cautious stance amid rising inflation risks limits support for the euro.

Geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East, are bolstering the US dollar as a safe haven, further pressuring the euro. Additionally, recent German PPI data indicates a slowdown in cost pressures, which may not fully account for the inflationary impacts of rising energy prices. As energy prices continue to rise, the market is closely watching inflation data from both the eurozone and the US for further implications.
