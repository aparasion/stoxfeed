Wall Street is experiencing heightened volatility driven by geopolitical tensions in the Persian Gulf, particularly surrounding Iran. After an initial market downturn in anticipation of military escalation, a five-day extension of the ultimatum to Iran announced by President Trump reversed sentiment, leading to a significant rebound in major U.S. indexes. The S&P 500, Dow Jones, and Nasdaq all gained over 1.5% as fears of an energy shock subsided.

This rapid shift underscores the market's sensitivity to geopolitical developments, particularly concerning oil prices, which directly impact inflation expectations and corporate performance. Stocks in the technology sector, including NVIDIA and AMD, are benefiting from improved sentiment, while Apple is buoyed by strong sales and device upgrades. Meanwhile, Berkshire Hathaway's investment in Tokio Marine signals confidence in international markets, despite ongoing uncertainties.

The key takeaway for investors is the necessity of closely monitoring geopolitical developments, as they can dramatically influence market dynamics, asset performance, and investor sentiment in real-time.
