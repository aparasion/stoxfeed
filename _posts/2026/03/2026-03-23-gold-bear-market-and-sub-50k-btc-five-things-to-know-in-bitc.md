Bitcoin (BTC) enters a new week under pressure, closing below the critical 200-week exponential moving average (EMA) and facing macroeconomic headwinds. With gold entering a bear market and oil prices hovering around $100 amid geopolitical tensions, BTC's price action mirrors January's bear flag pattern, raising concerns about potential multiyear lows. Traders are eyeing a range between $66,000 and $76,000, with forecasts suggesting a possible revisit to sub-$60,000 levels if bearish trends persist.

The implications for the broader financial markets are significant. As Bitcoin's long-term holders sell at a loss, the market's liquidity is strained, leading to heightened volatility. The recent decline in gold and rising oil prices could exacerbate inflation concerns, influencing the Federal Reserve's future rate decisions. The market is also reacting to historical patterns, which may provide some relief to risk assets, including cryptocurrencies.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of monitoring liquidity conditions and geopolitical developments, as these factors could trigger substantial price movements in Bitcoin and other risk assets this week.
