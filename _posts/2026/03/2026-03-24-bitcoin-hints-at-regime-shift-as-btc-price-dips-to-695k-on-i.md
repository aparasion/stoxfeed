Bitcoin (BTC) dipped below the $70,000 mark at the start of trading on Wall Street, driven by macroeconomic pressures tied to escalating tensions in the Middle East. This decline comes as Bitcoin struggles to maintain support at this critical level, with a 1.5% drop noted in its price, retreating from an earlier high of $71,800. The broader market reflects similar losses, with the Nasdaq Composite Index down nearly 1% and oil prices nearing $95 per barrel amid ongoing geopolitical uncertainty.

Despite the current downturn, analysts highlight Bitcoin's unexpected resilience, suggesting it may be entering a new phase where it no longer correlates closely with traditional risk assets. This shift could indicate a potential recovery, as evidenced by a series of higher lows in recent weeks. However, skepticism remains, with some traders warning of possible new lows and questioning the reliability of key technical indicators like the 200-week exponential moving average.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the dual nature of Bitcoin's current environment: while it faces immediate selling pressure, its ability to establish higher lows could signal a shift in its market dynamics, warranting close monitoring for potential trading opportunities.
