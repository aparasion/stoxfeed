Tesla (TSLA +1.16%) continues to capture investor interest, but recent trends raise questions about its growth trajectory. CEO Elon Musk is pivoting towards artificial intelligence, particularly in robotics and robotaxis, which he believes could redefine Tesla's revenue streams. However, the company is grappling with a 20% decline from its 52-week high and a broader market pullback, with the S&P 500 down about 2% this year.

Despite Musk's optimistic vision, Tesla's financial performance has been disappointing, with a staggering 61% drop in net income year-over-year, totaling just $840 million in Q4 2025. Coupled with a price-to-earnings ratio exceeding 350, the stock appears overvalued, making it a risky proposition for investors. The increasing competition in the EV space further complicates the outlook.
