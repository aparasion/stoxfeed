Unusual Machines (UMAC 15.97%) saw its stock plunge 15.6% early Friday following the announcement of a $150 million share issuance aimed at expanding its U.S. drone parts inventory and supporting general corporate needs. The company plans to sell 8.8 million new shares at $17 each, a discount to Thursday’s closing price of $18.60, which will dilute existing shareholders by 22.6%. 

This capital raise comes despite Unusual Machines having approximately $140 million in cash reserves and a relatively low cash burn rate of $23 million annually, suggesting it has sufficient liquidity for over six years. The decision to issue new shares appears to be a strategic move to capitalize on the stock's recent tripling in value, allowing the company to secure funds while its valuation is high.
