The South Korean stock market rebounded modestly on Friday, with the KOSPI rising 17.98 points, or 0.31%, to close at 5,781.20. This uptick followed a sharp three-day surge of over 430 points, but the index may face consolidation as global market sentiment weakens due to rising crude oil prices and concerns over interest rates. Notably, the automotive and technology sectors experienced declines, while financial and chemical stocks showed mixed performance.

The broader implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly as Wall Street ended the week on a negative note, with the Dow, NASDAQ, and S&P 500 all experiencing substantial losses. The volatility in crude oil prices, driven by geopolitical tensions, raises concerns about inflation and the Federal Reserve's interest rate trajectory, which could impact investor sentiment and market stability.

Market professionals should closely monitor the interplay between oil prices and interest rate forecasts, as these factors could influence trading strategies and sector performance in the coming weeks.
