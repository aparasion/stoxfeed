The S&P 500 Index fell 0.66% today, alongside declines in the Dow Jones and Nasdaq 100, as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and rising energy prices weighed heavily on market sentiment. This downturn marks a significant drop to 3.75-month lows, exacerbated by a surge in European natural gas prices, which climbed over 24% following damage reports from Qatar's major gas export facility.

These developments have heightened inflation concerns, particularly as U.S. economic data showed unexpected strength, with weekly jobless claims dropping to a nine-week low. The 10-year Treasury yield rose to a 6.75-month high, reflecting market apprehensions about future rate hikes amid persistent inflation pressures. Energy stocks are notably impacted, with crude oil prices remaining elevated due to ongoing conflicts, leading analysts to warn of potential record highs.
