Rivian Automotive (RIVN) shares surged nearly 10% in premarket trading after Uber Technologies (UBER) announced plans to invest up to $1.25 billion in Rivian to support the launch of an autonomous vehicle fleet. This partnership secures Rivian not only a significant cash infusion but also a guaranteed buyer for tens of thousands of vehicles, potentially enhancing its market position amid ongoing financial struggles.

However, while the deal offers a temporary boost, Rivian's path to profitability remains fraught with challenges. The company is still grappling with negative cash flow and high capital requirements for scaling production and developing advanced technology. In contrast, Uber benefits from a capital-light strategy, leveraging partnerships to mitigate risks while expanding its autonomous offerings without heavy investment in vehicle manufacturing.
