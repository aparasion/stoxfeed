President Trump expressed frustration over the reluctance of several nations to join a coalition aimed at escorting oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, a vital route for global oil exports. With tanker traffic significantly reduced due to Iranian attacks, crude oil prices have surged nearly 40% following recent U.S. and Israeli military actions against Iran. Trump highlighted the importance of international support, noting that while some countries are eager to participate, others, including the U.K. and Germany, have been hesitant.

The implications for the oil market are profound, as the ongoing instability in the region could exacerbate supply disruptions and keep prices elevated. The U.K. has indicated it is working on a plan with allies to restore navigation freedom, while Germany has ruled out military involvement. Japan remains non-committal, focusing on its legal framework for potential contributions.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments regarding the coalition's formation and any shifts in military readiness, as these factors will significantly influence oil supply dynamics.
