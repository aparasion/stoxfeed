CoreWeave (CRWV) is capturing investor attention with its impressive growth trajectory, reporting a staggering revenue increase from $223 million in 2023 to projected figures of $12.45 billion in 2024 and $23.14 billion in 2025. The company specializes in AI data centers and has secured significant contracts with major players like OpenAI, Meta, and Microsoft, positioning itself strongly amid a booming AI market expected to reach $700 billion this year. However, this growth narrative comes with a critical caveat.

Despite the promising revenue backlog of $66.8 billion, CoreWeave faces a daunting challenge with its mounting long-term debt, now exceeding $29 billion. This financial strain, coupled with negative free cash flow of $7.25 billion over the past year, raises concerns about the sustainability of its expansion. As the company continues to burn cash while scaling operations, the risk of dilution from potential stock issuances looms large.

Investors should weigh the allure of CoreWeave's rapid growth against the backdrop of its escalating debt and cash burn, as these factors could significantly impact future profitability and shareholder value.
