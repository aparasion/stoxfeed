As of February 2026, the average Social Security retirement benefit stands at $2,076 per month, revealing significant disparities, particularly for female beneficiaries who typically receive less than their male counterparts. This gap is largely attributed to lifetime income differences, making it crucial for women to understand their expected benefits at various claiming ages to plan effectively for retirement.

The data indicates a clear trend: older beneficiaries receive higher monthly checks, as benefits increase with delayed applications up to age 70. This strategy can be particularly beneficial for women, who often face challenges in accumulating retirement savings. However, the decision to delay benefits must align with individual financial situations, as early claiming can lead to reduced lifetime benefits and affect survivor payouts for spouses.

Professionals should consider the implications of Social Security claiming strategies in retirement planning. Coordinating benefits within couples can maximize overall household income, especially in cases of significant income disparity. Tailoring a claiming strategy to individual circumstances can enhance financial security in retirement.
