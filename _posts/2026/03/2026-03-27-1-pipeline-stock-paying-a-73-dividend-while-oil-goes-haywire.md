The oil market has experienced significant volatility this year, with WTI crude prices fluctuating dramatically due to geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict with Iran. After starting the year below $60 per barrel, WTI surged past $119 amid concerns over energy infrastructure, but has recently retreated to around $90 as tensions eased. This instability in oil prices presents both risks and opportunities for investors.

Amid this tumult, MPLX (NYSE: MPLX) stands out as a resilient investment option. The master limited partnership, which operates essential midstream infrastructure, benefits from government-regulated rate structures and long-term contracts that insulate it from commodity price swings. With a current yield of 7.3%, MPLX generated nearly $5.8 billion in cash flow last year, comfortably covering its distribution and allowing for ongoing expansion projects. 

Investors can view MPLX as a stable income source in uncertain times, with a strong balance sheet and a robust pipeline of growth projects that should support continued distribution increases and mid-single-digit earnings growth through the decade.
