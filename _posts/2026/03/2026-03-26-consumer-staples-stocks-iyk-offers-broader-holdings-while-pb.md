The iShares U.S. Consumer Staples ETF (IYK) and Invesco Food & Beverage ETF (PBJ) are two prominent options for investors targeting the consumer staples sector, but they cater to different investment strategies. IYK boasts a broader portfolio of 54 stocks, lower fees at 0.38%, and a higher dividend yield of 2.7%, making it attractive for income-focused investors. In contrast, PBJ's narrower focus on food and beverage stocks, with only 31 holdings, has led to a 5.8% return over the past year, outperforming IYK's modest 0.1% gain.

The distinction between these ETFs is crucial for portfolio management. IYK offers greater liquidity and diversification, including an 11% allocation to healthcare, while PBJ's concentrated approach may appeal to those looking specifically for food-related investments. 

Investors should weigh the trade-offs: IYK is suited for those prioritizing cost efficiency and income, whereas PBJ may attract those willing to embrace higher risk for potentially better performance in a focused sector.
