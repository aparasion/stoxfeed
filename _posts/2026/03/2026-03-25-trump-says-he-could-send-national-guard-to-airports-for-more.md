President Trump has indicated he may deploy the National Guard to U.S. airports amid ongoing disruptions caused by the partial government shutdown, which has affected the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) and led to significant delays for travelers. The shutdown, which began on February 14, has resulted in increased absenteeism among TSA officers, who are required to work without pay, exacerbating long wait times at major airports.

This situation is impacting airline operations and customer experience, with Delta Air Lines recently suspending special services for Congress members due to the shutdown. Airlines are warning passengers of potential delays, which could lead to decreased ticket sales and overall revenue if travel demand is affected. The ongoing political standoff over immigration enforcement funding adds another layer of uncertainty for the sector.

Market professionals should monitor the situation closely, as prolonged disruptions could impact airline stock performance and broader travel-related sectors, emphasizing the need for strategic adjustments in portfolios tied to these industries.
