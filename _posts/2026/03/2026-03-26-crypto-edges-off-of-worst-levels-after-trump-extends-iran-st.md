Bitcoin saw a slight recovery after U.S. President Donald Trump announced a 10-day extension of the pause on military actions against Iran, which had been contributing to heightened volatility in the markets. Following a rough day where Bitcoin dropped over 3% and the Nasdaq fell 2.4%, Trump's comments provided a momentary boost, stabilizing prices just above $69,000.

The geopolitical tensions have not only fueled a surge in oil prices but have also triggered a selloff in Western bond markets, with the U.S. 10-year Treasury yield climbing to 4.41%. This shift has altered market expectations, diminishing the likelihood of Federal Reserve rate cuts and increasing speculation around potential rate hikes. The broader implications of these developments are significant, particularly for tech stocks and cryptocurrencies, which are sensitive to interest rate changes.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the fragile state of both crypto and equity markets amid rising geopolitical tensions and bond yields. Traders should closely monitor support levels in key cryptocurrencies like XRP, as increased volatility and forced liquidations may signal further market shifts.
