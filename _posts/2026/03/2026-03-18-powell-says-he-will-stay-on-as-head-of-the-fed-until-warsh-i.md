Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell announced he will remain in his role beyond May if his successor, Kevin Warsh, is not confirmed by the Senate. Powell emphasized he will also retain his position on the Board of Governors until the Department of Justice concludes its investigation into him, which has stirred political controversy. He indicated he would serve as "chair pro tem" if necessary, to maintain stability at the Fed.

This development is crucial for financial markets as it underscores the ongoing uncertainty surrounding U.S. monetary policy. Powell's decision to keep interest rates unchanged amid inflation concerns and geopolitical tensions reflects a cautious approach, which could impact market sentiment. The investigation's political dimensions may also influence investor confidence, particularly if it affects the Fed's independence.
