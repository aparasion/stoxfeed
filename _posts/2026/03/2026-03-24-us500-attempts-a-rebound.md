S&P 500 futures have narrowed their losses from around -1% to approximately -0.2%, with the spot index reversing an early decline to rise nearly 0.1%. This shift follows reports from Iranian sources indicating that the U.S. has initiated diplomatic outreach, and Iran is open to considering proposals aimed at resolving ongoing tensions. While formal negotiations are absent, Iran's willingness to discuss terms that protect its national interests and allow for peaceful nuclear energy use has sparked optimism among investors.

The market's positive response suggests that traders are increasingly betting on a gradual de-escalation of geopolitical tensions as a catalyst for recovery. However, mixed signals from Iran complicate the outlook, as the nation maintains a hardline stance while potentially engaging in informal discussions. Additionally, tight conditions in the fuel market, with early signs of supply disruptions noted by Shell, could further influence market dynamics.

Market professionals should monitor developments closely, as any breakthrough in U.S.-Iran relations could significantly impact equity performance and energy prices.
