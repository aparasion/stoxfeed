Memory and data storage stocks have dominated the AI investment landscape, but energy stocks are emerging as key players due to the increasing demand for reliable power in data centers. Companies like Energy Transfer, Constellation Energy, and Enbridge are well-positioned to capitalize on this trend, as the ongoing expansion of AI data centers necessitates a robust energy supply.

Energy Transfer is leveraging its extensive pipeline network to supply natural gas to data centers, including agreements with Oracle and CloudBurst. With a forward P/E ratio of 11.4 and a dividend yield of 6.8%, it presents an attractive opportunity for income-focused investors. Similarly, Constellation Energy is securing long-term power purchase agreements with major tech firms, reflecting its potential for growth driven by nuclear energy. Meanwhile, Enbridge is diversifying its offerings with renewable energy contracts, while also collaborating with Microsoft to enhance operational efficiency.

Investors should consider these energy stocks as viable options for long-term growth, especially as AI continues to drive demand for stable and sustainable energy solutions.
