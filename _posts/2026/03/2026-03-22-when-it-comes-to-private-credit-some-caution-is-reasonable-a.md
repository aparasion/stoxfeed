Recent concerns regarding the private credit market have sparked investor apprehension, but experts suggest that fears of a widespread crisis are exaggerated. Crystal Cox, a senior vice president at Wealthspire Advisors, notes that while there are pockets of weakness, the current pressures are more indicative of a maturing market rather than systemic issues. The shift from a high-return environment to a more competitive landscape emphasizes the importance of manager selection and underwriting discipline in private credit investments.

Despite the rapid growth of private credit—now estimated at $1.7 trillion—there are increasing worries about default rates, particularly in sectors influenced by artificial intelligence. Morgan Stanley projects defaults in direct lending could rise to 8%, driven by disruptions in software and AI-adjacent industries. This evolving landscape necessitates a careful approach to investment in private credit, especially for retail investors who may have limited access to these assets.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to maintain a cautious allocation to private credit—ideally no more than 5% of an overall portfolio—while closely monitoring sector-specific risks, particularly those related to technology transitions.
