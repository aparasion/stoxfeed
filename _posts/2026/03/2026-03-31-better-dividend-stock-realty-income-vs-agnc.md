Realty Income (O) and AGNC (AGNC) are two prominent choices for income-focused investors, each with distinct portfolios and risk profiles. Realty Income, a major REIT, boasts over 15,500 properties globally and has maintained impressive occupancy rates, even in challenging economic conditions. In contrast, AGNC specializes in mortgage-backed securities, with a $94.8 billion portfolio primarily consisting of Agency MBS, making it sensitive to interest rate fluctuations.

The impact of rising interest rates diverges for these two entities. Realty Income faces higher acquisition costs and potential tenant strain, yet it has consistently upheld occupancy above 96%. Meanwhile, AGNC's profitability hinges on maintaining a favorable net interest rate spread, which may be jeopardized in a rising rate environment. Despite AGNC's higher forward yield of 14.6%, Realty Income’s lower payout ratio and simpler business model make it a more stable choice in the current unpredictable market.

For income-seeking investors, Realty Income’s consistent performance and manageable growth outlook may offer a safer haven compared to AGNC’s higher yield but more volatile earnings trajectory.
