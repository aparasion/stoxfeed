Snap Inc. (SNAP) saw its stock plunge 10.69% to $4.01 on Thursday, hitting an all-time low after the European Commission announced a formal investigation into its child safety practices under the Digital Services Act. This investigation will scrutinize Snapchat's age restrictions and protections against harmful content, raising concerns about potential regulatory penalties that could significantly impact the company’s margins.

The broader market also faced pressure, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite dropping 1.74% and 2.38%, respectively, as rising oil prices weighed on investor sentiment. Social media peers, including Meta Platforms and Pinterest, also experienced declines, reflecting a sector-wide reaction to heightened regulatory scrutiny and recent legal challenges.

For investors, Snap's regulatory challenges underscore the increasing compliance risks facing social media companies globally. A negative outcome from the E.U. investigation could lead to substantial fines and stricter oversight, further complicating Snap's already precarious financial situation and potentially influencing broader market sentiment in the sector.
