The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) is driving a supercycle in memory and storage chips, significantly benefiting Sandisk (SNDK). As AI applications require vast datasets for training, the demand for high-capacity NAND flash storage has surged, positioning Sandisk's solid-state drives (SSDs) as critical components in AI data center infrastructure. The resulting shortage in enterprise-grade NAND solutions has enhanced pricing power for providers, with Sandisk experiencing robust revenue growth and expanding margins due to increased adoption from AI hyperscalers.

With Sandisk's stock price soaring 1,070% over the past year, speculation around a potential stock split is gaining traction. A split could broaden ownership beyond institutional investors and improve trading liquidity by making shares more accessible to retail buyers. While no formal announcement has been made, such a move would signal management's confidence in continued growth, aligning with the ongoing AI-driven demand for scalable storage solutions.

For market professionals, Sandisk's strategic positioning in the AI landscape and the potential for a stock split present compelling opportunities for investment and portfolio diversification.
