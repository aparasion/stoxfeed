Wheat futures are experiencing losses across all major markets this morning, following a downward trend from last week. Chicago SRW futures dropped between 12 to 13.5 cents, with May contracts down 18.5 cents. Kansas City HRW and Minneapolis spring wheat also faced declines, with May contracts down 23.75 and 17.5 cents, respectively. This bearish sentiment comes amidst geopolitical tensions, as President Trump announced a temporary halt on military strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure, which may have implications for global oil prices.

The wheat market's performance is particularly notable given recent CFTC data indicating a reduction in the net short position for CBT wheat, suggesting a shift in trader sentiment. USDA export commitments are robust, up 14% year-over-year, and shipment data is ahead of schedule, which could provide some support to prices in the longer term.

Market professionals should monitor these developments closely, as geopolitical factors and export dynamics could significantly influence wheat prices in the coming weeks.
