European stocks are poised for a lower opening on Monday as the ongoing conflict in Iran escalates, now entering its fifth week. The U.K.'s FTSE 100 is expected to drop 0.2%, while Germany's DAX may decline by 0.6%. This downturn follows a negative trend in Asia-Pacific markets, with investors reacting to heightened geopolitical tensions, including U.S. President Trump's comments about potentially seizing Iranian oil.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly in the energy sector. Oil prices surged in early Asia trading, with West Texas Intermediate crude futures rising 2.58% to $102.19 per barrel. This spike reflects concerns over supply disruptions amid the conflict, which could further impact inflation and economic sentiment in Europe. The G7's emergency meeting today highlights the urgency of addressing these geopolitical risks.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments from the G7 meeting and the upcoming EU economic sentiment and German inflation data, as these factors could influence market volatility and sector performance in the near term.
