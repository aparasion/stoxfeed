Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's recent visit to the White House underscores the significant geopolitical influence of President Donald Trump, particularly regarding global energy markets. Takaichi's embrace of Trump highlights her acknowledgment of his role in shaping international stability, especially as tensions with Iran escalate. Trump's military decisions are reshaping the economic landscape, with U.S. gas prices surging nearly 33% in the last month due to fears surrounding the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil transit route.

The implications for financial markets are profound. Futures markets now reflect expectations of oil prices remaining above $80 a barrel through mid-2027, driven by concerns over prolonged conflict and potential disruptions in energy supply. Additionally, the risk of fertilizer shortages due to disrupted shipping channels poses a threat to U.S. agriculture, further complicating the economic outlook.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving situation, as Trump's military strategies and their impact on energy prices could have lasting effects on inflation and economic stability, influencing both investment strategies and policy responses from the Federal Reserve.
