OpenAI's advertising business has quickly surpassed $100 million in annual recurring revenue, just two months after its U.S. pilot launch. The company began testing ads with free users and ChatGPT Go subscribers, aiming to create a significant new revenue stream. Currently, OpenAI collaborates with over 600 advertisers, and despite some skepticism from competitors, it reports no negative impact on user trust metrics related to privacy.

This rapid revenue achievement underscores the growing monetization potential of AI platforms. With around 85% of U.S. users eligible to see ads, OpenAI is strategically rolling out its advertising program to refine user experience before broader implementation. However, some advertisers have expressed frustration at the cautious pace of the rollout, which OpenAI has defended as a necessary step for quality assurance.

For market professionals, the swift revenue growth highlights the increasing viability of AI-driven advertising as a lucrative sector, suggesting potential shifts in investment strategies and advertising dynamics within the tech landscape.
