Asia-Pacific markets experienced a downturn on Friday, influenced by a turbulent session on Wall Street. The uncertainty surrounding a potential peace deal in the Middle East, particularly between the U.S. and Iran, has contributed to market volatility. President Trump extended the deadline for military action against Iran's energy infrastructure by ten days, allowing for ongoing negotiations that have seen mixed signals from both parties.

This geopolitical tension has had immediate repercussions on oil prices, with West Texas Intermediate dropping 1.8% to $92.82 per barrel and Brent crude falling 1.92% to $105.90. The broader market sentiment reflected this, as South Korea's Kospi index plunged 3.6%, while other regional indices also reported declines. In the U.S., futures showed a slight recovery, suggesting that easing oil price concerns may provide some support for domestic markets.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for continued volatility driven by geopolitical developments. Investors should closely monitor the situation in the Middle East, as any escalation or resolution could significantly impact oil prices and broader market sentiment.
