The ongoing conflict between the U.S., Israel, and Iran is significantly disrupting the global economy, as highlighted by European G7 foreign ministers ahead of their summit in France. With energy prices soaring and critical infrastructure in the Gulf region severely damaged, the situation poses grave risks to global energy supplies, particularly through the Strait of Hormuz, which typically handles a fifth of the world’s oil and gas. The ministers are urging the U.S. to seek a diplomatic resolution, yet the prospect of a ceasefire remains uncertain.

The economic implications are profound, with estimates indicating that 30-40% of refining capacity in the Gulf is compromised, potentially leading to prolonged disruptions in energy supplies. As the G7 convenes, the focus is on how these developments could affect market stability and energy prices, with European leaders expressing concerns about the broader economic fallout, particularly for vulnerable nations.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape, as continued hostilities could exacerbate energy supply constraints and volatility in commodity markets, impacting investment strategies across sectors.
