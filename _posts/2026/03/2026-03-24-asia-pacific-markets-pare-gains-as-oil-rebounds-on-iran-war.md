Asia-Pacific markets experienced a pullback on Tuesday as oil prices rebounded sharply, reflecting ongoing uncertainty surrounding the Middle East conflict. Brent crude futures surged over 3.5% to $103.7 per barrel, while West Texas Intermediate jumped 4% to $91.72 per barrel, following a steep decline on Monday. The volatility in oil prices is underscored by geopolitical tensions, particularly after conflicting reports about negotiations between the U.S. and Iran, which have kept investors on edge.

This rebound in oil prices has significant implications for market sentiment and sector performance, particularly for energy stocks, which could see renewed interest amid rising crude prices. Additionally, Japan's inflation data, showing a decline to 1.3%, may influence the Bank of Japan's monetary policy, further affecting market dynamics in the region. The mixed performance of major indices, with South Korea's Kospi and Japan's Nikkei 225 both showing gains, indicates a cautious optimism tempered by external risks.

Market professionals should closely monitor geopolitical developments and their potential impact on oil prices, as these factors remain pivotal for both energy sector performance and broader market stability.
