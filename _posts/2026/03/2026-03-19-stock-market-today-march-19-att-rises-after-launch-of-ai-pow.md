AT&T (T) shares rose 1.30% to close at $27.77 on Thursday, buoyed by investor enthusiasm surrounding its new AI-driven customer app and broader digital initiatives set for 2025. The stock's trading volume surged to 49.7 million shares, significantly above its three-month average, indicating heightened interest in the telecom giant's strategic pivot towards enhanced customer engagement and network monetization.

This positive momentum comes amid a challenging market backdrop, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq both experiencing slight declines. AT&T's focus on AI tools aims to streamline self-service options for customers, potentially lowering operational costs while enhancing retention and upselling opportunities. The company’s commitment to digital transformation, supported by increased institutional ownership, positions it for potential revenue growth, although it will require ongoing capital investment.
