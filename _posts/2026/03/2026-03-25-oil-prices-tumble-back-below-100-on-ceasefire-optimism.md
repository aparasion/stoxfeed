Oil prices plummeted over 5% in early Asian trading on Wednesday, with WTI crude falling to $87.51 and Brent crude dipping below the critical $100 mark to $98.03. This sharp decline follows a volatile period triggered by fluctuating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly after President Trump’s mixed signals regarding U.S.-Iran negotiations. The market reacted to reports of a potential peace framework that could lead to a ceasefire, contrasting sharply with earlier threats of military action.

The drop in prices is compounded by an unexpected build in U.S. crude and gasoline inventories, as reported by the API, which adds to concerns about oversupply. While the current optimism surrounding a possible diplomatic resolution is driving prices down, underlying geopolitical risks remain high, with continued military threats and regional instability. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape closely, as any shifts could significantly impact oil supply dynamics and pricing in the near term.
