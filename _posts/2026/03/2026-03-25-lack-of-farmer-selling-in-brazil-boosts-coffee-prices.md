Coffee prices surged on Tuesday, with May arabica coffee (KCK26) climbing 3.96% to a seven-week high and May ICE robusta coffee (RMK26) rising 0.69%. This rally is largely driven by Brazilian farmers withholding supplies in anticipation of higher prices, resulting in a tighter cash market. Additionally, the closure of the Strait of Hormuz has disrupted global shipping, exacerbating supply constraints and increasing costs for coffee importers and roasters.

The bullish sentiment in coffee futures is underscored by declining inventories, particularly for robusta, which fell to a two-month low. However, the outlook for a robust Brazilian coffee crop remains a bearish factor, as production estimates have been raised significantly. Recent reports indicate a potential record coffee production of 75.3 million bags for Brazil in the 2026/27 season, alongside a 27% year-over-year drop in February green coffee exports, highlighting the volatility in supply dynamics.

Market professionals should closely monitor the interplay between supply constraints and production forecasts, as these factors will significantly influence coffee pricing trends in the near term.
