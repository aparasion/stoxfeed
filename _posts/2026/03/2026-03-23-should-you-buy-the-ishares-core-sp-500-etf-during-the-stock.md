The S&P 500 index closed at 6,506 on March 20, reflecting a 7% drop from its all-time high amid rising economic uncertainty and geopolitical tensions. Such sell-offs are not uncommon, but they present potential buying opportunities for long-term investors. The iShares Core S&P 500 ETF (IVV), which tracks the index, offers a cost-effective way to gain exposure to both growth and defensive sectors, including key players in artificial intelligence and healthcare.

The current decline highlights the importance of diversification, especially as the "Magnificent Seven" stocks—Nvidia, Apple, Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta Platforms, and Tesla—have underperformed, averaging a 12% drop during this period. The S&P 500, however, remains resilient, historically producing a compound annual return of 10.6% since its inception. 

For market professionals, the current volatility may present a strategic entry point into the S&P 500 ETF. Consider scaling into positions gradually to mitigate risk while capitalizing on potential long-term gains.
