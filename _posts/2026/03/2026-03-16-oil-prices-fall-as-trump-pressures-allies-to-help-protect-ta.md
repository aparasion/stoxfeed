Oil prices took a significant hit on Monday as President Trump intensified efforts to secure tanker traffic in the Strait of Hormuz, with Brent crude futures dropping 2.84% to $100.21 per barrel and U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures plummeting 5.28% to $93.50. This decline follows a surge of approximately 40% since the onset of the U.S.-Iran conflict, which has disrupted shipping routes critical for global oil supply.

The geopolitical tensions surrounding the Strait, a vital passage for 20% of the world’s oil, have led to heightened volatility in oil markets. Trump’s announcement of potential coalition partners to protect tanker routes comes alongside U.S. military strikes on Iranian assets, raising concerns about further escalation. Analysts warn that a direct strike on Iran's oil infrastructure could severely disrupt its exports, potentially leading to retaliatory actions that would exacerbate supply issues.

Market participants should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as any further escalation could significantly impact oil prices and supply stability.
