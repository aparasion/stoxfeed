CD&R is re-entering the leveraged finance market with a significant US$10 billion financing for Sealed Air, but investor sentiment is cautious due to past issues with Multi-Color and escalating risks from the Iran war. This hesitation reflects broader concerns in the market, as investors weigh the potential for higher pricing and volatility against the backdrop of geopolitical tensions.

In contrast, Electronic Arts has successfully priced approximately US$15 billion in debt to support its record-breaking US$55 billion acquisition, demonstrating robust investor appetite for large-scale financings despite recent market turbulence. The deal attracted around US$50 billion in orders, highlighting a strong demand for acquisition-related debt amid a challenging environment.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the contrasting investor responses in leveraged finance: while some deals face scrutiny due to geopolitical risks, others, like EA's financing, indicate a willingness to engage in substantial transactions, suggesting a bifurcation in market sentiment that could shape future deal-making strategies.
