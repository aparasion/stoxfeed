Retirement planning is becoming increasingly urgent for those aiming to retire by 2035, with significant implications for financial markets. The Social Security Administration warns that the Old-Age and Survivors Insurance (OASI) fund could face depletion by 2034, potentially leading to benefit cuts of up to 23%. This looming deficit underscores the importance of proactive financial strategies, as many retirees may find themselves with reduced income from Social Security.

Inflation has further eroded the purchasing power of Social Security benefits, which have lost approximately 20% of their value since 2010. With annual cost-of-living adjustments failing to keep pace with rising prices, retirees must consider alternative income sources to maintain their financial stability. 

For market professionals, this highlights a growing need for investment strategies that prioritize income generation, such as increasing contributions to 401(k)s or investing in dividend stocks. As individuals seek to supplement their retirement income, sectors focused on income-producing assets may see increased demand.
