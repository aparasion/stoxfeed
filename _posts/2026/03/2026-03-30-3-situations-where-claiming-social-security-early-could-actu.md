Social Security recipients face a critical decision when it comes to filing for benefits, especially those born in 1960 or later, whose full retirement age is 67. While the conventional wisdom advises against claiming benefits before this age due to reduced monthly payouts, there are specific scenarios where early claims may be advantageous. Factors such as poor health, job dissatisfaction, or substantial personal savings can make early filing a strategic choice.

For instance, individuals with health concerns may benefit from accessing funds sooner, potentially maximizing their lifetime benefits. Similarly, those unhappy in their careers may find early retirement a viable option to improve their quality of life. Additionally, affluent retirees with significant savings may opt to claim early to enhance their cash flow for experiences, rather than relying solely on their nest egg.

Ultimately, financial professionals should recognize that while early claims can lead to smaller checks, they may also serve as a strategic tool for certain clients, depending on their unique circumstances and financial goals.
