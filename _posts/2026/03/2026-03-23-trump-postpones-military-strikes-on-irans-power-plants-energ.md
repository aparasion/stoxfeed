U.S. President Donald Trump announced a five-day postponement of military strikes on Iran's energy infrastructure, citing "productive" discussions aimed at resolving hostilities in the region. This development follows Trump's earlier ultimatum for Iran to reopen the crucial Strait of Hormuz, a strategic corridor for global oil and gas shipments, which sees about 20% of the world's supply transit through it. 

The announcement has led to a notable decline in oil prices, reflecting market sensitivity to geopolitical tensions that could disrupt supply chains. Traders and analysts are closely monitoring these developments, as any escalation or resolution of conflict in the Middle East can have immediate repercussions on energy stocks and broader market sentiment.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for volatility in oil prices and related equities as negotiations unfold. Continued dialogue may stabilize markets, but any breakdown in talks could lead to renewed price spikes and increased risk in energy sectors.
