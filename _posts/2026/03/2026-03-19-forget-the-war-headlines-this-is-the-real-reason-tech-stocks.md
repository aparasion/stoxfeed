Tech stocks have faced significant headwinds recently, with the Nasdaq-100 index down over 3% year-to-date as of March 13. While the ongoing conflict in Iran has added to market uncertainty, the primary driver of volatility in the tech sector stems from massive capital expenditures on artificial intelligence (AI) infrastructure by major players like Alphabet, Amazon, Meta Platforms, and Microsoft. These companies are projected to spend a staggering $410.2 billion on capex in 2025, raising concerns among investors about the returns on such hefty investments.

The concern is compounded by the rapid obsolescence of AI data center components, which can lead to increased operational costs. However, these tech giants are financially robust, with Alphabet alone reporting $132.2 billion in net income over the trailing 12 months. For investors with a bullish outlook on AI, this dip may present a compelling buying opportunity rather than a reason to divest.
