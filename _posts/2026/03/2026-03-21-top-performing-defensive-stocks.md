The S&P 500 continues to serve as a critical benchmark for investors, with the SPDR S&P 500 ETF (SPY) leading as the largest and most liquid ETF tracking the index. Amid market volatility, a focus on defensive stocks—those with low correlation to SPY—has emerged as a strategic approach for risk-averse investors. Stocks like Procter & Gamble (PG), Merck (MRK), and General Mills (GIS) exhibit low betas, indicating they are less volatile than the broader market, thus providing potential stability during economic downturns.

Investing in these low-correlation defensive stocks can help mitigate market risk and reduce portfolio volatility. For instance, while the S&P 500 faced significant declines in 2022, many of these defensive stocks maintained positive returns, underscoring their resilience in challenging market conditions. Their strong fundamentals and consistent performance make them attractive options for investors seeking to protect their capital.
