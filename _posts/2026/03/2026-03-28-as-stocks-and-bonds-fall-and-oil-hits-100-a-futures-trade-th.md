Managed futures strategies are gaining traction as investors seek alternative returns amid market pressures from the U.S.-Iran conflict and fears of stagflation reminiscent of the 1970s. These strategies, typically employed by commodity trading advisors, utilize systematic models to trade futures contracts across various asset classes, focusing on capturing longer-term trends rather than short-term fluctuations. Notably, managed futures funds outperformed traditional assets in 2022, gaining 20% while the S&P 500 and Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index fell by 18% and 13%, respectively.

The growing interest in managed futures is underscored by significant inflows into ETFs, particularly the iMGP DBi Managed Futures Strategy ETF, which has attracted around $1 billion this year. Major asset managers like BlackRock, Invesco, and Fidelity have also entered the market, signaling strong demand. While these strategies offer potential benefits during volatile periods, they require a solid understanding and patience from investors, as they can underperform in calmer markets.

For market professionals, integrating managed futures into portfolios could provide a hedge against traditional asset volatility, with suggested allocations of 3% to 5% for diversification. As more products emerge, staying informed about their complexities will be crucial for effective portfolio management.
