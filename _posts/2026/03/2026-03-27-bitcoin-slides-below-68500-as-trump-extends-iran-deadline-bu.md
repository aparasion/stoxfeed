Bitcoin dipped below $68,500, falling about 3% as geopolitical tensions surrounding Iran continue to unsettle markets. The latest developments include U.S. President Trump's extension of the ceasefire deadline, which has led to mixed signals and volatility in both crypto and equity markets. Major cryptocurrencies, including Ether and Solana, also experienced declines, while Tron managed to post modest gains.

Despite the price drops, institutional interest remains strong, with Bitcoin ETFs attracting $2.5 billion in net inflows over the past month. This influx indicates a potential shift toward accumulation among institutional investors, particularly in Bitcoin and Ether, as they navigate the uncertainty surrounding the Iran situation. Analysts note that the overall crypto market cap is holding above its 50-day moving average, which could signal a bullish trend if sustained.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape and the upcoming April deadline, as these factors could drive significant price movements in both the crypto and equity markets. The ongoing accumulation trend among institutions may provide a counterbalance to short-term volatility.
