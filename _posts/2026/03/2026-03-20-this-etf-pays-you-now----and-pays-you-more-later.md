The Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIG) is gaining attention for its distinct approach to dividend investing, focusing on companies with a history of increasing their dividends for at least a decade. Unlike many high-yield dividend ETFs that can expose investors to significant risks—where falling stock prices often accompany rising yields—VIG aims to mitigate this by excluding the highest-yielding 25% of stocks, which are often the most vulnerable.

This strategy is particularly relevant in today's market environment, where income-seeking investors are navigating the complexities of dividend sustainability. By prioritizing companies with a proven track record of dividend growth, VIG not only seeks to provide reliable income but also aims to enhance long-term capital appreciation, making it a compelling choice for portfolio diversification.
