The UK stock market opened lower on Thursday, with the FTSE 100 down 1.33% to 9,972.27, as investor caution grew amid ongoing uncertainty surrounding U.S.-Iran peace negotiations. The banking and mining sectors faced significant declines, with major players like Anglo American and Antofagasta dropping 5.2% and 4.4%, respectively. The backdrop of rising oil prices, driven by concerns over supply disruptions, has intensified inflation fears and the potential for central bank rate hikes.

This market reaction underscores the interconnectedness of geopolitical tensions and financial markets. The dismissal of the U.S. proposal by Iran has exacerbated fears of prolonged conflict, impacting investor sentiment and leading to a sell-off in sectors sensitive to economic stability. Conversely, retail giant Next bucked the trend, surging 5.3% after raising its profit guidance, highlighting pockets of resilience amid broader market weakness.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape, as further developments could significantly influence sector performance and overall market sentiment.
