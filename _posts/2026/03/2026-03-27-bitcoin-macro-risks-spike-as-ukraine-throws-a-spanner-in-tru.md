Bitcoin faces increased macro risks as Ukraine's recent drone strikes on Russian oil infrastructure complicate an already volatile energy market. This disruption has significant implications for oil prices, which are expected to remain elevated due to supply constraints exacerbated by the ongoing Iran conflict. With about 40% of Russia's oil export capacity offline, the potential for persistent inflation looms, putting pressure on risk assets, including cryptocurrencies.

Currently, Bitcoin is trading within the $65,000–$75,000 range, but the combination of rising oil prices and inflationary pressures could challenge this stability. Traders are already pricing in a potential Federal Reserve rate hike, which could further dampen liquidity and negatively impact Bitcoin's performance. As of now, Bitcoin is trading around $68,500, reflecting a nearly 2% decline over the past day.

Market professionals should closely monitor these developments, as sustained high energy prices and tightening monetary policy may lead to a downside break in Bitcoin's current price range.
