SoFi Technologies (NASDAQ: SOFI) is experiencing a significant downturn in 2026, with its stock plummeting 35% year to date despite strong operational performance. The all-digital bank is capitalizing on a growing user base, having added a record 1 million accounts in Q4 2025, and reporting a 37% year-over-year increase in adjusted net revenue, surpassing $1 billion for the first time. However, macroeconomic pressures, including high inflation and geopolitical tensions, are weighing heavily on investor sentiment.

The company's diverse product offerings, from traditional banking to innovative financial services, position it as a formidable player in the industry. As the fee-based segment grows faster than lending, SoFi's earnings per share have also seen notable increases, from $0.05 to $0.13. Despite these encouraging metrics, the stock would need to increase nearly 490% to reach $100 by 2030—a challenging target given current market conditions.

For market professionals, SoFi presents a compelling case for long-term growth potential, but the current macro environment suggests caution. Investors may want to monitor the stock for signs of recovery as it navigates these headwinds.
