Nvidia (NASDAQ: NVDA) continues to show robust growth potential as CEO Jensen Huang emphasizes the increasing demand for the company’s AI chips, particularly in the data center segment, which accounted for 91% of its $68.1 billion revenue in fiscal Q4 2026. Huang's bullish outlook on "agentic AI"—advanced AI tools that independently execute tasks—suggests that this technology could drive substantial future demand, potentially leading to multi-trillion-dollar opportunities across various industries.

Despite concerns from market bears about a potential cooling in AI chip demand, Huang's confidence indicates that Nvidia's revenue growth trajectory remains strong. However, the company is notably reliant on a few key customers, with one accounting for 22% of total revenue. A slowdown in investments from these customers could pose risks to Nvidia’s financial stability.

For investors, this scenario presents a critical takeaway: while Nvidia's prospects appear promising, the concentration of revenue among a limited customer base warrants careful consideration. Diversifying investments or exploring alternative stocks may be prudent as the AI landscape evolves.
