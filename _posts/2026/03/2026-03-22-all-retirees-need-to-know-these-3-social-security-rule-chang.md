April brings significant changes for retirees that could impact their financial planning. Notably, the Medicare Advantage open enrollment window closes on March 31, allowing beneficiaries to switch plans if their current coverage is unsatisfactory. This deadline is crucial as it locks individuals into their choices until October, potentially affecting their healthcare costs and access.

Additionally, the taxable earnings cap for Social Security has increased to $184,500 in 2026, meaning higher earners will face an additional payroll tax burden of approximately $520. For retirees still in the workforce, the earnings limit before benefits are withheld has risen to $24,480, with a notable exception for those reaching full retirement age, where the limit jumps to $65,160. Understanding these thresholds is essential for retirees to optimize their income and benefits.

For market professionals, these changes underscore the importance of advising clients on retirement income strategies that account for evolving Social Security regulations and healthcare options, which can significantly influence financial stability in retirement.
