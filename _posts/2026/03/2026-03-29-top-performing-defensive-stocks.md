Investors are increasingly looking at defensive stocks with low correlation to the S&P 500 (SPY) as a strategy to mitigate risk and enhance portfolio stability. Notable companies such as Procter & Gamble (PG), Merck (MRK), and General Mills (GIS) have been highlighted for their low beta values—indicating less volatility compared to the broader market. These stocks not only exhibit sound fundamentals but also have demonstrated resilience during market downturns, making them attractive options for risk-averse investors.

The significance of low-correlation stocks lies in their potential to outperform during economic stress. For instance, while the S&P 500 faced a decline of over 16% in 2022, these defensive stocks maintained positive returns, showcasing their ability to weather market turbulence. This characteristic is particularly appealing for investors seeking steady income and reduced volatility, especially during uncertain economic conditions.

For market professionals, incorporating low-correlation defensive stocks into a diversified portfolio can enhance risk-adjusted returns and provide a cushion against broader market fluctuations. This strategy may be particularly beneficial for those with lower risk tolerance or shorter investment horizons.
