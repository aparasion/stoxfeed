Iran is advancing legislation to impose tolls on vessels navigating the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping corridor, as tensions escalate amid the ongoing conflict with the U.S. and Israel. Reports indicate that shipping traffic has largely halted in the strait, contributing to significant supply constraints and driving oil prices above $160 per barrel for Brent crude. The toll proposal aims to formalize Iranian oversight of the strait, with lawmakers expected to finalize the draft bill by next week.

This development has immediate implications for the energy markets, as any disruption in this critical route could exacerbate existing supply issues and further inflate oil prices. With Brent and West Texas Intermediate crude futures already surging, the potential for increased shipping costs and geopolitical tensions could lead to volatility in energy stocks and related sectors.

Market participants should closely monitor the situation, as the ability of Iran to enforce tolls may provoke a strong response from Gulf Cooperation Council states, potentially escalating regional tensions and impacting global oil supply dynamics.
