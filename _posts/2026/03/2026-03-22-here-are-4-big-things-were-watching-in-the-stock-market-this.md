Investors brace for a quieter week as earnings season wraps up, but heightened geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices are likely to keep markets on edge. Following Iraq's declaration of force majeure on oil fields operated by foreign companies, Brent crude surged past $112 a barrel, intensifying concerns about inflation and economic stagnation. With oil being a critical input cost, its rise could pressure corporate earnings and consumer spending, which are already under scrutiny.

As the week progresses, key economic indicators such as initial jobless claims and consumer sentiment surveys will be closely watched for insights into the labor market and spending behavior. The University of Michigan's consumer sentiment survey, conducted amid the escalating conflict in Iran, will be particularly telling about how these geopolitical dynamics are influencing U.S. consumer attitudes. Additionally, KB Home's earnings report will shed light on the housing market's response to these economic pressures.

Market professionals should remain vigilant regarding oil price fluctuations and their broader implications, as these developments could signal shifts in corporate profitability and consumer behavior moving forward.
