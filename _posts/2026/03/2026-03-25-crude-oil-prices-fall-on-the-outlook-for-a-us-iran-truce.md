WTI crude oil prices fell sharply today, with May futures down 3.53% amid hopes for a diplomatic resolution to the ongoing conflict in Iran. The decline was exacerbated by an unexpected rise in U.S. crude inventories to a 1.75-year high, which adds bearish pressure to the market. Although crude prices briefly recovered after Iran dismissed a U.S. peace proposal, the geopolitical landscape remains volatile, with concerns about potential escalations in the Middle East.

The implications for the energy sector are significant. The International Energy Agency reported extensive damage to energy infrastructure across the region, potentially prolonging supply chain disruptions. Additionally, with OPEC+ planning to increase output despite current production cuts due to the conflict, the market faces a complex interplay of rising inventories and geopolitical risks that could keep prices under pressure.

Market professionals should remain vigilant, as Goldman Sachs warns that if tensions persist and flows through the Strait of Hormuz remain restricted, crude prices could surpass previous highs, making strategic positioning essential in the current environment.
