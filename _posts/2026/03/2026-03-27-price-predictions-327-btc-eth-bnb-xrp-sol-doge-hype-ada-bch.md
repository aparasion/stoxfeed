Bitcoin has fallen below the critical $66,000 support level, raising concerns about a potential decline to $62,500. This downturn coincides with significant outflows from U.S. spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds, totaling $171 million—the largest since March. The bearish sentiment is compounded by geopolitical uncertainties, particularly regarding the U.S. and the Israel-Iran conflict, which are limiting upward momentum. Despite these pressures, large Bitcoin holders continue to accumulate, suggesting a possible shift in market dynamics.

The broader cryptocurrency market is feeling the impact, with major altcoins like Ethereum and BNB also breaking below key support levels. Ethereum's price has dipped below $2,111, while BNB is testing support around $607. If these altcoins fail to hold their respective levels, further declines could ensue, affecting overall market sentiment.

Market professionals should closely monitor these support levels, as a sustained breach could signal deeper bearish trends across cryptocurrencies. Conversely, any recovery above critical resistance points may indicate a potential reversal, warranting strategic reassessment.
