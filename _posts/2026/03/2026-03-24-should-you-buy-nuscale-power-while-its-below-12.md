NuScale Power (NYSE: SMR), a nuclear technology firm specializing in small modular reactors (SMRs), is positioned to capitalize on the anticipated surge in U.S. energy demand, projected to grow by 3.5% annually from 2025 to 2040. With the increasing strain on the power grid from new AI data centers, NuScale’s approved SMR technology offers a potential solution, as it is designed for quicker development and lower costs compared to traditional reactors. However, the company has yet to construct any commercial units, raising questions about its future profitability.

Morgan Stanley estimates that investments in the nuclear sector could reach $2.2 trillion by 2050, presenting a significant opportunity for NuScale. Yet, investors should be cautious, as the stock has declined over 37% in the past year, reflecting the high-risk nature of betting on a company whose valuation hinges on future growth rather than current revenue, which was a modest $31.5 million last year.

For market professionals, NuScale represents a high-stakes opportunity in the evolving energy landscape, but it is best suited for those with a high-risk tolerance who are prepared for the uncertainties ahead.
