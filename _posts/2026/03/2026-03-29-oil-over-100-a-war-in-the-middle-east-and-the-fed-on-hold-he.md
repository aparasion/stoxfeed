Artificial intelligence stocks are facing significant headwinds as escalating conflict in the Middle East and rising oil prices create a turbulent market environment. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz due to military operations has led to a sharp increase in oil prices, now hovering around $110 a barrel, while the Federal Reserve's decision to maintain interest rates reflects concerns over inflationary pressures stemming from these geopolitical tensions.

This situation is particularly challenging for AI-heavy portfolios, which are now grappling with higher energy costs for data centers, increased discount rates affecting long-duration growth stocks, and overall market volatility. As the Nasdaq and Dow Jones indices enter correction territory, investors are urged to reassess their holdings, especially those speculative growth names that lack earnings and depend on anticipated rate cuts.

To navigate this landscape, focus on AI-adjacent companies with solid fundamentals, such as Modine Manufacturing and Clearfield, which possess physical infrastructure and strong balance sheets. These firms are better positioned to withstand macroeconomic pressures, making them potential safe havens within an AI investment strategy.
