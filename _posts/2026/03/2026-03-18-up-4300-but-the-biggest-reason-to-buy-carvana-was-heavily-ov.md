Carvana (CVNA) has emerged as a standout in the used-car retail sector, with its stock soaring 4,300% over the past three years, significantly outpacing the S&P 500's 70% gain. The company recently reported a record net income of $1.895 billion for the full year, buoyed by a notable $685 million benefit from releasing its valuation allowance against deferred tax assets (DTAs). This shift indicates management's confidence in the company's profitability, suggesting a new era of stability after years of losses.

The release of the valuation allowance signals that Carvana now expects to utilize its accumulated DTAs to offset future tax obligations, a crucial development for its financial health. This noncash gain, while primarily an accounting adjustment, points to a more sustainable cash flow outlook as the company transitions from growth at all costs to a focus on profitability.
