Paola M. Arbour, a Board Director at Texas Capital Bancshares (NASDAQ: TCBI), executed a notable sale of 1,000 shares at $91.50 each on March 11, 2026, according to an SEC Form 4 filing. This transaction, valued at approximately $91,500, marks Arbour's largest disclosed sale in three years, contrasting with her recent filings that indicated no share trading activity since April 2023.

The implications of this sale are significant for investors. Arbour's decision to sell shares—rather than as part of a trading plan or stock options—suggests a deliberate move that may reflect her outlook on the company's performance. Texas Capital Bancshares has seen its stock rise about 1.59% in 2026, yet its recent financials reveal a decline in revenue and net income in Q4 FY 2025, raising questions about its operational focus limited to Texas.

Investors should consider the potential impact of insider transactions like Arbour's on stock sentiment, as well as the ongoing performance of TCBI amid fluctuating financial results.
