As retirement approaches, the timing of Social Security claims emerges as a critical decision for financial planning. Individuals can begin collecting benefits as early as 62, but doing so results in a permanent reduction—up to 30% less than if they wait until their normal retirement age of 67. Those who delay their claims can significantly increase their monthly benefits, with checks growing by two-thirds of 1% for each month past their normal retirement age until age 70.

This decision has profound implications for retirement portfolios and cash flow management. Professionals in financial markets should consider how clients' Social Security strategies can impact their overall retirement income and investment strategies. For instance, waiting to claim benefits could allow retirees to draw down other investments more efficiently, potentially enhancing long-term financial stability.
