Amazon (AMZN) shareholders received a significant boost from CEO Andy Jassy's ambitious forecast for Amazon Web Services (AWS), predicting it could generate $600 billion in annual revenue within the next decade. This projection comes as AWS capitalizes on the burgeoning demand for artificial intelligence (AI) tools and models, a shift that has prompted Jassy to revise his earlier estimate of $300 billion. The company is investing heavily—approximately $200 billion in capital expenditures—primarily to expand AWS's capabilities in response to this demand.

This forecast is crucial for financial markets as it underscores AWS's pivotal role in Amazon's growth strategy. With AWS already generating $128.7 billion in sales last year and growing at 20% annually, achieving Jassy's target would require a sustained growth rate of 17%. The current valuation at 29 times earnings suggests that the market may be underestimating AWS's potential, making Amazon an attractive investment opportunity.
