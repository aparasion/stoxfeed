S&P 500 and oil futures experienced a notable surge in trading activity early Monday, just minutes before President Donald Trump made a significant announcement on social media. At approximately 6:50 a.m. ET, S&P 500 e-Mini futures saw a sharp increase in volume, contrasting sharply with the otherwise quiet premarket conditions. A similar spike occurred in West Texas Intermediate May futures around the same time, raising questions among traders about the unusual timing of these movements.

The subsequent announcement from Trump at 7:05 a.m. regarding U.S.-Iran talks and the halting of planned military strikes led to a dramatic market response. S&P 500 futures jumped over 2.5% while WTI futures plummeted nearly 6%. The early trading volume spikes have drawn attention, particularly as they occurred without an apparent catalyst, suggesting potential strategic positioning ahead of the news.

Market professionals should consider the implications of such pre-announcement trading patterns, as they may indicate the presence of algorithmic strategies or informed trading that could influence future market behavior.
