European shares are poised for a lower opening on Tuesday, with the UK's FTSE 100 expected to decline by 0.3% and Germany's DAX and France's CAC 40 both set to drop around 0.5%. This comes as investors remain cautious amid conflicting reports regarding U.S.-Iran relations, which had initially buoyed markets after President Trump hinted at a potential resolution to the conflict. Tehran's denial of any discussions has rekindled uncertainty, impacting market sentiment.

The volatility in the Middle East is influencing oil prices, which rebounded by approximately 3% to surpass $100 per barrel after a sharp decline following Trump's comments. Meanwhile, U.S. futures indicate a negative open, reflecting ongoing tensions. Economic indicators from Europe, including manufacturing PMIs, could further shape market dynamics today.

A key takeaway for market professionals is to watch how geopolitical developments and economic data interplay, as they may create significant trading opportunities in both equity and commodity markets.
