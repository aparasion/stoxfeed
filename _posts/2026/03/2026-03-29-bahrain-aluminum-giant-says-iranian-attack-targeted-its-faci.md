Aluminium Bahrain (Alba), the world's largest aluminum smelter, reported an Iranian attack on its facility as regional tensions escalate amid the ongoing U.S.-Israel-led conflict. The company is currently assessing damage and has already reduced its production capacity by 19% to maintain operational continuity amidst supply disruptions affecting the Strait of Hormuz. This production cut raises concerns about a potential global aluminum shortage, as prices have surged to four-year highs, remaining 4.3% above February levels.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Aluminum is essential across various industries, including electronics, transportation, and construction. The ongoing conflict has already disrupted maritime traffic through critical shipping lanes, with the Bab el-Mandeb Strait accounting for a substantial portion of global oil and LNG trade. Rising oil prices, which recently hit three-year highs, further complicate the economic landscape, potentially leading to increased inflationary pressures.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the region, as continued disruptions could exacerbate supply chain challenges and elevate commodity prices, impacting broader market stability and sector performance.
