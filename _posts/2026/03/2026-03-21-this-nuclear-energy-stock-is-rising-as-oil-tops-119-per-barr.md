Geopolitical tensions have significantly impacted energy markets, pushing oil prices to $119 per barrel and disrupting vital infrastructure in the Middle East. With the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, which accounts for 20% of global LNG and oil transport, the uncertainty surrounding energy supply may accelerate a shift towards nuclear energy. This environment could favor Cameco (NYSE: CCJ), a leading player in the nuclear sector, particularly after securing a $2.6 billion long-term uranium supply agreement with India's Department of Atomic Energy.

Cameco's diversified operations—from uranium mining in Canada and Kazakhstan to its stake in Westinghouse Electric—position it well to capitalize on potential increased investment in nuclear energy. As countries in Asia reconsider their energy strategies due to reliance on unstable oil and LNG supplies, Cameco's existing agreements with key regional players may enhance its growth prospects.

For market professionals, the evolving geopolitical landscape presents a compelling case for monitoring Cameco, as a shift in energy policy could lead to a significant rerating of its stock in the coming months.
