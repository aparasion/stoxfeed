Oil prices rebounded in Asian trading on Tuesday, recovering from significant losses the previous day as traders reacted to ongoing tensions in the Middle East. Brent crude futures for May rose over 3% to $102.96 per barrel, while West Texas Intermediate futures climbed 3.6% to $91.27 per barrel. This recovery follows an 11% drop in Brent crude on Monday, triggered by former President Trump's announcement of a temporary halt to military actions against Iran amid claims of productive negotiations, which Iran subsequently denied.

The volatility in oil prices underscores the market's sensitivity to geopolitical developments, particularly in a region critical to global energy supplies. As José Torres from Interactive Brokers highlighted, concerns about potential disruptions to oil production and transportation remain high, especially given the frequency of attacks on energy infrastructure in the Middle East. Despite a brief uptick in equities, the risk of prolonged conflict continues to loom large.

Market participants should remain vigilant, as the potential for supply disruptions could keep oil prices elevated, even if diplomatic efforts progress. The situation emphasizes the need for traders to closely monitor geopolitical developments and their implications for energy markets.
