Investors are pivoting to cash as geopolitical tensions and inflationary pressures reshape market dynamics, leading to significant sell-offs in gold and bonds. The ongoing conflict in Iran has driven oil prices above $90, exacerbating inflation fears and prompting a defensive stance among traders. Bitcoin (BTC) is particularly vulnerable, having recently retested the critical support level of $67,500, while US Treasuries saw yields rise to a nine-month high of 4.10%, reflecting a strong demand for better returns amid heightened uncertainty.

The S&P 500 has hit a six-month low, underscoring a broader market trend towards liquidity as investors brace for potential further declines. The prospect of a 20% chance of a rate hike by the Federal Open Market Committee adds to the cautious sentiment, with fears of a cooling job market and rising national debt contributing to a cost-of-living crisis for consumers. 

In this environment, the decline in gold prices and the sell-off in Treasuries signal a marked risk aversion, suggesting that Bitcoin may face continued downward pressure unless macroeconomic conditions stabilize.
