Micron Technology (MU) shares have plunged nearly 18% in the past week and about 30% from recent highs, despite a remarkable 267% gain over the last year. This decline is driven by profit-taking and growing investor concerns regarding the sustainability of memory-chip pricing, particularly following Micron's announcement of aggressive capital expenditure plans to boost production. Additionally, Alphabet's new compression technique for AI workloads has further unsettled sentiment, raising fears about future memory demand.

However, the sell-off appears disconnected from Micron's robust fundamentals. The company reported a staggering 196% year-over-year revenue increase to $23.9 billion for fiscal Q2 2026, with earnings per share skyrocketing 682% to $12.20. Management anticipates continued revenue growth, projecting around $33.5 billion in Q3, supported by persistent high demand for memory driven by AI applications.

For market professionals, this recent dip may present a strategic buying opportunity, as Micron's supply constraints and strong demand dynamics suggest that the fundamentals remain solid despite short-term volatility.
