The S&P 500 Index is down 0.16% today, driven by ongoing geopolitical tensions as the conflict in Iran escalates. The Dow Jones Industrial Average shows a slight gain of 0.13%, while the Nasdaq 100 Index has dropped 0.50%. The war's impact is evident, with WTI crude oil prices rising over 4%, reflecting heightened risks that have disrupted global supply chains and elevated inflation expectations.

The energy sector is experiencing a boost, limiting broader market declines, as Saudi Arabia and the UAE signal potential involvement in the conflict. Economic indicators released today, including a stronger-than-expected manufacturing PMI and revised labor costs, suggest underlying resilience in the U.S. economy. However, the market is on edge, anticipating how sustained high oil prices could influence Federal Reserve policy and overall market sentiment.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for increased volatility as geopolitical risks persist. Investors should closely monitor energy prices and their implications for inflation and monetary policy, particularly ahead of the upcoming FOMC meeting.
