Pfizer (PFE) and Moderna (MRNA) are navigating a challenging landscape following a significant decline in COVID-19 vaccine sales, prompting both companies to explore new product launches. Pfizer, with a market cap of $156 billion, remains a dominant player with a diverse product portfolio and over $60 billion in annual revenue, despite facing upcoming patent expirations. Its financial stability allows for continued investment in R&D, making it a safer bet for conservative investors.

In contrast, Moderna, valued at approximately $22 billion, has demonstrated remarkable growth, with a 69% increase in stock price this year, driven by promising developments in mRNA technology, including a cancer vaccine in collaboration with Merck. However, its smaller size and reliance on innovative breakthroughs introduce higher risk, particularly if regulatory approvals falter.

For investors, the choice hinges on risk tolerance: those seeking growth and willing to embrace volatility may favor Moderna, while those prioritizing steady dividends and stability should consider Pfizer. For a deeper dive into their strategies and financial outlooks, check out the full article.
