Advanced Micro Devices (NASDAQ: AMD) and Broadcom (NASDAQ: AVGO) are positioned for significant growth as demand for AI infrastructure surges. AMD is poised to capitalize on the increasing need for data center CPUs, driven by the rise of agentic AI, which is expected to boost its earnings guidance ahead of its fiscal first-quarter report in May. Supply constraints in CPUs are likely to elevate prices, further enhancing AMD's market position.

Broadcom, on the other hand, anticipates robust revenue growth from custom AI chip production, projecting $100 billion in sales by fiscal 2027. The company’s networking components, essential for AI chip connectivity, are also expected to see substantial demand. However, maintaining strong gross margins in its AI ASIC business will be critical for Broadcom’s stock performance as it approaches its earnings report in June.

Investors looking to diversify their portfolios might consider allocating funds to both AMD and Broadcom, as their respective strengths in the AI sector could lead to favorable earnings outcomes and stock appreciation in the coming quarters.
