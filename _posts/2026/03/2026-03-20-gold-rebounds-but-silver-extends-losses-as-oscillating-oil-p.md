Gold prices saw a slight rebound on Friday, rising 0.3% to $4,662.51 per ounce, while silver continued its decline, dropping 1.7% to $71.62. Both metals are on track for significant weekly losses, with gold down nearly 9% and silver over 10%. This volatility follows a broader sell-off triggered by escalating fears surrounding the economic impacts of the ongoing conflict between the U.S. and Israel and Iran, which has also influenced fluctuations in oil prices.

The recent turmoil in precious metals reflects a shift in investor sentiment, as momentum trades unwind after a robust rally in 2025. Analysts note that the influx of speculative capital from retail and hedge funds is receding, potentially paving the way for a more stable environment for gold and silver in the long term. This dynamic suggests that central bank demand may be crucial for future price support.
