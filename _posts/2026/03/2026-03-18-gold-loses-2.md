The Federal Reserve is poised to maintain interest rates at 3.75% during its upcoming meeting, signaling a cautious pause in its monetary policy amid rising inflation driven by oil price shocks and early signs of economic softening. Market participants will closely monitor Fed Chair Jerome Powell's press conference and the updated economic projections, especially as yields decline and gold prices drop nearly 2%.

This meeting comes at a critical juncture, with inflation expectations likely to be revised upward to around 3.0%, while GDP growth forecasts may be lowered to 2.0%. The Fed's shift to a more cautious tone reflects concerns over a potential stagflation scenario, as higher energy costs could dampen household spending and weaken labor demand. Notably, recent labor market data shows a surprising drop in nonfarm payrolls, indicating a potential deterioration in employment conditions.
