Lululemon's recent earnings report revealed a disappointing outlook for 2026, as the athleisure brand grapples with rising tariffs, escalating expenses, and a contentious proxy battle with founder Chip Wilson. The company projects first-quarter sales between $2.40 billion and $2.43 billion, falling short of the $2.47 billion consensus estimate, while earnings per share are expected to be between $1.63 and $1.68, significantly below the anticipated $2.07.

This guidance reflects broader challenges in the retail sector, particularly as Lululemon contends with a shift from a premium pricing strategy to increased promotions to boost sales. The anticipated $380 million impact from tariffs further complicates the company’s financial landscape, with the Americas region expected to see a decline in same-store sales for a third consecutive year. In contrast, Lululemon expects robust growth in China, highlighting a potential divergence in regional performance.
