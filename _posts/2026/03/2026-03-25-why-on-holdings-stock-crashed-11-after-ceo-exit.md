On Holding (ONON) shares fell 11% after the unexpected announcement that CEO Martin Hoffmann will resign on May 1. Co-founders David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti will take over as Co-CEOs, but Hoffmann's departure raises concerns about the company's leadership stability, especially as this marks the second significant executive change within a year. Investors are reacting cautiously, given Hoffmann's prominence as the public face of the brand.

The market's reaction underscores the importance of consistent leadership during a critical growth phase for On Holding, particularly as the company anticipates record sales in 2025. Shifts in strategic direction, especially with the "Dream On" initiative, could impact investor confidence and future performance. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor how this leadership transition affects On Holding’s execution of its growth strategy and overall market sentiment in the coming months.
