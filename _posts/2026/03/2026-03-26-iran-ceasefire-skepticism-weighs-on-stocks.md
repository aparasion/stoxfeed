The S&P 500 Index is down 0.64% today, alongside declines in the Dow Jones Industrial Average and Nasdaq 100, as rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions weigh on market sentiment. WTI crude surged over 3% amid reports of potential U.S. military action against Iran, raising concerns about supply disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil transit route. The situation has led to increased inflation expectations, evident in the uptick in 10-year Treasury yields.

The escalation in oil prices is impacting various sectors, particularly those reliant on stable energy costs. Chipmakers are notably under pressure following a new memory compression technique from Google that could disrupt demand dynamics. Stocks like Sandisk and Lam Research are down significantly, reflecting investor anxiety over the implications for AI workloads and broader tech performance.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Middle East, as continued uncertainty could exacerbate volatility in energy prices and influence broader market trends. With inflationary pressures mounting, the Federal Reserve's policy trajectory may also come into sharper focus in upcoming meetings.
