Bitcoin remains resilient around the $70,000 mark despite external pressures such as geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices. This stability suggests strong underlying demand; however, key indicators like the Coinbase Premium and ETF inflows present a mixed picture. The Coinbase Premium, which reflects U.S. institutional demand, has turned notably negative, indicating softer interest from domestic investors. Additionally, while U.S.-listed bitcoin ETFs saw a net inflow of $1.53 billion this month, the pace has drastically slowed, raising concerns about sustained institutional support.

The current market dynamics highlight a divergence between price stability and demand signals. Analysts emphasize that consistent inflows are critical for maintaining bullish momentum, and the recent slowdown could hinder further price appreciation. 

For market professionals, the takeaway is clear: while Bitcoin's price resilience is noteworthy, the weakening demand indicators suggest a cautious approach. Monitoring institutional interest will be key to understanding the cryptocurrency's trajectory in the coming weeks.
