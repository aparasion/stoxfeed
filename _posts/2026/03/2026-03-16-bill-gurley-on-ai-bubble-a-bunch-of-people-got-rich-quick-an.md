Bill Gurley, a prominent general partner at Benchmark, cautioned on CNBC's "Money Movers" that the current artificial intelligence boom could lead to a significant market reset. He highlighted the cyclical nature of financial bubbles, noting that rapid wealth accumulation attracts a surge of new investors, which can inflate valuations unsustainably. Gurley referenced economic scholar Carlota Perez, emphasizing that while the AI wave is genuine, it often precedes inevitable corrections.

This reset could present opportunities for savvy investors, particularly in the beleaguered software-as-a-service sector, which has seen stocks like Salesforce and ServiceNow drop around 25% this year. The iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF (IGV) has also fallen about 20%. With major players like Amazon and Microsoft projected to invest $700 billion in AI this year, the landscape is shifting, and those prepared to act on undervalued stocks may benefit.
