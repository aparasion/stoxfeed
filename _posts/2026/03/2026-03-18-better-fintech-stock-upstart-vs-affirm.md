Upstart Holdings (NASDAQ: UPST) and Affirm Holdings (NASDAQ: AFRM) have both experienced significant declines, with their stocks down approximately 36% year-to-date. Despite strong business growth—Upstart reported an 86% increase in loan originations and Affirm saw a 36% rise in gross merchandise volume—investors are concerned about their high valuations and the potential for rising defaults in a weakening economy.

The fintech sector is under pressure, primarily due to elevated price-to-earnings ratios, currently around 58, down from much higher levels earlier this year. Both companies have applied for bank charters, which could transform their operations by allowing them to generate their own loans and reduce reliance on third-party banks. This shift could enhance profitability and provide a competitive edge, particularly for Upstart, which leverages advanced AI technology.

For market professionals, Upstart may present a more compelling long-term investment opportunity, given its asset-light model and scalability potential. For a deeper dive into the implications of these developments, I recommend checking out the full article.
