Medicare is implementing a significant shift in its service approval process with the introduction of a pilot program called the WISeR Model, which will require preapproval for 17 specific healthcare services. This initiative, which began in January and operates in six states, leverages artificial intelligence to assess the necessity of certain treatments, aiming to reduce what the government deems "wasteful" services. 

This change could have major implications for healthcare access among retirees, as many may now face challenges in obtaining essential care without prior authorization. The involvement of AI in determining medical necessity has raised concerns about the potential for increased barriers to necessary treatments, especially for seniors who rely on these services. If the pilot proves successful in reducing costs, it may lead to broader adoption of preapproval requirements, further entrenching AI's role in healthcare decision-making.
