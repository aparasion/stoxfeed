Coeur Mining (CDE) shares plummeted over 8% on Wednesday as gold and silver prices dropped more than 3% each, driven by the Federal Reserve's decision to maintain interest rates amid persistent inflation and rising oil prices. With nearly 70% of its revenue tied to gold and 20% to silver, Coeur is particularly vulnerable to fluctuations in these precious metals, which have lost their safe-haven appeal in the current economic climate.

The Federal Reserve's stance, combined with escalating geopolitical tensions, has led to a significant sell-off in precious metals, directly impacting Coeur's stock performance. Despite the immediate downturn, Coeur is poised for a major growth opportunity through its acquisition of New Gold, which is projected to more than double its free cash flow by 2026 and enhance operational efficiencies.

For investors, this volatility presents both challenges and opportunities. While short-term fluctuations may be unsettling, the potential for long-term growth through strategic acquisitions like New Gold could be a key factor to watch.
