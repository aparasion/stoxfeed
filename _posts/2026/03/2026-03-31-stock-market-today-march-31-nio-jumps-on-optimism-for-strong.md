Nio (NYSE:NIO) surged 9.44% on Tuesday, closing at $6.03, buoyed by strong technical momentum and the anticipation of March delivery numbers following the company's first-ever quarterly profit. Trading volume reached 70.1 million shares, significantly above the three-month average, indicating heightened investor interest. The backdrop of rising EV deliveries—up 77% year-over-year in January and February—adds to the optimism surrounding Nio’s growth trajectory.

This rally comes as Nio expands its international presence, recently opening its first Nio House in Costa Rica and seeing improved sales in Europe. Analysts are now focused on the upcoming delivery report, with expectations set at around 33,500 units for March. A beat on these numbers could further propel Nio’s stock, especially after a record monthly delivery performance at the end of 2025.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Nio’s ability to sustain momentum through delivery growth will be critical. A strong March report could solidify investor confidence and support further stock appreciation.
