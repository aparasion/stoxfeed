U.S. Treasury yields dipped on Tuesday morning as investors recalibrated their expectations regarding Federal Reserve interest rates amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The yield on the 10-year Treasury fell by 2 basis points to 4.321%, while the 2-year and 30-year yields also experienced slight declines. These movements reflect a cautious market sentiment, particularly as elevated oil prices raise inflation concerns and recession fears, complicating the monetary policy outlook.

The current market dynamics indicate that traders are largely pricing in no rate cuts from the Fed for the remainder of the year, with futures briefly suggesting a 52% chance of a rate hike by the end of 2026. Fed Chair Jerome Powell's recent comments suggest a measured approach to inflation, despite rising energy costs. As the situation in Iran continues to evolve, the implications for oil supply and prices remain critical for inflation and economic growth.

Market professionals should closely monitor upcoming economic data, such as the February JOLTs jobs report, as it could provide further insights into labor market conditions and influence Fed policy expectations.
