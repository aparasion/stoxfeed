Wall Street is experiencing a downturn ahead of the Federal Reserve's decision, with the Dow Jones dropping 351 points (0.8%) and the S&P 500 and Nasdaq both declining by 0.5%. This market movement reflects a cautious approach as investors react to a stronger-than-expected producer inflation report, which indicated a 0.7% month-over-month increase in PPI for February, significantly above the 0.3% consensus. The implications of rising inflation are compounded by geopolitical tensions, particularly the recent conflict involving Iran, which has led to surging oil prices.

The heightened inflationary pressures, driven by tariffs and escalating energy costs, raise concerns about the Fed's monetary policy stance. Investors are particularly focused on how these factors may influence the Fed's outlook on interest rates, especially as oil prices approach $99 per barrel for WTI and $109 for Brent. The market anticipates that the Fed will maintain current rates but is eager for insights on how persistent inflation and geopolitical risks will shape future policy.

As the Fed meeting approaches, the market remains defensive, with investor sentiment reflecting caution amid elevated oil prices and geopolitical uncertainty. Additionally, attention is turning to Micron Technology's earnings report, which could further influence market dynamics.
