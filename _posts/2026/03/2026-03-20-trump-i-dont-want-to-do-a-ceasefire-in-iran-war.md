President Donald Trump has ruled out a ceasefire with Iran, emphasizing a continued aggressive stance in the ongoing U.S.-Israel conflict. Speaking from the White House, Trump stated, “You don’t do a ceasefire when you’re literally obliterating the other side,” underscoring the U.S. military strategy as tensions escalate in the region. His comments coincide with reports of the Pentagon deploying an additional 2,500 Marines to the Middle East, marking the second troop increase in just a week.

This development has significant implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical tensions, such as energy and defense. The uncertainty surrounding military actions and regional stability could lead to volatility in oil prices and impact stocks of companies tied to defense contracts. Investors should closely monitor these developments as they may influence market sentiment and sector performance.
