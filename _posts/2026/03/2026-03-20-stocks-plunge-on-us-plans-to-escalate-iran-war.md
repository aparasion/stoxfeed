U.S. stock markets experienced significant declines on Friday, with the S&P 500 dropping 1.51%, the Dow Jones down 0.96%, and the Nasdaq 100 falling 1.88%. The downturn was driven by heightened concerns over the geopolitical situation in Iran, particularly the potential for U.S. military involvement and its implications for global oil supply and inflation. As energy prices surged, the markets reacted negatively, pushing major indices to their lowest levels in over six months.

The fallout from the ongoing conflict has led to rising bond yields, with the 10-year Treasury note reaching a 7.5-month high of 4.39%. This spike in yields is raising fears of tighter monetary policy from the Federal Reserve, as inflation pressures mount due to elevated energy costs. The situation is further complicated by the potential for increased interest rates from the European Central Bank, as European yields also hit multi-year highs.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the growing risk of inflationary pressures stemming from geopolitical tensions, which could lead to a tighter monetary policy environment.
