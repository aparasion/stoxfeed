Tesla (TSLA) has experienced a notable 37% increase in share price over the past year, yet analysts are raising alarms about a potential valuation correction. HSBC's Michael Tyndall has reiterated a "reduce" rating on the stock, projecting a price target of $131, implying a steep 65% decline from its current trading level of approximately $373. Tyndall attributes this bearish outlook to emerging challenges in the core electric vehicle (EV) market, particularly as consumer preferences shift toward domestically produced vehicles in key markets like China and the European Union.

The implications for Tesla are significant, as recent sales data shows an 8.6% drop in vehicle deliveries and a 3% revenue decline in 2025. These trends suggest that global growth prospects for Tesla may be weaker than previously thought, raising concerns about the sustainability of its stock performance amid intensifying competition and shifting market dynamics.

Market professionals should closely monitor Tesla's ability to navigate these challenges, particularly as it seeks to capitalize on new growth opportunities in the robotaxi and humanoid robotics sectors.
