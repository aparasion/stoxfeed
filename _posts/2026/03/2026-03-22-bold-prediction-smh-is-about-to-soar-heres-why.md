U.S. equities have navigated volatility in 2026, largely maintaining stability despite geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict in Iran. This uncertainty may present "buy low" opportunities, especially in the technology sector, where semiconductor stocks remain a focal point. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) has shown resilience, supported by the early stages of the artificial intelligence infrastructure boom, which is expected to drive above-average returns in the near term.

Despite soaring tech earnings over the past year, the semiconductor sector is projected to lead S&P 500 earnings growth through 2026 and 2027. This robust outlook underscores the sector's strong fundamental foundation, even as investors express concerns about high valuations. The ETF's trailing P/E ratio stands at 43, but a forward-looking P/E of 23 indicates a more reasonable valuation given the anticipated growth.

Investors should consider that the semiconductor boom is ongoing, suggesting that opportunities for further gains remain. The current market dynamics favor a strategic focus on this sector for potential long-term returns.
