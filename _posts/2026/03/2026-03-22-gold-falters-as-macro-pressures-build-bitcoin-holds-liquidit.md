Gold is facing significant downward pressure as rising real interest rates and inflation concerns weigh on its appeal, pushing it closer to a technical bear market. Despite ongoing geopolitical tensions, gold has declined nearly 20% from its January peak, reflecting a shift in market sentiment as expectations for interest rate cuts have been pushed back to late 2026. This environment, compounded by rising oil prices, poses a substantial headwind for gold, traditionally viewed as a safe haven.

In contrast, bitcoin is currently consolidating, having historically shown resilience during similar phases before reaching new cycle highs. Although bitcoin remains about 40% below its October peak, its performance relative to M2 money supply suggests it may be forming a cyclical floor. Notably, gold and bitcoin have recently exhibited a positive correlation, diverging from previous trends.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the contrasting trajectories of gold and bitcoin, with gold's struggle against macroeconomic pressures highlighting the importance of liquidity dynamics in both asset classes. This divergence may present unique trading opportunities as market conditions evolve.
