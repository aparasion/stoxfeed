Indian shares opened sharply lower on Friday, with the benchmark BSE Sensex dropping 1,080 points (1.43%) to 74,194, and the NSE Nifty index falling 332 points (1.4%) to 22,974. This decline follows U.S. President Donald Trump's announcement to delay military action against Iran, which has led to heightened tensions and uncertainty in the region. Iran's reported mobilization of over one million troops further exacerbates concerns, impacting investor sentiment.

The fallout is evident across various sectors, with major companies like Maruti Suzuki, Bajaj FinServ, and HDFC Bank experiencing declines of 2-3%. Additionally, Power Mech Projects saw a significant drop of 3.4% after a key tender was annulled. Conversely, Brigade Enterprises and Azad Engineering posted gains, highlighting the mixed market reaction amid broader geopolitical tensions.

Market professionals should monitor how these developments influence sector performance and investor sentiment, particularly in energy and banking, as geopolitical risks continue to shape market dynamics.
