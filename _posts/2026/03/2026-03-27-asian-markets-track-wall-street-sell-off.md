Asian stock markets are mostly down on Friday, following a significant sell-off on Wall Street amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. Concerns over the economic impact of the conflict have been exacerbated by rising crude oil prices, which surged nearly 5% to $94.83 per barrel. The situation intensified as Iran dismissed a U.S. peace proposal, and Gulf nations condemned Iran's actions, further complicating the geopolitical landscape.

In Australia, the S&P/ASX 200 Index fell 0.37%, with notable weakness in gold miners and mixed performances across sectors. Japanese markets also faced declines, with the Nikkei 225 dropping 0.86%. The broader trend reflects a risk-off sentiment, as investors react to geopolitical uncertainties and their potential implications for global supply chains and economic growth.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Middle East, as ongoing tensions could lead to further volatility in energy prices and broader market sentiment, influencing trading strategies and portfolio allocations.
