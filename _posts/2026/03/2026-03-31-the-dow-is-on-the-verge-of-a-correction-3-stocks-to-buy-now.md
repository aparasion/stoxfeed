The Dow Jones Industrial Average is teetering on the brink of correction territory, having briefly dipped below the 10% threshold from its previous high last week. As of March 30, 2026, the index managed to climb slightly above this mark, but any negative news could easily push it back into correction. This volatility presents potential buying opportunities for investors focused on blue-chip stocks.

Chevron stands out as a top pick, benefiting significantly from soaring oil prices due to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. With a projected annual earnings growth of over 10% and a solid dividend yield of approximately 3.4%, Chevron offers a resilient investment option amid uncertainty. Conversely, while JPMorgan Chase faces headwinds from inflation fears, its strong balance sheet and undervaluation make it a compelling choice for long-term investors. Walmart, known for its stability during market fluctuations, continues to attract investors with its low-price leadership and robust e-commerce growth.

In this environment, investors may want to consider these three stocks as strategic additions to their portfolios, balancing potential risks with solid fundamentals.
