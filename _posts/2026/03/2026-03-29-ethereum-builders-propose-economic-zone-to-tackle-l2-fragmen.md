Gnosis and Zisk, with support from the Ethereum Foundation, have unveiled a proposal for the "Ethereum Economic Zone" (EEZ), a framework designed to unify Ethereum’s fragmented layer-2 ecosystem. This initiative aims to allow rollups to interact seamlessly with each other and the mainnet within a single transaction, addressing the current issue of liquidity and user activity being split across multiple layer-2 networks. By enabling smart contracts on different rollups to execute synchronously, the EEZ could enhance throughput while reducing reliance on bridges and cross-chain transfers.

The proposal comes at a critical time as Ethereum's scaling strategy faces scrutiny. With over 20 active layer-2 networks managing nearly $40 billion in total value, the need for a cohesive approach is evident. Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin has highlighted the shortcomings of the existing rollup-centric model, suggesting that a new direction is necessary to optimize the ecosystem's performance.

A key takeaway is the potential for the EEZ to streamline operations across Ethereum’s layer-2 networks, which could lead to improved liquidity and efficiency. As the technical details emerge, market participants should monitor how this framework may reshape the landscape for decentralized applications and infrastructure development within the Ethereum ecosystem.
