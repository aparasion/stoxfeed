The Federal Reserve is expected to maintain its current interest rate range of 3.5%-3.75% during this week's meeting, as market participants anticipate no rate cuts until at least September. Amidst geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict in Iran, and mixed signals from the labor market, the Fed faces a complex economic landscape. Chair Jerome Powell's messaging will be crucial, especially as traders adjust their expectations for future rate cuts, with the consensus leaning toward just one potential cut this year.

This decision underscores the Fed's cautious approach to inflation and economic growth, with analysts noting that the bar for further cuts may be high. The upcoming Summary of Economic Projections will provide insights into the Fed's outlook, but few changes are expected, maintaining a status quo that reflects ongoing uncertainties.
