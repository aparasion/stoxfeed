Bitcoin faces a critical moment as over 90% of call options could expire worthless if the price fails to breach $71,000 by Friday. The cryptocurrency has been trading within a narrow range of $67,700 to $71,600, influenced by geopolitical tensions and rising inflation fears. The upcoming $18.6 billion Bitcoin monthly options expiry could be pivotal for bulls, who are hoping for a breakout above $75,000, but recent market conditions have favored bearish sentiment.

With $11.2 billion in call options dominating March's open interest, the overwhelming positioning at higher strike prices suggests a miscalculation by bulls. The current price stagnation means that 92% of these options could become worthless if Bitcoin remains below $71,000. Additionally, concerns over the US economy, particularly in the private credit sector, add to the uncertainty surrounding Bitcoin's price trajectory.

Traders should closely monitor the price action leading up to the options expiry, as a rally of just 6% is needed to shift the outcome in favor of call options. The implications of this expiry could set the tone for Bitcoin's performance in the near term, making it essential for market participants to stay vigilant.
