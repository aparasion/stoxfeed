Apple is set to launch a foldable iPhone later this year, a move that analysts believe could generate between $40 billion and $60 billion in revenue within 18 months. This development comes as Apple stock (NASDAQ: AAPL) has struggled, declining 2% over the past six months amid investor concerns over the company's slow integration of artificial intelligence (AI) features into its devices. Despite these challenges, strong iPhone sales in the last quarter suggest that customers remain loyal, indicating potential for growth with the new foldable model.

The introduction of a foldable iPhone could serve as a significant catalyst for Apple’s stock, potentially revitalizing investor interest and boosting both top and bottom lines. Historically, Apple has maintained a robust customer base and a comprehensive ecosystem, making it a relatively safe investment in the tech sector. 

For market professionals, the anticipated launch presents an opportunity to reassess Apple’s growth trajectory. While the stock trades at a high valuation of around 32 times trailing earnings, the foldable iPhone could provide a much-needed boost, making it worth considering for long-term portfolios.
