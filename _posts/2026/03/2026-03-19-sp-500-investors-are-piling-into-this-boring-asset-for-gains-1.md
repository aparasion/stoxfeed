Investors are increasingly turning to traditionally "boring" assets, such as utilities and consumer staples, as a strategy to navigate market volatility. This trend is reflected in the S&P 500, where these sectors are experiencing notable inflows, signaling a shift in investor sentiment towards stability amid economic uncertainty. 

The movement towards these defensive sectors indicates a broader concern about potential market downturns and inflationary pressures, which could impact earnings across more cyclical industries. As investors seek refuge in these less volatile stocks, we may see a divergence in performance, with defensive sectors potentially outperforming growth-oriented stocks in the near term.
