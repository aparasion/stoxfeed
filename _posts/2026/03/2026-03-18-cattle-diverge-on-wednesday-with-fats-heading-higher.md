Live cattle futures experienced a notable uptick on Wednesday, closing with gains ranging from 17 to 77 cents. However, cash trade remains subdued, as evidenced by the Fed Cattle Exchange auction, which reported no sales from the 1,026 head offered, with bids hovering between $233 and $234. In contrast, feeder cattle futures dipped by 32 cents, reflecting a mixed sentiment in the market.

The decline in wholesale boxed beef prices, with Choice boxes down $1.56 to $401.75 and Select boxes decreasing by 55 cents to $396.17, highlights potential pressure on cattle valuations. Additionally, USDA data indicates a decrease in federally inspected cattle slaughter, down 9,000 head from last week and 20,077 head compared to the same week last year, suggesting tightening supply dynamics that could influence future pricing.
