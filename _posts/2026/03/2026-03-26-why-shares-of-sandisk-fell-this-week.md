Shares of Sandisk (SNDK) plummeted 14.1% this week following Google's announcement of a new compression algorithm that significantly reduces memory requirements for AI models. This development raised concerns among investors about a potential decline in demand for memory products, particularly as macroeconomic fears grow regarding a slowdown in the U.S. economy linked to the ongoing war in Iran.

The tech sector has seen robust investment in AI infrastructure, with major players like Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, and Alphabet expected to spend a collective $650 billion this year. However, the new algorithm's implications for memory usage have led to uncertainty, prompting a sell-off in memory stocks, including Sandisk. While some analysts debate the long-term effects of this technology on memory demand, the immediate market reaction has been one of caution.

For investors, a wait-and-see approach may be prudent. Given the nascent nature of the technology and the broader economic context, holding onto Sandisk shares could be more beneficial than hastily exiting the position.
