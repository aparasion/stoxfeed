SoundHound AI (SOUN) is experiencing a significant downturn in 2026, with its stock plummeting 71% from October highs, largely driven by aggressive short-selling, which has seen over 35% of shares borrowed by bearish investors. Despite the stock's struggles, the company's fundamentals remain strong, as sales doubled in 2025 and over 100 new deals were secured in Q4, indicating robust demand for its voice control technology across various sectors.

The market's reaction appears disconnected from SoundHound's operational success. With a clean balance sheet, virtually no debt, and $248 million in cash, the company is well-positioned to weather the current volatility. Its technology is gaining traction in diverse industries, from automotive to government, further solidifying its market presence.

For investors, this situation presents a potential opportunity. As the market overreacts to short-term price movements, SoundHound AI may represent a compelling buy for those willing to look beyond the noise and focus on the underlying business strength.
