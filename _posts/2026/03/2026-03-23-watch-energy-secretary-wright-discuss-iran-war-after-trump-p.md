U.S. Energy Secretary Chris Wright is set to address an energy conference in Houston, coinciding with rising market hopes that President Trump may signal an end to the ongoing Iran conflict. This comes after Trump announced a five-day delay on strikes against Iran's power infrastructure, following what he described as "productive" discussions. The situation has led to unprecedented oil supply disruptions, with the Strait of Hormuz—responsible for about 20% of global oil exports—facing significant threats from Iranian actions.

The implications for financial markets are profound. Oil prices have surged over 40% since late February, topping $100 per barrel, while recent developments have caused oil prices to drop sharply, leading to a rally in Dow futures and a decline in the 10-year Treasury yield. The volatility underscores the delicate balance between geopolitical tensions and market reactions.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments from CERAWeek, as any signs of de-escalation could influence oil prices and broader market sentiment significantly.
