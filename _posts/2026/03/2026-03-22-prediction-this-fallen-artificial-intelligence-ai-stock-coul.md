In late February 2026, IBM's stock took a hit, dropping 20% from its 52-week high, following news that Anthropic's AI tools could handle COBOL coding tasks, raising concerns about IBM's relevance in the AI landscape. Despite this downturn, IBM is strategically positioned in the AI infrastructure space, focusing on hybrid cloud systems that integrate both internal and external data storage, which is crucial for businesses safeguarding sensitive information.

The current price-to-earnings ratio of approximately 23x is below IBM's five-year average of 30x, making it an attractive option for long-term investors looking to capitalize on the AI revolution. While the stock's price-to-sales and price-to-book ratios remain elevated, the sell-off may have created a buying opportunity for those willing to look beyond short-term fluctuations. Additionally, IBM's leadership in quantum computing could further enhance its capabilities in AI, positioning it as a diversified player in the tech sector.

For market professionals, IBM presents a compelling case as a long-term investment in AI, especially as its foundational technologies could enhance customer service and operational efficiency, countering recent negative sentiment.
