Cocoa prices faced downward pressure on Monday, with May ICE NY cocoa dropping 2.30% and May ICE London cocoa falling 1.91%. This decline comes amidst expectations of a significant West African cocoa crop, driven by favorable weather conditions in Ivory Coast and Ghana. With cocoa inventories reaching a 7.5-month high and Ghana announcing steep cuts in farmer payments for the upcoming growing season, market sentiment remains bearish.

The implications for the cocoa market are substantial, as both supply and demand dynamics shift. While ample supply from West Africa and increased exports from Nigeria contribute to lower prices, demand concerns persist. Notably, Barry Callebaut reported a 22% decline in cocoa sales volume, and European cocoa grindings fell 8.3% year-over-year, signaling waning consumer interest in chocolate products. 

Market professionals should closely monitor these trends, as the combination of rising inventories and weakening demand could lead to further price declines in the cocoa sector.
