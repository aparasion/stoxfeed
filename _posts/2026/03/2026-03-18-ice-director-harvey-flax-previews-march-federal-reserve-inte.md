The Federal Reserve has opted to maintain interest rates amid escalating inflation concerns exacerbated by the ongoing conflict in Iran, which has driven oil prices above $108 per barrel. This decision comes as inflation was already on the rise, with wholesale prices increasing by 0.7% in February—twice the anticipated rate—prior to the latest energy crisis.

The Fed's stance reflects a cautious approach to monetary policy as geopolitical tensions threaten to further destabilize the market. Analysts warn that if disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz continue, oil prices could soar to $150, which would have significant implications for inflation and consumer spending.
