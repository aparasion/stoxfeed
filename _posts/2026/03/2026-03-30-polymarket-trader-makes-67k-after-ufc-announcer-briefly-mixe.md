A Polymarket trader, operating under the alias LlamaEnjoyer, turned a $676 wager into an astonishing $67,608 during a UFC event on Saturday by seizing on a momentary error in the fight announcement. Initially, UFC presenter Bruce Buffer incorrectly declared Marcin Tybura the winner over Tyrell Fortune, causing Fortune's shares to plummet to one cent. Recognizing the mistake, LlamaEnjoyer quickly placed a bet before the announcement was corrected, yielding a near 100x return.

This incident highlights the volatility and rapid price shifts in prediction markets, particularly during live events. The trading activity on platforms like Polymarket has surged, with March 2026 seeing over $10.4 billion in trading volume—ten times that of the previous year. With more than 865,000 users engaging in diverse betting opportunities, this market's growth reflects increasing interest in alternative investment avenues.

For market professionals, this underscores the potential for significant returns in prediction markets, but also the inherent risks of rapid information changes that can lead to substantial losses if not managed carefully.
