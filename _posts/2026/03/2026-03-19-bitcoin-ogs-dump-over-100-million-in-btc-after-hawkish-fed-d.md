Bitcoin experienced a significant sell-off as prominent long-term holders offloaded over 1,650 BTC, valued at approximately $117 million, following a hawkish stance from the Federal Reserve. The Fed's recent decision to maintain interest rates and signal a slower pace of rate cuts has dampened sentiment across risk assets, including cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin's price dipped nearly 1% to $70,600, extending a 3.5% decline from the previous day.

This shift in the Fed's outlook, highlighted by a median projection of only one rate cut this year, has prompted a reevaluation of market expectations. Investors are now grappling with a "higher for longer" narrative, driven by persistent inflation and rising energy costs. Consequently, the broader crypto market, represented by the CoinDesk 20 Index, fell by 3%, reflecting a wider risk-off sentiment.
