European stocks are set to open sharply lower as escalating tensions in the Iran war weigh heavily on market sentiment. The U.K.'s FTSE 100 is projected to decline by 1%, while Germany's DAX and France's CAC 40 are expected to fall by 1.5% and 1.4%, respectively. The situation is exacerbated by threats from both the U.S. and Iran regarding energy infrastructure, particularly concerning the critical Strait of Hormuz, a vital maritime passage for oil shipments.

This geopolitical turmoil is likely to influence investor sentiment across the board, with energy stocks particularly vulnerable to price fluctuations. Crude oil prices have already experienced volatility, reflecting market anxiety. Additionally, U.S. stock futures remained stable, but the major benchmarks have faced a challenging streak, with four consecutive weekly losses.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving situation in Iran, as it could lead to further disruptions in energy markets and impact European earnings reports, particularly from companies like Kongsberg Gruppen and Galp Energia.
