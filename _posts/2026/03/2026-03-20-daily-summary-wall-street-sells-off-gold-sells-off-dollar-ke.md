U.S. index futures have adopted a defensive stance amid escalating tensions in the Middle East, with small-cap stocks in the Russell 2000 leading the decline at 2.7%. The Nasdaq, S&P 500, and Dow also posted significant losses, driven by Iranian attacks on oil infrastructure, which have pushed Brent crude prices up by 3.8% to nearly $107 per barrel. The geopolitical climate is further complicated by the U.S. preparing for potential military action, raising concerns over prolonged energy price shocks and inflation.

The implications for the financial markets are profound, as rising energy prices are boosting inflation expectations and prompting a shift in monetary policy outlook. The futures market is now pricing in a December interest rate hike, a significant change from previous expectations. Additionally, the Dollar Index is rebounding as investors flee to safety, impacting risk-on currencies and driving a sell-off in precious metals.
