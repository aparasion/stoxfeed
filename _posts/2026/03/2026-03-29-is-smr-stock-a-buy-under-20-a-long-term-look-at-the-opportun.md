NuScale Power is gaining traction as a key player in the small modular reactor (SMR) market, driven by a surge in global interest in nuclear energy, particularly to meet the power demands of artificial intelligence. The company, trading under the symbol SMR, is the first to receive approval from the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission for its SMR design and has secured a $400 million grant from the Tennessee Valley Authority to deploy 6 gigawatts of power across its service area, with commercial operations expected to begin in the early 2030s.

Despite its promising technology, NuScale faces challenges with revenue generation, which has been inconsistent and heavily reliant on ongoing projects. Recent figures show a decline in revenue from its Romanian project, raising questions about its financial stability. While the company is on the cusp of significant technological deployment, it remains vulnerable to investor sentiment and government funding.

For market professionals, NuScale presents an intriguing yet speculative investment opportunity. Its potential to lead in SMR deployment could yield long-term gains, but the risks associated with revenue volatility warrant cautious consideration.
