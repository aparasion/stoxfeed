Nuclear energy is poised for a significant resurgence, driven by escalating geopolitical tensions, particularly the recent closure of the Strait of Hormuz by Iran amid conflicts with the U.S. and Israel. This disruption has led to rising oil and gas prices globally, prompting countries to seek energy independence and pivot towards nuclear power. Currently, there are 75 nuclear reactors under construction worldwide, with the U.S. aiming to triple its nuclear production by mid-century, highlighting a strong trend towards carbon-free energy solutions.

NextEra Energy (NEE), a major player in the nuclear sector, is well-positioned to benefit from this shift. The company, which operates seven reactors and has plans for future expansion, has partnered with Alphabet to supply electricity from a revived nuclear plant in Iowa. With a solid dividend yield of 2.54% and a history of consistent dividend increases, NextEra represents a compelling long-term investment opportunity in the evolving energy landscape.

As nations accelerate their nuclear initiatives in response to energy security concerns, investors should closely monitor NextEra and similar companies that are strategically aligned with this trend.
