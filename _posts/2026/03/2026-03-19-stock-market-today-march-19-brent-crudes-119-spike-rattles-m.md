The S&P 500, Nasdaq Composite, and Dow Jones Industrial Average all closed lower today, with declines of 0.27%, 0.28%, and 0.44%, respectively, as rising oil prices contributed to market volatility. Energy stocks like ExxonMobil and Chevron saw gains, while Micron Technology struggled despite strong earnings, and Alibaba faced a sharp drop following disappointing results. The day was marked by a significant sell-off in gold and aerospace stocks, reflecting broader market unease.

The spike in Brent crude oil prices, which briefly exceeded $119 a barrel, heightened fears of sustained inflationary pressures, particularly in the wake of escalating conflicts in the Middle East. This environment has led to a cautious stance among investors, with JPMorgan cutting its 2026 S&P 500 target and warning that persistent high oil prices could dampen global growth.
