SoFi Technologies (NASDAQ: SOFI) closed down 1.09% at $17.18 on Wednesday as investors reacted to the company’s rebuttal of a short report by Muddy Waters and CEO Anthony Noto's $500,000 insider share purchase. The trading volume surged to 80.7 million shares, significantly surpassing the three-month average, indicating heightened investor interest amid ongoing regulatory scrutiny. The broader fintech sector also faced pressure, with LendingClub and Upstart experiencing declines of 5.70% and 7.19%, respectively.

The response to Muddy Waters’ claims highlights the contentious environment surrounding SoFi, as the company asserts that the report misrepresents its financials. While Noto's insider purchase may signal confidence, it represents a small fraction of his total holdings, suggesting that investors should exercise caution.
