The S&P 500 closed lower on Friday, reflecting growing uncertainty stemming from the ongoing conflict in the Middle East. This geopolitical tension has not only impacted major indices like the Nasdaq and Dow but has also pushed the Russell 2000 Index into correction territory, signaling broader market apprehension.

Market professionals should note that amidst this volatility, BNP Paribas upgraded ServiceNow, citing a favorable risk/reward profile following a significant sell-off. The firm highlighted the stabilization of ServiceNow's core business and promising growth prospects in AI, which could enhance margins. Conversely, Eli Lilly faced a downgrade due to concerns over its valuation, particularly regarding its ability to expand in the competitive obesity treatment market.

As market sentiment remains fragile, investors may want to closely monitor sector-specific developments and reassess positions in light of geopolitical risks and individual company fundamentals.
