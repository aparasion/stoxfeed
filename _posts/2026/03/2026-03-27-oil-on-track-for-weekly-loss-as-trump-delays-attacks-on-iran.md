Oil prices rose on Friday, with Brent crude climbing 2.2% to $110.34 per barrel and WTI crude up 1.9% at $96.32. Despite this uptick, both benchmarks are on track for a weekly decline of nearly 2% due to easing tensions in the Middle East, following U.S. President Donald Trump's decision to delay military action against Iran and extend negotiations regarding the Strait of Hormuz.

The potential for de-escalation in the region has sparked initial optimism, but ongoing military actions, including Israeli strikes on Iranian infrastructure and Iran's mobilization of ground troops, keep the threat of volatility alive. Market participants remain cautious, as geopolitical risks can significantly impact oil supply and prices, particularly with the looming deadline for negotiations.

For investors, the key takeaway is the delicate balance between rising oil prices due to current tensions and the potential for a more stable market if diplomatic efforts succeed. Monitoring developments in the Middle East will be crucial for assessing future price movements and sector performance.
