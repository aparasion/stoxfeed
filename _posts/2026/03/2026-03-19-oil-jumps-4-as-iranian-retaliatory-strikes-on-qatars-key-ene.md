Oil prices surged as tensions escalate in the Middle East, particularly following Iranian missile strikes that damaged a crucial liquefied natural gas (LNG) export facility in Qatar. Brent crude futures climbed 4% to $111.80, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures rose over 3% to $99.47. The attacks have raised concerns over potential supply disruptions, especially since Qatar is the second-largest LNG exporter globally, accounting for nearly 20% of shipments.

The implications for financial markets are significant, as the ongoing conflict threatens to deepen the supply shock already exacerbated by the Iran war. With tanker movements through the Strait of Hormuz—responsible for about 20% of global oil supplies—being compromised, analysts warn of escalating volatility. If the conflict spreads beyond the Gulf, targeting energy infrastructure in other regions, we could witness a dramatic shift in oil pricing dynamics, leading to extreme market reactions.
