Friday's market performance was overshadowed by rising oil prices and escalating concerns over potential fuel supply disruptions, primarily driven by the ongoing conflict in Iran. By midday ET, the S&P 500 and Dow Jones Industrial Average were down 0.7%, while the Nasdaq Composite saw a deeper decline of 1.1%. With all three major indexes down approximately 7% for March, the tech-heavy Nasdaq is particularly feeling the pressure, as the "Magnificent 7" stocks, including Meta and Amazon, dropped significantly.

The surge in oil prices, with the United States Oil Fund rising nearly 4% on Friday and up 48% over the past month, is acting like a tax on the broader market. This spike is expected to have downstream effects on consumer prices, as prolonged uncertainty regarding the Iran situation continues to loom. While energy and utility stocks gained, the tech sector's decline underscores the challenges facing cap-weighted indexes.

For market professionals, this environment highlights the importance of maintaining a diversified portfolio and staying patient amid volatility. The current geopolitical tensions may create both risks and opportunities, necessitating a strategic approach to navigate the evolving landscape.
