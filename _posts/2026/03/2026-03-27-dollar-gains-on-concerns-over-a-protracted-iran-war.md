The dollar index reached a one-week high today, rising 0.05% as escalating tensions in the Iran conflict heightened safe-haven demand. The dollar's strength is further supported by a 3% surge in crude oil prices, which is pushing inflation expectations upward and may compel the Federal Reserve to maintain a restrictive monetary policy stance. However, the dollar faced some pressure following a downward revision of the University of Michigan's consumer sentiment index, which came in lower than anticipated.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly as the Fed's potential interest rate decisions loom. With swaps markets pricing in a 4% chance of a 25 basis point rate hike at the upcoming FOMC meeting, the dollar's trajectory could be influenced by geopolitical developments and inflation trends. Meanwhile, the euro found some support from hawkish comments from the ECB, despite easing inflation expectations that weigh on its outlook.

Market professionals should closely monitor these geopolitical events and inflation indicators, as they will likely influence currency valuations and central bank policies in the coming weeks. The interplay between safe-haven demand and monetary policy expectations will be crucial for positioning in both currency and commodity markets.
