Volkswagen is in discussions with Rafael Advanced Defense Systems to potentially convert one of its Saxon plants into a facility for producing components for Israel's Iron Dome defense system. This initiative aims to save the plant from closure and comes as the company grapples with significant employment challenges, including a forecasted reduction of 30,000 to 50,000 jobs by 2030.

The implications for Volkswagen are multifaceted. The conversion could provide a critical lifeline for the Osnabrück plant, which employs around 2,300 people, while also positioning VW to benefit from an uptick in defense spending amid global security concerns. The company’s shares have reacted positively, showing a bullish gap and breaking above the €89–90 resistance level, although they remain near the lower end of a long-term upward trend.

For market professionals, this development underscores the potential for traditional automotive companies to diversify into defense sectors, which may offer new revenue streams in a challenging economic landscape.
