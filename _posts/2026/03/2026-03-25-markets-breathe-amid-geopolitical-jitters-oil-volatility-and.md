The Trump administration's introduction of a 15-point proposal to Iran has sparked a relief rally across global markets, leading to a pullback in oil prices and a firming of equity futures. This development has prompted a reversal in some stagflation trades, indicating a shift in investor sentiment. Meanwhile, South Korea's markets reflect a mixed picture, with the KOSPI benefiting from global tech momentum, while the Korean Won languishes near two-decade lows against the US Dollar, highlighting investor caution.

In the cryptocurrency sector, Bitcoin remains resilient above $70,000, but Ethereum struggles below key resistance levels, and XRP shows bearish momentum. The recent bankruptcy of crypto lender BlockFills adds to concerns about potential contagion in the crypto market, which is still reeling from recent corrections. Additionally, new restrictions on passive rewards for stablecoin holdings under the CLARITY Act have negatively impacted crypto stocks, notably Circle, which saw a nearly 20% drop.

For market professionals, the implications are clear: monitor geopolitical developments closely, as they can drive significant market shifts, and remain vigilant in the crypto space, where regulatory changes could reshape investment strategies.
