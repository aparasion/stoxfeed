Venezuelan opposition leader María Corina Machado is set to address oil and gas executives at S&P Global's CERAWeek conference in Houston, highlighting the ongoing hesitance to invest in Venezuela's energy sector following the U.S. ousting of Nicolás Maduro. Machado, a key figure in the push for democratic reforms and a market economy, aims to reassure industry leaders about the potential for investment in a country rich in crude reserves.

Despite the recent political upheaval, major U.S. oil companies like ConocoPhillips and Exxon Mobil remain skeptical about returning to Venezuela. Both firms have emphasized the need for significant political reforms and guarantees to protect their investments, particularly after facing asset seizures under Hugo Chavez. Conoco's CEO, Ryan Lance, pointed out that current reforms are insufficient to attract the necessary capital to revitalize the sector.

The key takeaway for market professionals is that without robust political stability and a clear commitment from both Venezuelan and U.S. administrations, the energy sector's recovery and investment influx will likely remain stalled, impacting global oil supply dynamics.
