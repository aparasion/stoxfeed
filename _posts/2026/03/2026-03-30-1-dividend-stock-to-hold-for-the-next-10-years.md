The stock market has faced significant turbulence, particularly following the onset of the Iran war, with the S&P 500 index down 4.2% year-to-date as of March 23. In this volatile environment, maintaining a long-term investment perspective is crucial, especially for those looking to capitalize on dividend-paying stocks. Realty Income (NYSE: O), a real estate investment trust (REIT), stands out due to its consistent dividend history and resilience in the retail sector.

Realty Income's business model, which requires it to distribute at least 90% of its taxable income, makes it attractive for income-focused investors. The REIT has successfully maintained high occupancy rates of nearly 99% and has achieved consistent rent increases, including a 3.9% rise on renewed leases last year. With an adjusted funds from operations (AFFO) growth of about 2% and a robust dividend coverage, Realty Income is well-positioned for future dividend hikes.

For market professionals, Realty Income's 5.4% dividend yield, significantly higher than the S&P 500's 1.2%, presents a compelling case for long-term investment in a challenging market landscape.
