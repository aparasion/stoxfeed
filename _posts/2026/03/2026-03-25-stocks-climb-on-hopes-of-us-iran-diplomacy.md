The S&P 500 Index rose by 0.83% today, buoyed by a significant decline in crude oil prices and falling bond yields following a U.S. peace proposal aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict with Iran. The proposal includes measures to limit Iran's nuclear program and missile capabilities in exchange for sanctions relief. This development initially sent WTI crude oil down over 4%, while the 10-year Treasury yield fell to 4.32%.

The implications for financial markets are notable. Lower oil prices typically ease inflationary pressures, which can influence Federal Reserve policy and interest rate expectations. However, the market's optimism was tempered after Iran's rejection of the U.S. proposal, leading to a rebound in oil prices and concerns over potential escalation in the region. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the volatility in energy markets and its ripple effects on equities and bonds. The situation underscores the importance of geopolitical developments in shaping market sentiment and investment strategies.
