Iran's recent missile attacks on Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG terminal mark a significant escalation in regional tensions, compelling Gulf states to confront a critical decision between restraint and retaliation. This follows a series of Iranian strikes targeting energy infrastructure across the Gulf, as Tehran responds to U.S. and Israeli actions against its own facilities. The situation has prompted Gulf leaders, including Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Faisal bin Farhan Al Saud, to assert that their patience is waning and that a coordinated response may soon be necessary.

The implications for financial markets are profound, particularly in the energy sector. As Gulf states weigh their options, the potential for military escalation could disrupt oil and gas supplies, impacting prices and investor sentiment. Analysts caution that a purely defensive posture may no longer be viable, as continued Iranian aggression could embolden further attacks, destabilizing the region.
