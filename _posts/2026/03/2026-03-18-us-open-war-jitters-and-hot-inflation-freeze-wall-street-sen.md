Israel's recent strike on Iran's South Pars gas field has ignited geopolitical tensions, resulting in a significant spike in oil prices, with Brent crude surging over 5% to $110 per barrel. This escalation is causing market participants to reassess energy asset risks in the Persian Gulf, while European gas prices also jumped to nearly €55/MWh. The backdrop of rising inflation, highlighted by a surprising 0.7% month-over-month increase in February's PPI, adds further complexity to the economic landscape.

As Wall Street grapples with these developments, the S&P 500 has retreated approximately 0.6%, reversing gains from earlier sessions. Investors are particularly focused on Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell's upcoming address, which could signal whether the central bank will maintain its restrictive policy in light of persistent inflation and energy supply shocks. The market is currently pricing out expectations for interest rate cuts in 2026, reflecting a shift in sentiment.
