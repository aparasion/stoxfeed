Fidelity Investments has urged the SEC to advance its regulatory framework for broker-dealers involved in the custody and trading of crypto assets on alternative trading systems (ATS). In a recent letter responding to the SEC’s Crypto Task Force, Fidelity emphasized the need for comprehensive rules governing tokenized securities, which encompass various asset classes and structures. The firm highlighted the complexities of tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) and called for clear guidance on the legalities and valuation models associated with these instruments.

This development is significant for the financial markets as it underscores a growing recognition of the need for regulatory clarity in the rapidly evolving crypto landscape. Fidelity's recommendations could pave the way for broader acceptance of tokenized securities, potentially enhancing liquidity and trading efficiency across asset classes. The SEC's ongoing support for 24/7 capital markets further signals a shift towards integrating decentralized finance (DeFi) with traditional trading systems.

Market professionals should note that Fidelity’s push for regulatory reform could lead to increased participation in tokenized trading, impacting liquidity and valuation dynamics in both crypto and traditional asset markets.
