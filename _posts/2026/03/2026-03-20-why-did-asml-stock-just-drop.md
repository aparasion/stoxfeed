ASML Holding (ASML) shares fell 3.3% on Friday, despite recent bullish sentiments from Goldman Sachs and positive earnings from its customer Micron Technology (MU). The decline comes as investors react to broader market dynamics rather than specific negative news, indicating a possible disconnect between analyst optimism and market behavior.

Goldman Sachs recently highlighted the accelerating demand for semiconductors, particularly following insights from Nvidia's GTC 2026 conference, positioning ASML's machinery as crucial for sector growth. As Micron ramps up capital expenditures, this spending directly benefits ASML, reinforcing the latter's strategic importance in the semiconductor supply chain. However, ASML's valuation remains a topic of debate, trading at 47 times trailing earnings, which raises questions about its pricing relative to growth expectations.
