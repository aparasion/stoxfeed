Bitcoin retreated to $68,200 after a weekend selloff triggered by geopolitical tensions, specifically U.S. President Donald Trump's threats against Iran. This decline has created a CME gap near the $70,000 mark, which traders are now closely monitoring for potential recovery. Over $400 million in crypto futures were liquidated, predominantly from long positions, indicating a significant unwind in bullish sentiment as investors shifted their focus toward commodities.

The broader implications for the financial markets are notable. With Bitcoin's recent drop, altcoins have lagged, although select privacy tokens like BCH and LINK have shown resilience. The volatility indices for both Bitcoin and Ether have surged, reflecting increased uncertainty as traders hedge against potential declines. Additionally, the Dollar Index has regained strength, driven by inflation concerns and a pause in the Fed's interest-rate cuts, further complicating the crypto landscape.

Market professionals should watch for a potential rebound towards the $70,000 level, as filling the CME gap could signal renewed bullish momentum. However, the recent liquidation of long positions suggests that caution is warranted, as bearish sentiment appears to be gaining traction across various tokens.
