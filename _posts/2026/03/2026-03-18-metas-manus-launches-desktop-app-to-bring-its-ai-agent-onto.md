Meta's recent acquisition of AI start-up Manus has taken a significant step forward with the launch of its new desktop application, allowing users to run Manus's AI agent directly on their personal laptops. This development enables the AI to interact with local files and applications, enhancing its functionality beyond the cloud-based service previously offered. The introduction of the 'My Computer' feature positions Manus as a direct competitor to OpenClaw, an open-sourced AI agent gaining traction in the market.

This new capability could impact the competitive landscape for AI solutions, particularly in sectors focused on productivity and automation. With Manus's paid subscription model, it aims to attract users seeking advanced file management and coding assistance, while also raising concerns about security and privacy. The integration of Manus's technology into Meta’s ecosystem may strengthen its position in the AI market, especially as companies increasingly adopt AI for operational efficiency.
