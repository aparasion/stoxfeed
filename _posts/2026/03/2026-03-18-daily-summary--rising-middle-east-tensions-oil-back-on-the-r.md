Brent crude surged past $108 a barrel, nearing $110 intraday, fueled by rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Israel's military action against Iran's South Pars gas field has halted gas flows, prompting retaliatory strikes from Iran on the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar, which reported a fire at the Ras Laffan LNG hub. This volatility has implications for global energy prices and inflationary pressures.

The Federal Reserve maintained interest rates but indicated a shift in the outlook, with fewer members anticipating rate cuts if inflation remains elevated. Fed Chair Jerome Powell's hawkish comments and acknowledgment of the oil shock's uncertain long-term effects contributed to a nearly 1% drop in major U.S. indices. Additionally, inflation expectations rose, impacting precious metals negatively, with gold and silver prices declining.
