President Donald Trump announced a 10-day extension of the pause on U.S. attacks against Iran's energy facilities, responding to a request from Tehran. This decision aims to de-escalate tensions amid ongoing negotiations for a resolution to the conflict, which has significantly impacted oil shipments through the critical Strait of Hormuz. The announcement coincided with a notable decline in U.S. stock indices and a sharp rise in oil prices.

The S&P 500 fell 1.74%, marking its largest daily drop since early 2026, while Brent crude oil futures surged over 5.6%, closing at $108.01 per barrel. The geopolitical tension surrounding Iran has heightened market volatility, particularly in energy sectors, as traders react to the potential for further disruptions in oil supply.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as any escalation or resolution could lead to significant shifts in oil prices and broader market sentiment, impacting investment strategies across sectors.
