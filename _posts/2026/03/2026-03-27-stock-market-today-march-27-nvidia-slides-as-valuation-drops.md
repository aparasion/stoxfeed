Nvidia (NVDA) shares closed at $167.46 on Friday, down 2.21%, as the stock's valuation dipped below the S&P 500's forward price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio for the first time in years. This shift comes amid a backdrop of new product optimism and ongoing geopolitical supply-chain risks, prompting investors to reassess the AI hardware landscape. Trading volume surged to 194.1 million shares, surpassing the three-month average, indicating heightened market interest.

The decline in Nvidia's P/E ratio to approximately 20—its lowest in five years—raises questions about its valuation despite a staggering 1,200% increase over the past five years. Competitors like AMD and Intel also faced pressure, reflecting broader concerns about AI hardware valuations. As investors weigh Nvidia's strong AI growth prospects against these valuation metrics, the current price may present a buying opportunity.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Nvidia's recent price action and valuation shift could signal a strategic entry point, particularly for those bullish on AI's long-term potential.
