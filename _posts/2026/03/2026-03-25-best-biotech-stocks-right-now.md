Biotech stocks are gaining attention as the sector continues to evolve in the wake of the pandemic, with companies like AbbVie, Johnson & Johnson, and Pfizer leading the charge. AbbVie’s reliance on Humira, which accounts for nearly half of its profits, highlights the critical nature of blockbuster drugs in driving revenue. Meanwhile, Johnson & Johnson's diverse portfolio across pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and consumer health emphasizes the importance of a multi-faceted approach in the healthcare market.

The current landscape presents both opportunities and risks for investors. Biotech firms are under pressure to innovate and secure FDA approvals for their drug candidates, which can significantly impact stock valuations. For instance, successful FDA approvals can lead to rapid stock price increases, while failures can have the opposite effect. As the market shifts, understanding the therapeutic focus of these companies will be crucial for portfolio management.

Investors should consider increasing their exposure to biotech stocks, particularly those under $20, as they may offer substantial upside potential amid ongoing medical advancements and a growing demand for innovative treatments.
