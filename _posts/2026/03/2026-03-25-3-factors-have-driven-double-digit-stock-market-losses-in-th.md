Gold prices are climbing as the U.S. reportedly drafts a 15-point plan to resolve the ongoing conflict in Iran, according to Bloomberg. This geopolitical development is impacting both gold and energy exchange-traded funds (ETFs), with gold ETFs seeing increased demand while oil prices are on the decline.

The rise in gold prices signals a flight to safety among investors amid heightened geopolitical tensions, which often drives demand for precious metals. In contrast, the falling oil prices suggest a potential easing of supply concerns, as the market reacts to the possibility of a diplomatic resolution. This divergence highlights the contrasting investor sentiment in commodities as the situation evolves.

Market professionals should monitor these trends closely, as shifts in gold and energy ETFs can indicate broader market sentiment and risk appetite. The ongoing developments in U.S.-Iran relations could further influence commodity prices and investment strategies in the coming weeks.
