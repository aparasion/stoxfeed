David Sacks, the former crypto and AI czar under President Trump, has concluded his 130-day tenure but will continue to influence technology policy as co-chair of the President’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST). His new role allows him to provide broader recommendations across various tech sectors while maintaining his focus on crypto and AI, which includes ongoing efforts to shape regulatory frameworks for the digital asset market.

Sacks has been pivotal in the administration's approach to technology, contributing to significant reports and legislative initiatives, including the GENIUS Act and the AI framework aimed at fostering innovation while safeguarding rights. His transition to PCAST, which includes prominent figures from major tech companies, signals a concerted effort to streamline regulations, particularly in AI, where inconsistent state laws pose challenges for innovators.

For market professionals, Sacks’ continued involvement in shaping tech policy, especially in crypto and AI, suggests potential shifts in regulatory landscapes that could impact investment strategies and market dynamics in these sectors.
