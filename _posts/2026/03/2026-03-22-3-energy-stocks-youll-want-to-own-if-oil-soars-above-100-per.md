Oil prices are nearing $100 per barrel, raising concerns about the stability of energy infrastructure in the Persian Gulf amid ongoing attacks. This volatility presents a compelling case for investors to hedge against prolonged high energy prices by considering stocks in the energy sector, particularly Equinor ASA, PBF Energy, and Chevron. Equinor, a key supplier of natural gas to Europe, is poised to benefit from rising demand as geopolitical tensions disrupt traditional supply routes, with analysts projecting a significant increase in its earnings per share.

PBF Energy has seen its stock surge, driven by a widening 3-2-1 crack spread, which reflects the profitability of refining operations amid tight crude supplies. However, as high prices could lead to demand destruction, integrating Chevron into a portfolio may provide a stabilizing effect, given its focus on crude production and downstream operations.

Investors should consider these energy stocks as a strategic move to mitigate risks associated with potential supply disruptions and elevated energy costs, while also capitalizing on favorable market dynamics.
