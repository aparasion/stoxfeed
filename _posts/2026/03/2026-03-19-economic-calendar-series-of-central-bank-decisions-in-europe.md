Market focus turns to pivotal central bank decisions in Europe, with the Swiss National Bank (SNB) likely to maintain its interest rate at 0%. The SNB aims to navigate deflationary pressures while managing a strong Swiss franc, opting for foreign exchange interventions over unconventional easing. Meanwhile, the Bank of England (BoE) is expected to keep rates steady at 3.75%, but the voting split among policymakers could reveal underlying tensions between hawkish and dovish members amid rising geopolitical risks.

The European Central Bank (ECB) is also in the spotlight, with inflation concerns resurfacing, particularly due to tensions in the Middle East. Although the ECB is anticipated to hold rates steady, a hawkish tone may signal potential hikes in the months ahead, reflecting market adjustments to inflationary pressures.
