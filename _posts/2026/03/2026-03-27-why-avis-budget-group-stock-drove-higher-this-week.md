Avis Budget Group (NASDAQ: CAR) experienced a dramatic surge this week, with shares climbing nearly 50% before settling around 38% higher by mid-day Friday. This spike comes as airport congestion drives travelers to rental car counters, with increased demand noted amid ongoing partial government shutdowns affecting domestic air travel. Concurrently, competitor Hertz Global has reported a 15% increase in web traffic, indicating a broader trend of consumers opting for rental cars over flying.

The volatility surrounding Avis Budget is noteworthy, particularly given the stock's history as a meme stock, which has left 23.5% of its float sold short as of mid-March. This creates potential for a short squeeze, which could further amplify price movements. However, the stock also faces risks from economic downturns and global unrest, making it a precarious choice for risk-averse investors.

For those considering an investment in Avis Budget, caution is advised. Analysts suggest exploring alternative stocks with stronger growth potential, as Avis Budget was notably excluded from a recent list of top investment picks.
