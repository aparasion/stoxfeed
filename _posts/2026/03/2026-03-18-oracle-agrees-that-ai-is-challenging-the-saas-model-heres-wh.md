Oracle (NYSE: ORCL) is positioning itself as a key player in the AI landscape, leveraging its infrastructure software segment and embedding AI into its applications. Despite this promising outlook, the company faces risks due to its increasing debt and declining free cash flow, raising questions about its financial health as it transitions to a subscription-based model in cloud computing.

The software sector is currently under pressure from AI advancements, with many SaaS companies experiencing significant sell-offs. However, Oracle's cloud infrastructure revenue surged 44% year-over-year, contributing to a forecasted total revenue jump to $90 billion in fiscal 2027. This growth is crucial as Oracle integrates AI capabilities across its applications, potentially enhancing its market position rather than diminishing it.

Investors should weigh Oracle's potential for growth against its financial risks. For those considering an investment, it's worth exploring the full analysis to understand the implications for Oracle's future in the evolving AI landscape.
