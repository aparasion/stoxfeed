Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced a significant escalation in military efforts against Iran, stating that while airstrikes are effectively targeting its ballistic missile and nuclear programs, true regime change necessitates a "ground component." He emphasized that any shift in power must come from the Iranian people themselves, suggesting that external military actions alone are insufficient for lasting change.

This development has far-reaching implications for the financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical tensions, such as energy and defense. Investors may want to monitor oil prices closely, as heightened military activity in the Middle East often leads to volatility in crude oil markets.
