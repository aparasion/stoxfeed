The Federal Reserve maintained its benchmark fed funds rate at 3.50%-3.75% during its latest meeting, a decision that aligns with market expectations amid rising inflation concerns and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Bitcoin, reacting to the Fed's stance and a surge in oil prices, plummeted nearly 4% to $71,600, while U.S. stocks, including the Nasdaq and S&P 500, also dipped by 0.55%.

The Fed's updated economic projections indicate a notable increase in inflation expectations, now at 2.7% for 2026, up from 2.4%. This reflects the central bank's struggle to balance a slowing employment market against persistent inflation, exacerbated by rising oil prices due to recent conflicts. The Fed's hawkish outlook, which includes only one anticipated rate cut in 2026, signals a tighter monetary policy environment that could further pressure risk assets like cryptocurrencies.
