Oil prices rebounded on Tuesday, with Brent crude futures rising over 2% to $98.25 a barrel and WTI crude up 3.4% at $91.09. This uptick follows a dramatic 10% drop in the previous session, driven by ongoing tensions in the Middle East. President Trump's decision to delay potential strikes on Iran's energy infrastructure for five days has added to the volatility, as Iranian officials dismissed claims of U.S. negotiations aimed at de-escalating the conflict.

The market's reaction underscores the significant impact geopolitical developments have on oil prices, particularly as the situation remains fluid. Reports of escalating tensions, including potential involvement from Saudi Arabia and the UAE, further complicate the landscape. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical shipping route, exacerbates concerns over supply disruptions, which could lead to sustained price fluctuations.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor geopolitical developments closely, as they can lead to abrupt changes in oil prices and broader market sentiment. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for effective portfolio management and trading strategies in the current environment.
