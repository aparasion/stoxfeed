Global financial markets are experiencing heightened volatility as the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict continues to weigh heavily on investor sentiment. Major stock indices in the U.S. are poised to close the month in negative territory, while international markets, particularly in Europe and Asia, are feeling the impact of rising energy prices and inflation concerns. South Korea's Kospi index, once a standout performer, has plummeted nearly 20% in March, highlighting the broader repercussions of the conflict on global equities.

The sell-off in stocks is accompanied by a significant rise in bond yields as investors adjust their expectations for central bank interest rates amid fears of stagflation. Goldman Sachs has warned that the current market conditions could lead to further equity downside, with historical data indicating poor performance during stagflationary periods. In the currency markets, the U.S. dollar has gained ground, driven by energy-related inflation risks, while gold has faced its worst monthly performance since 2008.

Market professionals should consider the implications of rising energy prices and potential stagflation on their portfolios. Diversification and a long-term investment strategy may be crucial in navigating this turbulent environment, as short-term trading could incur unnecessary costs and volatility.
