Social Security retirement benefits reach their maximum at age 70, yet many retirees overlook the financial implications of delaying their claims beyond this age. Not only do benefits cease to increase after this point, but waiting also results in lost income that cannot be recovered. For instance, a retiree who delays claiming until age 72 misses out on two years of benefits without any compensation, potentially leaving significant money on the table.

This is particularly relevant as retirees navigate their financial strategies in an uncertain economic environment. With the potential for increased longevity and rising living costs, claiming benefits at age 70 allows retirees to invest or utilize those funds in ways that can enhance their financial security. Additionally, charitable contributions made from these benefits can yield tax advantages, especially with upcoming changes in deduction rules.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is clear: advising clients to claim Social Security at age 70 can optimize their retirement income and provide them with greater flexibility in managing their finances.
