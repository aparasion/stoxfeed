Walt Disney (DIS) shares have faced a challenging start to 2026, dropping approximately 15% since January amid uncertainty surrounding its new CEO and ongoing struggles with the transition from linear TV to streaming. The company's first-quarter fiscal results, released on February 2, exceeded expectations but were overshadowed by the announcement of Josh D'Amaro's promotion to CEO, raising concerns about his content leadership experience.

This situation presents a unique opportunity for potential investors. With Disney currently trading at 15 times forward earnings—well below its historical average of 20 and significantly lower than peers like Netflix—there's room for recovery. Analysts suggest that D'Amaro could implement bold strategies, such as a spin-off of the linear TV business or increased investment in new franchises, to regain investor confidence.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that low expectations surrounding D'Amaro's leadership could lead to significant upside if he successfully navigates Disney's strategic challenges. Now may be an opportune time to consider positions in Disney shares ahead of potential positive sentiment shifts, particularly with the next earnings report on the horizon.
