Today's macroeconomic calendar is light, featuring only the German IFO index, the US trade balance, and US crude oil inventory data from the EIA. While the IFO index and trade balance are expected to have minimal impact on the markets, the crude oil inventory report could hold significant relevance, particularly given the ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.

Market participants are likely to focus on the EIA's inventory changes, as fluctuations in oil supply can influence prices amid heightened volatility. This comes at a time when oil markets are already responding to broader economic indicators, including mixed PMI data from the US, which showed strong manufacturing but a decline in services.

The key takeaway for traders is to monitor the EIA report closely, as any unexpected changes in crude inventories could lead to sharp price movements in oil and related equities, potentially affecting broader market sentiment.
