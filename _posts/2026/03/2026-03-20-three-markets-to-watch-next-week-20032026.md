Markets are poised for a critical week as the focus shifts to the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz amid escalating geopolitical tensions. The potential restoration of shipping routes could ease volatility and redirect attention to conventional macroeconomic indicators. Conversely, if the conflict escalates, particularly with attacks on energy infrastructure, it could exacerbate inflation expectations in the U.S., pushing yields higher and prompting a reassessment of market conditions.

Oil prices remain a key indicator of these tensions, with the risk of physical disruptions in the Gulf region impacting global supply chains, particularly in Asia. Rising fuel prices in the U.S. suggest that the energy shock is already influencing the economy. If credible signs of de-escalation emerge, oil prices could see a swift correction; however, continued disruptions would heighten inflation risks and pressure rate-sensitive sectors.
