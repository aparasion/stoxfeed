Meta Platforms (META) is experiencing significant top-line growth, with fourth-quarter revenue reaching $59.9 billion, a 24% year-over-year increase. Management anticipates this momentum to accelerate, projecting Q1 2026 revenue between $53.5 billion and $56.5 billion. However, the company’s aggressive investments in artificial intelligence are straining earnings growth, leading to a 10% decline in stock price year-to-date. 

The core issue lies in Meta's transition to a capital-intensive business model, with fourth-quarter expenses surging 40% year-over-year to $35.1 billion. This shift has compressed operating margins, and management forecasts total expenses to rise significantly in 2026, alongside capital expenditures expected to triple from 2024 levels. Such financial pressures could lead to a reevaluation of the stock’s valuation, which currently sits at a price-to-earnings ratio of about 25.

Given the potential for continued earnings pressure and a possible market rerating, investors may want to adopt a cautious stance on Meta, keeping positions small until the company demonstrates a solid return on its AI investments.
