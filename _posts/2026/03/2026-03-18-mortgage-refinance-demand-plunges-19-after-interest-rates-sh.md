Mortgage rates surged last week to their highest level since late 2022, with the average rate for a 30-year fixed mortgage climbing to 6.30%. This spike led to a significant 10.9% decline in total mortgage application volume, as reported by the Mortgage Bankers Association. The rise in rates is attributed to increasing Treasury yields, geopolitical tensions affecting oil prices, and concerns over inflation, which have collectively dampened refinance demand.

The impact of these rising rates is evident, particularly in the refinance sector, which saw a 19% drop in applications week-over-week, despite being 69% higher than the same period last year. Conversely, purchase mortgage applications managed a slight 1% increase, indicating a resilient demand in the housing market as the spring season approaches, albeit with improved affordability in some areas.
