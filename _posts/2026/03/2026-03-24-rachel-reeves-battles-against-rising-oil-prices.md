Chancellor Rachel Reeves reaffirmed her commitment to strict fiscal rules amid rising energy prices, indicating that any support for household energy bills will be limited to the most vulnerable. This stance comes as global bond yields rise and stock markets react negatively, reflecting concerns over escalating energy costs driven by geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East.

The Chancellor's refusal to implement broad-based energy subsidies is significant for the UK economy, as businesses face the highest input cost increases in over three decades. The energy price cap, affecting millions of households, is set to decline temporarily but is expected to rise again in July, exacerbating inflationary pressures. With oil prices surging above $103 per barrel, the financial markets are bracing for further volatility as Reeves navigates these challenges without additional government support for businesses.

Market professionals should note that the current fiscal constraints could limit the government's ability to cushion the economic impact of rising energy prices, potentially leading to increased market instability and pressure on consumer spending in the coming months.
