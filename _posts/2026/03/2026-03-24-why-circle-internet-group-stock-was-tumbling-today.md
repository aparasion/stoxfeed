Shares of Circle Internet Group (CRCL) plummeted 20.4% today amid reports of proposed U.S. legislation that could limit yield offerings on stablecoins. The Senate is currently reviewing the Clarity Act, which may prohibit platforms from providing yields on stablecoin holdings akin to traditional bank deposits, while allowing for activity-based rewards. This potential regulation has also negatively impacted Coinbase, where customers currently earn a 3.5% yield on their USDC holdings.

The implications for Circle are significant. While the Clarity Act wouldn't directly threaten its growth in services like Polymarket, it could discourage the holding of stablecoins, leading to reduced transaction volumes. This shift in user behavior could dampen Circle's revenue prospects and overall market activity in the crypto sector.

Market professionals should keep a close eye on the legislative developments surrounding the Clarity Act, as the inclusion of yield restrictions could trigger further declines in Circle's stock and broader market volatility in the crypto space.
