The SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) ETF has surged 60% over the past year, significantly outperforming Bitcoin (BTC), which has declined by 12%. This stark contrast raises questions about Bitcoin's reputation as "digital gold." With central banks ramping up gold purchases amid concerns over fiscal deficits, dollar weakness, and geopolitical instability, gold's status as a stable store of value is increasingly solidified. Its historical resilience during market downturns further strengthens its investment case.

In contrast, Bitcoin presents a more volatile investment landscape. While it benefits from ongoing adoption—evidenced by over $57 billion in net inflows to spot Bitcoin ETFs since 2024—its price can swing dramatically, posing risks for investors seeking stability. The upcoming halving event in 2028 and the decreasing supply of Bitcoin could support long-term price increases, but the asset’s short-term fluctuations remain a significant concern.

For investors weighing a $500 allocation, gold offers stability and a proven track record, making it a safer choice for those prioritizing capital preservation, while Bitcoin may appeal to those seeking higher risk and potential rewards.
