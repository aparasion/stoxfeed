Wall Street is experiencing a notable upward trend, with the Dow Jones rising approximately 1%, the S&P 500 gaining nearly 0.8%, and the Nasdaq up about 0.7%. This rally occurs despite oil prices remaining above $100 per barrel, as investors appear to be optimistic about a potential easing of tensions in the Middle East, fueled by speculative reports of negotiations. The market's current sentiment is heavily influenced by expectations rather than concrete developments, creating a precarious situation where any hint of progress could lead to substantial market movements.

Investor focus is also shifting toward the upcoming Federal Reserve meeting, with expectations for a rate cut in June now largely dismissed due to inflation uncertainties exacerbated by geopolitical tensions. Additionally, Micron's earnings report is anticipated to provide insights into the semiconductor sector's health, which could further sway market sentiment, particularly in tech stocks that have been pivotal in recent gains.

As optimism persists, professionals should remain vigilant about the potential for sharp corrections if expectations are not met, especially in light of the fragile geopolitical landscape and inflation concerns.
