XRP, the cryptocurrency linked to Ripple, has seen a significant decline of over 60% since its all-time high last July, continuing to lag behind its peers. Although the SEC lawsuit against Ripple concluded favorably, classifying XRP as a digital commodity rather than a security, the token still faces challenges. Its primary use as a bridge currency is threatened by the rise of stablecoins, which offer less volatility and similar functionality.

The lack of long-term catalysts raises concerns about XRP's future performance. While recent developments, such as the potential approval of spot-price ETFs, could enhance retail and institutional interest, XRP's inability to leverage scarcity like Bitcoin or support smart contracts limits its appeal. As the market gravitates towards established cryptocurrencies with clearer growth trajectories, XRP may struggle to regain momentum.

Market professionals should be cautious, as XRP is likely to trade sideways or decline further in the coming years, overshadowed by stronger competitors in the crypto space.
