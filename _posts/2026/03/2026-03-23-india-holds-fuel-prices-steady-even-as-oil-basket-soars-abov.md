Oil prices are poised for a significant rise, with the Indian oil basket recently surpassing $156 per barrel, driven by supply disruptions in the Middle East. This surge, which has seen prices increase by approximately 120%, comes despite assurances from Iran that Indian tankers will be allowed passage through the Strait of Hormuz. The situation is critical as India, the world's third-largest crude oil importer, relies heavily on imports to meet over 80% of its demand.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. With the Dubai/Oman benchmark exceeding $160 per barrel and Brent trading at $113, the volatility in oil prices could impact sectors reliant on energy costs, potentially leading to increased inflationary pressures. However, Indian refiners are currently absorbing the price hike, and the government has confirmed that adequate crude inventories are in place, alleviating immediate concerns over supply shortages.

Market professionals should monitor these developments closely, as sustained high oil prices could influence global inflation trends and impact equities across energy-dependent sectors.
