Nuclear energy is experiencing a resurgence as countries seek to bolster their nuclear capabilities, with Fluor Corporation (NYSE: FLR) emerging as a key player in this transition. The company has established a new office in Bucharest, Romania, to oversee significant nuclear projects, including the development of small modular reactors (SMRs) and the refurbishment of the Cernavodă Nuclear Power Plant. Fluor's involvement in these initiatives positions it to benefit from the growing demand for reliable, carbon-free baseload power.

The RoPower project, which aims to deploy six NuScale Power Modules, is generating considerable attention, while the larger refurbishment of the Cernavodă facility promises substantial immediate revenue for Fluor. With contracts valued at approximately $3.4 billion, the company's strategy to focus on reimbursable services mitigates risks associated with project cost overruns, enhancing its financial stability.

Investors looking to tap into the nuclear energy sector may find Fluor an attractive option, as it offers exposure to nuclear infrastructure development without the volatility associated with uranium mining.
