Costco (COST) has become an unexpected casualty of recent tariff disputes, as a customer has filed a lawsuit seeking refunds related to these tariffs after the Supreme Court's ruling. Despite the company's efforts to absorb costs and its prior legal actions against the Trump administration, this new litigation could introduce volatility for investors, although Costco's stock price remains stable for now.

The implications for the retail giant are significant. Costco's stock, which has historically thrived on consistent performance and impressive returns—yielding nearly 3,200% since 2000—now trades at a high P/E ratio of 51. While the company reported a 9% revenue increase to $137 billion in the first half of fiscal 2026, the looming uncertainty from the lawsuit and potential profit impacts may lead investors to reassess its valuation.
