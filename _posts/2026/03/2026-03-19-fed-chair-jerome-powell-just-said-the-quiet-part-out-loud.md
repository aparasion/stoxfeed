The Federal Reserve's recent FOMC meeting has sent shockwaves through the stock market, with Fed Chair Jerome Powell’s comments on rising energy prices and inflation stirring concerns among investors. While the committee voted to maintain the federal funds target rate at 3.50% to 3.75%, Powell’s warning that "higher energy prices will push up overall inflation" has raised the likelihood of an interest rate hike over the next three months, a stark shift from previous expectations of rate cuts.

This development is particularly significant given that the stock market is currently trading at its second-highest valuation in history. Investors had been banking on forthcoming rate cuts in 2026, but the geopolitical tensions and the resulting energy supply chain disruptions have introduced a new layer of uncertainty. The fractured nature of the FOMC, with dissenting opinions becoming more common, further complicates the outlook for monetary policy.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for a shift in interest rate expectations, which could challenge the sustainability of current equity valuations.
