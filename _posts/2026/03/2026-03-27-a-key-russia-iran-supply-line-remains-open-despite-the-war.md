Recent U.S. and Israeli military actions against Iran have failed to disrupt a vital trade route that both Iran and Russia rely on for their ongoing war efforts. Despite the tensions, Azerbaijani authorities have managed to keep the "western branch" of the North-South trade corridor operational, as confirmed by Russian Deputy Prime Minister Alexey Overchuk. Following a brief interruption linked to an Iranian drone attack on Azerbaijan, border checkpoints have reopened, allowing commercial traffic to resume.

This development underscores the resilience of trade networks in the face of geopolitical strife, with Azerbaijan playing a pivotal role in facilitating the flow of goods, including potential military supplies, between Iran and Russia. The planned construction of the Rasht-Astara railway, set to begin in April, further highlights the strategic importance of this corridor, promising to enhance trade capabilities significantly.

Market professionals should note that the continued operation of this trade route may influence commodity prices and regional stability, particularly in sectors related to defense and energy, as it underscores the complex interplay of geopolitical alliances and economic dependencies.
