Institutional interest in cryptocurrencies remains robust despite recent market turbulence, with a new survey revealing that 73% of large investors plan to increase their digital asset allocations this year. This sentiment persists even after a 40% sell-off since October, as many anticipate price recoveries over the next 12 months. Notably, interest is shifting towards stablecoins and regulated investment vehicles, indicating a desire for safer, compliant exposure to the crypto market.

In Japan, SBI VC Trade is launching a retail USDC lending service, marking a significant step in integrating stablecoins into regulated financial products. This initiative reflects a broader trend of stablecoins gaining traction among both retail and institutional investors, as regulatory clarity enhances their adoption. Meanwhile, crypto firms like Abra are pursuing public listings through SPACs, showcasing their commitment to accessing traditional capital markets amid ongoing regulatory scrutiny.
