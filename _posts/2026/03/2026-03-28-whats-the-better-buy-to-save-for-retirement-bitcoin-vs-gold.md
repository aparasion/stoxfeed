Gold's enduring status as a reliable store of value is being contrasted with Bitcoin's notorious volatility, highlighting the need for strategic asset allocation in retirement portfolios. While gold, exemplified by the SPDR Gold Shares ETF, has shown a 44% return over the past year despite a recent 15% decline, Bitcoin's price swings can be much more severe, averaging an 80% drop after its halving cycles. This stark difference in behavior underscores the importance of prioritizing gold in retirement planning, especially for those who may not have the luxury of time to weather Bitcoin's drastic fluctuations.

For investors, the sequencing risk associated with these assets is critical. A 15% dip in gold may be manageable within a diversified portfolio, while a 45% drop in Bitcoin could significantly impact retirement timelines. Therefore, it is advisable to establish a solid gold position before considering Bitcoin as a supplementary growth asset.

In conclusion, a prudent approach would involve allocating 2% to 5% of a portfolio to Bitcoin after securing a robust foundation with gold and other stable investments. This strategy allows investors to benefit from Bitcoin's potential upside while safeguarding their retirement savings against its inherent risks.
