European stocks are poised for a significant decline at the open on Monday as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East escalate, particularly following U.S. President Trump's aggressive stance towards Iran. Investors are on edge after threats of retaliatory actions from Tehran, which could disrupt the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial channel for global oil shipments. The situation has already contributed to rising energy prices, with Brent crude nearing $108 a barrel, raising concerns about inflation and its implications for monetary policy.

The market's reaction is evident, with Asian indexes experiencing sharp drops of 3-6% as fears of a broader conflict mount. The heightened energy costs are likely to compel central banks to maintain a hawkish approach to interest rates, as indicated by recent signals from the Federal Reserve and other major institutions. 

As tensions persist, market professionals should closely monitor energy prices and central bank communications, as these factors will likely influence stock performance and investment strategies in the coming weeks.
