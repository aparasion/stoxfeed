New Fortress Energy (NFE) experienced a dramatic 20.3% drop on Wednesday, despite an initial surge following a creditor agreement aimed at ensuring the company's survival. The deal, which allows New Fortress to split into two entities—publicly traded "NewNFE" and private "BrazilCo"—has left investors grappling with the implications of losing the Brazilian segment, a critical revenue driver.

This restructuring raises significant concerns for shareholders, who will see their stakes diluted to just 35% of NewNFE, with creditors claiming the remaining 65% and $2.5 billion in preferred shares. The market's reaction reflects skepticism about NewNFE's ability to execute a turnaround without its Brazilian operations, which were integral to its earnings.
