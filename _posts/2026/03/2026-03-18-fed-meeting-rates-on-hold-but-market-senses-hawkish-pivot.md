The Federal Reserve opted to keep interest rates unchanged during its latest meeting, amid rising oil prices and a significant stock market sell-off. Despite a more hawkish tone and upward revisions to GDP and inflation forecasts, Chair Jerome Powell emphasized that the Fed's economic outlook should be viewed with caution, particularly due to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The decision to abandon forward guidance reflects the Fed's struggle to navigate an unpredictable economic landscape.

This meeting's outcomes have led to a tightening of rate cut expectations, with the likelihood of a cut this year dropping significantly. Consequently, both the S&P 500 and Nasdaq saw declines of over 1%, as investors reacted to the Fed's stance and the surging oil prices, which spiked above $110 following attacks on energy infrastructure in Qatar. The broader implications suggest a challenging environment for risk assets, as central banks worldwide may follow the Fed's lead.
