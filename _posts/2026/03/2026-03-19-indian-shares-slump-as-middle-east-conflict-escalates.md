Indian shares opened sharply lower on Thursday, with the benchmark BSE Sensex dropping 1,703 points (2.2%) to 75,000 amid hawkish signals from the U.S. Federal Reserve and rising geopolitical tensions linked to Israel and Iran. The NSE Nifty index also fell by 524 points (2.2%) to 23,254, reflecting widespread investor concern. Notably, HDFC Bank's shares plummeted 5% following the resignation of its part-time chairman due to ethical concerns.

The broader market sentiment was negatively impacted as major stocks like Mahindra & Mahindra, Asian Paints, and Kotak Mahindra Bank saw declines of 3-4%. Airline stocks were particularly hard-hit, with Brent crude prices surging above $112 per barrel due to escalating tensions affecting energy assets in the Middle East, further pressuring the sector.
