Social Security can significantly impact retirement income, particularly for married couples who have unique claiming strategies to consider. Couples face the risk of making costly mistakes, such as failing to coordinate their claims effectively, which can lead to suboptimal benefits. For instance, the lower earner might benefit from filing early while the higher earner delays their claim to maximize household income.

Understanding spousal benefits is crucial, as they are capped at 50% of the higher earner's benefit at full retirement age and do not receive the same growth incentives as personal earnings. Moreover, overlooking survivor benefits can be detrimental; if the higher earner passes away, the lower earner may rely heavily on those benefits, which underscores the importance of strategic planning.
