National Counterterrorism Center Director Joe Kent announced his resignation on Tuesday, citing moral objections to the Trump administration's military actions against Iran. In a letter shared on his personal X account, Kent criticized the administration for what he perceives as undue influence from Israel, arguing that Iran does not pose an imminent threat to the U.S.

This development could have significant implications for U.S. foreign policy and defense spending, particularly in sectors tied to military contractors and geopolitical stability. Kent's departure raises questions about the continuity of counterterrorism strategies and how they may evolve under new leadership, especially considering the polarized views on military engagement in the region.
