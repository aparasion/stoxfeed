Major U.S. indexes closed lower on February 11, 2026, following a stronger-than-expected January jobs report that raised concerns about potential interest rate hikes. The report indicated a significant increase in job creation, which could prompt the Federal Reserve to maintain or even accelerate its tightening monetary policy. This development overshadowed a flurry of earnings reports from key companies, which generally showed resilience but failed to lift market sentiment.

The labor market's strength suggests that consumer spending may remain robust, but it also raises fears of inflation persistence. Investors are now recalibrating their expectations for corporate earnings in light of potential higher borrowing costs. Sectors sensitive to interest rates, such as technology and real estate, experienced notable declines, reflecting apprehension about future growth prospects.

Market professionals should closely monitor upcoming Federal Reserve communications for guidance on interest rate trajectories, as this will significantly impact market dynamics and sector performance in the near term.
