Wix.com (WIX) shares plummeted nearly 23% over the past week following two bearish analyst downgrades. The first blow came from Citizens' Andrew Boone, who downgraded the stock to "market perform" from "market outperform," citing concerns over Wix's recent $1.6 billion share buyback via a modified Dutch auction. Boone highlighted that this move has burdened the company with almost $1 billion in net debt and raised alarms about potential disruption from artificial intelligence in the web design sector.

Two days later, Barclays analyst Trevor Young cut his price target on Wix from $160 to $155 while maintaining an "overweight" rating. His adjustment reflects a cautious outlook amidst growing fears of AI's impact on traditional software services, further complicating Wix's growth narrative.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the heightened risk associated with Wix's financial strategy and the looming threat of AI disruption, which could weigh on future earnings and stock performance. Investors should closely monitor the company's debt levels and competitive positioning in the evolving tech landscape.
