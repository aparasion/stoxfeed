Rigetti Computing (NASDAQ: RGTI) recently announced its quantum platform achieved a notable 99.9% 2-qubit gate fidelity, a key metric for measuring quantum computing accuracy. While this milestone positions Rigetti favorably within the competitive quantum landscape, it still falls short compared to rivals like IonQ, which has reached 99.99% fidelity. This discrepancy highlights potential challenges in Rigetti's technology scalability, particularly as its larger systems only managed 99% fidelity.

The implications for investors are significant. Rigetti's small market cap of under $5 billion and the growing competition from tech giants like Microsoft and Alphabet raise questions about its long-term viability. As the quantum computing sector heats up, companies with superior technology or established leadership may present more attractive investment opportunities. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach Rigetti with caution. Diversifying through quantum computing ETFs could be a more strategic move, offering exposure to a broader range of players in this evolving field.
