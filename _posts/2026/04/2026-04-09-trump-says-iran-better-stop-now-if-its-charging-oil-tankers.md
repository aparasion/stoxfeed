President Donald Trump issued a stern warning to Iran, urging the nation to cease any plans to charge fees for oil tankers navigating the Strait of Hormuz. This statement escalates tensions in an already fragile ceasefire, just two days after the U.S. proposed a two-week suspension of hostilities contingent on the safe passage of vessels through this critical waterway, which accounts for roughly 20% of global oil supply.

The potential for Iran to impose fees on shipping firms, particularly in cryptocurrency, raises significant concerns for oil markets. Any disruption in the flow of oil through the Strait could exacerbate supply constraints and lead to price volatility. Traders and analysts will be closely monitoring developments, as the situation could impact not only oil prices but also broader market sentiment regarding geopolitical risks.

Market participants should prepare for potential fluctuations in oil prices and heightened volatility in energy stocks, particularly if tensions escalate or shipping routes are further threatened.
