SpaceX is preparing for a monumental IPO, having confidentially filed to raise up to $75 billion at a staggering valuation between $1.75 trillion and $2 trillion. If successful, this would mark the largest IPO in history, placing SpaceX among an elite group of stocks with market caps exceeding $1 trillion. The company, known for its reusable rockets and the Starlink satellite network, is poised to attract significant investor interest, particularly from retail investors, as it plans to allocate 30% of its raise to this group.

However, the potential for SpaceX to become a meme stock looms large, given its massive valuation—219 times trailing earnings and 109 times trailing revenue—driven more by future prospects than current fundamentals. Analysts caution that despite the hype, investors may be better off waiting for a post-IPO dip, as lock-up provisions could lead to significant insider selling, reminiscent of trends seen in other high-profile IPOs.

Investors should approach the SpaceX IPO with caution, considering the volatility and speculative nature of the stock. Patience may yield a more favorable entry point in the months following the IPO.
