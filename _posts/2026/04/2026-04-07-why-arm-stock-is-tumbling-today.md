Shares of Arm Holdings (NASDAQ: ARM) are experiencing a 5.7% decline following a downgrade from Morgan Stanley, which shifted its rating from overweight to equal weight and set a price target of $150. Analyst Lee Simpson cited concerns over margin pressure and slower growth prospects, particularly as Arm invests heavily in artificial intelligence with its new AGI-focused CPU amid a backdrop of geopolitical uncertainty, notably escalating tensions in Iran.

The downgrade reflects broader market anxieties, as the potential for a prolonged conflict in the Middle East could keep oil prices high and negatively impact global economic stability. Investors are wary that these macro factors, coupled with Arm's increased research and development spending, may hinder its growth trajectory through fiscal 2027. 

Despite the current pressures, Arm remains a key player in the semiconductor sector. Investors should consider that while near-term challenges exist, the company's advancements in AGI technology could position it favorably for future growth.
