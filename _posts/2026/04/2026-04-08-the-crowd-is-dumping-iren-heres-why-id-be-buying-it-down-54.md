Iren (IREN) has seen a dramatic decline of 54% from its peak of $76, but long-term fundamentals suggest it may still be a compelling buy. Despite recent capital raises causing investor unease due to potential share dilution, Iren's strategic moves, including a landmark $9.7 billion deal with Microsoft for AI data center capacity, highlight its growth potential. The company’s current market cap of $11 billion could be leveraged to minimize dilution risks if it waits to tap into its $6 billion at-the-market equity program.

The expansion of Iren's gigawatt pipeline to over 4.5 gigawatts, along with its recent acquisition of Nvidia chips, positions it well to capitalize on the increasing energy demands of the AI sector. While some investors are impatient for immediate results, the company is laying the groundwork for substantial future revenue, potentially exceeding $40 billion annually from similar deals.

For market professionals, Iren represents a long-term investment opportunity in a rapidly growing industry, suggesting that patience could yield significant returns as the company continues to scale its operations.
