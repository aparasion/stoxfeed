Rep. Sheila Cherfilus-McCormick has resigned from the House of Representatives amid serious allegations of stealing $5 million in federal disaster relief funds. Her resignation comes as the Ethics Committee prepares to address her violations of multiple House rules and ethical standards. Cherfilus-McCormick, a Florida Democrat, is the third House member to resign in recent weeks, following Reps. Eric Swalwell and Tony Gonzalez.

This development could have broader implications for the political landscape, particularly as it raises questions about governance and accountability in Congress. Such scandals can impact investor confidence and market sentiment, especially in sectors reliant on federal funding and disaster relief programs. The ongoing scrutiny of ethical standards may also influence legislative priorities and funding allocations.
