Joby Aviation and Archer Aviation are making significant strides in the electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) market, which could revolutionize urban transport. Joby has recently commenced flight tests on its FAA-conforming aircraft and is nearing type certification, bolstered by partnerships with Toyota, Uber, and Blade Air Mobility. With a solid cash position of $1.4 billion, Joby is well-positioned to scale operations and enter commercial passenger services.

Archer, on the other hand, has also made notable progress with FAA approvals and boasts $2 billion in liquidity. Its collaboration with Stellantis may reduce capital expenditures, while partnerships with defense firms like Anduril and Palantir enhance its strategic positioning. However, Joby’s lead in FAA certification and established partnerships may provide it with a competitive edge in the near term.

For market professionals, Joby appears to be the stronger investment for 2026, given its momentum in regulatory approvals and revenue-generating partnerships, suggesting a more immediate path to commercial viability compared to Archer.
