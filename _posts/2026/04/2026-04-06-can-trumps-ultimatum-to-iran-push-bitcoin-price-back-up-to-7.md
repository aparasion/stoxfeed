President Trump's ultimatum to Iran, set for Tuesday, could serve as a significant catalyst for Bitcoin (BTC) as it continues to diverge from gold. Should negotiations fail, Bitcoin may strengthen as a hedge against fiscal instability, potentially pushing its price above $75,000. Conversely, a successful ceasefire could boost risk assets, including Bitcoin, although its response may not be as direct as equities.

Market dynamics are shifting, particularly as concerns grow over central banks liquidating gold reserves. The Turkish Central Bank's recent gold sales and declining Russian reserves signal a broader trend that could further decouple Bitcoin from traditional assets. As geopolitical tensions impact energy costs and global logistics, equities may respond quickly to reduced risks, but Bitcoin's trajectory remains uncertain, especially if investor confidence in US Treasuries strengthens.

Ultimately, the outcome of the US-Iran negotiations will be pivotal for Bitcoin's near-term performance. A positive resolution could enhance risk appetite, but traders remain cautious, suggesting that any bullish momentum for Bitcoin may take time to establish.
