Modular nuclear reactor specialist Oklo (OKLO) has seen a remarkable surge in its stock, climbing nearly 28% this week, following a White House initiative to advance nuclear energy in space. The Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) has established a new government entity aimed at deploying nuclear reactors both in orbit and on the Moon, signaling a significant commitment to nuclear power as part of America's space exploration strategy.

This development not only elevates Oklo's profile but also highlights the growing interest in nuclear energy as a viable power source for future space missions. The involvement of key federal agencies like NASA and the Department of Defense suggests that Oklo could benefit from potential government contracts and partnerships, enhancing its business prospects despite being a pre-revenue company.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Oklo's alignment with federal initiatives could lead to increased investor confidence and potential revenue streams, making it a stock to watch as the nuclear sector gains traction in both terrestrial and extraterrestrial applications.
