Markets are currently in a wait-and-see mode as investors brace for the upcoming earnings season and navigate geopolitical uncertainties, particularly regarding the fragile ceasefire in Iran. U.S. markets are experiencing modest declines, with Dow futures down approximately 0.6%. Attention is shifting back to technology and AI, where a wave of speculation surrounding Anthropic's products has led to declines in several tech stocks, including Palantir and CrowdStrike.

Macroeconomic indicators reflect a weakening U.S. economy, with March CPI inflation rising to 3.3% due to increased fuel and fertilizer prices. Consumer sentiment has hit a historical low of 47.6, while durable goods orders have declined for eight consecutive readings. In Europe, indices are stable, but defense stocks are suffering significant losses amid renewed peace negotiations between Ukraine and Russia.

A key takeaway for market professionals is the mixed sentiment across sectors, particularly in tech and defense, as investors remain cautious ahead of earnings reports and grapple with inflationary pressures. The focus on technology stocks may present both risks and opportunities as the earnings season unfolds.
