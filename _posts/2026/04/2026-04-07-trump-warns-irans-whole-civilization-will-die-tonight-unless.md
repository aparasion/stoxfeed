President Donald Trump intensified his threats against Iran, warning of catastrophic consequences unless the country agrees to reopen the crucial Strait of Hormuz. This ultimatum follows U.S. military strikes on Kharg Island, Iran's primary oil export terminal, which have already contributed to a significant disruption in global oil supply and soaring energy prices. Trump's aggressive rhetoric underscores the geopolitical tensions that are currently shaping energy markets, as Iran's control over oil transit remains a critical leverage point.

The ongoing conflict has led to a historic oil supply shock, with analysts noting that Iran's military setbacks have not diminished its strategic capabilities. JPMorgan's recent analysis suggests that while Iran has suffered losses, it is focused on enduring the conflict rather than conceding, which could prolong market volatility. Energy prices are likely to remain sensitive to developments in this geopolitical landscape.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation, as any escalation could further disrupt oil supply and impact global energy prices, making it essential to reassess risk exposure in energy-related investments.
