Japanese and South Korean stocks experienced gains on Monday as investors reacted to escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly regarding Iran. President Trump issued a stern ultimatum to Iran, threatening military action if the Strait of Hormuz is not fully reopened by Tuesday. This critical waterway is vital for global oil supply, and Trump's comments have intensified market focus on geopolitical risks affecting energy prices.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly in the oil sector. West Texas Intermediate and Brent crude prices surged over 2.5%, reflecting heightened concerns about supply disruptions amid ongoing conflict. Meanwhile, the broader Asian market was muted due to several countries observing holidays, but Japan's Nikkei 225 and South Korea's Kospi showed resilience, indicating localized investor confidence despite global uncertainties.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation, as further developments could lead to volatility in oil prices and stock futures, particularly in energy-dependent sectors. The upcoming press conference from the White House could provide additional clarity on U.S. intentions, impacting market sentiment.
