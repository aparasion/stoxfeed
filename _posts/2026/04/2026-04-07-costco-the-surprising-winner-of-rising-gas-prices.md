Costco (COST +0.35%) is bucking the retail trend, emerging as a strong performer amid rising inflation, with its stock up over 10% this year. The warehouse giant benefits significantly from discounted gas prices, attracting consumers looking to save on fuel while also drawing them to bulk essentials at lower per-unit costs. With 147 million members worldwide, Costco’s resilient membership model is proving effective as shoppers seek value in a tightening economy.

The company's robust performance is underscored by a 6.7% increase in comparable store sales and a notable 22.6% surge in e-commerce sales. While trading at 50 times earnings may seem steep, investors are willing to pay a premium for Costco's consistent growth and international expansion.

For market professionals, Costco's ability to thrive in challenging conditions highlights the potential for defensive stocks in inflationary environments, making it a compelling option for portfolio diversification.
