President Trump's ultimatum regarding Iran has heightened market volatility, with a deadline set for Tuesday at 8:00 p.m. ET. The threat to destroy Iran's infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed has left investors on edge, although major indexes showed resilience, with two of the three finishing in positive territory despite earlier declines. Oil prices have surged, with Brent crude climbing from around $70 to over $110, raising concerns about inflation.

This geopolitical tension underscores the importance of a diversified investment strategy. Historical trends suggest that markets have weathered significant shocks, including wars and economic crises, often rebounding to reach new highs. The S&P 500, which includes a broad range of sectors, particularly energy, can provide stability during turbulent times.

Investors should view current market corrections as potential buying opportunities. While uncertainty looms regarding the conflict and its impact on the economy, history indicates that markets tend to recover, making long-term investment strategies more critical than ever.
