GE Vernova (NYSE: GEV) surged 14.6% this week following a robust first-quarter earnings report, underscoring its pivotal role in AI infrastructure development. CEO Scott Strazik highlighted a significant increase in the company's backlog, which now stands at $200 billion projected for 2027, up from previous estimates. The addition of $13 billion in just 90 days reflects strong demand, particularly from hyperscalers eager to secure power for AI data centers.

The company reported a notable rise in its gas power equipment backlog and slot reservation agreements (SRAs), which jumped to 100 Gigawatts (GW) from 83 GW. This surge indicates a pressing need for power solutions, equivalent to supplying 12.75 million homes for a year. With SRAs also increasing to 56 GW, GE Vernova is not only positioned for immediate growth but is also set to benefit from ongoing service revenue tied to its expanding equipment base.

For market professionals, GE Vernova's momentum suggests a compelling investment opportunity in the AI infrastructure sector, particularly as demand for power solutions intensifies.
