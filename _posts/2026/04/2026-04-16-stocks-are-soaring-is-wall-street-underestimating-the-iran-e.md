Major U.S. stock indexes have surged as investor sentiment rebounds following a ceasefire announcement in the Iran conflict. The S&P 500 recorded its 10th gain in 11 sessions, while the Nasdaq Composite has risen for 11 consecutive days, both reaching new all-time highs. This positive shift comes as concerns over geopolitical instability have eased, but the implications for energy prices remain complex.

Despite the ceasefire, Brent crude oil prices are still elevated, closing at $91.15, a significant increase from pre-war levels. The U.S. blockade of the Strait of Hormuz and extensive damage to energy infrastructure, estimated at over $50 billion, threaten to keep oil prices high even if the conflict subsides. This disconnect between stock market performance and energy prices suggests that volatility may persist in the coming months.

Investors should maintain their current strategies, as the recent rally underscores the importance of weathering market fluctuations rather than attempting to time exits. However, the potential for sustained high energy prices could influence stock valuations moving forward.
