Oil tankers are exercising caution in the Strait of Hormuz following Iran's recent declaration that the sea lane is open for commercial ships. Despite this announcement, confusion reigns as Iranian officials have imposed conditions for safe passage, leading to a 12% drop in West Texas Intermediate crude and a 9% decrease in Brent crude on Friday. Analysts suggest that the strait remains effectively closed, as vessels are required to follow routes designated by Iran and coordinate with its military.

The implications for the energy market are significant. With the strait's continued instability, refineries in Asia that rely heavily on Middle Eastern oil may face supply shortages, exacerbating the existing disruptions. The situation suggests a tightening oil supply, particularly in Asia, where onshore inventories are already low.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as the ongoing uncertainty could lead to further volatility in oil prices and supply chains in the coming months.
