Fermi (FRMI) shares plummeted 41% in March, continuing a downward trend that has seen the stock lose over 80% since its IPO less than a year ago. The real estate investment trust (REIT) aims to establish a private power grid to support the burgeoning AI sector but is facing significant challenges in securing tenants for its ambitious Project Matador in Texas. Despite raising $750 million to fund its operations, Fermi currently has no revenue and recently lost a key customer commitment worth $150 million.

The implications for the financial markets are stark. Fermi's struggles highlight the critical bottleneck in power supply for AI data centers, an area where demand is high but execution remains uncertain. Investors are wary as the company heads toward a lock-up expiration, which could flood the market with insider shares and further depress stock prices. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to exercise caution. With substantial cash burn and no immediate path to profitability, Fermi's stock may not be a prudent buy despite its long-term potential in a rapidly growing sector.
