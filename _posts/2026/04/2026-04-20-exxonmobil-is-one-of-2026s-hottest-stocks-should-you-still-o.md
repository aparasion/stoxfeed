ExxonMobil (XOM) has surged nearly 18% in 2026, outperforming a flat S&P 500, largely due to rising oil prices amid ongoing tensions in the Persian Gulf. Investors are now weighing whether to hold the stock, as the market grapples with conflicting views on the sustainability of elevated oil prices. While some analysts argue that a "higher for longer" scenario is underestimated, others caution that the current spike may be short-lived, with oil prices expected to decline as the conflict evolves.

The bullish case hinges on the belief that the market is misjudging the geopolitical risks and their impact on oil supply, particularly with the Strait of Hormuz closed. Conversely, bearish investors point out that ExxonMobil's growth potential is limited, relying heavily on oil price movements and facing challenges in production expansion. With analysts projecting modest earnings growth, the stock's current dividend yield may not justify the investment without a sustained rise in oil prices.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to consider alternative investments in the energy sector, such as Woodside Energy Group, which may offer better growth prospects in the current environment.
