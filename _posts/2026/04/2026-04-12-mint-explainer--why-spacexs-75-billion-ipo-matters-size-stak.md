Protests in Noida have drawn attention as the UP Police report that demonstrations have been influenced by workers from other states, with only one site turning violent. This unrest comes amid ongoing challenges for the MSME sector, which has faced significant stress from pandemic-related lockdowns and rising costs, complicating workforce recruitment due to low pay and high turnover rates.

The financial implications are noteworthy, particularly for oil marketing companies (OMCs), which may see weaker earnings in Q4 FY26 due to increased retail fuel losses and rising LPG under-recoveries. However, strong gross refining margins could provide some offset. Investors are currently rattled by market corrections, but a five-year performance perspective suggests that the overall impact may be less severe than it appears.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving situation in Noida and its potential ripple effects on the MSME sector, as well as the earnings outlook for OMCs, which could influence broader market sentiment.
