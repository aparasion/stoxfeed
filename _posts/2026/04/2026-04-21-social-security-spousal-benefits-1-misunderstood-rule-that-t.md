Social Security spousal benefits can significantly impact retirement income, particularly for couples with disparate earnings or where one spouse has not worked. However, many individuals misunderstand the rules, particularly regarding the timing of claims. Unlike individual benefits, which can grow by delaying claims, spousal benefits do not accrue delayed retirement credits. Claimants must wait until their spouse files for Social Security to access these benefits, which max out at 50% of the spouse's full retirement age benefit.

For financial professionals, this distinction is crucial as it can affect retirement planning strategies for clients. Advising clients to delay claiming spousal benefits past their full retirement age can lead to lost income opportunities, as there is no financial incentive to wait. Understanding these nuances can help clients optimize their Social Security strategy and avoid costly mistakes.

The key takeaway is to encourage clients to claim spousal benefits at their full retirement age to maximize their income and explore additional income sources if necessary.
