Toast (NYSE: TOST) has seen its stock plummet over 40% from its 52-week high, reflecting investor concerns around payments, consumer spending, and software. Despite the downturn, the company remains a compelling growth opportunity, particularly as it serves the restaurant sector, which is heavily reliant on its payment processing solutions. In Q4, Toast reported a 22% increase in gross payment volumes, reaching $51.4 billion, and expanded its customer base by 22% to 164,000 locations, demonstrating resilience amid a challenging economic environment.

The restaurant industry, with an estimated 700,000 to 1 million establishments in the U.S., presents a significant growth runway for Toast, especially as many operators still rely on outdated systems. While the company is not immune to spending slowdowns, its strategic embrace of AI to enhance customer insights and operational efficiency positions it well for future revenue growth.

For market professionals, Toast’s current valuation presents a potential buying opportunity, especially given its solid growth trajectory and limited exposure to AI disruption in a fragmented industry.
