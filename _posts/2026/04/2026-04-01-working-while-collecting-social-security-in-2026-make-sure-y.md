Recent changes to Social Security rules will significantly impact older Americans who plan to work while collecting benefits. Starting in 2026, the earnings limits for those under full retirement age will increase to $24,480, up from $23,400, and $65,160 for those who reach full retirement age during the year, up from $62,160. Exceeding these limits results in reduced benefits, with the Social Security Administration withholding $1 for every $2 or $3 earned over the thresholds.

This adjustment in earnings limits is crucial for financial planning, especially for retirees who may rely on both Social Security and employment income. While the increased limits offer some relief, they may still not be sufficient for many individuals who need to supplement their income. Understanding these rules is essential to avoid potential financial pitfalls during retirement.

Market professionals should consider the implications of these changes on consumer spending and retirement planning strategies, as many older Americans may need to adjust their financial expectations and investment approaches accordingly.
