Investing solely in a 401(k) can expose retirees to significant risks, particularly when market downturns occur. A recent personal account highlights the pitfalls of relying exclusively on this retirement vehicle, emphasizing that withdrawing funds during a market slump can lead to selling assets at unfavorable prices. The author reflects on their experience of maxing out contributions without considering diversification into after-tax accounts, which could provide more accessible funds without incurring heavy tax penalties.

This cautionary tale underscores the importance of a balanced investment strategy. As markets fluctuate, having a mix of assets—such as certificates of deposit, treasury bonds, or high-yield savings accounts—can provide liquidity and mitigate tax impacts during retirement. The author’s realization that they need an emergency fund to avoid tapping into their 401(k) during downturns illustrates the need for proactive financial planning.

For market professionals, this serves as a reminder to encourage clients to diversify retirement savings and consider the implications of tax liabilities on withdrawals, especially in volatile environments.
