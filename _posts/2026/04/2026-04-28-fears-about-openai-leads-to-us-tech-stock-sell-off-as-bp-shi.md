Oil prices have surged past $110 per barrel, driven by ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz and a stalled negotiation between the U.S. and Iran. Brent crude has risen over 2% to above $111, as President Trump reviews Iran's proposal for reopening the Strait. Despite the spike in oil prices, European indices are showing resilience, with the DAX and FTSE 100 both posting gains, buoyed by BP's strong Q1 earnings that exceeded expectations.

The implications for financial markets are significant. Elevated oil prices are likely to impact inflation forecasts and could lead to increased scrutiny of capital expenditures among major tech firms, particularly those heavily invested in AI, like Microsoft and Google. With OpenAI reportedly missing sales targets, concerns are mounting about the sustainability of the AI investment theme that has driven U.S. stock markets to record highs. This could catalyze a rotation from U.S. equities back to European markets.

Market professionals should closely monitor the developments surrounding oil prices and the tech sector's response to OpenAI's challenges. The upcoming earnings reports from major players in the AI space will be crucial in determining market sentiment and potential shifts in investment strategies.
