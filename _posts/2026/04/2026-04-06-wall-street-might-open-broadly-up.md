Geopolitical tensions are rising as President Joe Biden has authorized Ukraine to use U.S.-made long-range missiles against targets in Russia, a significant policy shift that could influence market sentiment. Investors are closely monitoring this development amid a backdrop of mixed Asian market performance and a decline in U.S. futures, with the Dow down 76 points and the Nasdaq falling sharply by 427 points on Friday.

As the market awaits key economic indicators this week, including the Housing Market Index and Treasury Bill auction results, the implications of Biden's decision could ripple through sectors sensitive to geopolitical risks. The mixed performance in Asian shares, with Japan's Nikkei down 1.09% and China's Shanghai Composite slightly lower, suggests cautious investor sentiment as markets digest these developments.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to watch for how geopolitical events might impact risk appetite and sector performance, particularly in defense and energy, as well as the potential influence on upcoming economic data releases.
