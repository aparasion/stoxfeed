The stock market's volatility in 2026 has prompted investors to reassess their strategies, with the S&P 500 experiencing a 9% drop followed by a 12% rebound. Amidst uncertainties surrounding the Iran war, inflation, and economic growth, many are looking for stability through long-term investment narratives rather than short-term market fluctuations. Focusing on quality and value in stock selection can help investors build wealth over time, leveraging the power of compounding.

Investing in high-quality companies with strong cash flows and manageable debt is essential, as these firms often form the backbone of successful portfolios. Additionally, targeting undervalued stocks can provide opportunities for significant returns when their inherent value is realized. Two ETFs exemplifying these strategies are the Schwab U.S. Dividend Equity ETF (SCHD), which emphasizes dividend growth and balance sheet quality, and the Avantis U.S. Small Cap Value ETF (AVUV), which focuses on profitable small-cap companies with attractive valuations.

For market professionals, integrating SCHD and AVUV into portfolios can enhance diversification and long-term returns, particularly in a market environment where tech stocks dominate. These ETFs represent solid investment opportunities that can be held indefinitely, making them valuable additions to any equity strategy.
