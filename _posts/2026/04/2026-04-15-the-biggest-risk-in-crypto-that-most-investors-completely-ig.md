Investors in cryptocurrencies face significant risks beyond the well-known volatility, particularly the threat of losing access to their tokens due to counterparty failures. While Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies are often touted as decentralized, they rely heavily on centralized platforms like exchanges and custodians, which can fail, as seen with the collapses of FTX, BlockFi, and Celsius Network. These incidents left investors as unsecured creditors, highlighting the vulnerabilities inherent in the crypto ecosystem.

This situation underscores the importance of due diligence when managing crypto assets. While platforms like Coinbase attempt to safeguard customer assets by segregating them from corporate funds, they remain susceptible to hacks and bankruptcy risks. Investors must be proactive in mitigating these risks by utilizing hardware wallets, diversifying their holdings across multiple platforms, and steering clear of high-yield staking products that may pose hidden dangers.

The key takeaway for market professionals is to prioritize asset security in the crypto space, as the failure to do so can lead to substantial financial losses, even in a thriving market.
