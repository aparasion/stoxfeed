CoreWeave (CRWV) has emerged as a frontrunner in the artificial intelligence sector, reporting a staggering 168% revenue growth to $5.1 billion in 2025, with projections exceeding $10 billion for 2026. However, the company’s financial health raises concerns, particularly given its ballooning debt, which surged from nearly $8 billion in 2024 to over $21 billion in 2025. With a significant portion of this debt tied to variable-rate facilities averaging around 11%, CoreWeave faces escalating interest expenses that already consumed nearly a third of its revenue in Q4 2025.

The customer concentration on major players like Microsoft and OpenAI adds another layer of risk. While these partnerships can be advantageous, they also expose CoreWeave to potential competitive pressures as these tech giants may eventually seek to internalize their computing needs. Additionally, OpenAI’s projected losses could complicate funding dynamics, which are crucial for CoreWeave to convert its substantial backlog into revenue.

Market professionals should closely monitor CoreWeave’s ability to manage its debt amid potential economic headwinds, as rising interest rates could significantly impact its financial stability and stock performance over the next year.
