SpaceX is poised to become the largest IPO in history, but it faces significant competition from Amazon, which is ramping up its space ambitions through the acquisition of Globalstar for approximately $11.6 billion. This deal not only adds 24 satellites to Amazon's fleet but also secures valuable radio spectrum licenses essential for data transmission. These assets could enhance Amazon's low Earth orbit satellite project, Amazon Leo, potentially positioning it as a formidable competitor to SpaceX's Starlink.

The implications for the financial markets are notable. Amazon's investment reflects a commitment to its space strategy, but it also signals increased capital expenditures, which could pressure free cash flow. With planned expenditures reaching $200 billion this year, investors may face heightened risk, especially as Amazon seeks to meet its FCC deployment deadlines for its satellite constellation. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Amazon's expansion into the space economy offers growth potential, it also introduces significant financial strain. Investors should weigh the risks against the current valuation as Amazon navigates this ambitious venture.
