Bloom Energy (NYSE: BE) has emerged as a late bloomer in the stock market, experiencing a remarkable resurgence after a rocky start post-IPO in 2018. Initially, shares soared 40% but plummeted 60% by year-end, leaving investors disheartened. Fast forward to the second half of 2025, and Bloom Energy's stock has skyrocketed, delivering a staggering 13x gain over the past year, turning an initial $10,000 investment into approximately $95,000.

This dramatic turnaround is largely attributed to Bloom's innovative solid oxide fuel cells, which have become essential for powering data centers amid the explosive growth of AI technologies. With major clients like Oracle, Amazon, and FedEx recognizing Bloom's ability to provide reliable, on-site power quickly, the company is well-positioned to capitalize on the increasing demand for energy solutions in the tech sector.

However, potential investors should tread carefully; Bloom's current valuation is steep, trading at 167 times forward earnings and a PEG ratio of 6.7. While strong revenue growth is expected, the sustainability of this momentum hinges on the longevity of the AI supercycle.
