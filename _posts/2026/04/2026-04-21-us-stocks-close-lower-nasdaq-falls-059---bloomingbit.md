U.S. stocks closed lower today, with the Nasdaq dropping 0.59% amid escalating geopolitical tensions as Iran announced it would skip upcoming negotiations with the U.S. This development has raised concerns over the stability of U.S.-Iran relations, impacting investor sentiment and contributing to the downward pressure on equities.

The uncertainty surrounding U.S.-Iran talks is compounded by Vice President JD Vance's canceled trip to Pakistan, which was intended to facilitate discussions. This backdrop of geopolitical instability has also affected risk assets, with Bitcoin falling to $75,000 following comments from Kevin Warsh, a Federal Reserve chair nominee, who indicated a lack of authority for a central bank digital currency (CBDC) and called for a fundamental policy overhaul at the Fed. 

Market professionals should closely monitor these developments, as the interplay between geopolitical events and monetary policy could significantly influence market dynamics and investment strategies in the near term.
