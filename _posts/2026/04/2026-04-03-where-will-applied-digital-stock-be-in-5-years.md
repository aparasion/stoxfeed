Applied Digital (APLD) has secured a substantial $16 billion in contracted lease revenue, positioning itself as a key player in the AI infrastructure space. The company is on track to deliver 100 megawatts of data center capacity at its Polaris 1 campus, a feat that contrasts sharply with delays faced by competitors. However, the stability of this revenue stream is questionable, as $11 billion of it hinges on a single customer, CoreWeave, which is grappling with significant financial challenges.

CoreWeave's rapid growth is overshadowed by its mounting debt, which has surged from $7.9 billion to over $21 billion in just one year. The company's reliance on OpenAI, which is also facing substantial losses, creates a precarious chain of dependencies that could jeopardize Applied Digital's future cash flows. If any part of this financial ecosystem falters, the implications for APLD could be severe.

Market professionals should closely monitor the health of CoreWeave and OpenAI, as their financial stability directly impacts Applied Digital's long-term outlook. The potential for substantial downside risk exists, particularly given the current macroeconomic climate.
