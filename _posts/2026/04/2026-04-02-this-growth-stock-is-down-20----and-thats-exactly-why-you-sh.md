Amazon (AMZN) shares have dipped 20% from their recent highs, presenting a rare buying opportunity for long-term investors. Despite the decline, the stock is nearing its lowest valuation in a decade when assessed against its cash flow from operations. The company's significant capital expenditures, particularly in artificial intelligence (AI), are a key factor in this downturn, as Amazon plans to invest $200 billion this year to bolster its AI capabilities.

This aggressive spending strategy is expected to pay off by enhancing operational efficiencies across both its cloud services and retail segments. With Amazon Web Services (AWS) already a market leader, the company’s investments in AI, including robotics for fulfillment and delivery, could yield substantial cost savings and further market share in the retail sector, which remains underpenetrated by e-commerce.

For market professionals, the takeaway is clear: now is an opportune moment to consider Amazon as a long-term investment. As AI initiatives begin to generate revenue, the potential for a rebound in cash flow and stock performance could be significant.
