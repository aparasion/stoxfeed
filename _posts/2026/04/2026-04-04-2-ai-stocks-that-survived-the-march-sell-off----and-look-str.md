Arm Holdings and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) have emerged as key players in the rapidly evolving AI infrastructure market, with both companies witnessing stock gains in March despite broader tech sell-offs. Arm is pivoting to design its own high-performance CPUs, targeting a $100 billion data center CPU market by 2031, while AMD continues to solidify its leadership in this space, generating $16.6 billion in data center revenue last year.

The shift from GPUs to CPUs for AI applications, particularly with the rise of agentic AI, presents a significant opportunity for both firms. Arm's focus on power efficiency and high core counts aligns well with the demands of AI data centers, while AMD's innovative chiplet architecture enhances its competitive edge. As the market for AI agents expands, the demand for CPUs is expected to surge, potentially benefiting both companies substantially.

Investors should note that while both stocks are positioned for growth, market analysts have identified other stocks as more favorable investments at this time. This highlights the importance of careful stock selection in a rapidly changing tech landscape.
