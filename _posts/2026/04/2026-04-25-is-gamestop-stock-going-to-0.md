GameStop (GME) is unlikely to face bankruptcy soon, bolstered by a robust balance sheet featuring $9 billion in cash and marketable securities, alongside nearly $370 million in Bitcoin. With $4.2 billion in convertible debt at 0% interest, the company’s financial position appears secure. However, its market cap exceeds $11 billion, and an enterprise value (EV) of around $6.5 billion suggests potential downside risk, especially given its declining revenue and a relatively high EV-to-earnings multiple of 15.5.

CEO Ryan Cohen has successfully pivoted the company towards profitability, notably through its collectibles segment, which has seen significant growth. This shift, alongside strategic partnerships like the one with PSA for trading cards, has diversified GameStop's revenue streams. However, the sustainability of this growth remains uncertain, particularly as the collectibles market can be volatile.

The key takeaway for investors is that GameStop's future hinges on how effectively it utilizes its cash reserves. Cohen's ambitions for a transformative acquisition could pose risks if not executed prudently, especially if it involves taking on additional debt.
