President Donald Trump has extended the two-week U.S. ceasefire with Iran, citing the country's "seriously fractured" government as a reason for the decision. The ceasefire will remain in place until Iranian leaders present a "unified proposal" aimed at resolving ongoing tensions with the U.S. and Israel. This announcement follows a prior indication that the ceasefire would conclude on Wednesday, highlighting a shift in strategy influenced by requests from Pakistani officials.

This development is significant for financial markets as it may affect geopolitical stability in the Middle East, a region critical to global oil supply and trade. Continued hostilities or a breakdown in negotiations could lead to increased volatility in energy markets, impacting oil prices and related equities. Conversely, a successful diplomatic resolution could bolster investor sentiment and stabilize market conditions.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation for any shifts in oil prices and sector performance, particularly in energy and defense, as developments unfold.
