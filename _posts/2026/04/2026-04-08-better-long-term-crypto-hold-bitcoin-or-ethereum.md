Bitcoin and Ethereum continue to dominate the cryptocurrency landscape, but their investment theses diverge significantly. Bitcoin, with 95% of its supply already mined, remains a stable store of value, relying on its scarcity and established network. The recent surge in spot Bitcoin ETFs, which have attracted $56 billion in net inflows since early 2024, underscores its growing institutional appeal. 

In contrast, Ethereum offers advanced functionalities, hosting a substantial portion of decentralized finance (DeFi) and tokenized real-world assets. However, its reliance on continuous development and competition from other blockchains introduces inherent risks. While Ethereum may outperform Bitcoin in the short term, its success hinges on maintaining developer interest and institutional confidence in a rapidly evolving market.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the differing risk profiles: Bitcoin's simplicity and scarcity make it a more reliable long-term hold, while Ethereum's innovative capabilities come with greater volatility and competitive pressures. As such, portfolio diversification may be prudent, balancing both assets according to risk tolerance and investment strategy.
