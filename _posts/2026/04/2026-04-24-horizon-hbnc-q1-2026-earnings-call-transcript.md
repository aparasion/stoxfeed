Horizon Bancorp reported strong first-quarter results, showcasing significant deposit and commercial loan growth. The bank achieved an annualized return on average assets exceeding 1.60% and a return on average tangible common equity above 19%. Notably, total deposits increased by $147 million, or 11% annualized, with a $61 million rise in noninterest-bearing deposits, reflecting effective organic growth strategies rather than market disruptions.

These results are critical for investors as they indicate robust capital and profitability metrics, with the Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio rising to 10.82%. The bank's net interest margin remained stable at 4.29%, despite a competitive lending landscape. Management expects continued growth in loans and deposits throughout 2026, with a focus on maintaining disciplined pricing strategies in commercial lending.

For market professionals, Horizon's solid performance underscores the importance of organic growth initiatives and disciplined credit management in navigating a dynamic rate environment. The bank's positive trajectory may signal a favorable outlook for its stock as it capitalizes on its strong market position.
