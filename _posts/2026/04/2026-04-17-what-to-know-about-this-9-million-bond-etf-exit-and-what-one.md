On April 16, 2026, Matthew Goff Investment Advisor reported to the SEC that it had completely divested its position in the Invesco BulletShares 2026 Corporate Bond ETF (BSCQ), selling 461,925 shares for an estimated $9.04 million. This move eliminated the ETF from the advisor's portfolio, which previously represented 1.6% of its assets under management (AUM).

The sale is notable as it reflects a strategic shift in the advisor's investment approach, particularly since it recently increased its stake in a high-yield version of the same ETF. With BSCQ's defined maturity approaching and limited growth potential, the advisor appears to be prioritizing capital preservation over income generation. The ETF's current yield stands at 4.1%, and its exit could signal a broader trend among investors seeking to manage risk as maturity dates near.

For market professionals, this transaction highlights the importance of monitoring shifts in institutional portfolios, as they can indicate changing sentiment towards specific asset classes and investment strategies.
