Nokia (NOK) shares rose 2.87% to close at $10.76 on Monday, buoyed by analyst upgrades and a surge in institutional buying. The stock's trading volume hit 138.5 million shares, significantly above its three-month average of 63.2 million, signaling heightened investor interest. Analysts highlighted the company's potential for revenue growth driven by increased demand for AI-focused networking solutions, particularly in data center connectivity.

This uptick in Nokia's stock reflects a broader trend in the communication equipment sector, where peers like Cisco Systems and Motorola Solutions experienced declines. The market is closely watching how Nokia capitalizes on the demand for high-capacity networks, essential for cloud providers and enterprises managing AI workloads. However, challenges remain, including inconsistent spending from telecom operators and cost pressures among network equipment vendors.

Investors should keep an eye on Nokia's upcoming Q1 2026 earnings report, as it will be crucial in determining whether the current demand for optical and IP networking translates into sustained revenue growth and improved margins.
