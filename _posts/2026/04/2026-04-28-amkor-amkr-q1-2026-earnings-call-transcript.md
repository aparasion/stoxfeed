Amkor Technology reported a record first-quarter revenue of $1.68 billion, a 27% increase year-on-year, driven by robust demand across all end markets, particularly in communications and automotive sectors. The communications segment surged 42%, fueled by strong iOS ecosystem performance, while computing revenue grew 19%, supported by record AI data center sales, despite some weakness in PCs. Gross margin reached 14.2%, surpassing expectations, and operating income rose significantly, reflecting disciplined cost management.

This impressive performance highlights Amkor's strategic positioning in the semiconductor industry, particularly as demand for advanced packaging technologies continues to grow. With a strong balance sheet, including $1.8 billion in cash and a debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 1.1x, Amkor is well-equipped to navigate supply chain challenges and capitalize on future growth opportunities. 

Looking ahead, Amkor projects Q2 revenue between $1.75 billion and $1.85 billion, indicating continued momentum. Investors should monitor the upcoming Investor Day on May 21 for insights into long-term earnings potential and capital expenditure plans, particularly regarding the Arizona facility expansion.
