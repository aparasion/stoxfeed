U.S. stock indices are experiencing sharp declines this morning, with the Dow Jones, S&P 500, and Nasdaq-100 all down approximately 0.8-0.9%. This downturn follows last week's record highs and is largely attributed to escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly following the U.S. military's engagement with an Iranian cargo ship. The situation has led to increased uncertainty in the markets, prompting investors to reduce risk exposure.

The energy sector is witnessing a significant rally, with WTI and Brent crude prices surging by 6-7% due to fears of prolonged inflation stemming from rising fuel costs. Conversely, the airline industry faces pressure as American Airlines shares drop 3% amid merger speculation, while travel-related stocks are also struggling with higher fuel prices. Upcoming economic data, including March retail sales and the Federal Reserve chair nominee hearing, will be critical for market direction.

Market professionals should closely monitor the energy sector's performance and the implications of geopolitical tensions on inflation, as these factors could influence trading strategies and sector allocations in the near term.
