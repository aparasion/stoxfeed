FB Financial Corporation (FBK) reported a mixed second quarter for 2025, with adjusted earnings per share (EPS) at $0.88, significantly impacted by a $60 million loss from a securities sale. While net interest income rose 3.5% quarter-over-quarter to $111.4 million, the firm faced challenges with a $34.6 million loss in noninterest income, primarily due to the aforementioned securities transaction. Loan growth was steady at an annualized rate of 4.2%, but management noted that pipeline delays could affect future performance.

The completion of the Southern States acquisition within 90 days is a pivotal development, expected to enhance scale and earnings. CFO Michael Mettee anticipates a net interest margin of 3.70%-3.80% for the latter half of the year, driven by improved asset deployment and cost synergies. Despite current pressures, including nonperforming loans and competitive deposit costs, the firm remains optimistic about growth prospects in the second half of 2025 and beyond.

Market professionals should note that FB Financial's strategic focus on integrating Southern States and optimizing its asset mix could position it well for improved profitability and operational efficiency in the coming quarters.
