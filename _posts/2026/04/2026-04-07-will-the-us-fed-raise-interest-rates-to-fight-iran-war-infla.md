The U.S. and Iran have reached a two-week truce, easing tensions that had escalated following President Trump's threats to target Iranian infrastructure. This agreement includes the reopening of the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz, a crucial passage for global oil shipments, which had been under heightened scrutiny amid rising geopolitical tensions.

This development is significant for financial markets, particularly in the energy sector, as it could stabilize oil prices that have been volatile due to fears of conflict in the region. A ceasefire may lead to increased supply and lower prices, impacting the profitability of oil companies and influencing broader market sentiment.

Market professionals should monitor how this truce affects oil futures and related equities, as well as any potential shifts in U.S. foreign policy that could arise after the two-week period. The situation remains fluid, and traders will need to stay alert for further developments.
