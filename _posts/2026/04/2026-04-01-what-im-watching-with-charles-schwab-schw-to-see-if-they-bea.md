Charles Schwab (SCHW) has seen a robust 21% stock price increase over the past year, outperforming the S&P 500's 18% gain. As a multifaceted financial services firm, Schwab's profitability hinges on its cash management strategies, primarily investing in U.S. Treasuries and mortgage-backed securities, alongside asset management fees and brokerage activities. However, the recent rise in interest rates has had a dual impact, boosting net interest income while simultaneously dampening trading volumes and growth in its brokerage and wealth management segments.

Looking ahead, analysts project Schwab's revenue and earnings per share to grow at compound annual growth rates of 8% and 15%, respectively, from 2025 to 2028. With the stock trading at 17 times this year's earnings, a potential rise to $130 could materialize if interest rates stabilize or decline. However, macroeconomic factors, including geopolitical tensions and inflation, pose risks that could influence the Fed's rate decisions, which are critical for Schwab's continued market performance.

For market professionals, monitoring the interplay between interest rates and Schwab's growth trajectory will be essential in assessing its potential to maintain an edge over the broader market.
