Intel (INTC) and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) are in a competitive struggle that highlights the complexities of stock selection beyond mere revenue figures. While Intel has historically dominated the CPU market, recent trends show AMD significantly outpacing it in quarterly revenue growth, driven by its expanding market share in both consumer and server segments.

This divergence is crucial for investors as AMD's revenue momentum, marked by a 16% net income margin, contrasts sharply with Intel's stagnating revenue and negative net income margin of -2%. AMD's strategic partnerships and new product launches, particularly in the data center GPU market, position it favorably against Intel, which is still grappling with a challenging revenue landscape despite recent positive developments, including a partnership with Google.

Investors should closely monitor AMD’s upcoming MI450 data center chip launch, which could further accelerate its growth trajectory and potentially lead to a revenue lead over Intel by 2027, reshaping the competitive dynamics in the semiconductor sector.
