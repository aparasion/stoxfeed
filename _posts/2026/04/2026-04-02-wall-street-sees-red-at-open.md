U.S. futures indicate a sharp decline for Wall Street on Thursday, driven by escalating tensions in the Middle East. President Trump has warned of significant military action against Iran, contributing to a surge in oil prices while gold prices plummeted nearly 3%. As of early morning trading, Dow futures were down 697 points, S&P 500 futures dropped 110 points, and Nasdaq 100 futures fell 512 points, reversing the positive momentum seen on Wednesday.

The geopolitical uncertainty is likely to weigh heavily on market sentiment, with investors reacting to the implications for energy prices and broader economic stability. The upcoming economic data releases, including the International Trade deficit and Jobless Claims, could further influence market direction as traders assess the potential impacts on corporate earnings and consumer confidence.

Market professionals should brace for volatility in the energy sector and related equities, as well as monitor safe-haven assets like gold and the dollar, which may continue to react to developments in the Middle East.
