Geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz have escalated sharply as Iran restricts vessel traffic amid a U.S. naval blockade. Reports indicate that Iran has turned back at least 20 vessels and issued warnings declaring the waterway closed to all traffic, reversing recent optimism following a temporary ceasefire agreement. The situation has deteriorated as Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps has reportedly attacked commercial vessels, further raising security risks in the region.

This disruption is particularly concerning for financial markets, as the Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil supply. With tanker availability in the Middle East sharply reduced and shipowners hesitant to return, the risk of prolonged disruptions to oil flows is increasing. This could lead to heightened market volatility and potential supply shocks, especially following last week’s selloff.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait, as ongoing restrictions could necessitate a reevaluation of oil prices and trading strategies in the face of tightening supply.
