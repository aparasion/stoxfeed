Tesla (TSLA) stock has experienced a sharp decline of 20% since reaching a record high in December, driven by disappointing electric vehicle (EV) sales and broader market volatility. As the company prepares to release its first-quarter 2026 operating results on April 22, investors are keenly focused on updates regarding future product platforms like the Cybercab robotaxi and the Optimus humanoid robot, which could shape the company's trajectory in the competitive EV landscape.

Despite a slight year-over-year increase in deliveries for Q1 2026, Tesla's overall automotive revenue remains under pressure, with a significant drop in profit margins due to price cuts aimed at boosting sales. The upcoming earnings report is expected to show revenue growth, but analysts caution that the stock's high valuation—trading at a P/E ratio of 327—suggests it may be overvalued compared to its peers in the Nasdaq-100.

Investors should approach Tesla stock with caution ahead of the earnings release, as the potential for continued downside remains significant. With the company's reliance on EV sales and the uncertain timelines for its autonomous vehicle initiatives, buying into the stock now may lead to disappointment if the anticipated recovery fails to materialize.
