Justin Sun, the founder of Tron, has publicly severed ties with World Liberty Financial (WLFI) following the project’s controversial $75 million DeFi loan, which he claims exploits users. Sun, once a significant backer of WLFI, criticized the team for treating investors as a “personal ATM” after they deposited 5 billion WLFI tokens on the Dolomite platform to secure the loan, which temporarily locked out ordinary depositors due to high utilization rates.

This development raises concerns about WLFI's governance and operational transparency, especially as the token's value plummeted to around $0.079, an 18% decline over the past week. Sun’s allegations highlight potential risks for investors in DeFi projects, particularly regarding liquidity management and the ethical treatment of participants. The dual role of Dolomite’s co-founder as an advisor to WLFI further complicates the situation, suggesting conflicts of interest that could undermine investor confidence.

Market professionals should closely monitor WLFI’s governance practices and liquidity management strategies, as these factors could significantly impact the project’s viability and investor sentiment moving forward.
