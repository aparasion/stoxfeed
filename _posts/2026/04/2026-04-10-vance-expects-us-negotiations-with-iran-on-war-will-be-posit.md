Vice President JD Vance expressed optimism about upcoming negotiations with Iran aimed at resolving the ongoing six-week conflict, cautioning Tehran's negotiators against manipulation. His comments come just before a U.S. delegation, including Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, heads to Islamabad for talks. However, Iranian parliamentary speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf warned that key preconditions, such as a ceasefire in Lebanon and the release of Iran's blocked assets, must be met before negotiations can proceed, complicating the already tenuous ceasefire.

The outcome of these negotiations is crucial for global oil markets, particularly given the strategic importance of the Strait of Hormuz, through which 20% of the world’s crude oil is transported. Continued tensions and delays in negotiations could exacerbate supply chain disruptions and elevate oil prices, impacting energy sector stocks and broader market sentiment.

Market professionals should closely monitor these developments, as any escalation or failure to reach an agreement could lead to increased volatility in oil prices and related equities.
