Cybersecurity stocks are experiencing a significant feedback loop driven by fears that artificial intelligence (AI) could render traditional security platforms obsolete. SentinelOne (S), for instance, has seen its stock plummet nearly 45% from its 52-week high, trading at approximately 4.5 times sales—well below its historical average. However, the market may be overlooking a key factor: SentinelOne's platform was designed with AI at its core, exemplified by its Purple AI product, which operates as an autonomous analyst within security operations.

This AI-native approach allows SentinelOne to dramatically reduce the time required for threat investigations, a crucial advantage for security teams inundated with alerts. Notably, over 50% of SentinelOne licenses sold in Q4 included Purple AI, indicating strong demand from existing customers. Additionally, a new partnership with Google Cloud enhances its appeal for enterprises utilizing cloud infrastructure, addressing a significant adoption barrier.

Despite concerns about profitability and competition from established players like CrowdStrike, SentinelOne's early integration of AI into its workflow positions it favorably in the market. Analysts project a price target of around $18, suggesting a potential 33% upside, making it a compelling consideration for portfolio managers.
