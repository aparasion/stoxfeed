U.S. President Donald Trump expressed optimism about reaching a favorable deal with Iran during a CNBC interview, citing significant military actions that have diminished Iran's naval and air capabilities. He noted that the removal of key leaders complicates the situation but believes it could lead to a rational negotiation from Iran's side. Trump's comments suggest a shift in U.S. strategy towards Iran, potentially influencing geopolitical stability in the region.

This development could have far-reaching implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical tensions, such as energy and defense. Investors may react to fluctuations in oil prices, which often rise amid conflict, as well as to the potential for sanctions relief that could impact companies operating in or trading with Iran.

Market professionals should monitor the evolving situation closely, as a resolution could alter supply dynamics in the oil market and affect broader market sentiment regarding risk assets.
