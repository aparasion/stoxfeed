The Fidelity MSCI Consumer Staples Index ETF (FSTA) and the Invesco S&P 500 Equal Weight Consumer Staples ETF (RSPS) present distinct investment strategies within the consumer staples sector, catering to different investor priorities. FSTA boasts a lower expense ratio of 0.08% and a significant asset base, while RSPS offers a higher dividend yield of 2.9% and a balanced exposure across 35 equally weighted stocks, reducing concentration risk in major players.

This differentiation matters as consumer staples typically provide defensive investment opportunities, especially in economic downturns. FSTA's heavy allocation to giants like Walmart and Costco positions it to benefit directly from consumer behavior shifts, while RSPS's approach spreads risk across mid-tier companies, appealing to income-focused investors seeking diversification without over-reliance on a few dominant firms.

Ultimately, the choice between FSTA and RSPS hinges on investor goals: FSTA serves as a low-cost, straightforward option for broad sector exposure, while RSPS may attract those prioritizing yield and a more balanced portfolio across the consumer staples landscape.
