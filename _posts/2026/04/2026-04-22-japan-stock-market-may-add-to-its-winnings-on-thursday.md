The Japan stock market has reached a new record high, with the Nikkei index climbing nearly 1,100 points, or 1.8%, over three consecutive trading days, closing just below the 59,590-point mark. This upward momentum is expected to continue, fueled by a cautiously optimistic global outlook, particularly regarding the potential resolution of the Middle East conflict, which has implications for market stability and investor sentiment.

The recent performance of the Nikkei has been bolstered by gains in financial and resource sectors, despite some notable declines among major automotive stocks like Toyota and Honda. The positive lead from U.S. markets, where major indices hit record highs following news of an extended U.S. ceasefire with Iran, has contributed to this bullish trend. Additionally, rising crude oil prices due to supply disruption concerns are likely to impact energy-related stocks in the region.

Market professionals should monitor the upcoming preliminary PMI figures from Japan, as these could provide further insights into economic health and influence trading strategies in the context of ongoing global developments.
