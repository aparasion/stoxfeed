Jenny Lei's handbag company, Freja, is gaining traction among young consumers who are increasingly favoring mid-priced retail items over both budget and luxury brands. Launched in 2019, Freja offers vegan leather bags priced between $258 and $398, appealing to millennials and Gen Z shoppers who seek affordable luxury. This trend reflects a broader shift in consumer behavior, where nearly a third of global customers are willing to splurge on fashion, as highlighted in a recent McKinsey report.

The rise of mid-priced products signifies a recalibration in the retail landscape, as brands like H&M and Bershka adjust their offerings to compete with both fast fashion and luxury labels. This shift allows consumers to indulge without the financial strain of high-end purchases, creating opportunities for brands to attract new customers with more accessible price points. 

For market professionals, the implication is clear: the growing demand for mid-priced goods could pressure both budget and luxury sectors to innovate and adapt, potentially reshaping competitive strategies across the retail industry.
