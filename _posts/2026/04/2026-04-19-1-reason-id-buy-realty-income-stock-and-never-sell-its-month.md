Realty Income (NYSE: O) continues to attract investors with its robust monthly dividend payments and consistent operational growth, making it a compelling choice for those seeking passive income. The REIT has maintained an impressive track record, delivering 669 consecutive monthly dividends and achieving a historical yield of around 5.7%. Its strategy of focusing on high-quality, diversified properties leased to resilient tenants has allowed it to increase earnings nearly every year since going public in 1994.

The significance of Realty Income lies in its ability to provide stable returns even amid economic fluctuations. With a conservative payout ratio of about 75% of adjusted funds from operations (AFFO) and a strong investment-grade balance sheet, the REIT is well-positioned to reinvest in its portfolio. Realty Income plans to invest $8 billion in new properties this year, tapping into a vast addressable market for net lease real estate.

For market professionals, Realty Income represents a solid investment opportunity, balancing high-yield income with the potential for long-term capital appreciation, making it a strategic anchor for income-focused portfolios.
