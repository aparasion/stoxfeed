Asia-Pacific markets showed resilience on Monday, with Japan’s Nikkei 225 rising 0.53% to reach a record high, while South Korea's Kospi surged 1% to also set a new benchmark. This positive sentiment persisted despite heightened tensions between the U.S. and Iran, which saw President Trump cancel diplomatic negotiations and oil prices spike. The S&P/ASX 200 in Australia, however, dipped 0.54%, indicating mixed regional performance.

The ongoing geopolitical tensions have kept oil prices elevated, with Brent crude rising over 2% to $107.49 per barrel. This price action reflects concerns over supply disruptions, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz, where Iran's Revolutionary Guard has reportedly seized cargo ships. Meanwhile, U.S. futures pointed lower, suggesting a cautious approach ahead of the market open, despite last week's record closes for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite.

Market professionals should monitor oil price trends closely, as sustained high prices could impact inflation expectations and consumer spending, influencing broader market dynamics in the coming weeks.
