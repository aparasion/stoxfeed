Market volatility surged as geopolitical tensions escalated following President Trump's declaration that the US-Iran ceasefire is unlikely to be extended. His warning of imminent military action and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical route for 20% of global oil shipments, has sent shockwaves through financial markets. The U.S. Navy's seizure of an Iranian cargo ship further intensified concerns, with only three commercial vessels reported passing through the strait today.

This geopolitical instability has immediate implications for energy markets, with WTI crude oil prices climbing over 4.98% to $87.20 per barrel and Brent crude surpassing $90. The European stock markets reacted negatively, with the Stoxx 600 index down nearly 1.1%, while U.S. indices showed more resilience, indicating that investors are not fully pricing in a catastrophic scenario. Earnings reports have generally exceeded expectations, with 88% of S&P 500 companies beating forecasts, suggesting underlying strength amid the turmoil.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the potential for sustained volatility in the energy sector and broader markets as geopolitical risks remain elevated. Investors should closely monitor developments in the Middle East and their impact on oil prices, as well as upcoming economic data that may influence market sentiment.
