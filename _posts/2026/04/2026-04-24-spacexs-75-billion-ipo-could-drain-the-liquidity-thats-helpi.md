SpaceX's impending IPO, aiming for a staggering $75 billion at a $1.75 trillion valuation, could significantly impact liquidity across the financial markets, particularly in tech and crypto sectors. Set for June, this offering is projected to be the largest stock-market debut in history, drawing a substantial amount of capital that may otherwise support high-growth equities and digital assets.

Analysts warn that the combined capital raises from SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic—totaling over $240 billion—could drain liquidity from risk-on assets, including cryptocurrencies. The correlation between crypto and tech equities has tightened, meaning that as retail and institutional investors allocate funds to these IPOs, capital available for bidding on cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin may diminish. Historical parallels, such as Coinbase's IPO coinciding with Bitcoin's peak in April 2021, raise concerns about potential market corrections following these large listings.

Market participants should closely monitor crypto performance leading up to and following the SpaceX IPO. A sustained rally in Bitcoin during this period may indicate a decoupling from traditional market flows, while a downturn could signal a liquidity squeeze as investors pivot to the new equity offerings.
