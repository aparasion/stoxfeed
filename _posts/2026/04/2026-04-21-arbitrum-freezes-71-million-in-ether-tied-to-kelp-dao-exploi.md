Arbitrum has taken decisive action by freezing 30,766 ETH, valued at approximately $71 million, linked to a recent exploit of Kelp DAO that resulted in a $292 million loss. This emergency measure, executed with law enforcement input, places the funds in a governance-controlled wallet, preventing the original hacker from accessing them. The freeze recovers about 25% of the stolen assets and intensifies the ongoing dispute between Kelp and bridge provider LayerZero regarding liability for the exploit.

This intervention highlights the complexities and risks within the decentralized finance (DeFi) space, particularly concerning governance and user funds. While Arbitrum's Security Council acted to protect the ecosystem, such governance actions can be controversial, as they introduce discretionary control over a typically permissionless network. The situation raises questions about the broader implications for DeFi security and the responsibilities of various stakeholders involved in these ecosystems.

Market professionals should closely monitor the developments surrounding Kelp DAO and LayerZero, as the resolution of this dispute could influence investor sentiment and regulatory scrutiny in the DeFi sector.
