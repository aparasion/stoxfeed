Apple (AAPL) is taking a different approach to artificial intelligence (AI) than its competitors, which could impact its long-term stock performance. While rivals like Alphabet and Microsoft are heavily investing in AI development—spending an estimated $650 billion collectively—Apple's capital expenditures for AI remain significantly lower, at under $13 billion. Despite this, Apple reported a 16% revenue increase in its latest quarter, driven by strong iPhone 17 sales and a 14% rise in service revenue, indicating that its core business remains robust.

The company's strategy appears to focus on enhancing its existing products rather than competing directly in AI model development. By leveraging partnerships, such as integrating Google’s Gemini AI into Siri, Apple aims to improve its services without the heavy costs associated with building its own AI infrastructure. This could allow Apple to maintain high profit margins while still benefiting from advanced AI capabilities.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Apple may not be leading the AI race, its unique strategy of focusing on device sales and partnerships could provide a sustainable competitive advantage. Observing the rollout of the new Siri and consumer response to Apple’s devices will be crucial in assessing the effectiveness of this approach.
