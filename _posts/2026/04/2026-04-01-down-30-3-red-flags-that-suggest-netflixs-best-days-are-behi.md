Netflix (NFLX) has been a standout performer in the stock market, boasting a staggering 22,700% increase in share value over the past two decades. However, the streaming giant has seen a 30% decline since its peak in June 2025, raising concerns about its future growth. Three key red flags suggest that Netflix may be facing significant challenges ahead.

First, Netflix's aborted $83 billion acquisition of Warner Bros Discovery indicates a potential shift in strategy as the company grapples with the need for a more extensive content library. Second, engagement metrics reveal a troubling trend: while Netflix's share of U.S. TV viewing time has slightly increased, competitors like YouTube have surged, capturing a larger audience. Finally, Netflix's planned $20 billion content budget for 2026 reflects the escalating costs in a fiercely competitive market, particularly as it ventures into live sports.

For market professionals, the takeaway is clear: Netflix's ability to maintain its competitive edge may be at risk, warranting close monitoring of engagement trends and content spending as indicators of future performance.
