Gotrade has launched a new investment platform allowing users to invest in fractional shares of major companies like Google, Apple, and Netflix with a minimum investment of just $1. This initiative aims to democratize access to equity markets, enabling over 500,000 investors from 150 countries to participate in wealth-building through fractional ownership.

This development could significantly impact retail trading dynamics, as it lowers the barrier to entry for individual investors. By allowing purchases of as little as 0.00001 shares, Gotrade taps into a growing trend of micro-investing, which may attract younger, tech-savvy investors eager to engage with the stock market without substantial capital. Such platforms could also influence the performance of the stocks involved, as increased retail participation often correlates with heightened volatility and trading volume.

For market professionals, this trend underscores the importance of monitoring retail investor behavior and its potential implications for stock liquidity and price movements.
