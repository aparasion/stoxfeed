Intel (INTC) shares surged 4.89% to close at $50.38 on Thursday, fueled by investor enthusiasm following the company's $14.2 billion repurchase of Apollo's 49% stake in its Fab 34 facility. This strategic move restores full ownership of the Ireland-based plant, enhancing Intel's control over a critical asset in its foundry strategy and positioning the company to better meet the growing demand for AI and data center capabilities.

The uptick in Intel's stock comes amid a broader positive trend in the semiconductor sector, with peers like AMD and Nvidia also posting gains. The increased trading volume of 116.1 million shares indicates heightened investor interest, surpassing the three-month average. Analysts view this transaction as a pivotal step in Intel's turnaround, although it also underscores the significant capital required to remain competitive in advanced chip manufacturing.

Investors should closely monitor Intel's upcoming quarterly results for insights into how this strategic acquisition is impacting profitability and utilization rates, as the company navigates both potential growth and execution risks associated with its ambitious manufacturing plans.
