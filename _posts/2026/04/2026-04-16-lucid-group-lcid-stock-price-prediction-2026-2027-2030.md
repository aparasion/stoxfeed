Lucid Group (NASDAQ: LCID) is facing significant challenges as analysts project its stock could reach only $6.64 by 2030. Following a dramatic rise during its meme-stock phase, Lucid has since lost nearly all its gains, leading to a reverse 1-for-10 stock split to avoid delisting. Current market conditions reflect a cautious outlook, with a consensus Sell rating and an average price target of $9.99, suggesting limited upside potential.

Despite a recent uptick in production and a 68% revenue increase year-over-year, Lucid continues to struggle with substantial cash burn and negative profit margins. The company reported a net loss of approximately $814 million in Q4 2025, raising concerns about its financial stability and ability to compete against giants like Tesla and BYD. As federal EV tax credits phase out, Lucid's growth prospects may further diminish.

Investors should approach LCID with caution, as the stock remains speculative and its financial fundamentals are weak. While there are some positive indicators, the risks associated with cash flow and market competition suggest a challenging road ahead.
