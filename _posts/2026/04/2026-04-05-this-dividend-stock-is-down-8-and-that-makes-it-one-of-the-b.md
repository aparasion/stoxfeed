Texas Instruments (TXN) is positioning itself as a key player in the semiconductor market, particularly with its analog chips that are essential for data centers, which saw a 70% sales increase year-over-year. While the focus has shifted towards AI semiconductors, Texas Instruments continues to benefit from the growing demand for high-performance power management and signal-chain chips that support AI servers. This trend underscores the company's relevance in a rapidly evolving tech landscape.

The company’s recent financial performance is noteworthy, with 2025 revenue reaching $17.7 billion, a 13% increase, and a 4.8% rise in earnings per share. Texas Instruments has also committed to substantial capital investments, including a new fabrication facility in Texas aimed at enhancing production capabilities and reducing reliance on external foundries. This vertical integration strategy is expected to yield cost advantages and bolster free cash flow, which surged 96% to $2.9 billion in 2025.

For market professionals, Texas Instruments presents a compelling investment opportunity, especially given its strong dividend history and recent stock dip. The combination of reduced capital expenditures and increased production efficiency positions the company well for sustained growth in both share price and dividends.
