Constellation Brands, the maker of Modelo, has withdrawn its fiscal 2028 outlook amid signs of weaker demand as consumers adjust to a shifting macroeconomic landscape. The company reported a slight uptick in momentum during the fourth quarter across its beer, wine, and spirits segments, but noted ongoing uncertainty in the operating environment. Following the appointment of Nicholas Fink as CEO, effective April 13, Constellation acknowledged that visibility remains limited in the near term.

Despite the cautious outlook, Constellation beat Wall Street expectations for both fourth-quarter and full fiscal-year results. Adjusted earnings per share came in at $1.90, surpassing the $1.72 forecast, while revenue reached $1.92 billion, exceeding estimates of $1.88 billion. However, the company anticipates adjusted EPS for fiscal 2027 to fall short of prior expectations, reflecting a 3% decline in net sales for fiscal 2026 and a more deliberate consumer spending behavior.

Market professionals should note the potential implications of Constellation's revised outlook and subdued demand trends, as these factors may influence investor sentiment and stock performance in the consumer staples sector, particularly within alcoholic beverages.
