President Donald Trump announced a two-week suspension of planned military attacks on Iranian infrastructure, contingent upon Iran's agreement to fully open the Strait of Hormuz. This decision followed urgent discussions with Pakistani leaders and was made just before a self-imposed deadline that had caused significant market anxiety. The announcement led to a sharp decline in oil prices, dropping as much as 16%, while U.S. stock futures rallied.

This development underscores the volatility of geopolitical tensions on financial markets, particularly in the energy sector. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil supply, and any instability in the region can have immediate repercussions on oil prices and related equities. The market's reaction reflects a temporary easing of fears surrounding potential military conflict, which had been weighing heavily on investor sentiment.

Investors should closely monitor the situation as negotiations unfold, as any further developments could significantly impact oil markets and broader market stability.
