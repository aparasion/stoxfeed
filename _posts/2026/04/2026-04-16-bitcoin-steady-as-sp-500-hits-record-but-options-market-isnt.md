Bitcoin remains steady near $75,000 as the S&P 500 hits record highs, driven by optimism surrounding U.S.-Iran ceasefire negotiations. Despite the spot rally, the options market signals caution, with derivatives desks showing a preference for downside protection, highlighting a disconnect between equity performance and crypto sentiment.

While Bitcoin's recent gains reflect a positive market response, derivatives indicators such as negative funding rates and softening open interest suggest that traders are not fully convinced of a sustained upward trend. The muted volatility in options pricing further underscores this cautious outlook, as market participants hedge against potential declines rather than betting on continued upside. In contrast, Ethereum is outperforming Bitcoin, with the ETH/BTC ratio rebounding from multi-year lows, indicating a shift in investor interest.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the divergence between spot market optimism and derivatives caution. This suggests that while Bitcoin may experience short-term rallies, the underlying sentiment remains fragile, warranting close monitoring of macro developments and potential shifts in risk appetite.
