In a robust quarter, the company reported an adjusted FFO per share of $0.36, marking a 4% year-over-year increase, and raised its full-year guidance to $1.42–$1.44 per share, indicating a projected 6% growth. Same-property net operating income (NOI) rose 4.7% for the quarter and 5.4% year-to-date, with guidance for full-year growth now set at a midpoint of 5.25%. The company’s leasing activity remains strong, highlighted by a 61% spread on new leases, driven by anchor signings with national retailers like HomeGoods and Ross.

This performance underscores the resilience of retail real estate, particularly in supply-constrained markets like Boston and New York, where tenant demand is high. The competitive capital markets environment is leading to tighter spreads, prompting disciplined acquisition strategies. The company’s capital recycling efforts have also enhanced portfolio quality, with nearly $600 million in acquisitions at an average cap rate of 7%.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the sustained demand for well-located retail spaces, which is likely to support continued pricing power and occupancy levels. The improved FFO guidance and NOI growth reflect a positive outlook for the sector, suggesting that disciplined strategies in a competitive landscape can yield significant returns.
