Amazon (AMZN) shares surged 5.61% to close at $233.65 on Thursday, following CEO Andy Jassy's shareholder letter that outlined the company's ambitious plans for AI, AWS, and chip development. The letter highlighted a significant $15 billion revenue run rate from AI services within AWS, which bolstered investor confidence amid rising capital spending on AI infrastructure.

The uptick in Amazon's stock comes as trading volume reached 64.9 million shares, exceeding the three-month average by 30%. This rally stands in contrast to more modest gains from peers like Alibaba and MercadoLibre, indicating a strong market sentiment specifically toward Amazon's growth strategy. The broader market also saw positive movement, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite rising 0.61% and 0.83%, respectively.

For investors, Jassy’s assurances about the substantial customer commitments backing AWS's capital expenditures signal a promising outlook for future revenue growth, making Amazon a key player to watch in the evolving tech landscape.
