Gold prices remained stable on Friday, inching up by 0.1% to $4,795.14 an ounce, marking a potential fourth consecutive weekly gain. This uptick comes as oil prices softened amid rising optimism surrounding a possible resolution to the Iran conflict. U.S. gold futures also saw a slight increase of 0.2%, reflecting a broader market trend as the dollar faced its second weekly loss, indicating a growing appetite for riskier assets.

The geopolitical landscape is shifting, with U.S. President Donald Trump hinting at renewed negotiations this weekend. The recent ceasefire agreement between Israel and Lebanon has further fueled optimism for a long-term peace deal, contributing to a decline in oil prices by over 3% this week. Investors are closely monitoring these developments, particularly how they may influence inflation and market policy signals.

Market professionals should note that ongoing negotiations with Iran could lead to increased volatility in both gold and oil markets, making it essential to stay informed on geopolitical developments and their potential economic implications.
