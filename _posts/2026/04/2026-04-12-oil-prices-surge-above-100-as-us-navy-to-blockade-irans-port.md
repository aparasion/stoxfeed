Crude oil prices experienced a significant spike on Sunday, with U.S. crude futures for May delivery rising nearly 8% to $104.20 per barrel, while international benchmark Brent for June delivery increased by 7% to $101.86. This surge follows the U.S. Navy's announcement of a blockade on Iranian ports after peace talks failed, a move that could disrupt a critical maritime route for global oil supply.

The blockade, set to begin Monday, targets all vessels entering or leaving Iranian ports, which are vital for transporting oil from the Middle East to global markets. With approximately 20% of the world’s oil supply transiting through the Strait of Hormuz, the blockade could exacerbate existing supply disruptions, especially as tanker traffic has already decreased significantly due to security concerns. The geopolitical tensions surrounding Iran's nuclear ambitions further complicate the situation, with U.S. officials indicating potential military actions if negotiations fail.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in this situation, as ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz could lead to sustained volatility in oil prices and impact broader energy sector performance.
