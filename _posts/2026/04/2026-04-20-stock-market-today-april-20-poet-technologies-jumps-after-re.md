Poet Technologies (POET) saw a significant stock surge of 18.32%, closing at $8.59, following a detailed rebuttal to a short-seller report and clarifications regarding its PFIC tax status. The company’s trading volume soared to 44.6 million shares, over three times its average, as investors reacted positively to management's efforts to address governance and tax concerns that have previously dampened sentiment.

This development is particularly relevant as it comes amid a broader market decline, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite both slipping. The semiconductor sector, where POET operates, is experiencing notable dispersion, with stock-specific news driving performance. While POET's positioning in photonics for data center connectivity is gaining traction, the company still faces challenges, including limited revenue and reliance on external funding for its AI-focused growth initiatives.

Investors will be closely monitoring POET's ability to convert development partnerships into tangible revenue streams and customer orders, which will be crucial for sustaining momentum and justifying its elevated valuation.
