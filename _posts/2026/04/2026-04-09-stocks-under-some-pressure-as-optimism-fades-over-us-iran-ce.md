U.S. stock indexes are experiencing modest declines today, with the S&P 500 down 0.04%, the Dow down 0.23%, and the Nasdaq down 0.18%. This pullback follows a strong rally on Wednesday, as market optimism regarding the U.S.-Iran ceasefire wanes amid rising geopolitical tensions and mixed economic data. Crude oil prices surged over 5% due to ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, further complicating the energy market landscape.

The weaker-than-expected economic indicators, including a downward revision of Q4 GDP to 0.5% and a rise in initial jobless claims to an eight-week high, are contributing to the bearish sentiment. Investors are now reassessing the likelihood of a Federal Reserve rate hike in the upcoming meeting, with current expectations reflecting only a 2% chance of a 25 basis point increase. This backdrop is influencing bond yields and market volatility.

A key takeaway for market professionals is the heightened sensitivity of equities to geopolitical developments and economic data, which could lead to increased volatility in the near term as investors navigate these uncertainties.
