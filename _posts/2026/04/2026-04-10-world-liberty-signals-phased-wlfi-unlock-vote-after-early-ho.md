World Liberty Financial, a decentralized finance (DeFi) platform linked to the Trump family, is set to propose a governance plan next week aimed at establishing a phased unlock schedule for WLFI tokens held by early retail purchasers. This proposal, which will invite community feedback before a formal vote, seeks to address the concerns of early investors whose tokens remain largely locked, with only 24.67% of the total 100 billion WLFI tokens currently released.

The implications of this governance proposal are significant for the financial markets, particularly for retail investors who have expressed frustration over prolonged lockups and the limited transferability of their tokens. The proposed structured vesting plan could enhance liquidity for early buyers, potentially impacting WLFI’s market dynamics as it transitions from a governance-only utility to a more accessible asset. This comes amid rising tensions, with some investors threatening legal action against the platform due to dissatisfaction with the current token restrictions.

Market professionals should monitor the outcome of this governance proposal closely, as it could set a precedent for token liquidity and investor rights in the DeFi space, influencing both market sentiment and regulatory scrutiny in the sector.
