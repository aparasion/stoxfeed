The ongoing conflict in Iran has escalated tensions in global oil markets, with the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) effectively halting traffic through the critical Strait of Hormuz. This disruption, which impacts 20% of the world's oil supply, has driven crude prices above $100 per barrel, exacerbating inflationary pressures in the U.S. economy and increasing operational costs for businesses.

As the reliance on foreign oil becomes increasingly problematic, the case for investing in renewable energy stocks strengthens. The geopolitical landscape is shifting, with renewable energy now viewed as a strategic asset for energy security, rather than solely an environmental concern. With renewables comprising only 9% of total U.S. energy production in 2024, there remains significant growth potential in this sector.

Market professionals should consider the renewed focus on renewable energy as a viable investment opportunity. ETFs like the iShares Global Clean Energy ETF and Invesco Solar ETF have shown impressive gains over the past year, signaling a shift in investor sentiment towards sustainable energy solutions amid geopolitical uncertainties.
