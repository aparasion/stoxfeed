Kaiser Aluminum Corp (NASDAQ: KALU) is positioning itself in the semi-fabricated aluminum market, primarily serving industrial clients in the aerospace and automotive sectors. The company hedges aluminum costs to mitigate price volatility, ensuring stable margins as it processes raw materials into specialized products. With nearly all revenue generated in the U.S. and operations also in Canada, Kaiser is a key player in the North American aluminum landscape.

The aluminum sector is experiencing notable growth, driven by demand across various industries, including aerospace, automotive, and construction. Companies like Howmet Aerospace (NYSE: HWM) and Century Aluminum (NASDAQ: CENX) are also capitalizing on this trend, with Howmet focusing on high-value aerospace components and Century securing significant contracts with major buyers like Glencore. This expanding market is projected to see the aluminum alloy segment grow by $150 billion by 2024, highlighting the material's importance in modern manufacturing.

For investors, diversifying into aluminum stocks could be a strategic move, given the sector's resilience and growth potential. Monitoring earnings reports and market trends in construction and transportation will be crucial for assessing future performance in this space.
