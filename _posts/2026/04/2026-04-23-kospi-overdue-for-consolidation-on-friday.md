The South Korean stock market continues its upward momentum, with the KOSPI climbing for four consecutive sessions to reach a record close of 6,475.81, marking a 4.4% increase. Despite this rally, investors may be poised to lock in profits as global market sentiment weakens, particularly due to rising crude oil prices amid escalating tensions in the Middle East.

Sector performance on Thursday was mixed, with financial stocks like Shinhan Financial and Samsung Electronics showing notable gains, while automotive giants such as Hyundai Motor and Kia Motors faced declines. The broader market was influenced by a weak lead from U.S. indices, which fell due to profit-taking and concerns over geopolitical risks, particularly regarding the Strait of Hormuz and its impact on oil supplies.

Market professionals should monitor the KOSPI's performance closely, as potential profit-taking could lead to volatility. Additionally, the interplay between rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions may create ripple effects across Asian markets, impacting investment strategies in the region.
