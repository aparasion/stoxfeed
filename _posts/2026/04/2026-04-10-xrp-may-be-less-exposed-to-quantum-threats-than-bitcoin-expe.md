XRP is emerging as a more quantum-resistant cryptocurrency compared to Bitcoin, according to experts. The design of XRP minimizes the exposure of its supply to potential quantum attacks, primarily due to its unique features such as key rotation and escrow time-locks, which Bitcoin lacks. A recent audit of the XRP Ledger revealed that around 300,000 accounts holding 2.4 billion XRP have never sent transactions, thus keeping their public keys hidden and safe from quantum threats.

This distinction is crucial for market participants as quantum computing poses a theoretical risk to legacy blockchains. While Bitcoin's vulnerability could affect between 2.3 million to 7.8 million BTC—representing up to 37% of its circulating supply—XRP's architecture significantly mitigates this risk. The presence of dormant accounts in both networks highlights the importance of proactive measures in safeguarding assets against future quantum capabilities.

For investors, the key takeaway is that XRP's built-in defenses may offer a more secure alternative in a rapidly evolving technological landscape, making it an attractive option for those concerned about quantum vulnerabilities in their crypto holdings.
