Indian shares rebounded sharply on Monday, with the benchmark BSE Sensex gaining 787.30 points (1.07%) to close at 74,106.85, following reports of indirect negotiations between the U.S. and Iran for a potential 45-day truce. This diplomatic development comes amid heightened tensions, as President Trump threatened military action against Iran if critical waterways are not opened. The market also benefited from a strong rally in the Indian rupee, which appreciated significantly after the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) measures against currency speculation.

The positive sentiment was further supported by a robust performance in mid-cap and small-cap stocks, with the BSE mid-cap and small-cap indexes rising 1.3% and 1.1%, respectively. Notable gainers included Trent, HDFC Bank, and Axis Bank, which surged between 3-8%. Despite a slowdown in services activity, investors appeared unfazed, focusing instead on the potential for geopolitical stability and the RBI's upcoming monetary policy decisions.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the resilience of Indian equities amid geopolitical uncertainties, suggesting that investor sentiment remains buoyed by potential diplomatic resolutions and domestic monetary policy stability.
