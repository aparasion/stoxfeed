The ongoing conflict in Iran and the subsequent constriction of the Strait of Hormuz have heightened fears of long-term energy disruptions, leading to significant volatility in oil and gas prices. Amid this turmoil, Wall Street analysts are increasingly bullish on Venture Global (VG), a prominent U.S. liquefied natural gas (LNG) exporter, which has seen its stock price more than double in 2026, rising 51% since the war's onset. With LNG being a critical global commodity, Venture Global is well-positioned to capitalize on soaring prices and supply shortages.

Analysts from major firms like JPMorgan Chase and Morgan Stanley have raised their price targets for Venture Global, citing its strong market position and exposure to rising LNG prices. The company recently settled a dispute that allows it to supply more LNG to Europe and completed an $8.6 billion development deal, further solidifying its ambitions to become the largest U.S. LNG exporter.

For market professionals, Venture Global represents a compelling opportunity for growth, albeit with higher risk. As LNG demand remains stable and supply tightens, investors with a risk appetite may find potential rewards in this volatile sector.
