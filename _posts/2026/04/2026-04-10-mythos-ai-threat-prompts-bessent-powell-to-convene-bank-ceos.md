U.S. regulators and top Wall Street bank executives convened urgently to address cybersecurity threats posed by Anthropic’s AI model, Mythos. This advanced AI can rapidly identify software vulnerabilities and craft sophisticated exploits, raising alarms about systemic risks within the banking sector. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell led discussions with CEOs from major banks, including Citigroup, Morgan Stanley, and Goldman Sachs, all of which are deemed systemically important.

The implications for financial markets are significant. The ability of Mythos to uncover and weaponize zero-day vulnerabilities has already raised concerns among crypto and DeFi experts, highlighting a shift in how regulators perceive AI risks—not merely as technological challenges but as potential triggers for systemic disruptions. This growing awareness may lead to increased regulatory scrutiny and investment in cybersecurity measures across the financial sector.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the urgent need to reassess risk management strategies in light of AI advancements, particularly in cybersecurity, as these technologies evolve and become more integrated into financial systems.
