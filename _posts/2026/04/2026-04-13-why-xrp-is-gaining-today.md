XRP is experiencing a notable uptick in Monday's trading, with its price rising 1.3% as of 4:40 p.m. ET, while Bitcoin and Ethereum also saw gains of 2.9% and 2.3%, respectively. This increase comes amid heightened macroeconomic and geopolitical tensions due to the ongoing conflict in Iran, particularly following President Trump's announcement regarding U.S. actions in the Strait of Hormuz. Investors are interpreting the situation with cautious optimism, especially after reports of potential negotiations between the U.S. and Iran.

The broader market is reacting positively, with both stocks and cryptocurrencies rebounding after an initial dip triggered by geopolitical news. This correlation suggests that XRP's valuation may be influenced by macroeconomic factors, particularly the Federal Reserve's interest rate policies. If the conflict leads to inflationary pressures, the Fed could raise rates, negatively impacting XRP. Conversely, any developments that favor a path toward rate cuts could bolster its price.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor geopolitical developments closely, as they could have significant implications for XRP's valuation and the broader cryptocurrency landscape, particularly in relation to interest rate movements.
