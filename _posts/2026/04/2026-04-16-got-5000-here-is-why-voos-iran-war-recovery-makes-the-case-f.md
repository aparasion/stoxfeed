The Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO) has experienced significant volatility over the past two months, dropping nearly 9% after reaching an all-time high in early February, before rebounding to new highs in just three weeks. This fluctuation coincided with troubling economic indicators, including a projected increase in the annualized inflation rate from 2.4% in February to 3.6% in April, and a sharp rise in Brent crude oil prices to around $100 per barrel. Additionally, consumer sentiment hit a historic low, reflecting growing unease among investors.

This market behavior underscores the importance of maintaining a long-term investment strategy, particularly during geopolitical-driven downturns. Historical data shows that the S&P 500 typically recovers from such declines within two months, and this year's rapid rebound supports the argument against market timing. Notably, the Vanguard ETF saw a net outflow of $11 billion in March, suggesting that many long-term investors missed out on the subsequent gains.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is clear: short-term volatility can be misleading, and sticking to a long-term investment strategy is crucial for capturing market recoveries. As the S&P 500 continues to demonstrate resilience, it remains a strong vehicle for wealth creation amidst uncertainty.
