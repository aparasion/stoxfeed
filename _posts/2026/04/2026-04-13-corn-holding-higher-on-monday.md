Corn futures are experiencing modest gains, with prices rising by 2 to 3.5 cents at midday on Monday, reflecting a national average cash corn price of $4.05 1/4. This uptick comes amid fluctuating crude oil prices, which increased by $3.03 but remain over $5 below recent highs following the collapse of U.S.-Iran negotiations. Analysts are anticipating an update on crop progress later today, with expectations that 6% of the U.S. corn crop was planted as of Sunday.

In the broader context, export inspections data revealed a 13.15% decline in wheat shipments week-over-week, totaling 1.782 million metric tons. Despite this drop, year-to-date shipments are up 33.9% compared to last year, indicating strong demand, particularly from Mexico, Japan, and South Korea. Additionally, the Commitment of Traders report showed a reduction in managed money net long positions, suggesting a shift in market sentiment.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential volatility in agricultural commodities as planting progresses and export dynamics shift, particularly with upcoming USDA data expected to influence trading strategies.
