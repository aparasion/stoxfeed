Lamb Weston Holdings reported a 3% increase in net sales to $8 billion for the third quarter, buoyed by a 7% rise in volume, primarily driven by a robust 12% growth in North America. However, profitability faced challenges, with adjusted EBITDA declining by $101 million to $272 million due to an unfavorable price mix and a $33 million write-off of excess raw potatoes in the International segment. Despite these headwinds, the company achieved $100 million in cost savings ahead of schedule and raised its fiscal 2026 guidance for net sales and adjusted EBITDA.

The performance highlights a stark contrast between the North American and International segments, with the former benefiting from strong customer retention and new acquisitions, while the latter struggled with lower demand and market saturation. The ongoing focus on cost efficiency and strategic operational adjustments, including plant closures and production consolidations, positions Lamb Weston to navigate these challenges effectively.

Market professionals should note the potential for improved margins as tariff relief on imported palm oil is expected to enhance profitability in the upcoming quarter, alongside a favorable outlook for North American potato supply contracts.
