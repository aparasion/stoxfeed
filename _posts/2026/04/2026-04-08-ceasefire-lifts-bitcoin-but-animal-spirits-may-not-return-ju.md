Bitcoin surged to $71,600 following a temporary ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran, marking a significant technical breakthrough as it eyes the $76,100 level. This geopolitical development has also led to a sharp 16% drop in oil prices, easing inflation concerns and diminishing the likelihood of immediate Federal Reserve rate hikes, which typically supports risk assets like cryptocurrencies. Additionally, Morgan Stanley's debut of its bitcoin ETF today further bolsters institutional interest in the crypto space.

The easing of geopolitical tensions has reignited market confidence, but caution remains warranted. The recent rally was partly driven by the unwinding of short positions, with $431 million liquidated in a single day. While the market shows signs of bullish momentum, analysts warn that without sustained demand, gains could be short-lived. 

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving dynamics in oil prices and geopolitical developments, as these factors could significantly influence crypto market stability and performance in the coming weeks.
