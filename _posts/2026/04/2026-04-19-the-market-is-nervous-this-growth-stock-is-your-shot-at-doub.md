Equity investors are grappling with heightened concerns as the Iran war escalates and oil prices surge, potentially squeezing consumer discretionary spending amid already high inflation. Despite a modest recovery, with the S&P 500 up 1.7% year-to-date, the market’s tepid performance presents an opportunity for aggressive purchases in growth stocks, which have lagged behind.

Chewy (CHWY) stands out as a compelling investment amidst these challenges. The company reported an 8.3% revenue growth, driven by a 4% increase in active customers and a significant rise in its Autoship program, which now accounts for 83.3% of sales. Chewy's expansion into healthcare services and its recent acquisition of Modern Animal, expected to add $125 million in annual sales, further bolster its long-term growth prospects.

For market professionals, Chewy’s current valuation presents a buying opportunity, especially given the resilience of pet spending during economic downturns. With a P/E ratio of 52, while still high, it reflects a strong growth narrative in a non-cyclical sector, making it an attractive option for those willing to navigate short-term volatility.
