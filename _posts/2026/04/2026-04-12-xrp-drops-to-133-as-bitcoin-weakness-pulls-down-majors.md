XRP experienced a significant drop to $1.33, driven by a sudden selloff that coincided with broader weakness in Bitcoin and other major cryptocurrencies. The rapid decline from $1.36 to $1.33, occurring on heavy trading volume, indicates aggressive selling rather than a mere liquidity issue. This breakdown has pushed XRP below the critical $1.35 support level, which has now turned into resistance, alongside the $1.40 to $1.41 zone that has repeatedly capped any recovery attempts.

The implications for the market are notable, as the weak recovery following the drop suggests that sellers maintain control, reinforcing a bearish trend. Analysts are divided on the outlook; while some anticipate further declines, others speculate on a potential recovery cycle. The current trading environment, characterized by high volume during the selloff and fading volume during the bounce, points to distribution rather than accumulation.

Traders should closely monitor the $1.35 resistance level; a failure to reclaim this could lead XRP toward the next demand zone at $1.32-$1.31. Conversely, a successful recovery above this level may signal a shift in momentum.
