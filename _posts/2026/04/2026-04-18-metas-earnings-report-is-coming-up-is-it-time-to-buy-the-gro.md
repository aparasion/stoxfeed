Meta Platforms (META) is set to release its first-quarter earnings for 2026 on April 29, and investor interest is piqued as the stock has rebounded sharply following earlier declines. The company has reported impressive revenue growth, with fourth-quarter sales up 24% year over year to $59.9 billion, yet profits have lagged, rising only 9%. This disparity highlights the impact of Meta's aggressive spending on artificial intelligence (AI), with capital expenditures projected to soar to between $115 billion and $135 billion in 2026.

The significant investment in AI infrastructure is a double-edged sword; while it positions Meta for future growth, it also places pressure on current profitability. CEO Mark Zuckerberg has emphasized the importance of these investments, indicating that 2026 will see a major acceleration in AI initiatives. However, management's cautious guidance suggests that earnings growth may remain constrained as these expenditures ramp up.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Meta's stock may appear attractive for long-term investors, particularly given its substantial user base and cash reserves, the increased risk profile warrants careful monitoring of the upcoming earnings report to assess the balance between revenue growth and profitability amid heavy spending.
