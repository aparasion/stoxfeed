Coffee futures surged on Wednesday, with May arabica coffee rising 4.64% and May robusta coffee increasing 2.37%, driven by geopolitical tensions affecting supply chains. The potential closure of the Strait of Hormuz due to ongoing US-Iran conflict has raised concerns about global coffee supply disruptions, leading to short covering in the futures market. Additionally, a significant drop in ICE robusta inventories to a 16-month low has further bolstered prices.

Despite the bullish sentiment in the near term, the long-term outlook remains complex. Projections indicate a record Brazilian coffee crop for 2026/27, with estimates reaching up to 75.9 million bags, which could lead to a global coffee surplus expanding to 10 million bags. In contrast, robusta coffee exports from Vietnam are also on the rise, complicating the price dynamics for robusta.

Market participants should remain vigilant about the interplay between geopolitical risks and production forecasts, as these factors will heavily influence pricing trends in the coffee market moving forward.
