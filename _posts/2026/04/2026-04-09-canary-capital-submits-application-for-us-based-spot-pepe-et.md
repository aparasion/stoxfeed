Canary Capital has filed with the SEC to launch the CANARY PEPE ETF, a spot exchange-traded fund designed to track the performance of the PEPE memecoin. This ETF will hold PEPE tokens, with a provision to allocate up to 5% of its assets in Ether (ETH) for transaction fees. Canary Capital, which has previously introduced several crypto ETFs, is venturing into the niche memecoin market, despite PEPE's significant decline from its all-time high.

The proposed ETF arrives amid a broader discussion about the future of crypto ETFs and their potential to drive the next altcoin cycle. While some analysts suggest that institutional investors are shifting focus towards yield-bearing digital assets, others believe that the success of new ETF filings could reignite interest in altcoins. Notably, PEPE's ownership is highly concentrated, with the top ten wallets holding about 41% of the total supply, raising concerns about market stability.

The launch of the CANARY PEPE ETF could signal a pivotal moment for memecoins in the ETF space, potentially impacting market dynamics as regulatory clarity evolves. As the landscape for crypto ETFs develops, professionals should monitor how this product performs and its implications for investor sentiment in the altcoin market.
