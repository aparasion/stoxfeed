Spirit Airlines is facing an urgent liquidity crisis, with its available cash set to run out within days, according to a lawyer representing the airline at a bankruptcy court hearing. Marshall Huebner emphasized the need for immediate access to either new financing or nearly $240 million in restricted cash to sustain operations. The airline is currently in advanced discussions with the Trump administration for a potential $500 million loan that could result in the government acquiring a 90% stake.

This development is critical for the airline sector, especially as Spirit grapples with rising fuel costs and ongoing operational challenges. The company had initially aimed to emerge from bankruptcy by midyear, but the recent surge in fuel prices has complicated its recovery efforts. With significant cash locked away under bankruptcy terms, the airline's future hinges on securing this financing, which could also position it as a key player in potential consolidation within the budget airline market.

Market professionals should monitor Spirit's negotiations closely, as the outcome could influence broader trends in airline valuations and the competitive landscape among low-cost carriers.
