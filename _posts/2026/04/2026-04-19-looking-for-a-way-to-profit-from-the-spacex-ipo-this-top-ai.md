SpaceX is poised to make headlines with what could be the largest IPO in history, targeting a staggering $75 billion at a $2 trillion valuation. This valuation would position SpaceX among the top five companies in the S&P 500, surpassing giants like Nvidia and Apple. The funds raised are expected to fuel the expansion of its Starlink satellite network and support rocket development and AI initiatives. The IPO could occur as early as June.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly for Alphabet (GOOGL), which holds a 5% stake in SpaceX, valued at approximately $100 billion if the IPO meets its targets. This stake, along with Alphabet's continued dominance in search and booming cloud business, makes GOOGL an attractive investment. The company reported over $40 billion in operating income last quarter, underscoring its robust financial health.

Investors should consider the potential upside of Alphabet shares, particularly in light of its exposure to SpaceX's anticipated growth and the ongoing strength of its core and cloud businesses.
