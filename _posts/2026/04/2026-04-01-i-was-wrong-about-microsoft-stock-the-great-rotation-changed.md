Microsoft (MSFT) has seen a dramatic nearly 30% decline in its share price over the past six months, marking its steepest drop since the 2008 financial crisis. Despite strong earnings performance, the market's reassessment of the company's aggressive AI investment strategy has overshadowed its growth narrative. Investors had previously priced in high-margin returns from AI, but the reality of significant capital expenditures—projected at $146 billion for fiscal 2026—has led to a shift in sentiment.

This sell-off reflects a broader trend where capital is moving away from mega-cap tech stocks toward more cyclical sectors, as investors prioritize immediate cash flows over long-term growth potential. However, Microsoft's core business remains robust, with continued growth in its Azure cloud services and sustained enterprise demand. 

For market professionals, this presents an opportunity. With Microsoft's valuation now more reasonable, it may be an opportune time to consider entry points for long-term investment, especially if the company's substantial AI investments begin to yield returns.
