Defense contractors Textron (NYSE: TXT), Huntington Ingalls (NYSE: HII), and Rocket Lab (NASDAQ: RKLB) have all seen impressive stock performance this year, with gains of at least 38%. The ongoing geopolitical tensions, particularly the 2026 conflict in Iran, have heightened demand for defense capabilities, benefiting these companies as they navigate substantial backlogs tied to long-term military and commercial contracts.

Textron, with a diverse portfolio including military aircraft and corporate jets, reported a revenue increase of 8% in 2025, supported by an $18.8 billion backlog. Huntington Ingalls, the largest U.S. shipbuilder, boasts a staggering $53.1 billion backlog, driven by the need for naval modernization. Rocket Lab, while primarily focused on space, also has defense contracts and a backlog of $1.85 billion, enhancing its growth trajectory.

The key takeaway for market professionals is that these companies are positioned for sustained growth beyond immediate geopolitical concerns, making them compelling investment opportunities as they capitalize on structural demand in the defense sector.
