WSFS Financial Corporation reported strong first-quarter results for 2026, with core earnings per share (EPS) rising 49% year-over-year to $1.68, driven by a significant loan recovery of $15.7 million. Core return on assets (ROA) improved to 1.65%, while net income grew 35% year-over-year, supported by a 10% increase in core pre-provision net revenue. The bank's tangible book value per share also saw a 15% increase, reflecting robust operational performance despite challenges in the real estate sector.

The results highlight a shift in WSFS's deposit composition, with noninterest deposits increasing to 34% of total deposits, up from 29% a year ago. Wealth & Trust and Institutional Services segments exhibited notable growth, with fee revenues rising 11% year-over-year. However, management cautioned about increased competition for deposits and potential constraints on future loan growth due to a high maturity pipeline in commercial real estate.

A key takeaway for market professionals is WSFS's commitment to capital return, having repurchased 12% of its outstanding shares since early 2025 and increasing its quarterly dividend by 18%. This strategic focus on shareholder value, alongside strong earnings growth, positions WSFS favorably in a competitive banking landscape.
