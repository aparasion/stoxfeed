President Donald Trump announced that U.S. military forces will remain deployed in and around Iran until Tehran fully complies with a "real agreement," warning of a significant military response if any breaches occur. This statement follows a two-week ceasefire brokered by Pakistan, which briefly lifted market sentiment amid hopes for resumed energy flows through the critical Strait of Hormuz. However, the ceasefire's terms remain contentious, with Iran rejecting U.S. proposals and escalating tensions with Israel complicating the situation further.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial, particularly in the energy sector. Oil prices surged on Thursday, with Brent crude rising 2.46% and West Texas Intermediate climbing 3.4%, reflecting concerns over ongoing hostilities and the potential for supply disruptions. As geopolitical risks mount, traders should brace for volatility in energy markets, which could impact broader market sentiment and economic forecasts.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in U.S.-Iran negotiations and regional tensions, as any escalation could lead to further spikes in oil prices and affect global economic stability.
