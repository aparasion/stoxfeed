Community Bancorp's President Christopher L. Caldwell made a notable move by acquiring 1,000 shares of CMTV at $35.30 each, totaling $35,299. This purchase marks Caldwell's fourth investment in CMTV over the past year, bringing his total investment in the stock to $77,266 at an average price of $25.76 per share. Currently, CMTV is trading flat, but Caldwell is already seeing a 1.7% gain based on intraday highs.

In a separate transaction, CEO Robert E. Dixon invested $34,972 in Mackenzie Realty Capital (MKZR), buying 10,000 shares at $3.50 each. Dixon's cumulative investment in MKZR over the past year has reached $81.48 million, with an average purchase price of $723.13 per share. MKZR is currently down about 4.1%, presenting a potential buying opportunity for investors at a price lower than Dixon's recent acquisition.

These insider purchases highlight confidence from company leadership, particularly in CMTV, while MKZR's dip could attract bargain hunters looking to capitalize on a lower entry point.
