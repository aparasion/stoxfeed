Bitcoin's ongoing consolidation below the $70,000 mark may signal a potential breakout, according to crypto analyst Michael van de Poppe. He suggests that the longer Bitcoin remains stagnant, the more substantial the eventual price movement could be, with a key resistance level at $71,000 that hasn't been breached since March. Currently trading at $66,890, Bitcoin has seen an 8.25% decline over the past month, oscillating between $60,000 and $74,000 since February.

This period of low volatility comes amidst a broader bearish sentiment in the crypto market, with the Crypto Fear & Greed Index reflecting "Extreme Fear" at a score of 11. While van de Poppe remains optimistic about a potential reversal, other analysts like Willy Woo caution against a deeper bear market, citing macroeconomic factors that could impact Bitcoin's trajectory.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor Bitcoin's price action closely, as a decisive move above $71,000 could signal renewed bullish momentum, while continued consolidation may indicate further volatility ahead.
