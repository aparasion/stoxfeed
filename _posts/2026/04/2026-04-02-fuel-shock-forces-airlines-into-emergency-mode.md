Jet fuel prices have surged dramatically due to escalating tensions from the Iran war, more than doubling in just a month. This spike has forced airlines to raise ticket prices and ground flights as they grapple with soaring operational costs. The conflict has severely disrupted oil and fuel supplies, particularly at the Strait of Hormuz, leading to constrained refinery outputs in Asia and a looming global jet fuel shortage.

The implications for the aviation sector are significant, as airlines face unprecedented fuel costs, which are their largest expense. Major carriers like Qantas and Korean Air have already responded by increasing fares and preparing for emergency measures. The International Air Transport Association warns that rapid fuel price hikes create shocks that are challenging for airlines to manage, potentially impacting profitability and operational capacity in the coming months.

Market professionals should closely monitor the jet fuel supply situation, as the current crisis is expected to worsen before any relief is seen. With airlines already adjusting strategies and grounding flights, the ripple effects on stock performance and sector stability could be profound, warranting strategic reassessments for stakeholders in the aviation and energy markets.
