Bitcoin experienced a pullback from a 12-week high of nearly $79,400, reversing gains during Asian trading. This marks the third unsuccessful attempt to breach the $79,000 level in just eight sessions, raising concerns among traders as selling pressure mounts. Analysts highlight that the $80,000 mark is critical for recent buyers, who may be looking to exit positions after substantial gains, contributing to the downward momentum despite Bitcoin's 16% rise in April.

The recent rally was initially spurred by geopolitical developments surrounding Iran and U.S. negotiations, which boosted broader market sentiment, including a 1.7% rise in the MSCI Asia Pacific Index. However, Bitcoin's inability to sustain its gains raises questions ahead of key monetary policy decisions from the Federal Reserve and European Central Bank, as well as upcoming earnings reports from major tech firms that could serve as catalysts for a breakout or further consolidation.

Market professionals should closely monitor the upcoming central bank decisions and tech earnings, as these events could either catalyze a breakout for Bitcoin or reinforce the current trading range, particularly given the negative funding rates on perpetual futures that suggest a potential squeeze if momentum shifts.
