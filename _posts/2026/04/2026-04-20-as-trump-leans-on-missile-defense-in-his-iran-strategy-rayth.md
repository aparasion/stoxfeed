Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz escalated dramatically over the weekend as Iran regained control of the vital waterway, firing on ships and raising concerns about shipping disruptions. This volatility has implications for the stock market, particularly for defense contractors like RTX (formerly Raytheon Technologies), which saw its shares decline by 2.55% last week despite broader market gains. Investors might assume that RTX's fortunes are tied to ongoing conflict, but the company is poised for growth regardless of the geopolitical landscape.

RTX is ramping up production of critical military equipment, including Tomahawk missiles, to meet increased demand from the U.S. government, which has already depleted significant portions of its arsenal. The company is set to double its production capacity, reflecting a broader trend of rearmament that aligns with U.S. military strategy. This positions RTX as a strong player in the defense sector, with potential for long-term growth as the government prioritizes military readiness.

For market professionals, RTX represents a compelling investment opportunity, particularly in light of increased government orders and a renewed focus on domestic manufacturing. As geopolitical tensions persist, the defense sector may see sustained demand, making RTX a noteworthy stock to watch.
