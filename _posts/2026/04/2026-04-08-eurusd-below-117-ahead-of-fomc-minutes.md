The upcoming release of the FOMC minutes is poised to reinforce the Committee's hawkish stance regarding interest rates, which were held steady at 3.50–3.75% during the March meeting. Despite Fed Chair Jerome Powell's moderate tone, the minutes are expected to reveal strong support for a "no rush to cut" approach, particularly in light of inflationary pressures stemming from rising oil prices due to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.

This development is crucial for financial markets as it signals a potential shift in monetary policy expectations. With inflation concerns heightened, particularly from the oil market, the likelihood of interest rate cuts has diminished, and some FOMC members are even hinting at the possibility of future hikes if inflation persists. The market's reaction to the dollar and currency pairs like EUR/USD will be closely tied to the nuances revealed in these minutes, especially regarding the Fed's inflation outlook.

Market professionals should prepare for the potential implications of a hawkish tone in the minutes, which could lead to a stronger dollar and recalibrated expectations for future rate adjustments. The balance of risks outlined in the minutes will be critical in shaping market sentiment moving forward.
