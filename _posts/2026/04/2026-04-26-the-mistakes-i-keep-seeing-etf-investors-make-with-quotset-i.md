The recent rally of the "Magnificent Seven" tech stocks has led to increased concentration in tech-heavy ETFs, particularly the Invesco QQQ Trust (NASDAQ: QQQ), which now has 44% of its index weighted in just seven stocks. This overconcentration raises potential downside risks, especially as these stocks begin to trade below their all-time highs, highlighting the dangers of relying heavily on a few high-performing stocks.

For investors, this scenario underscores the importance of diversification and regular portfolio rebalancing. Many investors mistakenly believe that simply owning multiple funds equates to diversification, but overlapping holdings can lead to similar performance and increased risk. Without periodic rebalancing, portfolios can drift from their intended asset allocation, potentially exposing investors to greater volatility.

The key takeaway is to critically assess your portfolio's composition and ensure it aligns with your investment goals. Regularly reviewing and rebalancing your holdings can mitigate risks associated with overconcentration and help maintain a balanced approach to investing.
