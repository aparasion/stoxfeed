Markets are experiencing uncertainty as traders await a critical deadline set by Donald Trump regarding Iran's access to the Strait of Hormuz, due tonight at 2000 ET. Although Brent crude oil prices peaked at $111 a barrel earlier in the day, they have since retreated amid skepticism that Iran will comply with U.S. demands. The market is bracing for two potential scenarios: continued U.S. military action, which could elevate oil prices and dampen risk sentiment, or an extension of the deadline, which might alleviate pressure on oil prices and improve market confidence.

In the oil market, WTI is trading at an unusual premium to Brent, currently at $114.70 compared to Brent's $111.60. This backwardation suggests immediate supply concerns, with traders anticipating lower future prices. The outlook for oil prices hinges on geopolitical developments, particularly the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, which could lead to a sharp decline in WTI prices.

As earnings season kicks off, with Delta Airlines set to report tomorrow, the interplay between geopolitical tensions and corporate performance will be crucial. Investors are also eyeing M&A activity, such as Unilever's strategic focus on health and wellness, indicating that even in turbulent times, opportunities for growth and consolidation exist.
