The decision of when to start collecting Social Security is crucial for retirees, impacting their overall financial health. The earliest age to begin receiving benefits is 62, but doing so results in a permanent reduction in monthly payments compared to waiting until full retirement age, which varies between 65 and 67. Delaying benefits until age 70 can maximize monthly payouts, but the increases cease after that age.

This decision carries significant implications for retirees' financial strategies, especially as Social Security serves as a foundational income source. Factors such as personal savings, job satisfaction, health issues, and age can influence the timing of benefits. For instance, those with substantial savings or facing burnout may benefit from early collection, while others may opt to delay for higher payments.

Ultimately, understanding the nuances of Social Security can empower retirees to make informed decisions that align with their financial goals, potentially allowing for earlier retirement or a more comfortable lifestyle.
