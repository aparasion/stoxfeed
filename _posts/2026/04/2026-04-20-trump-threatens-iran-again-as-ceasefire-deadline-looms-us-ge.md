President Trump has intensified his rhetoric against Iran, threatening "overwhelming military force" if a deal is not reached before the expiration of a fragile ceasefire on Tuesday. His comments, made during phone interviews, signal a sharp escalation in tensions, with Trump warning of potential bombings targeting Iranian infrastructure if negotiations fail. This comes as U.S. officials prepare for a second round of peace talks in Pakistan, although the status of Iran's participation remains uncertain.

The renewed threats from the U.S. could have significant implications for global markets, particularly in the energy sector. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical passage for oil shipments, has already been a flashpoint, with U.S. Navy actions against Iranian vessels heightening concerns over supply disruptions. Any military escalation could lead to increased oil prices and volatility in energy stocks, impacting broader market sentiment.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as the outcome of these negotiations could influence not only oil prices but also overall market stability in the face of geopolitical risks.
