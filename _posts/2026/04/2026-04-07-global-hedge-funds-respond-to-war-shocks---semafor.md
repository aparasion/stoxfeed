Donald Trump announced a two-week suspension of attacks on Iran, contingent upon Iran reopening the Strait of Hormuz. This development follows recent US and Israeli strikes targeting Iran's critical infrastructure, including railroads and energy hubs, which are vital to its economy.

The ongoing conflict is prompting hedge funds to adjust their trading strategies, with a notable shift towards shorting European stocks while adopting a bullish stance on wheat. Additionally, airlines are feeling the pinch as jet fuel prices soar, leading to increased flight cancellations and fare hikes, which could impact travel-related sectors.

Market professionals should monitor these geopolitical tensions closely, as they may influence commodity prices and stock performance across various sectors, particularly in energy and transportation. The evolving situation in Iran could lead to further volatility in global markets, making strategic positioning essential for portfolio management.
