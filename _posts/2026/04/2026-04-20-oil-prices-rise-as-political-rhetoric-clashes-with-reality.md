Oil prices surged this week, with OIL rising 3.50% to $90 and WTI gaining 4.50% to $87, following renewed tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. The escalation was triggered by attacks on commercial vessels and Iran's announcement of a renewed closure of the strait, coupled with the seizure of an Iranian-flagged vessel by US forces. This situation has raised significant concerns over the stability of oil supply routes and has led to a reevaluation of geopolitical risks in the region.

The market's reaction reflects a growing apprehension about sustained disruptions to energy flows, especially as the current US-Iran ceasefire is set to expire soon, with Tehran indicating it may not participate in upcoming negotiations. The reduced vessel traffic through Hormuz is already impacting global energy supply chains, prompting some Gulf states, including the UAE, to consider financial support and alternative trading measures to mitigate risks.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the oil market remains tight, with prices reflecting both current supply constraints and the potential for prolonged geopolitical instability. Investors should closely monitor developments in the region, as the dynamics in the Strait of Hormuz could significantly influence oil price trajectories and overall market sentiment.
