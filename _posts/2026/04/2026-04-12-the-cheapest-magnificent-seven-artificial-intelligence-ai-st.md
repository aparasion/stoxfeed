Mark Zuckerberg's Meta Platforms (NASDAQ: META) is facing renewed investor skepticism, despite its stock price tripling after a pivot to artificial intelligence. Once celebrated for its ambitious vision, Meta is now seen as reckless following a $135 billion infrastructure commitment amid a potentially recessionary environment. This shift in sentiment has led to a significant sell-off, with the stock down as much as 20% this year, as investors worry about the impact of economic uncertainty on its advertising revenue, particularly from small and medium-sized businesses.

The market's reaction conflates two distinct risks: potential near-term advertising softness and long-term capital misallocation. Despite these fears, Meta's competitive advantages—three ecosystems with over a billion users each—remain intact, and its advancements in AI-powered advertising suggest significant growth potential. Currently, Meta trades at a lower forward P/E than its peers in the "Magnificent Seven," indicating a mispricing of its earnings power.

For market professionals, this sell-off presents a compelling buying opportunity. The emotional market response to Meta's strategic decisions may overlook the company's robust fundamentals, making it an attractive entry point before broader market clarity emerges.
