Bearish sentiment surrounding Bitcoin has surged to its highest level since late February, according to data from crypto sentiment platform Santiment. The ratio of bullish to bearish comments on social media platforms has dropped to 0.81, reflecting a growing pessimism among investors. Santiment noted that such negative sentiment often precedes price rebounds, suggesting that the market could move contrary to prevailing expectations.

Currently trading at $67,100, Bitcoin has declined by 5.53% over the past month, with many traders looking to market sentiment as a guide for their decisions. The ongoing uncertainty surrounding the US CLARITY Act, which is anticipated to influence regulatory frameworks for cryptocurrencies, is also contributing to this cautious outlook. With the Crypto Fear & Greed Index remaining in "Extreme Fear" territory, investors are adopting a wait-and-see approach.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that heightened bearish sentiment may signal a potential buying opportunity, as historical trends indicate that markets frequently rebound when pessimism peaks.
