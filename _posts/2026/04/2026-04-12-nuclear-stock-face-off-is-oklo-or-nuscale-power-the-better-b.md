Elon Musk’s SpaceX has applied to launch up to a million data centers into orbit, a groundbreaking initiative that could significantly impact the energy sector and the burgeoning AI landscape. This ambitious plan aims to leverage the advantages of space—such as reduced cooling costs and abundant solar energy—to support the global demand for data center infrastructure, which is rapidly expanding due to the AI revolution.

As the need for energy to power these data centers surges, analysts are increasingly optimistic about a nuclear energy renaissance, with Bank of America estimating a $10 trillion opportunity in this space. Companies like Oklo and NuScale Power, both focused on small modular reactor technology, are poised to benefit. However, Oklo's strategy of developing smaller, more adaptable systems aligns better with the needs of data centers, positioning it as a stronger investment choice for those looking to capitalize on the AI data center boom.

For market professionals, Oklo emerges as a compelling option, particularly given its partnership with Meta Platforms and its targeted approach to meet the energy demands of the AI sector.
