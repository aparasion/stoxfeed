Apple (AAPL) is facing scrutiny over its perceived slow rollout of artificial intelligence (AI) features, with investors worried that the tech giant is lagging behind competitors like Meta Platforms, which plans to invest up to $135 billion in AI. However, Apple’s conservative approach—projecting just $14 billion in capital expenditures—may ultimately position it for long-term success. By avoiding aggressive spending, Apple can selectively invest in AI opportunities as they arise, focusing on safety and privacy while maintaining its vast ecosystem.

This strategy could mitigate risks associated with rushed AI development, as seen in the DeepSeek AI controversy. While Apple may not be first to market with cutting-edge AI, its methodical approach could allow it to catch up without compromising its brand integrity. 

For investors, this suggests that Apple remains a solid long-term holding. The company’s ability to prioritize strategic investments over rapid deployment could enhance its competitive edge as the AI landscape evolves.
