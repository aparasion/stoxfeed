Tensions between the U.S. and Iran are escalating as a ceasefire approaches its expiration, with both sides intensifying their rhetoric ahead of renewed peace talks. Iranian parliamentary speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf criticized President Trump for perceived violations of the ceasefire and hinted at Iran's newfound leverage, stating that they are prepared to reveal "new cards on the battlefield." Meanwhile, Trump has threatened military action if negotiations fail, raising concerns about potential conflict in the region.

This standoff is significant for financial markets, particularly regarding oil prices and energy supply stability. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil transit, could see renewed disruptions if military actions escalate. Analysts warn that any conflict could lead to a spike in oil prices, impacting not only energy stocks but also broader market sentiment as investors react to increased geopolitical risks.

Market professionals should closely monitor the outcomes of the upcoming peace talks in Islamabad, as any failure to reach an agreement could trigger immediate volatility in oil markets and broader risk-off trading strategies.
