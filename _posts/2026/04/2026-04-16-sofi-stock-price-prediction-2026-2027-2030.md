SoFi Technologies Inc. (NASDAQ: SOFI) is generating buzz among analysts, with projections suggesting its stock could reach $60 by 2030. The company, which has transformed from a student loan originator to a comprehensive neobank, is capitalizing on the growing demand for digital banking and personal finance solutions. As of April 2026, SoFi's stock is trading around $18.79, reflecting a significant recovery from last year’s lows and a 76% one-year return, underscoring investor enthusiasm amid its expanding product offerings.

The fintech landscape remains competitive, with SoFi facing challenges from both traditional banks and emerging digital players. Analysts currently express a mixed sentiment, with a Hold consensus and price targets averaging around $19.38. The company's ability to maintain growth and profitability hinges on effective cross-selling, navigating regulatory changes, and sustaining its appeal among digitally savvy consumers.

Investors should closely monitor SoFi's upcoming product launches and member engagement metrics, as these will be critical in determining its trajectory amid a rapidly evolving financial services environment.
