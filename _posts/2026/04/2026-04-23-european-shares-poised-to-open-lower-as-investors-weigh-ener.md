European stocks are poised for a lower opening today as inflation and growth concerns intensify, following escalating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's recent seizure of two ships has raised alarms about regional stability, pushing Brent crude prices to approximately $104 per barrel. This spike in oil prices reignites inflation fears, impacting interest rates and economic growth forecasts, particularly as Germany has already revised its growth outlook downward for 2026 and 2027.

The geopolitical uncertainty is contributing to a risk-averse sentiment in financial markets, with U.S. stock index futures dipping after a record-setting day for Wall Street. Despite strong earnings reports, including from major companies like Tesla and Honeywell, the ongoing U.S.-Iran tensions are casting a shadow over market performance. Asian markets have also reacted negatively, reflecting broader concerns about the implications for global trade and economic stability.

Market professionals should closely monitor how these geopolitical developments may influence commodity prices and overall market sentiment, particularly as key earnings reports are released today.
