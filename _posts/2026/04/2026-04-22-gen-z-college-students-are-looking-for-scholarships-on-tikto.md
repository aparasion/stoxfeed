A recent survey by Sallie reveals that 20% of Gen Z college students turn to TikTok weekly for scholarship opportunities, but consumer advocates caution that this trend may expose them to scams. Approximately one-third of these students reported encountering misleading content, raising concerns as college costs continue to rise—averaging $60,920 for private colleges and $25,850 for public institutions in the upcoming school year. With stricter federal loan regulations and an increasing reliance on scholarships, students are more vulnerable to fraudulent schemes.

While social media can raise awareness about scholarships, it also risks disseminating inaccurate information. Experts warn of “red flags” such as vague contact details and requests for personal data, which may indicate scams. Trusted resources like college financial aid pages and established scholarship databases are recommended for students seeking legitimate funding opportunities.

For market professionals, this trend underscores the importance of financial literacy among younger consumers. As they navigate rising educational costs and complex funding options, the demand for reliable financial resources and education will likely grow, presenting opportunities for financial institutions and advisory services.
