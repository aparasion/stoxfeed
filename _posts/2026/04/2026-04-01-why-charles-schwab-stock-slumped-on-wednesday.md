Charles Schwab (SCHW) shares fell nearly 2% on Wednesday following a price target reduction from Truist Securities analyst David Smith, who lowered his estimate to $120 from $122 while maintaining a buy rating. This seemingly minor adjustment spurred investor concern, leading to a sell-off despite Smith's overall positive outlook on the company's performance amid anticipated short-term market volatility.

The analyst's report highlights a forecast for lower revenue per trade through 2027, but he raised his earnings estimates for 2026 and 2027, projecting net income of $5.95 and $7.30 per share, respectively. This suggests that while trading activity may face challenges, Schwab's diversified services could help it weather economic pressures better than many of its peers.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that despite the recent dip, Schwab's strong fundamentals and projected earnings growth may present a buying opportunity, especially for those anticipating a market rebound in the latter half of the year.
