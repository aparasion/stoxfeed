Indian shares are expected to open lower on Monday as investors react to escalating tensions in the Middle East and rising oil prices, which have surpassed $110 a barrel. The situation intensified after U.S. President Trump threatened military action against Iran, prompting concerns about potential disruptions to oil supply. OPEC+ is attempting to mitigate a global supply shortfall by increasing output by 206,000 barrels per day in May, but the geopolitical landscape remains precarious, especially with Iran's defiance regarding the Strait of Hormuz.

The implications for financial markets are significant, particularly for sectors sensitive to oil prices and geopolitical risks. Last week, U.S. markets rallied on hopes of de-escalation, but the renewed threats from Trump could shift sentiment quickly. Additionally, the strong U.S. employment data may affect expectations around Federal Reserve interest rate cuts, influencing market dynamics.

Market professionals should closely monitor oil price movements and geopolitical developments, as these factors could drive volatility in both Indian and global markets in the coming days.
