Schneider Electric is emerging as a crucial player in the artificial intelligence (AI) landscape, not through chip manufacturing or AI model development, but by providing the essential energy infrastructure that supports these technologies. As demand for AI-driven data centers surges, Schneider's role in delivering and managing energy solutions becomes increasingly vital, positioning the company as an indirect beneficiary of the AI boom.

The company’s revenue growth is significantly driven by its energy management segment, particularly within data centers, which are expanding rapidly to accommodate AI's energy-intensive requirements. In 2025, Schneider reported organic revenue growth of approximately 10%, with a notable increase in free cash flow, highlighting its ability to convert profits into reinvestable capital. This growth is supported by a diversified portfolio across various sectors, including buildings, industry, and infrastructure, which mitigates risks associated with reliance on a single market.

For market professionals, Schneider Electric represents a compelling investment opportunity, with a current share price of 270 euros and a DCF valuation suggesting a potential upside of around 13%. Its strong financial performance, combined with favorable long-term trends in AI and energy management, underscores its position as a stable yet growth-oriented industrial partner.
