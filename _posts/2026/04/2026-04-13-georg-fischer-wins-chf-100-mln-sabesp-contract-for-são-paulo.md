Georg Fischer AG has secured a significant contract valued at approximately CHF 100 million with Companhia de Saneamento Básico do Estado de São Paulo (SABESP) to modernize water distribution networks in São Paulo state. This 24-month agreement, one of Georg Fischer's largest, involves supplying polyethylene pipes to enhance municipal water infrastructure, thereby improving efficiency and expanding access to safe water for over 28 million residents.

This development is crucial for both companies, as it not only strengthens Georg Fischer's position in the industrial manufacturing sector but also aligns with Brazil's ambitious goal of achieving universal water and sanitation access by 2033. The contract is expected to positively influence Georg Fischer's earnings outlook and could bolster investor confidence, as evidenced by a 1.10% increase in its stock price following the announcement.

Market professionals should note that such large-scale infrastructure projects can lead to sustained revenue growth for Georg Fischer, while also enhancing SABESP's operational capabilities, potentially impacting its long-term performance.
