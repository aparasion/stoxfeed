The S&P 500 Index closed down 0.41% on Thursday, alongside declines in the Dow Jones Industrial Average and Nasdaq 100, as geopolitical tensions with Iran intensified. The U.S. and Iran remain at an impasse over the Strait of Hormuz, which has led to increased military posturing and uncertainty in global oil supply. This backdrop contributed to a sell-off in equities, overshadowing early gains driven by strong earnings from chipmakers like Texas Instruments, which surged over 19%.

Despite a promising earnings season with 81% of S&P 500 companies beating estimates, the overall sentiment was dampened by disappointing results from major software firms, including ServiceNow and IBM, which highlighted the adverse effects of the ongoing conflict in the Middle East. Mixed economic indicators, such as rising unemployment claims and a strong manufacturing PMI, added to the market's volatility.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as ongoing tensions could exacerbate energy prices and further impact sectors sensitive to oil supply disruptions.
