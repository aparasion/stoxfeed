AMP's CEO Blair Vernon outlined a strategic focus on growth in wealth management, AI integration, and enhanced capital allocation during a recent address. He acknowledged the ongoing challenges in the retail banking sector, particularly regarding AMP Bank's return on capital, and emphasized a medium-term strategy aimed at improving this through lower-cost funding initiatives while maintaining a steady loan book.

The comments come as AMP's share price has faced downward pressure since the release of its full-year results, despite a reported strong financial performance. Chair Mike Hirst highlighted external factors, including high-frequency trading and passive investment strategies, that are distorting market prices and overshadowing the company's fundamentals. Currently, AMP shares are trading at A$1.36, reflecting a 4.20% increase.

Market professionals should note the potential for AMP's strategic initiatives to reshape investor sentiment and improve capital efficiency, which could lead to a more favorable valuation in the medium term.
