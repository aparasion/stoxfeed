The iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF (NASDAQ:IBIT) and iShares Ethereum Trust ETF (NASDAQ:ETHA) are both single-asset ETFs designed to provide exposure to Bitcoin and Ether, respectively. While both funds charge the same 0.25% expense ratio, ETHA has outperformed IBIT over the past year, returning 40.7% compared to IBIT's decline of 14.1%. However, ETHA's smaller asset base of $7.6 billion and greater volatility, with a maximum drawdown of -61.66%, indicate a riskier investment profile.

For market professionals, the contrasting performances of these ETFs underscore the divergent price movements of Bitcoin and Ether, reflecting broader market trends. IBIT's larger AUM of $63.7 billion suggests stronger institutional support, while ETHA’s recent launch means it lacks a lengthy performance history. Investors should weigh the benefits of ETF convenience against the potential drawbacks of fees and limited trading flexibility compared to direct cryptocurrency holdings.

Ultimately, the choice between IBIT and ETHA hinges on individual risk tolerance and investment strategy, particularly as both funds offer unique advantages in managing cryptocurrency exposure within traditional brokerage accounts.
