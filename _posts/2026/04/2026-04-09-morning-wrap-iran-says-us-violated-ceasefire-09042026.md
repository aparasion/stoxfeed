Brent crude prices are experiencing a rebound, rising 1.2% to around $97 per barrel, largely due to escalating tensions between Iran and the U.S. The Iranian Parliament has accused the U.S. of violating a ceasefire, which has heightened regional instability amid ongoing Israeli airstrikes. This geopolitical uncertainty is contributing to fluctuations in oil prices, impacting market sentiment as traders weigh the implications for supply routes, particularly the Strait of Hormuz.

Asian markets are reflecting this cautious sentiment, with significant losses in South Korea's KOSPI (-1.8%) and Japan's index (-0.7%), as investors grapple with the durability of the U.S.-Iran truce. In the currency markets, the New Zealand dollar is gaining traction following hawkish comments from RBNZ Governor Anna Breman, while the Dollar Index remains flat, indicating a pause in broader market movements.

Market professionals should closely monitor oil price trends and geopolitical developments, as these factors could significantly influence sector performance and overall market volatility in the coming days.
