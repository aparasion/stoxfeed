The decision to claim Social Security benefits at age 62 can significantly impact retirement planning, particularly for those born in 1960 or later, who face a 30% reduction in monthly benefits compared to waiting until their full retirement age of 67. While early claiming decreases monthly income, it can also alleviate pressure on retirement portfolios during market downturns, allowing retirees to avoid locking in losses from their investments.

This choice is particularly relevant in the current market environment, where volatility may prompt individuals to reconsider their retirement timelines. By opting for early benefits, retirees may preserve their investment capital, thus maintaining a more stable financial footing. Additionally, for those with shorter life expectancies or specific retirement goals, claiming early could yield a higher total lifetime payout.

Ultimately, the decision to claim Social Security at 62 should be based on individual circumstances, including health, financial needs, and market conditions. Understanding the trade-offs involved can help retirees make informed choices that align with their long-term financial strategies.
