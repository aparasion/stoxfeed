The market is reacting to a fragile ceasefire between Iran and the US, with stocks and bonds giving back some gains as Brent crude oil prices hover around $95 per barrel. Tensions escalated when Iran threatened to withdraw from the ceasefire following continued Israeli attacks in Lebanon, which it deemed a violation. While the Strait of Hormuz is reportedly closed, the situation remains fluid, with ongoing discussions between the US and Iran offering a glimmer of hope for a sustained peace deal.

This environment underscores the volatility in oil prices, which could spike if the ceasefire collapses completely. Analysts note that the market's current optimism is reflected in a VIX index trading at 21.5, indicating that while fear levels are elevated, they remain below previous peaks. The market's direction hinges on President Trump's response to the situation, as any significant escalation could lead to a resurgence in oil prices and a downturn in equities.

Market professionals should closely monitor news developments, as the outcome of US-Iran relations will be pivotal for both oil prices and overall market sentiment in the coming days.
