Oracle (ORCL) shares have experienced a significant downturn in early 2026, plummeting over 26% year-to-date and more than 50% from their September peak of $345.72. Despite reporting record sales growth, with third-quarter revenue rising 22% year-over-year to $17.2 billion, investor concerns center around the hefty infrastructure investments required to support its ambitious AI initiatives. The company plans to raise up to $50 billion in debt and equity financing, raising questions about its financial flexibility amidst a backdrop of layoffs aimed at streamlining operations.

The tech giant's cloud services continue to drive growth, with cloud revenue surging 44% to $8.9 billion and a staggering 84% increase in cloud infrastructure revenue. However, the $553 billion remaining performance obligations indicate a capital-intensive growth strategy that could pressure free cash flow and increase risk. Investors are left weighing whether the current stock price, now around 18 times forward earnings, presents a buying opportunity or reflects the inherent risks of Oracle's aggressive expansion plans.

For market professionals, Oracle's situation underscores the delicate balance between growth potential and financial risk in the tech sector. The substantial backlog and strong sales momentum may appeal to those bullish on AI, but caution is warranted given the company's capital demands and the volatility of market conditions.
