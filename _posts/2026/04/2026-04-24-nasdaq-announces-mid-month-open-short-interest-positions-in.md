The U.S. government’s nearly 10% stake in Intel has yielded a $27 billion paper gain following the company’s strong quarterly earnings, driven by increased demand for AI-related CPUs. Intel's positive forecast for the upcoming quarter adds further optimism, suggesting robust revenue growth may continue. This development aligns with broader market trends, as strategists anticipate a return to 1970s-style inflation, potentially fueling additional stock gains.

Despite the stock market nearing record highs, underlying support factors such as sidelined cash and labor dynamics are expected to sustain the rally. However, volatility remains, as evidenced by Avis's recent speculative surge of over 700% before a sharp decline. Concerns about a potential 10% oil and gas shortage persist, even amid geopolitical tensions.

Investors should remain cautious; Goldman Sachs indicates a higher likelihood of a market pullback rather than a sustained rally in the near term, suggesting that risk assets may face increased headwinds.
