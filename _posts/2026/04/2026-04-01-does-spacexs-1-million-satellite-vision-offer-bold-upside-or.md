SpaceX is on the brink of an IPO, positioning itself as a pivotal player in the burgeoning space economy. With plans to launch 1 million satellites to support orbital AI data centers, the company aims to expand its current fleet of approximately 10,140 satellites. This ambitious goal, while impressive, raises concerns about regulatory scrutiny and environmental impact, particularly as SpaceX already dominates the U.S. launch market with around 80% of missions.

For investors, the potential $1.75 trillion valuation—equating to 113 times its estimated $15.5 billion in revenue for 2025—signals both opportunity and risk. The hype surrounding Elon Musk and SpaceX could drive initial trading volatility, but the lofty valuation necessitates sustained growth to justify such expectations. 

As SpaceX prepares for its market debut, investors should remain cautious. The stock may face significant downward pressure if the initial excitement wanes, making it critical to assess entry points carefully amidst the anticipated frenzy.
