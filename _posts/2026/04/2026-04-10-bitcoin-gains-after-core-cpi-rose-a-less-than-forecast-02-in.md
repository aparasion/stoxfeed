Bitcoin experienced a modest uptick following the release of March's Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, which showed core inflation rising by only 0.2%, below the anticipated 0.3%. While the headline CPI increased by 0.9%, primarily driven by soaring energy prices amid the ongoing conflict in Iran, the lower-than-expected core inflation could influence market sentiment regarding Federal Reserve monetary policy.

The divergence between core and headline inflation suggests that while energy costs are impacting overall prices, underlying inflation pressures may be stabilizing. This has shifted market expectations from potential Fed rate cuts to a more cautious stance, with traders now pricing in the likelihood of no changes in interest rates during upcoming meetings. The Nasdaq 100 also saw a slight gain, reflecting a broader market response to the inflation data.

Market professionals should note that the subdued core CPI could provide a temporary cushion for risk assets like Bitcoin, but ongoing geopolitical tensions and energy costs remain critical factors influencing both inflation and monetary policy outlooks.
