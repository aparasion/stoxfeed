Bitcoin has rebounded above $76,000 as the cryptocurrency market navigates heightened geopolitical tensions related to Iran, while decentralized finance (DeFi) faces significant turmoil following a $292 million exploit of KelpDAO. Despite these challenges, Bitcoin's recent performance, climbing about 2.4% over the past 24 hours, indicates a stable demand, bolstered by steady spot ETF inflows and limited leverage, according to Wintermute trader Jasper De Maere.

The DeFi sector is experiencing a severe downturn, with total value locked plummeting by $14 billion to a one-year low of approximately $85 billion. The KelpDAO hack has led to widespread withdrawals, particularly affecting major protocols like Aave, which saw around $10 billion in deposits withdrawn. This incident highlights the fragility within DeFi, as users become increasingly wary of risks associated with lending pools amid ongoing exploits.

Market professionals should note the contrasting trajectories of Bitcoin and DeFi; while Bitcoin shows resilience, capital is consolidating in large-cap assets, leaving riskier altcoins lagging. The ongoing geopolitical landscape will likely continue to influence market dynamics, with potential implications for both crypto and traditional equities.
