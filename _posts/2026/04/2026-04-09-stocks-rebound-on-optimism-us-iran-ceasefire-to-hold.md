The S&P 500 Index closed up 0.62% on Thursday, buoyed by optimism surrounding a potential ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran, as well as upcoming direct negotiations between Israel and Lebanon. The Dow Jones Industrial Average and Nasdaq 100 also posted gains, reaching multi-week highs, despite initial declines due to geopolitical tensions and disappointing economic data, including a rise in jobless claims and a downward revision of Q4 GDP.

The market's recovery highlights a complex interplay between geopolitical developments and economic indicators. The rise in crude oil prices, which surged over 3% amid concerns about the Strait of Hormuz, adds inflationary pressure that could influence Federal Reserve policy. Additionally, weaker-than-expected personal income and spending figures suggest a cooling domestic economy, which may temper expectations for aggressive monetary tightening.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the heightened sensitivity of stocks to geopolitical events and economic data. Investors may need to adjust their strategies in response to evolving conditions, particularly in sectors sensitive to oil prices and economic growth.
