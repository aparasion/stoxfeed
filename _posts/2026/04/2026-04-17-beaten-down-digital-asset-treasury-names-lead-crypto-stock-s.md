Bitcoin surged to a two-month high near $78,000, driven by geopolitical developments that eased investor concerns over energy supply disruptions. U.S. President Donald Trump announced that Iran committed to keeping the Strait of Hormuz open, a crucial passage for global oil transport. This news, coupled with reports of potential U.S. moves to unfreeze $20 billion in Iranian assets, sparked a broader risk-on sentiment across financial markets.

Digital asset treasury firms, which had faced significant declines in recent months, led the rally with gains of up to 20%. Notable performers included American Bitcoin (ABTC) and MicroStrategy (MSTR), which rose 21% and 13%, respectively. The positive momentum extended to major cryptocurrencies, with Ethereum (ETH), Solana (SOL), and XRP climbing 4%-5%. Meanwhile, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq also reached new record highs, buoyed by the overall market optimism.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the renewed investor confidence in risk assets, particularly in the digital asset sector, as geopolitical stability appears to be returning. This shift could signal a significant rotation back into high-beta equities and cryptocurrencies, potentially reshaping portfolio strategies in the coming weeks.
