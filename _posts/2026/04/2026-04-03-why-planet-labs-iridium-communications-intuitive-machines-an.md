SpaceX is positioning itself for a potential blockbuster IPO in 2026, with reports indicating it has filed for an offering that could value the company at a staggering $1.75 trillion. This news, coupled with acquisition rumors involving Amazon and Globalstar, has sparked significant interest in the space sector, driving stock prices for companies like Intuitive Machines, Iridium Communications, and Planet Labs higher.

The surge in space stocks reflects a broader trend in the market, as investors anticipate increased activity and investment in satellite technology and space exploration. Intuitive Machines, for instance, recently secured a $180 million contract with NASA, enhancing its profile in the industry. Meanwhile, Amazon's interest in acquiring Globalstar to bolster its satellite-based internet services could reshape competitive dynamics in the sector.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the heightened momentum in space-related equities, which could present lucrative opportunities as the sector evolves. Investors may want to monitor these developments closely, especially with SpaceX's IPO on the horizon.
