U.S. derivatives regulators are investigating unusual oil futures trades that occurred just minutes before President Trump's unexpected announcement regarding a pause in military action against Iran. The Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) is leading the inquiry, focusing on trading activities on CME Group and Intercontinental Exchange platforms. Reports indicate that trading volumes surged significantly shortly before key announcements over a two-week period, prompting scrutiny into the identities behind these trades.

This investigation raises concerns about market integrity, particularly as the sudden spikes in trading volume for S&P 500 e-mini and WTI crude futures coincided with Trump's announcement, which caused the S&P 500 to jump over 2.5% while WTI crude fell nearly 6%. Such anomalies could signal potential misuse of nonpublic information, prompting calls from lawmakers for deeper scrutiny into trading practices.

Market professionals should closely monitor the CFTC's findings, as any implications of misconduct could lead to heightened regulatory scrutiny and impact trading strategies across the derivatives market.
