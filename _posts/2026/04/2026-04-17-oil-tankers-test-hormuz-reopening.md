Oil prices have seen a notable decline as crude carriers begin to navigate the Strait of Hormuz following Iran's announcement of a ceasefire and the reopening of this critical shipping route. At least eight tankers have started to move toward the chokepoint, which has been largely inactive since late February due to regional conflict, significantly impacting global oil flows. Brent crude prices dropped over 10% on Friday, reflecting market optimism about the potential resumption of oil transport through this vital corridor.

However, the reopening is not without its caveats. While the initial movement of tankers is encouraging, many remain stranded, and Iran has hinted at conditional access based on geopolitical considerations. This uncertainty leaves the market in a state of operational ambiguity, as the backlog of vessels and ongoing disruptions in regional output could hinder a swift recovery in oil supply.

Market participants should remain cautious, as the situation in the Strait of Hormuz could quickly shift, impacting both oil prices and supply chain dynamics in the coming weeks.
