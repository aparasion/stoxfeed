Mortgage rates have declined for the third consecutive week, revitalizing demand in the housing market. According to the Mortgage Bankers Association, total mortgage application volume surged by 7.9% last week. The average interest rate for 30-year fixed-rate mortgages dropped to 6.35%, contributing to a 10% increase in applications for home purchases compared to the previous week and a notable 14% rise year-over-year.

This uptick in mortgage activity comes as financial markets reacted positively to geopolitical developments, including a ceasefire in the Middle East and declining oil prices. Despite ongoing uncertainties, the job market remains robust, supporting housing demand. The increase in conventional purchase loans, up 11%, signals a shift toward a more favorable environment for buyers, especially with higher inventory levels.

Market professionals should note that while mortgage rates are experiencing volatility due to mixed economic signals, the recent trends suggest a potential rebound in housing sector activity, which could have broader implications for related financial markets.
