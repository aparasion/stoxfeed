GE Vernova (GEV) has seen its stock price soar over eightfold since its 2024 market debut, driven by robust demand across its three business segments: Power, Electrification, and Wind. The Power segment, which accounts for 55% of its 2025 orders, has benefited from a 51% increase in orders, fueled by the growing needs of cloud, data center, and AI markets. Meanwhile, the Electrification segment also showed strong performance with a 23% rise, despite the Wind segment facing challenges from supply chain issues.

The surge in orders has led to an impressive 34% organic growth in total orders for 2025, contrasting with a mere 7% growth in 2024. Analysts project that from 2025 to 2028, GE Vernova's revenue and adjusted EBITDA will grow at CAGRs of 15% and 55%, respectively. With an enterprise value of $236 billion, the stock trades at a premium, but the increasing global demand for energy, projected to rise by 40% by 2035, supports its valuation.

For market professionals, GE Vernova presents a compelling long-term investment opportunity. If the company meets analysts' growth expectations and maintains its diversified energy portfolio, its stock could potentially increase fivefold over the next decade, making it a noteworthy candidate for those looking to capitalize on the energy sector's evolution.
