Lucid Group (NASDAQ: LCID) faces significant challenges as its stock plummets 99% from its peak in early 2021, with the Saudi Arabian government, its largest stakeholder, feeling the impact. Despite a promising 123% year-over-year revenue increase to $522.7 million in Q4, driven by the launch of the Gravity SUV, Lucid's operating losses surged 45% to $1.06 billion, raising concerns about its financial viability. 

The macroeconomic landscape presents both challenges and opportunities for Lucid. Rising energy prices, exacerbated by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, have led to increased interest in electric vehicles, with EV registrations in the EU up 51% year-over-year. This trend could bolster government support for the EV sector, potentially benefiting Lucid if it can effectively pivot to more affordable models.

Investors should remain cautious, as Lucid's future hinges on managing cash burn and securing continued backing from stakeholders like Saudi Arabia and Uber, which recently committed an additional $200 million to the company.
