Tesla (TSLA) shares have plummeted over 30% from their highs and are down nearly 25% year-to-date, following disappointing first-quarter delivery figures. The company reported a 6% increase in deliveries to 358,023 vehicles, falling short of the 365,000 analysts expected. Additionally, its energy storage business underperformed, with deployments of 8.8 GWh, significantly below the anticipated 14.4 GWh. This weak performance comes amid growing competition in the EV market, particularly from domestic players in China and emerging rivals like Rivian.

For investors, these developments raise concerns ahead of Tesla's upcoming earnings report on April 22. The company's core EV segment is grappling with reduced demand due to the expiration of the federal EV tax credit and reputational challenges tied to CEO Elon Musk's controversial public persona. As competition heats up, Tesla's ambitious future promises, including autonomous driving and robotaxis, seem increasingly distant.

The key takeaway for market professionals is to approach Tesla stock with caution, as its current struggles in execution and market position may overshadow its long-term growth narrative.
