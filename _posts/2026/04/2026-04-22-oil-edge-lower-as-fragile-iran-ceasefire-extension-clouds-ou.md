Oil prices dipped on Wednesday amid ongoing uncertainty surrounding the U.S.–Iran conflict, as President Trump extended a fragile ceasefire with Tehran. Brent crude fell 0.68% to $97.81 per barrel, while U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures dropped 0.29% to $89.04 per barrel. The ceasefire extension, which hinges on Iran presenting a unified proposal to end hostilities, reflects the complex political dynamics within Iran and the lack of a clear path toward de-escalation.

This development is significant for the financial markets as it underscores the volatility in oil prices driven by geopolitical tensions. The temporary truce may alleviate immediate fears of military action, yet the continuing blockade of Iranian ports and stalled negotiations could keep supply concerns at the forefront. Market participants should remain vigilant, as fluctuations in oil prices can directly impact energy sector stocks and broader market sentiment.

Traders and analysts should monitor developments closely, as any escalation or breakthrough in negotiations could lead to sharp price movements in the oil market.
