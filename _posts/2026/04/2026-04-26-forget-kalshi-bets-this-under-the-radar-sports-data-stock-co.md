Genius Sports (NYSE: GENI) is positioning itself as a critical player in the evolving landscape of sports betting and prediction markets, despite its shares experiencing a significant decline of over 60% year-to-date. As a technology provider rather than a sportsbook or prediction market operator, Genius Sports offers essential data and analytics that facilitate sports wagering, making it a quintessential “picks-and-shovels” investment amid the growth of platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket.

The company’s recent announcement of a $900 million acquisition of sports media firm Legend, along with an additional $300 million earnout, could mark a pivotal moment for its financials. Analysts project that this merger could boost Genius's adjusted EBITDA from $190 million to as much as $320 million, significantly enhancing its operating margins and earnings potential in the coming years. With forecasts suggesting earnings could reach $0.28 per share in 2027 and $0.60 in 2028, the stock’s current price of just under $4.50 may present an attractive entry point for long-term investors.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Genius Sports, while currently undervalued, could rebound sharply as it capitalizes on the burgeoning prediction market trend and its enhanced profitability post-merger. This makes it a stock worth monitoring closely for potential upside.
