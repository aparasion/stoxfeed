Iran's parliamentary speaker, Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, has accused the U.S. of breaching a two-week ceasefire agreement, citing violations that include ongoing Israeli attacks on Lebanon and incursions into Iranian airspace. Ghalibaf's remarks highlight a significant deterioration in U.S.-Iran relations, as he emphasized the historical distrust stemming from the U.S.'s failure to uphold commitments.

This development could have immediate implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical tensions, such as energy and defense. Heightened tensions in the Middle East often lead to volatility in oil prices, which can affect global markets and investor sentiment. Additionally, any escalation could impact negotiations surrounding Iran's nuclear program, further complicating the geopolitical landscape.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation, as continued instability in the region could lead to fluctuations in commodity prices and affect broader market performance.
