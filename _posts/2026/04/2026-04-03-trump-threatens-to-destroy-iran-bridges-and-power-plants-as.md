U.S. President Donald Trump's recent threats to target Iran's infrastructure, including bridges and power plants, escalate tensions in the Middle East, with implications for global oil markets. His comments followed reports of a U.S. F-35 fighter jet allegedly shot down over Iran, intensifying concerns about military conflict in a region critical to oil supply. The Gulf Cooperation Council has called for urgent international action against Iranian aggression, while Trump’s rhetoric suggests a prolonged military engagement could disrupt oil flows through the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping route.

The potential for military action against Iran raises significant risks for energy prices and market stability. With Iran already restricting tanker traffic, any escalation could lead to supply shocks, driving up oil prices and affecting related sectors. Additionally, legal experts warn that attacks on civilian infrastructure could constitute war crimes, further complicating the geopolitical landscape.

Market professionals should monitor developments closely, as heightened military tensions could lead to volatility in oil prices and impact broader market sentiment, particularly in energy and defense sectors.
