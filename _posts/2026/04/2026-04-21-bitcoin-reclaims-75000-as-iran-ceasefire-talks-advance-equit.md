Bitcoin has reclaimed the $75,000 mark, trading at $75,733, amid advancing ceasefire talks between Iran and Pakistan. The cryptocurrency rose 1.5% over 24 hours as investors reacted to the potential for a diplomatic resolution, despite the current truce nearing its deadline. However, Bitcoin's performance remains overshadowed by a broader equity rally, with negative funding rates in perpetual futures indicating persistent bearish sentiment.

Despite the recent gains, Bitcoin's upward trajectory faces challenges from record selling by public miners and a decline in mining difficulty, raising concerns about the sustainability of prices above $76,000. The MSCI All Country World Index has resumed its rally, highlighting a divergence in momentum between equities and cryptocurrencies. Notably, inflows into spot Bitcoin ETFs reached nearly $1 billion last week, reflecting growing institutional interest.

Market professionals should monitor Bitcoin's ability to break through the $76,000 resistance level, as this could trigger a short squeeze. Conversely, failure to maintain momentum could lead to a retreat below $74,000 if ceasefire talks stall.
