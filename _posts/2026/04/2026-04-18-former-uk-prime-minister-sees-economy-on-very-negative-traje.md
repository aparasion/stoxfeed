Former UK Prime Minister Liz Truss has voiced strong concerns about the U.K. economy, describing it as being on a "very negative trajectory." In a recent interview, she attributed the country's economic struggles to high taxes, excessive regulation, and a lack of sound monetary policy, which she argues has led to the debasement of the currency and rising inequality. Truss advocates for bitcoin as a potential solution, positioning it as part of a broader movement to combat centralized control and promote financial independence.

Truss's remarks come as she launches CPAC UK, a conference aimed at rallying support for economic reform and liberty. She contends that the current economic environment disincentivizes entrepreneurship, warning that without significant change, the U.K. risks long-term decline. Her criticism of central banking and monetary policy reflects a growing sentiment among some market participants who are increasingly skeptical of traditional financial systems.

For market professionals, Truss's endorsement of bitcoin and her critique of monetary policy may signal a shift in the narrative surrounding cryptocurrency as a hedge against economic instability. This development could influence investor sentiment and trading strategies, particularly in the context of rising inflation and regulatory scrutiny.
