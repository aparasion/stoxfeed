WTI crude oil surged by 11.41% to close at a three-and-a-half-week high, while RBOB gasoline rose 6.36%, driven by escalating tensions in the Middle East. President Trump’s commitment to intensify military action against Iran has heightened concerns over the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil supplies, which remains effectively closed. This uncertainty is exacerbated by reports of Iranian plans to monitor traffic through the strait, leaving oil markets under pressure.

The implications for the energy sector are significant, as the International Energy Agency warns that even a resolution to the conflict may not restore normal supply flows quickly due to damaged infrastructure. Additionally, OPEC+ plans to increase production may falter as Middle Eastern producers face operational challenges amid the ongoing war. The situation has led to a notable reduction in production capacity, with local storage nearing capacity limits.

Market participants should closely monitor geopolitical developments, as prolonged disruptions could keep oil prices elevated. The current dynamics suggest that traders should prepare for volatility in energy markets, particularly if military actions escalate further.
