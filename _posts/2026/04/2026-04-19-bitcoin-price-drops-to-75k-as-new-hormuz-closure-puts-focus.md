Bitcoin (BTC) faced significant pressure as it attempted to maintain the $75,000 level into Sunday’s weekly close, amid escalating tensions from the US-Iran conflict. After reaching a ten-week high of $78,400, BTC's price retreated as fears intensified over the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil shipping route. This geopolitical uncertainty has reignited concerns about potential oil price surges, impacting market sentiment across both crypto and traditional sectors.

The current market dynamics are particularly sensitive, with traders noting that a single social media post could shift sentiment dramatically. Recent data indicated a wave of liquidations in the crypto market, totaling $260 million, as long positions came under pressure. Analysts are closely monitoring Bitcoin’s interaction with its 21-week exponential moving average, currently near $78,900, as a rejection from this level could signal a retest of support around $73,000 in the coming week.

Market participants should remain vigilant, as the interplay between geopolitical developments and Bitcoin's price action could create volatility, impacting trading strategies and risk management decisions.
