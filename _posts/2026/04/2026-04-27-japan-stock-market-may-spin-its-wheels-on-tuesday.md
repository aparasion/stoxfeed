The Japanese stock market has faced a challenging week, with the Nikkei 225 index declining for three consecutive sessions, losing nearly 900 points or 2.4%. It closed at 39,190.40 on Friday, reflecting significant pressure from financial, technology, and automotive sectors. As the market prepares for a holiday on Monday, sentiment remains weak, with expectations of a soft start on Tuesday.

This downturn in Japan comes amid mixed signals from global markets. While U.S. indices showed some resilience, bolstered by a late-session rally despite tech sector struggles, the Asian markets are expected to experience volatility. The rise in oil prices, driven by geopolitical tensions and sanctions on Russian exports, could provide some support, particularly for energy stocks, but the overall outlook remains uncertain as technology shares continue to weigh heavily on investor sentiment.

Market professionals should monitor the upcoming economic data releases from Japan, including current account figures and bank lending statistics, which could provide insights into domestic economic health and influence market direction in the near term.
