Three notable stocks—Etsy, Nike, and Tesla—are facing significant challenges that could impact their long-term viability and investor sentiment. Etsy has seen an 80% drop from its all-time highs, largely due to declining gross merchandise sales and a reduction in active buyers and sellers. Despite recent revenue growth, the company’s decision to sell its Depop and Reverb segments raises concerns about its future profitability.

Nike, once a dividend growth favorite, has struggled with a 31% year-to-date decline and flat revenue growth. The company is losing market share, particularly in key regions like China, and its reliance on wholesale revenue may not be enough to offset declines in direct sales. Insider buying has not translated into a sustainable recovery, with analysts eyeing its upcoming earnings report for further insights.

Tesla, while still enjoying a high market cap, faces its own set of risks. Revenue dipped by 3% year-over-year, and its automotive sales have decreased significantly. With a P/E ratio exceeding 300, the stock appears overvalued, especially as growth initiatives in AI and energy have yet to materialize. Investors should proceed with caution as these companies prepare to report earnings in the coming months.
