As the April 15 tax deadline approaches, workers have a final opportunity to maximize their individual retirement account (IRA) contributions for 2025. Notably, married couples can leverage spousal IRA rules, allowing one spouse to contribute to both their own and their partner's IRAs, provided they have sufficient earned income. This strategy can significantly enhance retirement savings, especially for couples where one partner may not have earned income.

Understanding the income limits is crucial for effective planning. For 2025, the deduction for traditional IRAs phases out for modified adjusted gross incomes (MAGI) above $149,000, while Roth IRA contributions are limited for those with MAGI exceeding $246,000. However, couples can still consider backdoor Roth strategies to circumvent these limits, allowing for greater flexibility in retirement planning.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the importance of timely IRA contributions and the potential tax benefits they offer. Encouraging clients to maximize their contributions can lead to enhanced long-term financial stability and investment growth.
