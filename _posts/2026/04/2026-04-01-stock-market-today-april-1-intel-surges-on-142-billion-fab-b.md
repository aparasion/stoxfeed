Intel (INTC) surged 8.84% to close at $48.03 on Wednesday, following its announcement to buy back Apollo Global Management's 49% stake in its Fab 34 plant for $14.2 billion. This strategic move is seen as a pivotal step in reinforcing Intel’s commitment to its AI-focused foundry roadmap, which investors are keenly watching. The trading volume spiked to 128.9 million shares, significantly surpassing the three-month average, reflecting heightened investor interest.

The broader market also showed positive momentum, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite rising 0.72% and 1.16%, respectively. In the semiconductor sector, rivals like AMD and Nvidia also experienced gains, indicating a reassessment of AI chip demand across the industry. Intel’s reacquisition of the Fab 34 stake not only enhances its manufacturing capabilities but also signals a stronger position in the competitive landscape, particularly in AI technologies.

For investors, this deal underscores Intel’s recovery trajectory and its potential to capitalize on the growing AI market, suggesting a favorable outlook for its stock performance moving forward.
