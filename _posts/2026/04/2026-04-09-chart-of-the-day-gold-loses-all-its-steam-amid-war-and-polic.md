Gold prices have stalled near $4,720–$4,730 per ounce after a failed attempt to break above $4,770, reflecting stagnant sentiment amid ongoing geopolitical tensions and uncertainty in U.S. monetary policy. The recent inverted hammer pattern suggests indecision in the market, with the RSI indicating a neutral stance slightly below 50. This lack of direction comes as the fragile ceasefire in the Middle East faces renewed scrutiny, particularly following accusations from Iranian officials against the U.S. regarding violations.

The current market environment is characterized by a mix of risks, including the potential for stagflation as energy prices rise and labor market stability remains uncertain. Investors are grappling with the implications of the latest FOMC minutes, which reveal a lack of consensus on interest rate hikes and have led to a muted outlook for gold relative to interest-bearing assets. 

As traders await today's PCE inflation reading, the interplay between geopolitical developments and monetary policy will be crucial for shaping market sentiment and influencing gold and Treasury note prices.
