Avis Budget's stock experienced a dramatic surge, peaking at over $700 after a 600% rally, only to plummet more than 70% in just two days. This volatility was fueled by a high short interest of approximately 64% of the free float and a limited supply of shares, creating ideal conditions for a short squeeze. The ownership concentration, with two hedge funds holding over 70% of shares, further amplified the price movements as forced buybacks from short sellers added to the demand.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. The stock's meteoric rise was not driven by improved fundamentals but rather by market mechanics typical of "meme stocks," where speculative trading and retail investor activity dominate. This has led to extreme volatility, with options activity surging and brokers imposing 100% margin requirements, signaling heightened market instability.

As the market corrects, the shift back to a more fundamentals-driven valuation raises caution among analysts. Investors should remain vigilant, as the uncertainty surrounding Avis Budget suggests that while a rebound could occur, the path ahead is fraught with risks.
