Lumen Technologies (LUMN) is attempting a turnaround after years of investor disinterest, primarily due to its legacy customer base and a slow pivot to fiber internet and cloud services. Recently, the company has carved out a niche by providing connectivity for AI data centers, a move that aligns with current tech trends. A significant step in this transformation was its 2024 agreement with Microsoft for private connectivity fabric, coupled with the $5.75 billion sale of its consumer fiber business to AT&T, which has helped reduce its debt burden.

Despite these developments, Lumen's financials remain under pressure. The company reported over $17.3 billion in long-term debt, though the recent sale has lowered this to under $13 billion. However, it also lost a substantial revenue stream, with a projected decline of 6-7% due to the offloaded consumer business. Investors should approach Lumen with caution; while there are signs of potential recovery, the heavy debt load and reduced revenue make it a speculative investment for now.

In summary, Lumen Technologies is in a critical phase of transformation, but its financial health remains tenuous. Market professionals should monitor the company's progress closely before considering any investment.
