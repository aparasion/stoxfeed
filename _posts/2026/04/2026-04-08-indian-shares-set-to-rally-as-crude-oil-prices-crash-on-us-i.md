Indian shares are set to open positively as a two-week ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran, contingent on reopening the Strait of Hormuz, has led to a significant drop in oil prices. This ceasefire also includes a halt to hostilities between Israel and Hezbollah, with negotiations set to begin in Islamabad. The easing tensions have contributed to a surge in global markets, with Asian indices rising sharply.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. Brent crude prices plummeted 13% to $95 a barrel, while WTI crude fell 15% below $97. This decline in oil prices could benefit sectors reliant on energy costs, particularly transportation and manufacturing. Additionally, the Reserve Bank of India is expected to maintain interest rates amid rising inflation, which could influence domestic investor sentiment.

Market professionals should monitor how these geopolitical developments affect commodity prices and regional stock performance, especially in energy-sensitive sectors.
