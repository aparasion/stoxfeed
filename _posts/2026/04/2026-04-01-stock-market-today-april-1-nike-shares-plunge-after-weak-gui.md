Nike (NKE) shares plummeted 15.52% to $44.62 on Wednesday, following disappointing fiscal Q3 guidance and multiple analyst downgrades. The stock's sharp decline came as investors grappled with a prolonged turnaround timeline, concerns over demand in China, and ongoing challenges in margin recovery. Trading volume soared to 109.4 million shares, significantly surpassing the three-month average of 17.7 million.

Despite beating earnings expectations, Nike's management projected a 3% sales decline over the next nine months, raising concerns about the effectiveness of its turnaround strategy. While the North American wholesale segment saw an 11% increase, the company's reliance on clearance pricing to manage excess inventory continues to pressure margins. 

For market professionals, Nike's current valuation at just 1.4 times sales marks its lowest point since 2009, suggesting a potential buying opportunity amidst the turbulence. However, the path to recovery appears longer than anticipated, warranting close monitoring of future performance metrics.
