Two cargo ships were attacked in the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial maritime corridor for global oil and gas, amid ongoing U.S.-Iran diplomatic efforts for peace. The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations reported gunfire directed at a vessel near the Iranian coast, although no injuries or damage were reported. This incident follows another attack involving an Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps gunboat, which caused significant damage to a container ship but left the crew unharmed.

These developments heighten tensions in a region that already accounts for roughly 20% of global oil transit, raising concerns about potential disruptions to supply chains and oil prices. The attacks come just as the U.S. extended a ceasefire with Iran, complicating the diplomatic landscape. Increased military activity in the area could lead to volatility in energy markets, as investors weigh the implications for oil supply and geopolitical risk.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as further escalations could impact oil prices and broader market sentiment regarding geopolitical stability.
