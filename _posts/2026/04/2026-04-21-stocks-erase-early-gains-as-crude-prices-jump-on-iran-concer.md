The S&P 500 and Dow Jones Industrial Average both dipped by 0.13% today, while the Nasdaq 100 edged up by 0.02%, reflecting a mixed market after an early advance. This shift occurred as WTI crude oil rebounded over 2%, driven by speculation surrounding Iran's participation in upcoming peace talks, which could impact global oil supply and prices. Positive earnings from UnitedHealth Group and General Electric, alongside stronger-than-expected U.S. economic reports on retail and housing, provided some support for stocks.

The mixed performance of major indices indicates a cautious sentiment among investors, particularly as the market grapples with geopolitical tensions and the upcoming Federal Reserve Chair confirmation hearing. Notably, 81% of S&P 500 companies reporting Q1 earnings have exceeded estimates, suggesting resilience in corporate performance despite broader economic uncertainties.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of monitoring geopolitical developments and economic indicators, as these factors are likely to influence market volatility and sector performance in the near term.
