The WLFI token, backed by former President Trump, plummeted 12% to record lows as World Liberty Financial defended its controversial lending strategy on the Dolomite DeFi platform. The company acknowledged using its governance token as collateral to borrow stablecoins, which critics argue exacerbates risks for both WLFI holders and Dolomite depositors. The firm claims it can avoid liquidation by supplying more WLFI, but this circular strategy raises concerns about the sustainability of its position.

This decline highlights significant vulnerabilities within the DeFi space, particularly for tokens tied to complex lending mechanisms. WLFI's strategy has resulted in a treasury buyback that is now significantly underwater, with the token trading nearly 48% below its average buyback price. The situation is compounded by the presence of billions of WLFI tokens in an intermediary wallet, which could further strain liquidity if deployed into the already drained lending pool.

Market professionals should closely monitor WLFI's ongoing strategy and its implications for liquidity and risk management within the DeFi ecosystem, as the situation could influence broader market sentiment and investor confidence in similar ventures.
