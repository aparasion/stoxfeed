Asia-Pacific markets experienced a notable uptick on Tuesday, buoyed by gains on Wall Street as traders digested ongoing developments in the U.S.-Iran conflict. President Trump issued a stark ultimatum, threatening to target Iran's civilian infrastructure unless a peace deal is reached by a specified deadline, while also suggesting that negotiations were progressing. This geopolitical tension is particularly relevant as it impacts global energy supply routes, notably the Strait of Hormuz.

The implications for financial markets are significant, particularly for oil prices. West Texas Intermediate crude futures rose 0.7% to $113.25 per barrel, while Brent crude increased by 0.68% to $109.77. The volatility in oil markets can affect sector performance, especially for energy stocks, and may influence broader market sentiment as investors weigh the risks associated with potential escalations in the conflict.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the U.S.-Iran negotiations, as any resolution or escalation could lead to sharp movements in oil prices and related equities, impacting overall market stability.
