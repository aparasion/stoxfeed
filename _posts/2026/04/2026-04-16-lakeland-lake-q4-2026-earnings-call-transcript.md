Lakeland Industries (LAKE) reported a 15.2% increase in full-year net sales to $192.6 million, primarily driven by a robust performance in fire services, which accounted for nearly half of total revenue. However, the fourth quarter saw a slight decline in net sales, down 1.7% to $45.8 million, with U.S. sales rising but European sales falling due to order timing issues. The company faced significant profitability challenges, with adjusted EBITDA dropping 78% year-over-year in Q4, largely attributed to gross margin compression from cost pressures and execution issues.

Despite these setbacks, management is optimistic about future growth, projecting single- to high-single-digit revenue increases and positive operating cash flow for fiscal 2027. Strategic divestitures have enhanced liquidity, generating $14 million in cash, while the company aims to optimize inventory and improve operational efficiencies. Key certifications have also unlocked new commercial opportunities, particularly in the fire services segment.

For market professionals, Lakeland’s focus on core competencies and operational improvements signals potential for recovery in profitability, making it a company to watch as it navigates through current challenges.
