Iran has announced the full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz to commercial vessels, a crucial maritime route for global oil and trade. However, President Donald Trump has reiterated that the U.S. blockade on Iranian ships and ports will remain in effect until a comprehensive deal addressing Iran's nuclear program is reached. This development comes amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East, particularly between the U.S., Israel, and Iran.

The reopening of the Strait could impact oil prices and shipping logistics, given that approximately 20% of the world's oil passes through this narrow waterway. Market participants will be closely monitoring how this situation evolves, especially in light of Trump's firm stance on sanctions, which could deter foreign investment and trade with Iran. The interplay between geopolitical tensions and energy markets remains a critical area of focus for traders.

As the situation develops, market professionals should watch for fluctuations in oil prices and shipping costs, which may present trading opportunities or risks depending on the outcome of U.S.-Iran negotiations.
