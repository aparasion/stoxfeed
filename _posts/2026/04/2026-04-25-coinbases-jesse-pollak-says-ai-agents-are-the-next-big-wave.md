Coinbase's Jesse Pollak highlights the transformative potential of AI agents in the crypto payments landscape, emphasizing the importance of the open-source protocol x402. Speaking ahead of Consensus Miami 2026, Pollak argues that as AI agents gain autonomy, they will increasingly rely on programmable payment systems, positioning crypto infrastructure like Base as essential for what he terms "agentic commerce." This shift points to a future where transactions occur seamlessly in the background, with users unaware they are utilizing blockchain technology.

Pollak notes that the x402 protocol, developed in collaboration with major players like Microsoft and Mastercard, enables on-demand API payments, streamlining transactions without traditional billing systems. Early adoption is evident, with approximately $48 million in payment volume processed through x402, predominantly on the Base network. This rapid integration across AI and service platforms suggests a burgeoning ecosystem where agents can autonomously access and pay for services.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for crypto to become a foundational layer in the evolving landscape of AI-driven transactions, emphasizing the need for infrastructure that supports seamless, invisible payment processes.
