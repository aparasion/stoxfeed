Sandisk (SNDK) has emerged as a standout performer in a turbulent market, skyrocketing 285% in 2026, driven by soaring demand for NAND flash storage chips. While the broader market grapples with volatility from the Middle East crisis and rising oil prices, the Nasdaq-100 index has only appreciated 5% this year. The surge in Sandisk's stock is attributed to a significant increase in NAND flash prices, which rose 246% in 2025, fueled by the booming demand from AI data centers requiring extensive storage solutions.

This trend is expected to continue, with market research firm Gartner projecting another 234% spike in NAND flash prices this year. Sandisk's earnings have already reflected this growth, with non-GAAP earnings reaching $7.55 per share in the first half of fiscal 2026, a 150% year-over-year increase. Analysts anticipate even stronger earnings in the coming quarters, suggesting that Sandisk could potentially double its stock price as demand for its products remains robust.

For market professionals, Sandisk presents a compelling investment opportunity, particularly as analysts forecast a 2.5-fold increase in earnings for fiscal 2027. With the stock still trading at a reasonable multiple relative to its growth potential, it may be worth considering for those looking to capitalize on the ongoing NAND flash market boom.
