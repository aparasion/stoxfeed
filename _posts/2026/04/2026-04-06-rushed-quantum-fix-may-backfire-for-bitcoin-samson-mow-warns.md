Samson Mow, founder of Jan3 and a vocal Bitcoin advocate, cautioned against hasty implementations of quantum-resistant solutions for Bitcoin in response to Coinbase executives’ calls for urgency. Mow emphasized that while post-quantum (PQ) cryptography could safeguard Bitcoin from future quantum threats, rushing its adoption might introduce new vulnerabilities, such as compatibility issues and inefficiencies due to significantly larger signature sizes. He warned that a poorly timed transition could leave Bitcoin exposed to current threats from conventional computing.

This discussion is particularly relevant as recent research from Google and Caltech has reignited concerns about advancements in quantum computing. Mow’s comments highlight the potential for a new wave of disputes reminiscent of the "Blocksize Wars," where performance issues could arise from increased transaction data sizes, further complicating Bitcoin’s scalability and security.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the importance of a measured approach to integrating quantum-resistant technologies, balancing future-proofing with current network integrity to avoid exacerbating existing vulnerabilities.
