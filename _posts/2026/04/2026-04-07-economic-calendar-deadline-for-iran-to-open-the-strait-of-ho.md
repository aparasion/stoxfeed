Tensions in the Middle East are escalating as President Trump has set a deadline for Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz, threatening to target its energy infrastructure if his demands are not met. While Iran has dismissed the ultimatum as “arrogant rhetoric,” U.S. officials view this as part of a broader negotiation strategy, suggesting that diplomatic talks are still ongoing. This geopolitical uncertainty is likely to weigh heavily on market sentiment, particularly in the energy sector.

As traders return from the holiday break, they will closely monitor both the unfolding situation in Iran and the upcoming March PMI data for the European services sector. These developments could influence stock performance, especially in energy-related equities and European markets, as investors gauge the potential for supply disruptions and economic implications.

Market professionals should prepare for volatility in oil prices and related stocks, as geopolitical tensions and economic data releases will drive trading decisions in the coming sessions.
