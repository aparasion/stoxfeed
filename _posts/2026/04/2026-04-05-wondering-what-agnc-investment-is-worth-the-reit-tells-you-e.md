AGNC Investment (AGNC) has drawn attention with its impressive 14% dividend yield, but investors should exercise caution. As a mortgage real estate investment trust (REIT), AGNC manages a portfolio of mortgage-backed securities and reports its tangible net book value (TBV) quarterly. While its TBV rose to $8.88 per share at the end of 2025, it has significantly declined from $22.59 in 2015, reflecting the challenges of its business model and a shrinking dividend.

The substantial yield may entice dividend-focused investors, but it's crucial to understand that AGNC's dividend has been decreasing over time. As mortgage payments are self-amortizing, investors are essentially receiving capital back, which impacts the overall value of their investment. Despite outperforming the S&P 500 in total return when dividends are reinvested, relying on AGNC for consistent income could be problematic.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to assess AGNC's stock price relative to its TBV. A price significantly above TBV may indicate overvaluation, emphasizing the need for a cautious approach when considering this high-yield investment.
