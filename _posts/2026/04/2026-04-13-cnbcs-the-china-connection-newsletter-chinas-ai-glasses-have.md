Rokid, a Chinese startup, is gaining traction in the smart glasses market, outperforming competitors like Alibaba despite a higher price point. The company's AI-powered frames, which feature a virtual display, have dominated sales on JD.com for the past three months, appealing particularly to business professionals for their teleprompting capabilities. This success comes as Rokid prepares for a global expansion and an anticipated IPO in Hong Kong.

The significance of Rokid's performance lies in its ability to capture a price-sensitive consumer base in China, where smart glasses are projected to see substantial growth. The global AI glasses market is expected to expand by over 70% this year, with Chinese sales predicted to double. Rokid's strategic integration of AI features, such as mobile payments and smart home connectivity, positions it well against competitors like Meta, which is facing delays in its product rollout.

For market professionals, Rokid's rise underscores the potential for innovative technology to disrupt established players in the consumer electronics space. As the smart glasses segment evolves, companies that can effectively leverage AI and consumer preferences may secure significant market share.
