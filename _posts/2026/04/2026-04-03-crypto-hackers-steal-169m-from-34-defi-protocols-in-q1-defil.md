In the first quarter of 2026, hackers siphoned off over $168.6 million from 34 decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols, a notable decline from the $1.58 billion stolen during the same period in 2025, according to DefiLlama. The largest breach this quarter involved a $40 million private key compromise at Step Finance, followed by a $26.4 million exploit at Truebit. Despite the decrease, experts caution that the threat of crypto hacks remains high, particularly during market upswings when liquidity is concentrated.

This trend highlights the evolving nature of cyber threats in the cryptocurrency sector. As market conditions improve, attackers are drawn to areas with significant value accumulation, making DeFi protocols particularly vulnerable. Nick Percoco, chief security officer at Kraken, emphasized that while attacks tend to spike during bullish phases, vulnerabilities can be exploited at any time, underscoring the need for continuous security measures.

Market professionals should remain vigilant, as the decline in thefts does not imply reduced risk. The landscape is shifting, with potential increases in sophisticated attacks, making robust security protocols essential for safeguarding assets in the rapidly evolving crypto environment.
