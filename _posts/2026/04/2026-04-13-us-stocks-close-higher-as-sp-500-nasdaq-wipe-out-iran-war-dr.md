US stocks closed higher, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq recovering from previous losses attributed to tensions surrounding the Iran conflict. Investor optimism surged as talks of a potential diplomatic resolution gained traction, lifting market sentiment and driving a broad-based rally across major indices.

The rebound is significant for the financial markets, as it underscores the sensitivity of equities to geopolitical developments. The S&P 500 climbed back above key technical levels, while the Nasdaq outperformed, buoyed by gains in technology stocks that had previously been under pressure. This shift could signal a renewed appetite for risk among investors, particularly in sectors that had been adversely affected by the uncertainty.

Market professionals should note that the potential for a diplomatic deal could lead to increased stability in oil prices and broader economic implications. As negotiations progress, monitoring sector-specific responses will be crucial for portfolio strategies moving forward.
