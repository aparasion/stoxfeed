President Donald Trump expressed optimism regarding ongoing peace talks with Iran, despite reports indicating that the crucial Strait of Hormuz has been closed to shipping traffic again. While Trump noted progress in negotiations, particularly around Iran's nuclear program, Iranian media claimed the U.S. failed to meet its obligations, leading to renewed restrictions on maritime traffic. This development follows a complex backdrop of U.S. and Israeli military actions against Iranian targets and a ceasefire agreement with Lebanon.

The implications for financial markets are significant, particularly in the oil sector. Oil prices dropped over 10% to below $90 per barrel as concerns about supply disruptions intensified. The Strait of Hormuz is a vital artery for global oil transport, and any blockage could exacerbate already volatile energy markets, impacting prices and potentially leading to broader economic ramifications.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving situation, as the potential for renewed military actions or further negotiations could drastically influence oil supply dynamics and pricing in the near term.
