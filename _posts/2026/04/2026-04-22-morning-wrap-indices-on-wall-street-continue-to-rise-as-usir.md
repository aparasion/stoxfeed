US equity futures gained ground following Donald Trump's announcement of an indefinite ceasefire with Iran, signaling a potential easing of geopolitical tensions. S&P 500 futures rose 0.2%, while Nasdaq 100 futures increased by 0.3%, as investors began to factor in the possibility of reduced oil price pressures and improved economic growth prospects. However, the US dollar weakened, indicating a cautious shift away from safe-haven assets.

Despite this positive momentum, the broader market remains cautious. US benchmark indices had declined for two consecutive days prior to this rebound, reflecting ongoing uncertainty regarding US-Iran negotiations. In the commodities market, Brent crude prices remained elevated around $98 per barrel, suggesting that geopolitical risks are still significant, particularly concerning supply disruptions in the Middle East.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the delicate balance between improved sentiment and persistent risks. While the short-term trend appears positive, caution is warranted as technical indicators suggest overbought conditions, with potential resistance levels around 26,930 and 27,100 for the US100 index.
