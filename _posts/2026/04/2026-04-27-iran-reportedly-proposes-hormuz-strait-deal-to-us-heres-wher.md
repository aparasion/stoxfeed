Global markets are grappling with a complex mix of resilient risk appetite and renewed geopolitical tensions following U.S. President Trump's decision to halt envoy negotiations with Iran. This move comes amidst Iran's latest proposal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and defer nuclear talks, highlighting ongoing uncertainty in energy markets. Oil prices reacted by inching higher, with Brent crude futures rising about 1% to $106.55 per barrel, as Goldman Sachs forecasts a prolonged period of elevated prices due to persistent supply disruptions.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Analysts are noting that while equities have shown surprising resilience, balancing geopolitical risks against strong structural drivers like AI, the sentiment may be stretched. The potential for volatility remains, with some strategists viewing it as an opportunity for investors to add risk assets. Meanwhile, the broader commodity complex is beginning to reflect deeper disruptions, particularly in natural gas and food supply chains, which could lead to inflationary pressures.

In summary, while the bull market appears intact, the interplay between technological advancements and ongoing energy shocks presents a complex landscape for investors. Monitoring developments in the Strait of Hormuz and their impact on supply chains will be crucial in the coming weeks.
