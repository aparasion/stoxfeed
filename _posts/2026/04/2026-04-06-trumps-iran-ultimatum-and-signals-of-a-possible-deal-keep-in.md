Investors are navigating a volatile landscape as President Trump's ultimatum to Iran intensifies geopolitical tensions surrounding the Strait of Hormuz. With oil prices surging—Brent crude hitting $109.77 per barrel—traders are weighing the likelihood of a diplomatic resolution against the potential for further escalation. Trump's mixed signals have left markets on edge, with analysts warning of significant inflation risks and a looming stagflation scenario if the conflict persists.

The ongoing war has severely disrupted global energy supplies, with shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz plummeting to 95% below pre-war levels. This disruption is reflected in rising bond yields, with the 10-year Treasury yield climbing to 4.362%, as investors adjust their inflation outlook and reconsider interest rate expectations from the Federal Reserve. The situation has created a highly reactive market environment, characterized by sharp swings in response to headlines.

As traders brace for a pivotal week, the key takeaway is the necessity for adaptive positioning amid heightened uncertainty. With economic data releases on the horizon, including the February personal consumption expenditures index, market participants must remain vigilant to navigate the binary outcomes of potential diplomatic breakthroughs or escalated conflict.
