Lebanon is urgently seeking clarity on its status regarding a newly announced ceasefire in the ongoing conflict involving Iran and its allies, as mixed signals emerge from regional powers. Lebanese Economy Minister Amer Bisat stated that while some reports suggest Lebanon is included in the ceasefire, Israeli officials have explicitly contradicted this, asserting that the truce does not extend to Lebanon. This uncertainty comes amid escalating tensions, with Israel conducting strikes against Hezbollah targets in Lebanon, leading to significant humanitarian and economic repercussions for the country.

The conflict has already inflicted a devastating toll on Lebanon's economy, with Bisat estimating a loss of 5-7% of GDP in just five weeks. As over 1.1 million people have been displaced, the humanitarian crisis compounds the economic challenges Lebanon faces, which had shown signs of recovery prior to the recent hostilities. 

Market professionals should monitor the situation closely, as the stability of the ceasefire will have implications for regional security, oil prices, and broader market sentiment, particularly if further escalations occur.
