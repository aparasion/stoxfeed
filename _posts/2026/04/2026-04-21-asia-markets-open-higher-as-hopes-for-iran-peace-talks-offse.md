Asia-Pacific markets opened higher on Tuesday, buoyed by optimism for a resolution to the ongoing Middle East conflict, despite escalating tensions between Iran and the U.S. Iranian parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf warned against negotiations under threat, while President Trump issued a stark warning about potential military action if a ceasefire is not reached. The geopolitical backdrop is contributing to cautious investor sentiment as peace talks loom.

The broader market response reflects a bullish outlook among investors, with Wells Fargo's chief equity strategist Ohsung Kwon expressing confidence in the economy's stability over the next three months. Notably, the West Texas Intermediate and Brent crude futures saw declines, indicating a potential easing of oil price pressures amid these developments. In Asia, major indices like South Korea's Kospi and Japan's Nikkei 225 posted gains, signaling positive sentiment in the region.

One key takeaway for market professionals is the resilience of equities in the face of geopolitical tensions, suggesting that investors are prioritizing long-term economic fundamentals over short-term volatility.
