Oil prices surged sharply as President Donald Trump signaled potential military escalation against Iran, undermining hopes for a quick resolution to the ongoing conflict. U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude futures climbed 4.1% to $104.21 per barrel, while Brent crude futures rose 5% to $106.42. Trump's remarks, which included a warning of "extreme" actions against Iran, have heightened market volatility and risk aversion among investors, as they brace for prolonged uncertainty.

The disruption of oil tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical transit route for global oil supplies, has compounded the situation. Analysts indicate that the U.S. has largely stepped back from ensuring safe passage, leaving stakeholders to navigate the increasingly perilous environment independently. This uncertainty is likely to keep energy prices elevated as traders react to geopolitical developments.

Market professionals should prepare for continued volatility in oil prices, with potential for further spikes as military tensions persist and diplomatic efforts remain tenuous.
