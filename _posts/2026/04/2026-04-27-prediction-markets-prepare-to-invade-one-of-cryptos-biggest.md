A significant shift is underway in the U.S. crypto landscape as prediction markets Kalshi and Polymarket aim to capitalize on the burgeoning demand for perpetual futures trading. These contracts, which allow for high leverage and have surged in popularity since the Trump administration, now account for over 70% of centralized crypto exchange volume. With trading volumes reaching $61.7 trillion in 2025, the potential for U.S. onshore perpetual futures could reshape trading dynamics and introduce new volatility to the market.

The entry of prediction markets into the perpetual futures arena could intensify competition with established platforms like Robinhood and Coinbase. Analysts suggest this move is more defensive, aimed at safeguarding market share rather than aggressively disrupting existing players. However, the combination of prediction markets and leveraged trading raises concerns about increased volatility and regulatory scrutiny, particularly given past incidents of market manipulation.

If successful, the expansion of perpetual futures could extend beyond crypto, potentially impacting other asset classes like equities and commodities. This development warrants close attention from market professionals, as it may redefine trading strategies and risk management practices across the financial landscape.
