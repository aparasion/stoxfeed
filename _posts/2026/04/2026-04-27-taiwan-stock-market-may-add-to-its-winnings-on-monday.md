The Taiwan stock market has surged for three consecutive sessions, gaining over 775 points or 3.2%, and is poised for further gains as it opens above the 23,550-point mark on Monday. This rally is buoyed by a positive global outlook on interest rates, although some profit-taking may occur later in the day. On Friday, the Taiwan Stock Exchange rose modestly, driven by financial shares, while technology and plastics sectors exhibited mixed performance.

The broader Asian market sentiment appears optimistic, influenced by strong U.S. market performance, where major indices reached record highs following a favorable jobs report. The report indicated robust job growth but also highlighted an uptick in the unemployment rate, leading to speculation about potential interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve. This backdrop is likely to support continued investor interest in Taiwanese equities, particularly in the financial sector.

Market professionals should monitor the potential for profit-taking in Taiwan, as well as the implications of U.S. economic data on regional market sentiment and sector performance.
