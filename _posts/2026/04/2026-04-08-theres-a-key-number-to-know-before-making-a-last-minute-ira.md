As the April 15 tax deadline approaches, investors are ramping up contributions to individual retirement accounts (IRAs), with Fidelity Investments reporting an 18% increase in average IRA deposits in the two weeks leading up to March 20. Notably, nearly 75% of these contributions are directed toward after-tax Roth IRAs, highlighting a shift in investor preference. The IRA contribution limit for 2025 stands at $7,000, with an additional $1,000 for those aged 50 and older, but eligibility for Roth contributions is contingent on modified adjusted gross income (MAGI), which can complicate decision-making.

This surge in contributions is significant as it reflects broader trends in retirement planning and tax strategy, with many investors seeking the tax-free growth potential of Roth IRAs over traditional options. However, understanding the nuances of MAGI calculations and eligibility criteria is crucial for maximizing tax benefits and aligning contributions with long-term financial goals.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to advise clients not just to rush contributions due to the impending deadline, but to ensure that their choices align with their overall financial strategy and tax situation.
