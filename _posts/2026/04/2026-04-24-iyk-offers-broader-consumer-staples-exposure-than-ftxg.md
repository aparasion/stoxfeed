The iShares U.S. Consumer Staples ETF (IYK) and First Trust Nasdaq Food & Beverage ETF (FTXG) offer distinct investment approaches within the consumer staples sector, with IYK emerging as the more cost-effective and liquid option. IYK boasts a lower expense ratio of 0.38% compared to FTXG’s 0.60%, alongside a broader portfolio that includes major players like Procter & Gamble and Coca-Cola. In contrast, FTXG focuses narrowly on the food and beverage segment, which may appeal to investors looking for targeted exposure but comes with higher fees and limited liquidity.

Given the stability of consumer staples, these ETFs serve as defensive positions during economic uncertainty. IYK's larger asset base and diversified holdings have allowed it to perform better in recent volatile markets, while FTXG has struggled with steeper drawdowns and lower trading volume. 

For investors prioritizing cost and resilience, IYK appears to be the superior choice, while those with a specific interest in food and beverage may consider supplementing their strategy with FTXG or individual stocks in that space.
