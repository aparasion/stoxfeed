Commercial ship traffic through the Strait of Hormuz saw a temporary surge over the weekend, with at least 20 vessels transiting the critical waterway, including the oil tanker FPMC C Lord carrying 2 million barrels of Saudi crude to Taiwan. However, following a series of attacks on commercial ships, including incidents involving Iranian forces, traffic plummeted on Sunday, highlighting the ongoing volatility in the region.

The immediate impact on financial markets was evident, as oil prices spiked approximately 6% on Monday amid concerns over supply disruptions. The situation underscores the fragility of maritime security in a key oil transit route, which could have broader implications for energy markets and shipping costs if tensions escalate further.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as sustained disruptions could lead to increased volatility in oil prices and affect the broader energy sector, particularly if shipping routes remain compromised.
