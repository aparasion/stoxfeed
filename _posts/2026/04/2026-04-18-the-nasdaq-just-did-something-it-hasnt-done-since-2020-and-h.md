The Nasdaq-100 index recently rebounded to a record high after a tumultuous period marked by geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices. Following a ceasefire agreement between the U.S. and Iran, which had previously escalated oil prices and raised concerns about consumer spending, the index surged 17% from March 30 to April 17, marking its strongest performance over a 13-day period since the COVID-19 recovery in 2020.

This resurgence is significant for the financial markets, particularly as the technology sector, which comprises 60% of the Nasdaq-100, faces potential headwinds. Concerns about reduced AI infrastructure spending, highlighted by OpenAI's revised projections, could impact major players like Oracle, Microsoft, and chip suppliers such as Nvidia and AMD. Despite the recent highs, many tech stocks remain below their peaks, indicating investor caution amid ongoing supply chain issues and energy costs.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach investments in the Nasdaq-100 with a balanced strategy. While the index's long-term growth trajectory remains strong, dollar-cost averaging may be prudent in the face of potential volatility stemming from the AI sector and broader economic uncertainties.
