Kratos Defense & Security Solutions (NASDAQ: KTOS) saw its stock tumble 7.8% today, despite announcing a significant contract with the U.S. Space Force valued at up to $446.8 million. This contract is intended to enhance ground operations for the Resilient Missile Warning and Tracking program, which aligns with Kratos' ongoing business expansion. However, the stock's decline highlights a "sell the news" mentality among investors, who may be reacting to the company's already rich valuation.

Despite the positive contract news, Kratos shares have been trading at a premium, with a price-to-sales ratio of approximately 8, indicating that much of the good news may have already been priced in. This decline follows a more than 20% drop in the stock over the past month, suggesting that investors are reallocating funds in search of better opportunities.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Kratos has a strong underlying business, its current valuation may deter new investment until the stock consolidates further.
