As trading winds down ahead of the Easter weekend, market participants are bracing for significant volatility, particularly with the upcoming U.S. Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) data set for release at 2:30 p.m. today. This data could provide crucial insights into the labor market and influence market sentiment as most global markets will see reduced activity over the holiday.

In Asia, economic indicators show mixed signals. China's Caixin Services PMI fell sharply to 52.1 in March, indicating a slowdown in growth, particularly in export orders and employment. Meanwhile, Japan's services PMI also dipped slightly to 53.4, suggesting waning momentum amid rising energy costs. These developments could weigh on regional equities, despite Japan and South Korea’s stock markets posting gains today. Additionally, escalating tensions in the Middle East have pushed oil prices to multi-year highs, with WTI and Brent crude rising significantly, which may impact inflation and energy-related stocks.

The key takeaway for investors is to monitor the NFP data closely, as strong employment figures could bolster U.S. equities and further drive oil prices, while any signs of economic weakness might prompt a reassessment of growth expectations across sectors.
