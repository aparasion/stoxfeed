Tesla's latest earnings report has intensified the ongoing debate between bulls and bears regarding its stock, with both sides finding elements to support their arguments. While the report highlighted a resurgence in electric vehicle (EV) demand, particularly in Europe, it also revealed delays in the anticipated rollout of robotaxis and unsupervised full self-driving (FSD) capabilities. This mixed outcome underscores the tension between current performance metrics and future growth potential.

The report indicated a 16.4% increase in FSD subscriptions and a significant backlog of orders, suggesting that Tesla's core EV business remains robust. However, CEO Elon Musk's cautious approach to the robotaxi rollout—now expected to take longer than previously indicated—may dampen investor enthusiasm. The anticipated capital expenditures, projected to reach at least $25 billion by 2026, further complicate the outlook.

Investors should brace for potential near-term volatility as the market digests these mixed signals. While Tesla's strong EV sales and growing FSD subscriptions are encouraging, the slower-than-expected rollout of robotaxis could lead to a reassessment of the stock's valuation in the coming months.
