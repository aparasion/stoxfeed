Investors are closely monitoring the upcoming U.S.–Iran negotiations in Islamabad, which are expected to influence market sentiment ahead of a ceasefire deadline on Wednesday. This diplomatic focus, coupled with easing geopolitical tensions, has supported a rally in major stock indices and boosted Bitcoin's recent performance, extending last week's gains.

Bitcoin's market dynamics reveal a pattern of rallies followed by swift pullbacks, indicating a struggle to maintain momentum at critical resistance levels. Institutional interest remains strong, with large holders accumulating Bitcoin during dips, which has helped stabilize prices. Analysts suggest that Bitcoin could test the $82,000–$83,000 range, although a drop below $75,000 may signal a bearish trend.

As market participants await the confirmation hearing for Kevin Warsh, President Trump’s nominee for Federal Reserve Chair, his insights on monetary policy and financial oversight could significantly impact digital asset regulation and market structure in the U.S. This development is crucial for investors assessing the future landscape of cryptocurrencies.
