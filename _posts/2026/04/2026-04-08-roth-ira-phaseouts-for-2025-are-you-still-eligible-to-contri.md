The deadline for contributing to a Roth IRA for the 2025 tax year is approaching, with April 15 marking the last day to make contributions. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, making them an attractive option for retirement savings. However, potential contributors must navigate income restrictions that could limit their eligibility based on modified adjusted gross income (MAGI). 

For 2025, the contribution limit is set at $7,000 per person, but those earning above specific thresholds may see their contribution amounts phased out entirely. For instance, a married couple with a MAGI of $241,000 would only be eligible to contribute half of the limit. Notably, individuals who exceed these limits might consider a backdoor Roth IRA strategy, which involves contributing to a traditional IRA before rolling over to a Roth IRA, although this can trigger tax implications if pre-tax funds are involved.

Market professionals should be aware of these IRA contribution limits and strategies, as they can influence individual investment behaviors and overall market participation in retirement accounts.
