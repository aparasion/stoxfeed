European stocks are poised to open lower on Wednesday as U.S.-Iran peace talks have stalled, exacerbating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. Following failed negotiations, President Trump has extended the ceasefire with Iran indefinitely, while also maintaining a naval blockade that Iran has labeled an act of war. In a bid to disrupt Iran's military capabilities, the U.S. has imposed sanctions on multiple individuals and entities linked to Iran, further complicating the geopolitical landscape.

This escalation is likely to weigh on market sentiment, particularly in sectors sensitive to oil prices and geopolitical risks. Brent crude remains elevated above $98 per barrel amid fears of potential supply disruptions, while gold has rebounded after a recent decline. U.S. stocks also ended lower, reflecting investor concerns over the implications of ongoing tensions for economic stability and corporate earnings.

Market professionals should monitor developments closely, as continued instability in the region could lead to increased volatility in commodity prices and broader market indices.
