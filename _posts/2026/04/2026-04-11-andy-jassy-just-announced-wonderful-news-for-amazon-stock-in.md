Amazon (AMZN +2.05%) continues to present compelling investment opportunities, as highlighted by CEO Andy Jassy in a recent shareholder letter. With a market cap of $2.6 trillion, the company has established itself as a leader in both e-commerce and cloud computing, sectors that are still believed to be underpenetrated despite their current prominence. Jassy noted that while Amazon's retail business approaches $600 billion, a staggering 80% of global retail sales still occur in physical stores, indicating significant growth potential. Similarly, Amazon Web Services (AWS) is on a $142 billion revenue run rate, yet 85% of global IT spending remains on-premises.

For investors, this suggests that Amazon's long-term prospects remain robust, particularly as it leverages its established brand, extensive infrastructure, and network effects to maintain its competitive edge. Given the recent market volatility, now may be an opportune time for long-term investors to consider adding Amazon to their portfolios, positioning themselves for potential outsized returns over the next two decades.
