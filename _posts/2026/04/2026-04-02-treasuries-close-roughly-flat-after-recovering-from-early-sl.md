Treasuries exhibited a lack of direction on Thursday, closing roughly flat after an initial dip. The yield on the benchmark ten-year note decreased slightly to 4.313 percent, following a high of 4.362 percent earlier in the session. Market sentiment was influenced by President Trump's recent address, which raised concerns about escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly regarding Iran. His remarks, while reiterating optimism about the conflict’s resolution, included threats of significant military action, leading to heightened volatility in crude oil prices.

The immediate market reaction saw U.S. crude oil futures surge over 10%, reversing earlier declines amid hopes for a swift resolution. However, a report from Iran indicating cooperation with Oman to ensure safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz helped stabilize sentiment. On the domestic front, initial jobless claims unexpectedly fell to 202,000, suggesting resilience in the labor market, which could have broader implications for economic growth and interest rates.

For market professionals, the interplay between geopolitical tensions and economic indicators highlights the importance of monitoring both international developments and domestic economic data, as they can significantly impact asset prices and yield movements.
