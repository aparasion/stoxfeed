Meta Platforms (NASDAQ: META) is attracting bullish sentiment from analysts, with projections suggesting the stock could reach as high as $1,631 by 2030. Currently trading around $677, Meta’s strong performance is underpinned by its robust digital advertising business and increasing daily active users, which reached 3.01 billion—a 2% year-over-year rise. The company's investments in artificial intelligence are seen as a key driver for future revenue growth, even as analysts express caution about rising debt levels and competitive pressures.

The stock boasts a market cap of $1.71 trillion, with a trailing P/E ratio of 28.82, aligning with its historical averages. Despite a recent dip in stock price due to less favorable 2025 results, the consensus Buy rating and average price target of $846.57 indicate a potential 17% upside from current levels. However, risks such as decelerating ad revenue growth and increased competition in AI remain critical considerations for investors.

For market professionals, Meta's combination of a strong user base, ongoing AI initiatives, and attractive valuation metrics make it a compelling long-term investment, albeit with notable risks that could impact its growth trajectory.
