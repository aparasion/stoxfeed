European stocks are poised to open lower on Monday as escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly around the closed Strait of Hormuz, lead to a spike in crude oil prices. Iran's refusal to engage in further peace talks under U.S. pressure has heightened concerns, with accusations of international law violations and military provocations surfacing. The situation has contributed to a more than 5% increase in Brent crude prices, now exceeding $95 a barrel, reflecting market anxiety over potential supply disruptions.

This geopolitical instability comes at a critical time, with major U.S. companies like 3M, UnitedHealth, and Tesla set to report quarterly earnings this week. Investors will be watching closely for any impact on stock performance and sector dynamics, especially in energy and defense. Additionally, the Federal Reserve's upcoming testimony by Kevin Warsh could influence market sentiment amid these developments.

Market professionals should prepare for volatility as geopolitical tensions intersect with earnings season, potentially impacting both European and U.S. equity markets.
