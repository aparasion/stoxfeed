The S&P 500 has seen a 3.8% decline this year as geopolitical tensions, particularly the war in Iran, have driven Brent crude oil prices up by approximately 50%. This surge in oil prices is impacting consumer spending, particularly on discretionary items, as rising gas prices force consumers to tighten their budgets. However, this environment may present an attractive entry point for long-term investors in consumer discretionary stocks, particularly Chipotle Mexican Grill (CMG).

Despite a 10.4% drop in its stock price this year, Chipotle remains a compelling investment due to its strong brand and consistent same-restaurant sales growth over the years. While the company faced challenges last year, including a 1.7% decline in comps, its management projects continued expansion, with plans to open up to 370 new locations this year. The current price-to-earnings ratio of 29 is significantly lower than its five-year median of 53, suggesting that Chipotle shares may be undervalued.

For market professionals, Chipotle represents a potential bargain within the S&P 500, particularly for those looking to capitalize on long-term growth opportunities despite short-term economic pressures.
