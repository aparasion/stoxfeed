As retirement approaches, many individuals face the significant transition of relying on savings rather than a steady paycheck, with Social Security playing a crucial role in this shift. However, understanding the limitations of Social Security is essential, as it is designed to replace only about 40% of pre-retirement income for average earners. This means retirees often need to supplement their income from other sources to cover their expenses effectively.

The calculation of Social Security benefits is based on the highest-earning 35 years of an individual's work history, adjusted for inflation. Factors such as the age of claiming benefits and current income can significantly impact the monthly payout. Given the looming insolvency of the Social Security program, potential changes to benefits or tax structures could further complicate retirement planning.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to proactively assess retirement income strategies now, considering the potential for reduced Social Security benefits in the future. This foresight can help ensure financial stability in retirement, particularly as the landscape of Social Security evolves.
