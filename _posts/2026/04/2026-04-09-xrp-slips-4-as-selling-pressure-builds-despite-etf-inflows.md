XRP has experienced a 4% decline, slipping from approximately $1.37 to $1.33 as selling pressure intensifies, despite a modest $3.32 million in ETF inflows. Traders are observing that rising volume coinciding with falling prices indicates distribution rather than accumulation, suggesting that sellers are using price rallies to exit their positions. The cryptocurrency's inability to maintain gains above $1.35 signals continued weakness, with immediate support at $1.33 and a critical level at $1.28 that, if breached, could accelerate further downside.

The current market dynamics show XRP underperforming compared to the broader market, with significant resistance around $1.37 to $1.38. This trend reflects a potential rotation of capital away from XRP, as liquidity on exchanges has thinned, increasing the risk for sharper price movements once key support levels are tested. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of monitoring the support levels at $1.33 and $1.28, as well as the resistance at $1.35 and $1.38, to gauge XRP's short-term momentum and overall market sentiment.
