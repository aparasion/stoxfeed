Apple (AAPL) is poised for a leadership transition as Tim Cook steps down as CEO, handing the reins to John Ternus, the current senior vice president of hardware engineering, effective September 1. This change comes as Apple continues to dominate the smartphone market, with the iPhone retaining its position as the best-selling device globally. Under Cook's tenure, Apple expanded its services revenue to over $100 billion, capitalizing on its massive user base of more than 2.5 billion active devices.

However, Apple's slow adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies has raised concerns, especially as competitors have quickly integrated these innovations, boosting their stock performance. Ternus, known for his product engineering expertise, may be tasked with enhancing Apple’s AI capabilities and driving further product innovation, key areas that could influence future growth.

Investors might consider this transition a strategic opportunity. With Ternus at the helm and Cook remaining as executive chairman, the potential for a renewed focus on AI and product development could signal a new growth phase for Apple, making it an attractive stock to buy now.
