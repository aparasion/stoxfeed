US stocks closed mostly higher, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq reaching record highs, driven by optimism surrounding the ongoing conflict in Iran. Investors are responding positively to the potential for diplomatic resolutions and the implications for oil supply stability, which has been a significant concern in recent weeks.

The surge in these indices reflects broader market sentiment that geopolitical tensions may lead to favorable conditions for economic growth. Energy stocks, in particular, have shown volatility as traders react to fluctuating oil prices, while tech shares continue to benefit from a risk-on environment. This upward momentum suggests that market participants are weighing the potential for a rebound in corporate earnings amid improving geopolitical dynamics.

Market professionals should note the resilience of the S&P 500 and Nasdaq, as their record closes could signal a sustained bullish trend, offering investment opportunities across sectors that may benefit from a stabilizing geopolitical landscape.
