U.S. equity markets are temporarily closed for the holiday, providing investors a brief respite amid heightened volatility driven by geopolitical tensions, particularly the Iranian conflict. Following a turbulent trading session, U.S. index futures are experiencing slight corrections, but the focus remains on the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, set to release at 2:30 PM CET. This report will be critical for Federal Reserve members as they prepare for their month-end interest rate meeting.

The NFP data is expected to show a rebound in payrolls, with a forecasted increase of 65,000 jobs in March, following a decline of 92,000 in February. However, concerns linger over inflationary pressures exacerbated by the Iranian war, as energy prices remain a significant factor. With unemployment steady at 4.4%, the Fed's approach to interest rates may hinge on whether recent inflation spikes are a temporary blip or a sign of persistent pressure.

Market participants should closely monitor the NFP results, as any significant deviation from expectations could lead to notable shifts in currency pairs, particularly EURUSD, and influence the Fed’s monetary policy direction.
