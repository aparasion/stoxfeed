NuScale Power (NYSE: SMR) is facing significant challenges, with its stock plummeting nearly 30% since the beginning of 2026, despite a brief rally in April. The company, which specializes in small modular reactors (SMRs), is operating in a promising market projected to be worth $10 trillion. However, it continues to struggle financially, posting a net loss of over $660 million last year, marking its fourth consecutive year of losses.

The implications for NuScale Power are concerning, as analysts predict that the company may not achieve profitability for at least the next two fiscal years, with some suggesting it could take even longer. This extended timeline raises questions about its ability to secure necessary capital, especially as its market capitalization has dropped to $4 billion from $8 billion just months ago. The reliance on external funding sources, coupled with a challenging market environment, could hinder its growth and operational viability.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential liquidity risk facing NuScale Power. As the company navigates a difficult financial landscape, the ability to raise capital on favorable terms may become increasingly constrained, impacting both its strategic initiatives and shareholder value.
