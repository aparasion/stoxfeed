ServiceNow (NOW) has seen its stock plummet nearly 50% since last summer amid a broader sell-off in software stocks, primarily driven by the rise of artificial intelligence (AI). Despite doubling its free cash flow over the past three years and maintaining a strong presence within Fortune 500 companies, ServiceNow’s valuation has reached a low point at approximately 26 times trailing free cash flow. The company’s core asset, its Configuration Management Database, remains critical for large organizations, but the advent of AI poses a potential threat to its traditional business model.

The shift towards AI has significant implications for ServiceNow's financial health and growth trajectory. While the company is investing heavily—approximately $11 billion in acquisitions to bolster its AI and security capabilities—its traditional growth model may face challenges as 85% of large companies are already using its services. The introduction of its generative AI product, Now Assist, could further strain margins due to increased computing costs.

Market professionals should note that ServiceNow's declining stock reflects investor concerns over its ability to maintain competitive advantage amid AI advancements. As the landscape evolves, the company must navigate the dual pressures of enhancing its AI offerings while retaining its established customer base to sustain profitability.
