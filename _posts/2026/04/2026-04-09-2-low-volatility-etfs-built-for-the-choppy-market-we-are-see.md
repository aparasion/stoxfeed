April has historically been a turbulent month for markets, and this year is no exception, with geopolitical tensions, notably the conflict in Iran, creating significant uncertainty. The S&P 500 has faced volatility, reminiscent of past April declines, as investors grapple with inflation and fluctuating energy prices. Amid this backdrop, market professionals are advised to consider both high-upside bargain opportunities and stable investments to mitigate risk.

Two exchange-traded funds (ETFs) stand out as strategic options during these uncertain times. The Federated Hermes U.S. Strategic Dividend ETF (FDV) focuses on high-dividend-paying U.S. stocks, providing a lower volatility profile with a beta of 0.59. It has delivered solid returns, up 7.5% year to date. Meanwhile, the Franklin International Low Volatility High Dividend ETF (LVHI) offers global diversification and has outperformed with a 36.5% return over the past year, showcasing its resilience in challenging market conditions.

For professionals navigating this volatile landscape, these ETFs represent a prudent approach to balancing risk and return, emphasizing the importance of incorporating low-volatility, dividend-focused investments into portfolios.
