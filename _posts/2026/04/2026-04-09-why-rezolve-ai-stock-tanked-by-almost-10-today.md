Rezolve AI (RZLV) saw its shares tumble nearly 10% on Thursday as investors reacted to an escalating hostile takeover attempt of fintech Commerce.com (CMRC). The AI-focused company proposed an unsolicited buyout offering two shares of Rezolve for every share of Commerce.com, which the latter's board deemed a "significantly lowball offer" that undervalued its stock by 47%. Commerce.com has publicly rejected further engagement, prompting Rezolve to appeal directly to its shareholders with a critical message about the potential benefits of the deal.

This situation highlights the volatility that can arise from aggressive acquisition strategies, particularly when the target company perceives the offer as inadequate. The market's lukewarm reception to Rezolve's bid suggests a lack of confidence in the viability of the merger, which could impact both companies' stock performance moving forward.

Market professionals should monitor this unfolding scenario closely, as the dynamics of hostile takeovers can create significant trading opportunities and influence investor sentiment in the tech and fintech sectors.
