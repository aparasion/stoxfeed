Apple (AAPL) is gearing up to launch its first foldable iPhone this September, entering a market that has been dominated by competitors since 2019. Despite facing technical challenges related to design and durability, the device is expected to be priced above $2,000, positioning it as a high-end offering. While foldable smartphones have not captured significant interest among U.S. consumers, Apple's loyal customer base may drive sales, particularly in China, where demand for such devices is growing rapidly.

The introduction of the foldable iPhone could provide a sales boost, especially as Apple aims to leverage its ecosystem to encourage upgrades. While the stock may not be an immediate buy based solely on this new product, the potential for increased sales and subscriptions from an expanded user base remains a key consideration. Apple's strong brand loyalty and successful service model continue to underpin its long-term growth strategy.

Investors should view the foldable iPhone as a strategic move to enhance Apple's ecosystem rather than a standalone reason to buy the stock.
