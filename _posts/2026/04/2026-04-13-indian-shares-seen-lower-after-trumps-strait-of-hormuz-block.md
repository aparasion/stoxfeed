Indian shares are expected to open significantly lower on Monday following the collapse of U.S.-Iran peace talks, which has escalated tensions in the Middle East. President Trump’s directive to block all maritime traffic in and out of the Strait of Hormuz raises concerns about potential disruptions to vital energy supplies. As a result, Brent crude prices surged nearly 8% to around $103 a barrel, intensifying fears of renewed inflationary pressures.

The fallout from these developments is likely to ripple through global markets, with Asian stocks already showing caution and gold prices dipping as bond yields rise. U.S. consumer sentiment has also taken a hit, reflecting growing anxiety over the geopolitical situation and inflation expectations. While the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite managed a slight gain, the S&P 500 and Dow Jones experienced minor declines, indicating a mixed response to the broader market conditions.

Market professionals should brace for heightened volatility and potential impacts on energy-related stocks, as well as broader implications for inflation and consumer sentiment in the coming weeks.
