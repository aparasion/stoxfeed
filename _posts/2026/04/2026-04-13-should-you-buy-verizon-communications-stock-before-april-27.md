Verizon Communications (VZ) has seen a notable 12% increase in its stock this year, buoyed by strong Q4 earnings that exceeded expectations, including the highest net additions since 2019. With the upcoming earnings report on April 27, investors are keenly watching whether the company can sustain this momentum under new CEO Dan Schulman, who emphasizes a focus on healthy growth and fiscal responsibility.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. If Verizon can replicate its recent success in Q1, it could signal a turnaround in a company that has struggled to attract investor interest in recent years. Moreover, with a low price-to-earnings ratio of nine and a robust dividend yield of 6.2%, Verizon remains an attractive option for buy-and-hold investors, even if the upcoming results fall short of expectations.

In summary, Verizon presents a compelling case for investment, combining potential capital appreciation with a reliable income stream, especially as it expands its fiber network following the acquisition of Frontier.
