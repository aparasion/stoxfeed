India's textile industry faces renewed challenges as the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict disrupts supply chains and inflates costs, undermining hopes for a recovery. After a brief respite from U.S. tariffs, which had previously made Indian exports less competitive, the war has led to increased prices for raw materials and packaging, pushing some workers to leave the industry. The sector, which employs over 45 million people, is now grappling with reduced demand and production cuts, as companies like Filatex India have already scaled back output by 25%.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. With India's textile exports declining slightly to $29.5 billion, the industry is now projected to grow at a lower rate of around 9% instead of the anticipated 12-15% CAGR. If demand continues to weaken, especially in the U.S. market, companies may struggle to maintain profitability, echoing the challenges faced during the Ukraine conflict that previously dampened retail sales.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the textile sector's recovery is contingent on geopolitical stability and consumer demand. Without a lasting resolution to the conflict, India's ambitions to reach $100 billion in textile exports by 2030 may remain elusive, necessitating close monitoring of both geopolitical developments and market responses.
