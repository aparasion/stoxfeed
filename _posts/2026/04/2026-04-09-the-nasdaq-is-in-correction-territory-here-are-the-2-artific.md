The Nasdaq Composite has officially entered correction territory, dropping about 10% from its previous highs, primarily due to macroeconomic uncertainties, including tariff concerns, geopolitical tensions, and a rotation away from high-valued tech stocks. Despite this turbulence, long-term investors see corrections as opportunities, particularly in the artificial intelligence (AI) infrastructure sector, which remains robust amid the market's volatility. Notably, Micron Technology and Broadcom are emerging as key players in this space.

Micron is positioned as a critical supplier of high-bandwidth memory (HBM) essential for AI models, with its competitive edge bolstered by a significant ramp-up in manufacturing capacity. Trading at roughly six times forward earnings, Micron's stock is viewed as undervalued compared to peers, especially given its triple-digit revenue growth and expected quadrupling of earnings per share this year. Meanwhile, Broadcom specializes in custom silicon for AI applications, benefiting from long-term customer relationships and a stable cash flow from its VMware software, making it a compelling investment at around 28 times forward earnings.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that both Micron and Broadcom present attractive opportunities amid the Nasdaq's correction, given their integral roles in the AI infrastructure buildout and their potential for sustained growth in a challenging economic landscape.
