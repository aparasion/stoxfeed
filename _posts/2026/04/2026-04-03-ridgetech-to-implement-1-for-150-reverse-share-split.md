Ridgetech, Inc. (RDGT) has announced a 1-for-150 reverse share split, set to take effect on April 7, 2026. This move will reduce the outstanding shares from approximately 134.8 million to about 898,906, adjusting for fractional shares. The reverse split aims to consolidate the company's equity structure, which may help improve its stock price and appeal to institutional investors.

The decision comes as RDGT's stock has been under pressure, closing at $0.0246 with an 11.51% decline on the Nasdaq prior to the announcement. Reverse splits are often employed by companies to meet minimum listing requirements or to enhance perceived value, but they can also signal underlying issues. 

For market professionals, this development highlights the volatility and challenges facing RDGT. Investors should monitor how the reverse split impacts trading liquidity and investor sentiment in the coming weeks, as well as any potential implications for the company’s future performance.
