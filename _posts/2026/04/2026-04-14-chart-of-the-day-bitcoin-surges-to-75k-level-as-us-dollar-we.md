Bitcoin has surged back above $74,000, marking a notable relief rally amid a broader resurgence of risk appetite in financial markets. The uptick follows a softening geopolitical climate surrounding Iran and the Strait of Hormuz, which has encouraged capital flows back into cryptocurrencies, equities, and other higher-beta assets. Bitcoin's 1.7% rise to $74,500, alongside Ethereum's impressive 7.9% gain, signals a constructive yet fragile market environment driven by macroeconomic factors.

This rally is not isolated to crypto; U.S. equities also rebounded, reflecting a collective risk-on sentiment. The easing of geopolitical tensions has contributed to falling oil prices, which in turn alleviates inflationary pressures and supports risk assets. However, the market remains headline-driven, with critical resistance levels between $72,000 and $75,000 determining the sustainability of this upward momentum.

For investors, the key takeaway is to monitor ETF inflows and Bitcoin dominance closely. While the current market structure shows promise, the fragility of the geopolitical landscape and sensitivity to new developments mean that caution is warranted as the market navigates this event-driven terrain.
