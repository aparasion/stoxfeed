Decentralized email platform Dmail Network is shutting down after five years, citing high infrastructure costs, poor monetization, and failed funding efforts. The company will begin ceasing services on May 15, urging users to export their data before all nodes become inaccessible. Despite achieving significant user engagement—ranking second among AI DApps in January 2025 with 4.9 million active wallets—Dmail struggled to sustain its business model amid rising operational expenses and a lack of viable monetization strategies.

This closure underscores the broader challenges faced by Web3 projects, where user activity alone is insufficient to maintain infrastructure-heavy operations. Dmail's difficulties reflect a trend of increasing pressures on decentralized platforms, as seen in the recent shutdowns of other projects like Tally and Balancer Labs, which have also cited weak demand and funding issues.

Market professionals should note the implications for investor sentiment in the Web3 space, as Dmail's token has already hit an all-time low, signaling potential caution among stakeholders in similar ventures.
