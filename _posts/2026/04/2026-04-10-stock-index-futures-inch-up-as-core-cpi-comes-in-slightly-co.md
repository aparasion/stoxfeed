Stock index futures are showing modest gains this morning following the release of March's core Consumer Price Index (CPI), which came in slightly below expectations. Specifically, Nasdaq 100 futures rose 0.3%, while both S&P 500 and Dow Jones futures also increased by 0.3%. This data suggests a potential easing in inflationary pressures, which could influence the Federal Reserve's monetary policy decisions moving forward.

The cooler-than-expected inflation figures are significant for the financial markets, as they may alleviate some concerns regarding aggressive interest rate hikes. Lower inflation could lead to a more favorable environment for equities, particularly in growth sectors that are sensitive to borrowing costs. Market participants will likely be analyzing sector performance closely, especially in technology and consumer discretionary, which often react strongly to changes in interest rate expectations.

Investors should watch for subsequent market reactions as more economic data is released, particularly any signals from the Fed regarding future rate adjustments. This could set the tone for market volatility in the coming weeks.
