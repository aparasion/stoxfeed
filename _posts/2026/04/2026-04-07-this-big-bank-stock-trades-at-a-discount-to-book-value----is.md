Stocks have stumbled at the outset of 2026, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average entering correction territory for the first time since last year's tariff announcement. Amid this backdrop, Truist Financial (TFC +0.90%) stands out as a compelling opportunity, especially with recent regulatory changes favoring regional banks. The newly proposed Basel III Endgame rules, which reduce capital requirements for banks, are particularly beneficial for Truist, a Category III bank.

The updated regulations decrease Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) requirements by up to 5.2% for certain banks, easing previous stringent capital demands. This shift allows Truist to enhance its lending capacity, support share buybacks, and maintain a robust dividend yield of 4.4%. The bank has authorized a substantial $10 billion share repurchase program, with plans to buy back $4 billion in 2026, signaling confidence in its financial health.

For investors looking to capitalize on regulatory relief in the banking sector, Truist, currently trading at a 1.3% discount to book value, presents an attractive buying opportunity.
