Tesla (TSLA) is set to report its first-quarter 2026 earnings on April 22, with CEO Elon Musk and senior management addressing analysts in a live conference call. Investors will be keenly watching for updates on the company's core electric vehicle (EV) business, which recently saw a disappointing 358,023 deliveries—its lowest in a year and below Wall Street expectations. This shortfall raises concerns about inventory levels and potential impacts on free cash flow amid substantial capital expenditures.

The spotlight will likely be on Tesla's ambitious robotaxi initiative and humanoid robotics, which are critical to the company's valuation. While the robotaxi fleet is operational in select cities, UBS analyst Joseph Spak suggests that the rollout will be slower than anticipated due to Tesla's safety culture. With the stock's valuation still high at approximately 191 times forward earnings, any delays in these projects could disappoint investors and weigh on TSLA shares.

As earnings approach, market professionals should closely monitor Musk's comments regarding the pace of robotaxi expansion and production timelines for humanoid robots, as these factors are pivotal to Tesla's future growth narrative.
