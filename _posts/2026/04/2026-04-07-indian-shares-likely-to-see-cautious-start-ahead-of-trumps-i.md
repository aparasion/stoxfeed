Indian shares are expected to open cautiously as U.S. President Donald Trump escalates tensions with Iran, threatening military action if the country does not comply with demands regarding the Strait of Hormuz. Trump's rhetoric includes a promise to decimate Iranian infrastructure within hours if an agreement is not reached by tonight, a situation that could have significant geopolitical implications for global markets.

The Indian market showed resilience on Monday, with benchmark indexes Sensex and Nifty recovering from early losses to close up over 1%. The rupee also strengthened against the dollar, buoyed by the Reserve Bank of India's measures against currency speculation. However, foreign institutional investors sold off shares, highlighting potential volatility as geopolitical tensions rise. Meanwhile, Asian markets are showing positive momentum, and U.S. stocks continued their upward trend, reflecting a complex interplay between local and global economic indicators.

Market professionals should monitor the developments surrounding U.S.-Iran relations closely, as any escalation could impact oil prices and, subsequently, sectors sensitive to energy costs and geopolitical stability.
