Shares of Meta Platforms (META) have seen a significant decline in early 2026, dropping approximately 14% year-to-date and trading 28% below their late 2025 peak of $780. This downturn is primarily driven by investor concerns over the company’s aggressive capital expenditure plans, projected at $135 billion for 2026, alongside fresh regulatory challenges reminiscent of issues faced by Big Tobacco. However, demand for Meta's core advertising products remains strong, fueled by improved monetization through AI enhancements.

Despite the current sell-off, Meta's fundamentals appear robust. The company reported $201 billion in revenue for 2025, a 22% increase from the previous year, with steady operating margins at 41%. Investors are encouraged to view the recent price drop as a potential buying opportunity, as Meta's history suggests that significant investments often lead to substantial returns once the technology matures.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Meta's current valuation, with a forward P/E near 19, presents a compelling entry point for long-term investors, especially given the company’s strong revenue growth and AI-driven improvements in its advertising ecosystem.
