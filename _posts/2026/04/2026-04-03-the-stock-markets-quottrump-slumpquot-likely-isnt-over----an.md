The stock market has faced significant turbulence in recent weeks, with the Dow Jones, S&P 500, and Nasdaq Composite all experiencing declines, particularly under the backdrop of President Trump's renewed military actions in Iran. While historical data shows strong market performance during Trump's first term, the current environment has shifted, with all three major indexes entering correction territory as of late March.

This downturn is compounded by rising inflation, driven in part by escalating crude oil prices following the closure of the Strait of Hormuz due to conflict. The Core PCE inflation rate has climbed to 3.1%, well above the Federal Reserve's 2% target, raising concerns about potential interest rate hikes that could further pressure equity valuations. Historically high valuations, combined with fears of an AI bubble burst, create a challenging landscape for investors.

For market professionals, this period of volatility may present buying opportunities. Historically, pullbacks have been short-lived, and long-term investors often benefit from market corrections. As inflationary pressures persist, maintaining a strategic focus on quality investments will be crucial for navigating this uncertain terrain.
