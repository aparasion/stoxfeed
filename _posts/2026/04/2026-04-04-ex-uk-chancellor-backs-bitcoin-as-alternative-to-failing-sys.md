Former UK Chancellor Kwasi Kwarteng is pivoting towards bitcoin as a remedy for what he describes as a "fiscal doom loop" in the UK, following his brief tenure marred by a controversial mini-budget. In an interview with CoinDesk, Kwarteng reflected on the rushed rollout of fiscal policies that led to soaring gilt yields and exposed vulnerabilities in the UK’s pension system. Now, as executive chairman of Stack BTC, a UK bitcoin treasury firm, he advocates for a longer-term perspective on economic policy and digital assets.

Kwarteng's involvement with Stack BTC, which holds 31 BTC on its balance sheet, signals a growing interest in cryptocurrencies amid traditional market challenges. He argues that the UK is lagging behind Europe in embracing digital innovation, highlighting a cultural gap that could impact future investment strategies. The firm has also attracted political attention, with Reform UK leader Nigel Farage acquiring a stake.

The key takeaway for market professionals is Kwarteng's call for a shift in monetary policy thinking, emphasizing resilience and innovation, which could influence institutional adoption of cryptocurrencies as viable alternatives in uncertain economic times.
