The S&P 500 has surged over 10% in the first half of 2023, raising questions about the sustainability of this bull market. Investors are urged to focus on technical indicators, particularly the weekly 200 simple moving average (SMA), which has historically acted as a strong support level during previous market recoveries. As stocks begin to trade above their daily 200 SMA, market sentiment appears to be shifting, but the potential for further weakness remains a concern.

Sector performance has been uneven, with energy and healthcare stocks leading gains while technology and communication services lag behind. This divergence underscores the importance of sector analysis when selecting stocks. For instance, McKesson (MCK) has seen substantial gains, contrasting sharply with the declines of companies like PayPal (PYPL) amid broader market volatility.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to align stock selection with prevailing market trends. Focusing on stocks that are breaking out and trending upward in a strengthening market can enhance the probability of significant returns, while patience is essential until a confirmed bull trend emerges above the daily 200 SMA.
