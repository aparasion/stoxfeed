Asia-Pacific markets are gearing up for a higher open on Monday, driven by investor optimism despite escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran. President Trump announced the U.S. Navy's seizure of an Iranian-flagged cargo ship in the Gulf of Oman, marking a significant escalation in the ongoing blockade of Iranian ports. This development follows Iran's recent attacks on commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, raising concerns about potential disruptions in one of the world's key oil transit routes.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial, particularly in the energy sector. West Texas Intermediate crude surged nearly 8% to $90.53 per barrel, while Brent crude rose nearly 7% to $96.59. These price increases reflect heightened geopolitical risks and potential supply constraints. Meanwhile, U.S. futures indicate a mixed sentiment, with the Dow and S&P 500 experiencing declines after hitting record highs.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation, as further escalations could lead to increased volatility in oil prices and broader market reactions, particularly in energy stocks and related sectors.
