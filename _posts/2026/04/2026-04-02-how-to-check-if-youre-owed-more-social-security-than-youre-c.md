Social Security beneficiaries may not realize their payments can fluctuate due to several factors, including annual cost-of-living adjustments (COLA) and potential clerical errors by the Social Security Administration (SSA). These errors can lead to underpayments, prompting beneficiaries to verify their earnings records and ensure they are receiving the correct amount.

This is particularly relevant for financial professionals advising clients on retirement planning and income strategies. A thorough review of an individual’s earnings history, updates on spousal deaths, and adjustments after the earnings test can significantly impact the total benefits received. With the earnings test threshold set to rise to $24,480 in 2026, ensuring proper recalculation of benefits post-retirement age is crucial for maximizing income.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of proactively managing Social Security benefits. Encouraging clients to verify their records could result in increased cash flow, which may influence their investment strategies and overall financial health.
