South Korea's ruling Democratic Party has introduced a "Digital Asset Basic Act," aiming to establish a comprehensive legal framework for digital assets, particularly stablecoins. This draft bill mandates strict authorization, reserve requirements, and operational standards for issuers of value-linked digital assets, which include those pegged to fiat currencies or real-world assets. The initiative seeks to position South Korea as a leader in the digital finance landscape while addressing regulatory concerns that have stalled previous negotiations on stablecoin issuance.

The proposed legislation comes amid increasing regulatory scrutiny, including new measures requiring domestic exchanges to implement withdrawal delays to combat fraud. By introducing licensing and reporting requirements for digital asset businesses, the bill aims to enhance investor protection and market integrity, addressing gaps in the current regulatory framework.

For market professionals, this development signals a potential shift in the regulatory environment for cryptocurrencies in South Korea, which could influence investment strategies and operational compliance for firms involved in digital assets.
