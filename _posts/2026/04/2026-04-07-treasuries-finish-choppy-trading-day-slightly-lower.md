Treasuries experienced a volatile trading session on Tuesday, ultimately closing slightly lower as the yield on the benchmark ten-year note edged up to 4.343%. Market participants were particularly attentive to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, especially with President Trump's ultimatum regarding Iran's compliance with demands related to the Strait of Hormuz, a vital global energy corridor. His provocative statements have heightened uncertainty, impacting investor sentiment.

The bond market's fluctuations reflect broader concerns over potential conflict and its implications for global energy supplies, which could lead to increased volatility across financial markets. Additionally, the Commerce Department's report indicating a 1.4% decline in durable goods orders for February—much steeper than the anticipated 0.5% drop—adds to worries about economic growth and may influence Federal Reserve policy discussions.

As geopolitical risks loom and economic indicators falter, market professionals should brace for potential volatility in both bond and equity markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to energy prices and manufacturing output.
