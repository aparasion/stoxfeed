S&P 500 index futures are trading higher this morning, buoyed by Donald Trump's announcement of an indefinite ceasefire extension with Iran. This development, alongside significant buying activity from algorithmic Commodity Trading Advisors (CTAs), has contributed to the index's recent gains, as reported by Goldman Sachs Prime data.

While CTAs continue to support the S&P 500, their buying momentum appears to be waning. Goldman Sachs projects approximately $16 billion in buying over the next week, a decrease from previous forecasts. Notably, the medium-term outlook is shifting, with potential selling emerging if market conditions deteriorate. The recent surge in CTA buying—totaling around $86 billion—has historically led to short-term consolidations, suggesting that while the market may still experience upward movement, caution is warranted.

Investors should remain vigilant as the S&P 500 approaches overbought territory, indicated by a recent daily price reversal and an RSI above 70. Immediate support levels are around 7075, with upside targets in the 7170–7230 range, highlighting the need for strategic positioning amid evolving market dynamics.
