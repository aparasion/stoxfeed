U.S. Treasury yields fell sharply on Wednesday following a two-week ceasefire in the Middle East conflict, with the 10-year note dropping over 10 basis points to 4.2399%. This decline reflects a significant shift in investor sentiment as they flocked to U.S. bonds, leading to similar drops in shorter- and longer-dated yields. The easing of inflation concerns, driven by the cessation of hostilities and a sharp decline in energy prices, has contributed to this movement, with Brent crude falling 13.5% to below $100 a barrel.

This development is crucial for financial markets as it signals a potential recalibration of expectations regarding Federal Reserve interest rate cuts. With the March meeting minutes of the FOMC set to be released later today, traders are keenly assessing how these geopolitical dynamics might influence monetary policy. 

Market professionals should closely monitor the upcoming inflation data and mortgage rates, as these indicators could further shape investment strategies in the current environment of declining yields and shifting economic signals.
