As retirement approaches, individuals planning to retire in 2028 have several strategies to maximize their Social Security benefits. By working longer in high-paying roles, retirees can replace lower-earning years in their benefit calculations, which are based on the highest 35 years of earnings. This approach not only enhances benefits but also mitigates the impact of any previous low-income years.

Delaying Social Security claims until age 70 can significantly increase monthly payouts, as benefits grow the longer one waits to access them. For those considering early retirement at 62, it may be wise to maintain a part-time job or side hustle to boost income and delay claiming benefits, ultimately leading to a more substantial payout.

The key takeaway for market professionals is that proactive income management and strategic retirement planning can lead to enhanced financial stability in retirement, underscoring the importance of maximizing Social Security benefits as part of a comprehensive retirement strategy.
