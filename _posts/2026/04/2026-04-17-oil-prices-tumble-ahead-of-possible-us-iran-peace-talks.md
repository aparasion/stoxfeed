Oil prices dropped sharply on Friday, with Brent crude falling nearly 2% to $97.40 a barrel and WTI crude down 2.4% at $92.41, as the market anticipates potential weekend negotiations between the U.S. and Iran. President Trump has hinted at a second round of talks, emphasizing the urgency for a deal to prevent renewed conflict. This decline comes amid a broader weekly drop of over 3% for both contracts, spurred by a temporary ceasefire agreement between Israel and Lebanon, which has raised hopes for stability in the region.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, as ongoing geopolitical tensions and supply constraints continue to impact oil prices. The International Monetary Fund has warned that Europe has limited jet fuel reserves, and restoring disrupted oil output could take up to two years. This backdrop creates uncertainty for investors, particularly in energy sectors.

Market professionals should closely monitor the outcomes of the U.S.-Iran negotiations, as any breakthroughs could shift supply dynamics and influence oil prices in the near term.
