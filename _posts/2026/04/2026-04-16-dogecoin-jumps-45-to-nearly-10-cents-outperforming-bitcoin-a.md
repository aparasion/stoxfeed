Dogecoin surged 4.5% to nearly 10 cents, outpacing both Bitcoin and Ether as traders shifted capital into higher-risk crypto assets. This rally, characterized by strong late-session buying and significant volume, suggests heightened interest, although concerns linger regarding its sustainability due to weak on-chain activity and declining daily active addresses.

The current momentum in Dogecoin appears to be driven more by derivatives and leveraged positioning rather than organic demand, as evidenced by the divergence between rising open interest and falling on-chain metrics. Traders are closely monitoring the $0.096 support level, with a breakout above $0.104 signaling a potential bullish shift. Conversely, a drop below the $0.092-$0.090 range could indicate a deeper pullback.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Dogecoin's recent price action shows promise, its reliance on derivatives and lack of robust on-chain activity raises questions about the longevity of this rally. Traders should remain vigilant for signs of broader participation to confirm any sustained upward movement.
