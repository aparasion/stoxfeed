Critical Metals (NASDAQ: CRML) surged 35.6% to close at $12.56 on Friday after Greenland approved the company's increased stake and expanded operating authority at the Tanbreez rare-earth project. Trading volume hit 72.8 million shares, a staggering 427% above its three-month average, indicating strong investor interest. This approval allows CRML to acquire the remaining 50.5% equity in the project, which is touted as one of the world's largest rare earth deposits, crucial for EVs and defense technologies.

The broader market saw the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite rise 1.19% and 1.52%, respectively, but other players in the diversified metals and mining sector, like MP Materials and Lithium Americas, experienced declines. The approval for Critical Metals is significant as the U.S. aims to strengthen its critical minerals supply chain, positioning CRML favorably in a high-demand sector.

Investors should note that while CRML's stock has rallied, it remains pre-revenue and carries inherent risks, including potential shareholder dilution as production plans develop. Caution is advised for those considering entry into this volatile stock.
