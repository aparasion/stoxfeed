Stock index futures declined on Monday as optimism surrounding a potential U.S.-Iran peace deal diminished, leading to increased geopolitical tensions. Dow futures dropped by 0.78% to 49,062.07, while S&P 500 futures fell 0.65% to 7,079.71, reflecting a broader market reaction to the uncertainty.

This decline in futures suggests that investors are recalibrating their expectations amid rising geopolitical risks, which could have implications for market volatility and sector performance. Energy stocks, in particular, may face pressure if tensions escalate further, while sectors sensitive to international trade could also be adversely affected. The market's reaction indicates a cautious sentiment as traders weigh the potential for conflict against economic recovery narratives.

Market participants should closely monitor developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as ongoing tensions could lead to fluctuations in energy prices and broader market instability, impacting investment strategies in the near term.
