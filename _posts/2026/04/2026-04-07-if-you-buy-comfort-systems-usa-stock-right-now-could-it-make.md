Comfort Systems USA (NYSE: FIX) has emerged as a standout performer in a declining market, with shares soaring over 54% year-to-date as of April 6. The HVAC company is capitalizing on the booming demand for AI infrastructure, where cooling solutions are critical for data centers. CEO Brian Lane highlighted "unprecedented demand," with the company's backlog nearly doubling to $12 billion since the start of the year, contributing to a robust 41.7% year-over-year sales growth.

This impressive performance is backed by a solid historical growth trajectory, with a 10-year revenue compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 21.3%. Comfort Systems USA's strategy of reinvesting in the business through acquisitions, alongside organic growth, positions it well for continued expansion. The recent increase in its quarterly dividend and a substantial cash reserve further underscore its financial health.

Investors should consider Comfort Systems USA as a strong addition to a diversified portfolio, particularly given its ability to convert backlog into revenue and its strategic growth initiatives in a rapidly evolving market.
