Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East are prompting investors to reassess their stock holdings, with many opting for caution amid uncertainty. This restraint could be prudent as the market navigates potential volatility. Investors are encouraged to prepare a watch list of stocks to capitalize on potential price declines, particularly in a bear market scenario.

Costco (COST) stands out with its steady growth, reporting $137 billion in revenue for the first half of fiscal 2026, a 9% increase year-over-year. Despite its robust performance, the current P/E ratio of 53 raises questions about valuation, suggesting that buying below a 30 P/E could yield favorable returns. Meanwhile, Dutch Bros (BROS) is expanding rapidly, with plans to grow from 1,136 locations to over 2,000 by 2029. Its recent revenue surge of 28% and a P/E of 84 indicate strong market interest, but the price-to-sales ratio suggests potential for stock price appreciation if it aligns more closely with competitors.

Investors should closely monitor these stocks, as both Costco and Dutch Bros offer unique opportunities for growth in a turbulent market environment.
