U.S. President Donald Trump confirmed the successful rescue of two military officers in Iran following the downing of an F-15 fighter jet, marking a significant escalation in the ongoing conflict. The operation, described as one of the most daring in U.S. history, underscores the heightened military tensions between the U.S. and Iran, especially as Tehran has now successfully shot down a U.S. combat aircraft for the first time since the war began.

This incident has immediate implications for the financial markets, particularly in the energy sector. The spot price for Brent crude oil surged to $141.36, the highest since the 2008 financial crisis, reflecting concerns over supply disruptions caused by Iran's actions in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil transportation. The stark difference between immediate delivery prices and futures contracts indicates a tightening supply scenario that could further impact oil markets.

Market participants should closely monitor developments in the U.S.-Iran conflict, as continued military actions could lead to further volatility in oil prices and broader market ramifications.
