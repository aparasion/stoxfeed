Ethereum (ETH) has emerged as more than just a cryptocurrency; it is a vital blockchain ecosystem and decentralized computing platform, appealing to both individual investors and major financial institutions. With its pioneering smart contracts, Ethereum has become a cornerstone of decentralized finance (DeFi), capturing 57% of the Total Value Locked (TVL) in the sector and establishing itself as the go-to blockchain for tokenized assets and stablecoin initiatives.

Despite its robust utility, Ethereum's price has seen a significant decline, down nearly 60% from its all-time high. This downturn is attributed to broader market concerns, including the rise of artificial intelligence, which has shifted investor focus away from traditional software, impacting Ethereum's perceived value. Nevertheless, the White House recognizes Ethereum as a strategic asset, and its ongoing adoption by Wall Street underscores its potential.

For market professionals, Ethereum presents a compelling opportunity; its foundational role in DeFi and emerging sectors suggests it may be undervalued, making it a defensive play worth considering in the current market environment.
