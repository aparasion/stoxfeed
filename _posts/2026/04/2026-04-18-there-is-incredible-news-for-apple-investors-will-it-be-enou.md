Apple (NASDAQ: AAPL) defied the broader smartphone market downturn in Q1 2026, recording a 5% increase in iPhone shipments while overall smartphone sales fell by 6%. This achievement solidifies Apple’s position as the leading smartphone vendor, capturing a 21% market share, primarily driven by strong demand in India, China, and Japan, along with effective trade-in programs that have bolstered sales even amid macroeconomic challenges.

Despite a volatile share price, down 0.6% year-to-date, analysts project a 14% increase in earnings for Apple this fiscal year, reaching $8.51 per share. The company's strategic focus on integrating AI features, potentially leveraging Google's Gemini AI model, could enhance device appeal and further drive sales. Additionally, with a significant number of older iPhones in need of upgrades, Apple is well-positioned to capitalize on replacement demand.

For market professionals, Apple’s resilience in a contracting market and its potential for future growth through AI integration present a compelling case for maintaining or increasing exposure to the stock, especially with analysts targeting a 15% upside in the next year.
