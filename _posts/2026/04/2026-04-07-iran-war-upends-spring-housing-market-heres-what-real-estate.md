The spring housing market is facing significant headwinds as rising mortgage rates and geopolitical tensions, particularly the war in Iran, dampen buyer sentiment. Real estate agents surveyed in the CNBC Housing Market Survey reported that concerns over the economy and mortgage rates have eclipsed worries about home prices, with one-third of agents citing economic uncertainty as their buyers' primary concern. The average rate on a 30-year fixed mortgage has surged to around 6.5%, up from 5.99% just before the conflict began, leading to a notable decline in buyer demand.

This shift in focus among buyers is impacting market dynamics, with many homes lingering on the market longer and an increase in contract cancellations. Agents noted that affordability issues are causing 19% of buyers to withdraw from the market, a significant rise from previous quarters. As sellers become increasingly concerned about extended listing times, the market appears to be transitioning from a seller's advantage to a more balanced or buyer-favorable environment.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that the combination of rising mortgage rates and economic uncertainty is likely to continue suppressing homebuyer demand, potentially leading to a more prolonged period of stagnant sales and price adjustments as we move through the spring season.
