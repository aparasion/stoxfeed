Toyota Motor Corp (TM) shares experienced a significant sell-off on Friday, with the stock hitting an RSI of 28.0, indicating it has entered oversold territory. The shares traded as low as $156.73 before closing at $160.12, well below its 52-week high of $248.47. In comparison, the S&P 500 ETF (SPY) recorded an even lower RSI of 24.8, suggesting broader market weakness.

This oversold condition may signal a potential reversal for Toyota, attracting bullish investors who see the recent heavy selling as nearing exhaustion. With a notable gap between its current price and the 52-week high, traders might begin to identify entry points for long positions, especially if market sentiment shifts.

For market professionals, monitoring Toyota’s price action in the coming days will be crucial. A rebound could not only benefit TM investors but also reflect broader trends in the automotive sector, particularly as it navigates macroeconomic challenges.
