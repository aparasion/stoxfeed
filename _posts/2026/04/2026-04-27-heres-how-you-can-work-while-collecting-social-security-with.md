Social Security recipients can continue working while receiving benefits, but they must navigate specific earnings limits to avoid losing some of their payments. For those under full retirement age, the earnings limit for 2026 is set at $24,480, with $1 withheld for every $2 earned above this threshold. If recipients are nearing full retirement age, the limit increases to $65,160, where $1 is withheld for every $3 earned over that amount. 

Understanding these limits is crucial for financial planning, as working beyond these thresholds can lead to temporary reductions in Social Security benefits. However, it’s important to note that withheld benefits are not permanently lost; they will be recalculated and compensated with larger checks once full retirement age is reached. 

Market professionals should advise clients to weigh the timing of claiming Social Security against their potential earnings, ensuring they maximize both immediate income and long-term benefits.
