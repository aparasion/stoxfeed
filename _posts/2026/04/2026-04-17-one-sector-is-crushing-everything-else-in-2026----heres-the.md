The energy sector is experiencing a significant surge in 2026, with the S&P Energy Select Sector index climbing nearly 25%, largely driven by escalating oil prices due to the ongoing conflict with Iran and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Amid this backdrop, Genesis Energy (NYSE: GEL) emerges as a compelling investment opportunity, particularly for those seeking exposure to oil without direct reliance on fluctuating oil prices.

Genesis Energy has undergone a strategic transformation, divesting its underperforming soda ash business to strengthen its balance sheet. This has allowed the company to reduce high-cost debt and improve its financial health, positioning it for growth. Its primary focus now lies in offshore oil pipelines, with significant contributions expected from the recently operational Shenandoah and Salamanca projects, which are set to enhance its EBITDA by 15% to 20% in the coming years.

Investors should note that Genesis is not just a turnaround story; it offers a stable cash flow and a strong distribution coverage ratio. With no major capital expenditures planned, the company is poised to leverage its offshore pipeline capabilities amid increasing demand for oil security, making it a noteworthy player in the evolving energy landscape.
