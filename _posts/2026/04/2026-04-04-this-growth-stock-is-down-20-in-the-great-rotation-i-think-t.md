Artificial intelligence (AI) stocks have faced a sharp downturn in 2026, prompting a significant rotation into value and small-cap stocks. Despite this shift, Micron Technology (MU) stands out as a compelling opportunity, trading at a remarkably low forward price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio of under 4 times fiscal 2027 estimates. This valuation comes despite Micron’s strong recent earnings and positive guidance, highlighting investor skepticism about the sustainability of its earnings amid ongoing volatility in the DRAM market.

The current DRAM market dynamics are driven by a shortage that has elevated prices, largely due to the growing demand for high bandwidth memory (HBM) in AI chip production. Unlike typical commodity cycles, this situation is underpinned by structural growth in AI infrastructure, suggesting that the floor for DRAM prices may be higher than in previous cycles. 

For market professionals, Micron’s steep valuation and the evolving landscape of the DRAM market present a unique buying opportunity, particularly as the company and its competitors shift towards longer-term contracts that could stabilize earnings.
