Shares of Nebius Group (NBIS) have surged 21.1% this week, building on a 7.9% gain from last week, driven by acquisition speculation and a bullish rating from Cantor Fitzgerald. The company, which develops infrastructure for artificial intelligence computing, is reportedly in talks to acquire Israeli start-up AI21 Labs, prompting increased interest from investors. Cantor Fitzgerald's analyst Brett Knoblauch initiated coverage with an overweight rating and a price target of $129, further fueling the stock's momentum.

This uptick in Nebius's stock is significant as it highlights the growing investor appetite for AI-related companies, particularly those poised for expansion through acquisitions. The market's reaction underscores the potential for Nebius to enhance its competitive edge in the rapidly evolving AI sector.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Nebius shows strong short-term performance, the valuation—trading at 896 times operating cash flow—suggests that investors may want to be cautious and consider waiting for a more favorable entry point before investing.
