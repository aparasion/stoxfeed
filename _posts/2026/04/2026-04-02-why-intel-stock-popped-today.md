Intel (INTC) shares surged 8.84% on Wednesday following the announcement of its plan to reacquire a 49% stake in its Fab 34 manufacturing facility in Ireland from Apollo Global Management for $14.2 billion. This move comes after Intel sold the stake in 2024 for $11.2 billion, using the proceeds to bolster its manufacturing capabilities. The company intends to finance the repurchase through cash reserves and approximately $6.5 billion in new debt, with management projecting an earnings per share boost by 2027.

This acquisition is significant as it positions Intel to capitalize on the growing demand for CPUs, particularly in the rapidly evolving AI sector. With a focus on AI-enabled personal computers and data center servers, the Fab 34 facility is central to Intel's strategy to regain market share lost to competitors like Nvidia and AMD. As companies transition from AI model training to inference, the demand for Intel's CPU offerings is expected to rise.

Market professionals should note that while Intel is poised for growth, it faces intense competition in the CPU market, making its strategic moves crucial for maintaining a competitive edge.
