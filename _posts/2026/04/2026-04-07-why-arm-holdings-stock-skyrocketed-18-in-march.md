Arm Holdings (NASDAQ: ARM) saw its shares surge by up to 18.7% in March following the announcement of its first in-house AI chip, the Arm AGI CPU, designed for AI infrastructure. This marks a significant shift for the semiconductor company, which has historically focused on licensing chip designs. The new processor, featuring up to 64 CPUs and 8,700 cores, promises double the performance-per-watt compared to traditional x86 systems, addressing the industry's growing emphasis on energy efficiency.

The market's reaction underscores the potential impact of this development on Arm's financial trajectory. Analysts are optimistic, with Guggenheim raising its price target for Arm to $240, suggesting a 61% upside from recent levels. CEO Rene Haas projects that the company could achieve $25 billion in annual revenue by 2031, with substantial contributions from the Arm AGI CPU, which could significantly elevate Arm's earnings profile.

For investors, this pivot into in-house silicon presents a compelling opportunity, particularly given the stock's valuation metrics in relation to projected future earnings.
