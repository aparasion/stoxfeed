The SEMI Foundation has announced the extension of its SEMIquest program at the Arizona Science Center through May 31, 2026, enhancing access to STEM education focused on the semiconductor industry. Originally launched alongside SEMICON West 2025, this initiative aims to provide thousands more students and educators with hands-on experiences that connect them to potential careers in microelectronics, supported by key industry players like TMSC and Amkor.

This extension is significant as it addresses the growing demand for skilled workers in the semiconductor sector, which is critical to technology innovation and economic growth. With over 80,000 visitors already engaged, SEMIquest serves as a vital platform for career exploration and workforce development, linking education with real-world applications and job opportunities in a high-demand field.

For market professionals, the SEMIquest initiative underscores the importance of workforce development in sustaining growth within the semiconductor industry, highlighting a proactive approach to bridging the skills gap as demand for talent continues to rise.
