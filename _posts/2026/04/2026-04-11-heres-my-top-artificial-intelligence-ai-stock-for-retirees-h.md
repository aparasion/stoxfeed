Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) continues to solidify its position as a leader in the AI sector, leveraging its established customer base and cloud services to drive growth. The company’s Azure platform offers AI tools that enable businesses to develop custom solutions without heavy upfront investments, while its integration of AI into products like Copilot enhances user experience and productivity. This strong foundation not only supports Microsoft's AI ambitions but also ensures a stable revenue stream, particularly from enterprise contracts.

For investors, especially retirees, Microsoft presents a compelling case. Although its dividend yield is around 1%, the company has increased payouts by 153% over the last decade, supported by substantial free cash flow and a conservative payout ratio of 33.6%. This positions Microsoft as a more resilient choice compared to other volatile AI stocks that may struggle in economic downturns.

In summary, while Microsoft may experience market fluctuations, its robust business model and consistent dividend growth make it an attractive option for those seeking exposure to AI without sacrificing income stability.
