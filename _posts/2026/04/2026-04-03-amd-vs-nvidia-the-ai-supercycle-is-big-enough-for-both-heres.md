The AI boom is reshaping the tech landscape, with Nvidia (NVDA) and Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) emerging as key players. Nvidia has solidified its dominance in AI infrastructure, boasting a staggering 90% market share in GPUs essential for training large language models. Its revenue is projected to soar from $17 billion in fiscal 2021 to $216 billion by fiscal 2026, driven by its CUDA software platform and strategic acquisitions. 

Conversely, AMD, while trailing Nvidia in the GPU market, is strategically positioned for growth in inference and agentic AI. Recent GPU deals with OpenAI and Meta will significantly boost its revenue, while its leadership in data center CPUs places it at the forefront of emerging AI demands. 

For investors, AMD presents a compelling opportunity, poised to capitalize on the next phase of the AI supercycle, making it a more attractive buy compared to Nvidia's already inflated valuation.
