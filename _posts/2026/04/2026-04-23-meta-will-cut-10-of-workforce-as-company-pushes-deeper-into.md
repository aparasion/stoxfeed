Meta is set to lay off 10% of its workforce, approximately 8,000 jobs, as part of its strategic shift towards artificial intelligence investments. The layoffs, which will begin on May 20, follow previous job cuts aimed at enhancing efficiency and refocusing efforts on generative AI, an area where Meta has been trailing competitors like OpenAI and Google. The company is also halting plans to fill 6,000 open positions, further indicating a tightening of its operational structure.

This move underscores a broader trend in the tech sector, where companies are recalibrating their workforce in response to the AI boom. Meta's stock has seen a slight decline of 2.4% recently, reflecting investor concerns as it prepares to report first-quarter earnings alongside other tech giants. The layoffs may signal a shift in investor sentiment as the market adjusts to the evolving competitive landscape in AI.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Meta's aggressive pivot towards AI, coupled with significant workforce reductions, may influence its upcoming earnings report and overall stock performance, particularly in the context of heightened scrutiny on tech sector profitability amid ongoing layoffs.
