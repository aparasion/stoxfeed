Negotiations between the U.S. and Iran to resolve the ongoing conflict have collapsed, prompting President Trump to impose a blockade on the Strait of Hormuz, aimed at curtailing Iranian oil exports. Initially, this led to a dip in stock futures, but by Monday afternoon, all three major indexes rebounded, indicating that investors are willing to overlook the geopolitical tensions for now. Oil prices experienced a brief uptick but ultimately lost ground during the trading session.

The market's resilience suggests that investors may be reassured by the recent ceasefire, viewing a prolonged conflict as less likely. However, the situation remains fluid, and the fate of the Strait of Hormuz is critical, as its closure could trigger significant volatility in global energy markets. 

As attention shifts from the Iran conflict, software stocks are gaining traction, with the iShares Expanded Tech-Software Sector ETF rising over 5%. Investors are capitalizing on perceived opportunities in this sector, potentially signaling a broader market recovery despite geopolitical uncertainties.
