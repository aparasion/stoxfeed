Applied Digital (NASDAQ: APLD) is capitalizing on the booming AI infrastructure market, reporting a remarkable 139% year-over-year revenue growth to $127 million in its latest quarter. The company operates by designing and building data centers, primarily renting capacity to clients like CoreWeave, which services major players such as Meta Platforms. With its recent groundbreaking on the Delta Forge 1 data center, Applied Digital is poised for significant expansion, although it currently operates only a fraction of its contracted capacity.

However, the company faces challenges, particularly regarding its debt levels. A recent $2.15 billion capital raise at a 6.75% interest rate highlights the financial risks involved. While the potential for substantial returns exists if AI demand continues to surge, investors must weigh this against the heightened risk associated with the company’s capital structure.

For market professionals, Applied Digital presents an intriguing opportunity; its rapid growth and strategic positioning in the AI space could yield substantial rewards, but caution is warranted due to its debt load.
