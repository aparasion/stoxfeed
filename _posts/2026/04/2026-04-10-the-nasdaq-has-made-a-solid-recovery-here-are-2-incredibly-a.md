The Nasdaq Composite has rebounded nearly 9% since late March after briefly entering correction territory, yet not all tech stocks have followed suit. This divergence presents a compelling buying opportunity for investors, particularly in the artificial intelligence (AI) sector, where adoption rates continue to rise significantly. 

Micron Technology, for instance, has seen its shares decline nearly 12% from their recent highs, despite strong demand for memory chips essential for AI infrastructure. Analysts project a staggering 600% increase in Micron's earnings this fiscal year, driven by surging prices for dynamic random-access memory (DRAM) and NAND flash, making its current valuation attractive at just 17 times trailing earnings. Similarly, SoundHound AI has experienced a 17% drop in shares but is capitalizing on the growing demand for voice AI solutions, with revenue skyrocketing 99% last year. 

Investors should consider these stocks as strategic additions to their portfolios, given their potential for robust growth amid the ongoing AI boom.
