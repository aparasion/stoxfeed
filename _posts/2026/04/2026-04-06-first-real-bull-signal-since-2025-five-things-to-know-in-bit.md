Bitcoin (BTC) is poised for a pivotal week as it navigates macroeconomic pressures while showing signs of a bullish price trend reversal. A key BTC price metric is on the verge of flipping bullish for the first time in nearly a year, reminiscent of a previous surge that saw prices jump $25,000 in two months. As BTC recently tagged $70,000, aggressive traders contributed to over $250 million in liquidations, signaling heightened speculative activity in the derivatives market.

The interplay of geopolitical tensions, particularly the escalating conflict involving Iran, and upcoming U.S. inflation data adds layers of uncertainty to the market. Analysts are closely monitoring the impact of rising oil prices on inflation metrics like the Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) Index and the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which could influence investor sentiment and risk appetite across asset classes, including cryptocurrencies.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the critical importance of monitoring Bitcoin's support levels and the broader macroeconomic landscape. A failure to hold key support could lead to a significant downturn, while sustained bullish momentum could challenge previous highs, making this week a crucial period for BTC traders.
