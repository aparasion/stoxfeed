Joby Aviation (NYSE: JOBY) saw its stock plunge 17.9% in March, aligning with a broader sell-off in risk assets, as investors flocked to safer options amid market volatility. This trend was echoed by its competitor, Archer Aviation, which experienced a more significant decline of 27.4%. The geopolitical tensions in the Persian Gulf have compounded challenges for both companies, particularly as Joby’s partnership with Uber for air taxi services in Dubai coincided with escalating conflict in the region.

Despite these setbacks, March brought some positive operational news for Joby. The company was selected for multiple projects under the White House-backed Electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL) Integration Pilot Program, potentially allowing operations in ten states by 2026. Additionally, Joby initiated flight testing for FAA certification, marking a critical step toward commercial deployment.

For market professionals, the mixed results present a nuanced outlook. While geopolitical risks pose a significant hurdle, the recent operational advancements could position Joby favorably for future growth, suggesting that current price levels may offer a compelling entry point for investors with a long-term view.
