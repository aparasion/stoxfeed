Senator Bill Hagerty announced that the US Senate Banking Committee is poised to advance a significant digital asset market structure bill, originally titled the CLARITY Act, in the coming weeks. Speaking at the Digital Assets and Emerging Tech Policy Summit, Hagerty expressed optimism about resolving outstanding issues and moving the legislation through the committee as early as next week. This bill aims to shift oversight of cryptocurrencies from the SEC to the CFTC, potentially reshaping the regulatory landscape for digital assets in the US.

The implications for the financial markets are considerable. The passage of this legislation could provide much-needed clarity and stability in the crypto sector, which has faced regulatory uncertainty and pushback from various stakeholders. As lawmakers work to finalize the bill, its timing could also intersect with the upcoming midterm elections, influencing how candidates position themselves on crypto-related issues.

Market professionals should monitor the progress of this bill closely, as its approval could signal a turning point for institutional investment in cryptocurrencies and affect market dynamics leading into the 2026 elections.
