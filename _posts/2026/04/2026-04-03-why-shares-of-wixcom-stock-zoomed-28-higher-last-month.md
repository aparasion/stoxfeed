Wix.com (WIX) shares surged 27.8% in March following the release of its fourth-quarter earnings and the announcement of an aggressive share repurchase program. The website-building platform, which has faced investor concerns over potential disruption from artificial intelligence, reported a 14% year-over-year revenue growth to $524 million. This growth was bolstered by its recent acquisition of Base44, which has quickly generated $50 million in annual recurring revenue.

The stock's rise reflects investor confidence in Wix's strategy to combat AI competition while enhancing shareholder value through a Dutch Tender Offer to repurchase 31.6% of outstanding shares at $92 each. Currently trading at around $82, the stock is positioned attractively for potential buyers, especially considering Wix’s solid cash flow of $600 million and double-digit growth trajectory.

For market professionals, Wix's recent performance and strategic moves suggest it may be undervalued, presenting a potential buying opportunity for those who believe the AI threat is overstated.
