Sandisk (SNDK) will join the Nasdaq-100 on April 20, marking a significant milestone for the company, which has surged over 2,700% in the past year due to heightened demand for its data center storage solutions. Despite this remarkable growth, Wall Street analysts express caution, with a median target price of $843 per share indicating an 8% downside from the current price of $921. However, some analysts, like Amit Daryanani at Evercore, project a bullish scenario where the stock could reach $2,600, suggesting a potential upside of 182%.

The inclusion in the Nasdaq-100 typically boosts stock performance, as funds tracking the index are required to purchase shares. Historically, stocks added to the index have returned an average of 18% over the subsequent year, although there are notable exceptions. Sandisk's rapid growth is fueled by a memory chip supply shortage, particularly in the AI sector, but concerns linger regarding the cyclical nature of the industry and potential future price declines.

Investors should weigh Sandisk's impressive growth against the inherent risks of the memory chip market. While the current demand is robust, the looming possibility of a supply glut could significantly impact future valuations, making it essential for market professionals to monitor earnings and market sentiment closely.
