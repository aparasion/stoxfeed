Alphabet (GOOG, GOOGL) is set to release its Q1 2026 earnings on April 29, following an 18% stock surge in the past month, raising investor expectations. Key metrics to watch will be earnings per share and Google Cloud's year-over-year revenue growth, as the company’s substantial investments in AI infrastructure aim to drive growth without heavily impacting profitability. In Q4, Google Cloud revenue soared 48% year-over-year, significantly boosting operating income and margins, underscoring its growing importance to Alphabet's overall narrative.

The upcoming report is particularly critical as Alphabet plans to double its capital expenditures to between $175 billion and $185 billion in 2026. This aggressive spending reflects the company's commitment to AI but also raises concerns about rising depreciation costs, which could pressure earnings. If Google Cloud continues its momentum but earnings per share fall short, the stock could face downward pressure despite its strong growth prospects.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to monitor Alphabet's earnings closely, particularly the interplay between Google Cloud's growth and the impact of rising infrastructure costs on profitability. This earnings report could set the tone for how investors perceive Alphabet's risk-reward profile moving forward.
