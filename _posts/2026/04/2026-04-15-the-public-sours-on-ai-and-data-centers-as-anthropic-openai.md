Artificial intelligence is facing a significant backlash in the U.S., posing challenges for major players in the tech sector. The sentiment shift is underscored by a violent incident involving OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, which highlights growing public anxiety about AI technology. As companies like OpenAI and Anthropic prepare for IPOs, the negative perception could impact their valuations and market entry strategies.

This rising skepticism toward AI is reflected in recent polling data, with a majority of Americans expressing concerns about the technology's risks. The tech giants, including Amazon, Google, and Microsoft, are heavily investing in data centers—estimated at $700 billion this year—to support AI development. However, local opposition to these projects is mounting, with Maine recently passing a statewide data center ban, signaling potential hurdles for future expansions.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that public sentiment could significantly influence the trajectory of AI companies and their IPO prospects. Investors should closely monitor how these dynamics affect valuations and the broader tech sector's growth strategy.
