The S&P 500 has faced a rocky start to 2023, dipping over 7% in March before recovering slightly to a -4% by April 1. This trend reflects broader market challenges, particularly for tech-heavy indexes, which have made investors cautious about further market investments. However, historical patterns suggest that such fluctuations are not unusual for the index, and maintaining a long-term perspective is crucial.

In light of the current environment, the Invesco S&P 500 Equal Weight ETF (RSP) emerges as a strategic alternative for investors. Unlike the standard S&P 500, which is heavily influenced by a few large tech stocks, the equal-weight approach reduces concentration risk, making it more resilient during tech downturns. While the S&P 500 has outperformed RSP in the past decade, the equal-weight ETF has shown better performance during market slumps, as evidenced by its smaller decline compared to the S&P 500 in 2022.

Investors looking for S&P 500 exposure might consider allocating a portion of their portfolio to RSP, especially given its lower reliance on tech and more attractive valuation. This approach can provide a hedge against volatility while still capitalizing on the index's long-term growth potential.
