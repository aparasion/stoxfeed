Nokia (NYSE: NOK) saw a notable surge, closing up 6.65% at $8.82 on Thursday, driven by a spike in bullish options activity. The stock's trading volume reached 88.7 million shares, significantly exceeding its three-month average of 46.5 million, indicating heightened investor interest. This uptick in call options, with over 58,000 contracts traded—27% above typical levels—suggests a growing confidence in Nokia's short-term prospects.

This momentum aligns with Nokia's ongoing restructuring efforts, which include cutting around 4,000 jobs to enhance margins and expanding its optical networking capabilities through the Infinera acquisition. While these initiatives have sparked renewed investor interest, the critical factor remains whether they translate into increased orders from telecom operators. Investors will be closely monitoring upcoming earnings reports for indications that demand for optical equipment related to AI and 5G is robust enough to offset restructuring challenges.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that Nokia's recent performance may signal a turning point, but the sustainability of this momentum hinges on tangible improvements in order flow from telecom clients.
