The European Central Bank (ECB) recently convened to assess the impact of the ongoing Middle East conflict on monetary policy, revealing a significant shift in market dynamics. Following the escalation of geopolitical tensions, energy prices surged, with Brent crude surpassing $100 per barrel, leading to heightened volatility in both equity and bond markets. The ECB noted a marked increase in inflation expectations, particularly in the euro area, where near-term inflation compensation has risen sharply, prompting a reassessment of monetary policy that now anticipates potential rate hikes by the end of 2026.

This shift is critical for financial markets, as the ECB's outlook reflects a departure from prior expectations of rate cuts. The ongoing energy supply shock is expected to exert upward pressure on inflation, with the ECB projecting an average inflation rate of 2.6% for 2026, up from previous estimates. This environment has also led to a deterioration in risk appetite, particularly within the euro area, impacting asset prices across sectors.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving inflation landscape and the ECB's policy trajectory, as the interplay between energy prices and monetary policy will likely dictate market volatility and investment strategies in the coming months.
