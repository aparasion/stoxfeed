The stock market faces potential turbulence as Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland President Beth Hammack hints at a possible shift in monetary policy. Her recent comments suggest that the FOMC may need to raise interest rates if inflation remains stubbornly high or if the labor market weakens significantly. With inflation above the Fed's 2% target for over five years and recent spikes in the rate, investors are on alert for a reversal in the Fed's rate-easing stance.

This uncertainty comes at a time when stock valuations are historically elevated, with the S&P 500's Shiller P/E Ratio hovering between 39 and 41—levels not seen since the dot-com bubble. The market's reliance on low interest rates to sustain its AI-driven rally could make it particularly vulnerable if the Fed opts for a rate hike, which could dampen investor enthusiasm and lead to a market correction.

Market professionals should closely monitor FOMC signals and inflation trends, as any shift in monetary policy could significantly impact stock performance and overall market sentiment.
