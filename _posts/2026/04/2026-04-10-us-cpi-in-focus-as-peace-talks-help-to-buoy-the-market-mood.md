European indices are experiencing modest gains today, defying a recent trend of risk aversion as traders react to ongoing geopolitical tensions. Brent crude oil is trading just above $97 per barrel, remaining below the $100 threshold. The uptick in European stocks may signal optimism surrounding the impending peace talks between Iran and the US, despite the fragile ceasefire and mixed signals from both sides.

The potential for a resolution in the Strait of Hormuz could have significant implications for both oil prices and European equities. While Brent crude has seen a weekly decline of 10%, the prospect of successful negotiations could stabilize the market and alleviate some pressure on European energy costs. However, ongoing concerns about stagflation and energy security vulnerabilities in Europe remain, particularly as the earnings season approaches and companies provide forward guidance.

Traders should closely monitor developments from the peace talks and upcoming US CPI data, which is expected to show a sharp increase in inflation. A higher-than-expected CPI could complicate the Federal Reserve's rate-cutting plans and impact risk sentiment across markets.
