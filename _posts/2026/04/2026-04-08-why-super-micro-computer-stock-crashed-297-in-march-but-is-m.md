Super Micro Computer (SMCI) experienced a steep decline in March, with shares plummeting nearly 30% after three individuals associated with the company were indicted on smuggling charges related to banned AI chip exports to China. This significant drop occurred amidst a broader market downturn, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite falling 5.1% and 4.8%, respectively. The charges allege that Supermicro’s co-founder and others conspired to divert Nvidia's advanced AI technology, raising serious concerns about the company's compliance with U.S. export laws.

The fallout from these allegations poses a substantial risk to Supermicro’s business, which heavily relies on Nvidia chips for its AI-focused server products, accounting for approximately 70% of its revenue. Although the stock has shown some recovery in early April, gaining around 2.6%, the lack of a long-term contract with Nvidia and ongoing investigations add uncertainty to its future performance.

Market professionals should monitor Supermicro closely, as continued volatility is likely, especially if the investigation reveals further compliance issues or if Nvidia takes action that could sever ties with the company.
