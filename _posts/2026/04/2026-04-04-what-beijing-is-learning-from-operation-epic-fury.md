The ongoing US-Israel air campaign against Iran has escalated tensions in the Middle East, significantly impacting global oil markets. Brent crude prices have surged to $114 a barrel, while the closure of the Strait of Hormuz has been labeled the largest supply disruption in oil market history, prompting concerns about energy security and supply chains. With China closely monitoring these developments, the conflict offers insights into US military operations that could shape Beijing's strategic planning.

For financial markets, the implications are profound. The disruption in oil supply could lead to increased volatility in energy stocks and broader market instability. Additionally, China's need to secure alternative oil supplies amid the conflict may shift its energy strategy, potentially affecting global trade dynamics. The depletion of US missile interceptors during the campaign raises alarms for defense analysts, suggesting a potential strategic window for China as it assesses its military options.

Investors should remain vigilant as the geopolitical landscape evolves, particularly regarding energy stocks and defense-related equities. The interplay between military actions, energy prices, and strategic positioning could create both risks and opportunities in the coming months.
