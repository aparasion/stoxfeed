Polish cryptocurrency exchange Zondacrypto is facing significant scrutiny as Prime Minister Donald Tusk accused the company of attempting to influence legislation against crypto regulation. This comes amid reports of frozen and delayed customer withdrawals, raising concerns about the exchange's financial stability. CEO Przemysław Kral maintains that Zondacrypto is solvent, but he disclosed that the firm cannot access a wallet containing 4,500 BTC due to missing private keys from a former CEO who has been missing for four years.

The implications for Zondacrypto are severe, as Tusk's allegations could hinder its operations and regulatory standing in Poland. The exchange's troubles are compounded by a dramatic decline in its hot wallet balances, reportedly down 99% since mid-2024, which raises questions about liquidity and customer trust. As Zondacrypto grapples with these challenges, its ability to maintain operations and restore confidence among investors will be critical.

Market professionals should closely monitor Zondacrypto's situation, as it underscores the potential volatility and regulatory risks in the cryptocurrency sector, particularly in jurisdictions with heightened scrutiny.
