Toast (NYSE: TOST) has faced significant headwinds in 2026, with its stock plunging nearly 25% in Q1 amid declines in the software, payments, and restaurant sectors. Despite these challenges, the company's growth narrative remains intact. Toast continues to expand its footprint among U.S. restaurants, tapping into a sizable market of legacy platform users while also exploring new revenue streams through AI and international ventures.

The current market environment presents a potential buying opportunity for Toast. With annual recurring revenue (ARR) growing at a low- to mid-20% rate, the stock's valuation has become attractive, trading at roughly six times its forward enterprise value-to-ARR multiple. Analysts suggest that, given its growth trajectory, Toast could command a higher multiple, potentially pushing its stock price to $42.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that despite short-term volatility, Toast's strong growth fundamentals and niche market position make it a compelling stock to consider for portfolio diversification, particularly as consumer spending stabilizes.
