Capital Group CEO Mike Gitlin emphasized the need for Gen-Z investors to adopt a long-term investment perspective during his remarks at CNBC's Converge Live conference in Singapore. He urged younger investors to move away from "hobby investing" and instead focus on building diversified portfolios that emphasize fundamentals, rather than reacting to short-term market fluctuations or geopolitical events. Gitlin's comments come in light of a growing skepticism among Gen-Z towards traditional financial institutions, with many expressing distrust as a barrier to market participation.

This generational shift is significant for the financial markets, particularly as global equities have shown resilience amid ongoing geopolitical tensions. The MSCI World Index has rebounded, trading above pre-war levels, highlighting a broader market recovery despite the uncertainty surrounding oil prices and inflation. Gitlin noted that successful investing requires a focus on long-term profitability and macroeconomic trends, rather than getting caught up in commodity market timing.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the potential for a paradigm shift in investor behavior. As Gen-Z prioritizes ethical considerations and long-term growth over immediate gains, asset managers may need to adapt their strategies to align with these evolving preferences, ensuring they remain relevant in a changing investment landscape.
