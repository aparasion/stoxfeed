SpaceX is generating significant buzz as anticipation builds for its potential initial public offering (IPO), which could become the largest in history, targeting a valuation between $1.75 trillion and $2 trillion. While the IPO is not yet scheduled, investors can gain exposure through companies and funds that hold stakes in SpaceX, such as Alphabet, Baron Partners Fund, ARK Venture Fund, and Destiny Tech100.

Alphabet stands out as a primary beneficiary, with a stake that could be worth over $100 billion if SpaceX reaches its valuation target. The Baron Partners Fund and ARK Venture Fund also provide substantial exposure, with SpaceX constituting 33% and 17% of their portfolios, respectively. However, investors should consider the broader financial health and volatility associated with SpaceX, which has faced substantial losses despite its growth potential.

As SpaceX gears up for its IPO, market professionals should evaluate these investment vehicles carefully, weighing their exposure to SpaceX against the inherent risks and volatility in the space sector.
