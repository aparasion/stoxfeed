Bitcoin remains resilient at around $67,100, maintaining a trading range between $65,000 and $73,000 despite the most negative social sentiment since the onset of the Iran conflict. While bearish discussions on social media have surged, with a Fear and Greed Index reading of just 9, institutional demand is providing critical support. Notably, March saw record ETF inflows, and Morgan Stanley's approval for a low-fee bitcoin ETF is expected to attract significant investment.

The divergence between sentiment and price action is striking. Despite the extreme fear in the market and aggressive distribution by large holders, institutional buying is keeping bitcoin from breaking lower. However, with a 30-day apparent demand of negative 63,000 BTC, selling pressure from retail investors is outpacing institutional absorption, raising concerns about the sustainability of this price floor.

Market professionals should closely monitor the balance between institutional support and retail sentiment. If bearish sentiment persists without a corresponding price drop, it could signal a critical inflection point for bitcoin, especially as April historically offers strong performance.
