Bankers have pushed back against a recent White House report that downplayed the potential threat of stablecoin yields to traditional bank deposits. The American Bankers Association (ABA) argues that the Council of Economic Advisers misinterpreted the risks by not considering the implications of allowing stablecoin yields, which could entice depositors away from banks. This ongoing dispute is a key issue in the stalled negotiations over the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, which aims to regulate U.S. crypto markets.

The ABA's stance highlights concerns that if stablecoin yields remain unchecked, they could grow from $300 million to as much as $2 trillion, significantly impacting bank deposit levels and lending capabilities. Lawmakers have attempted to find a compromise by proposing a ban on yield for stablecoins resembling deposit accounts, yet the banks remain cautious, fearing that even this measure may not adequately protect their interests.

As the debate continues, the urgency for legislative clarity increases. If the Clarity Act does not progress soon, the risk of destabilizing the banking sector could heighten, making it imperative for market professionals to monitor developments closely.
