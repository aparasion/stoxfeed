Claiming Social Security benefits at age 62 may seem appealing, but it can significantly impact long-term financial stability. Filing early results in a permanent reduction of monthly benefits—approximately 30% less than what one would receive at full retirement age (67 for those born in 1960 or later). For instance, a $2,000 monthly benefit at 67 would drop to $1,400 if claimed at 62, leading to a potential lifetime loss of over $216,000, not accounting for the effects of cost-of-living adjustments.

The financial implications extend beyond mere numbers; smaller checks can lead to increased stress and anxiety as retirees face unexpected expenses, such as healthcare costs or home repairs. As savings dwindle, the reliance on reduced Social Security benefits could create a precarious financial situation, affecting mental health and overall quality of life.

Market professionals should consider these factors when advising clients on retirement planning. A strategic approach to claiming Social Security can preserve financial health and enhance long-term stability in retirement.
