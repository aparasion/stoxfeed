French stocks declined on Thursday, breaking a three-day winning streak as geopolitical tensions escalated following U.S. President Donald Trump's threats of further strikes on Iran. This development raises concerns about potential damage to energy infrastructure in the Gulf, which could exacerbate inflation and hinder global economic growth. Brent crude prices surged nearly 7.7% to around $109 per barrel, intensifying worries about rising costs and interest rate hikes.

The benchmark CAC 40 fell 1.1% to 7,891.95, with notable losses among major players such as STMicroelectronics, Schneider Electric, and Societe Generale, which dropped between 3.6% and 3.7%. While TotalEnergies gained 3% from the rising oil prices, the overall market sentiment remained bearish, reflecting the broader implications of energy volatility on European equities.

Market professionals should closely monitor energy price movements and geopolitical developments, as these factors could significantly influence inflation expectations and monetary policy decisions in the coming months.
