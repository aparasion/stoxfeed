Brent oil prices surged over 3% on Thursday, closing at $105.07 per barrel, amid escalating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. The U.S. and Iran are enforcing blockades, with Iran demanding permission for ships to cross while President Trump asserted U.S. control over the vital shipping lane. This geopolitical friction has led to significantly reduced oil tanker traffic, as both nations have seized vessels during a precarious ceasefire.

The implications for the financial markets are pronounced. Higher oil prices can strain global supply chains and increase inflationary pressures, potentially impacting earnings across multiple sectors, particularly those reliant on energy inputs. The uncertainty surrounding U.S.-Iran negotiations, compounded by reports of internal strife within Iran's leadership, raises concerns about future oil supply disruptions, which could further elevate prices.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as sustained tensions could lead to increased volatility in oil markets and broader economic repercussions.
