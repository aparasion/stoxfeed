President Trump is set to make history by attending oral arguments at the Supreme Court on Wednesday regarding his executive order that could potentially alter the long-standing constitutional guarantee of birthright citizenship in the U.S. This case, known as Trump v. Barbara, challenges the interpretation of the 14th Amendment, which has granted citizenship to anyone born on U.S. soil, regardless of their parents' immigration status.

The implications of this case are significant for the financial markets, as a ruling in favor of Trump's executive order could affect the citizenship status of tens of thousands of babies born each month to undocumented immigrants. This could have ripple effects across various sectors, particularly in labor markets and social services, potentially impacting consumer spending and economic growth.

Market professionals should monitor the outcome of this case closely, as it could influence immigration policy and demographic trends, which are critical factors in long-term economic forecasting and investment strategies.
