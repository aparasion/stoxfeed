Wall Street is experiencing a cautious session, with major indices erasing early gains and dipping into the red. The Dow Jones is down 0.2%, while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq are both off by approximately 0.5%. Investor sentiment is being weighed down by escalating tensions regarding U.S.-Iran relations, particularly after Donald Trump's announcement that he will not extend the ceasefire, raising the specter of renewed conflict.

This geopolitical uncertainty has led to a rebound in oil prices, with Brent crude nearing $95 per barrel, reflecting concerns over supply disruptions. Meanwhile, U.S. retail sales have exceeded expectations, indicating robust consumer demand, which could support economic resilience amidst these tensions. However, precious metals and cryptocurrencies are also under pressure, with gold and silver prices dropping significantly.

Market professionals should closely monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape and its implications for energy prices and broader market sentiment, especially as the Federal Reserve's stance on monetary policy may also be influenced by these developments.
