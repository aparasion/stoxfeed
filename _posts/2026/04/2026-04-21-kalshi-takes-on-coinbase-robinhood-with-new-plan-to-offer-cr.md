Kalshi is set to launch crypto perpetual futures in the U.S., directly challenging established platforms like Coinbase and Robinhood. This expansion marks a significant shift for Kalshi, which has primarily focused on prediction markets, as it seeks to capitalize on the burgeoning demand for regulated digital asset derivatives. The initial offerings will include perpetual futures tied to major cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, leveraging Kalshi's existing licenses from the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC).

This move is particularly relevant as it highlights the increasing overlap between prediction markets and crypto exchanges, with both sectors vying for the same traders amid declining crypto trading volumes. Analysts at Cantor Fitzgerald note that investors are shifting their focus from weak near-term trading results to the potential growth driven by new product launches, including those from Kalshi, Coinbase, and Robinhood. 

For market professionals, Kalshi's entry into crypto derivatives could reshape competitive dynamics, potentially attracting traders looking for regulated options in a space traditionally dominated by offshore platforms.
