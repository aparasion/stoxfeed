Indian shares are poised for a cautious opening on Wednesday as U.S.-Iran peace talks stall, following President Trump's unilateral extension of a ceasefire. This extension hinges on Tehran presenting a "unified proposal," while Trump maintains a naval blockade that Iran has labeled an act of war. The geopolitical tensions have implications for market sentiment, particularly in the energy sector, as Iran threatens to disrupt oil supplies if attacked.

On Tuesday, Indian benchmark indexes Sensex and Nifty rose by 1% and 0.9%, respectively, amid hopes for a resolution. However, the rupee weakened against the dollar, closing at 93.44, influenced by the Reserve Bank of India's easing of speculative trading restrictions. Foreign investors sold shares worth Rs 1,919 crore, contrasting with domestic institutional purchases of Rs 2,221 crore, highlighting divergent market behaviors amid geopolitical uncertainty.

Market professionals should monitor the evolving U.S.-Iran situation closely, as any escalation could impact oil prices and broader market stability, particularly in Asia.
