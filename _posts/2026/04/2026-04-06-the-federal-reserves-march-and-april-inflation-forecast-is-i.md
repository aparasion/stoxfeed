Wall Street's recent bull market has faced turbulence, with the S&P 500, Nasdaq Composite, and Dow Jones Industrial Average all experiencing significant volatility in the past six weeks. The onset of military operations in Iran has exacerbated concerns, particularly following the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, which has led to a dramatic spike in oil prices. Crude oil surged, pushing U.S. gas prices to $4.08 per gallon, marking a 36% increase—the largest monthly jump in three decades.

This energy supply shock raises critical inflationary concerns, with the Federal Reserve's inflation forecast for March and April projected to worsen significantly. The Cleveland Fed's Inflation Nowcasting tool indicates a potential rise in the trailing 12-month inflation rate to 3.28%, which could disrupt the Fed's rate-easing cycle and even prompt interest rate hikes. Such shifts in monetary policy could have profound implications for the stock market, especially given its current high valuations.

Market professionals should brace for heightened volatility as inflationary pressures mount, potentially leading to a reassessment of stock valuations and investment strategies in the face of changing monetary policy.
