In a recent address, President Donald Trump announced that the U.S. is nearing completion of its strategic goals in Iran and plans to initiate powerful strikes in the coming weeks. This hawkish rhetoric, aimed at addressing rising energy prices, resulted in immediate spikes in oil prices and declines across major stock indices, with small caps in the Russell 2000 dropping 1.9% and the Nasdaq down 1.6%. 

The President's comments about shifting responsibility for the Strait of Hormuz to other nations have intensified fears of Iranian dominance and potential inflationary pressures, particularly affecting energy-dependent markets. As Brent crude surged approximately 6.7% to $107, the broader market sentiment turned negative, with Asian indices like South Korea's KOSPI falling 3.7%. The U.S. dollar strengthened against multiple currencies amid rising risk aversion.

Market professionals should closely monitor the implications of rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions on inflation and central bank policies, as these factors could significantly influence market dynamics in the coming weeks.
