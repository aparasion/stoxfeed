U.S. President Donald Trump has issued an ultimatum to Iran, demanding the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz by 8 p.m. Eastern time Tuesday, or face potential U.S. military action against key Iranian infrastructure. This critical maritime route, essential for global oil transport, is already feeling the pressure, with oil and gasoline prices spiking in response to the heightened tensions.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz could lead to supply shortages, driving prices even higher and impacting sectors reliant on stable energy costs. Additionally, the threat of military confrontation raises geopolitical risks, which could further destabilize the region and influence investor sentiment. As lawmakers express divisions over the administration's approach, market volatility is likely to persist, with traders closely monitoring developments for signs of either diplomatic resolution or escalation.

Market professionals should brace for potential price fluctuations in energy stocks and commodities, as well as increased volatility across broader markets, depending on how this situation unfolds.
