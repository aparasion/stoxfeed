Tesla is set to report its Q1 results this Wednesday, with expectations for revenues of $22.05 billion and profits of $1.21 billion, both lower than Q4 2025 figures. Investors will be closely watching gross margin, projected to drop to 17.5%, and free cash flow, expected to turn negative at -$1.57 billion. These metrics could indicate margin pressures linked to increased spending on AI, raising questions about the sustainability of Tesla's ambitious investments.

The implications for the stock are significant. While year-over-year comparisons may show growth—13% for revenue and over 30% for earnings per share—rising inventory levels and unsold vehicles could dampen sentiment. With Tesla's stock trading at elevated multiples compared to traditional automakers, any disappointment in margins or cash flow could lead to a reassessment of its valuation. 

As the earnings call approaches, investors will be looking for clarity on Tesla's AI strategy and future revenue streams, particularly concerning its driverless technology and humanoid robots. Elon Musk's ability to articulate a convincing roadmap could be crucial for maintaining investor confidence and supporting the stock's recent gains.
