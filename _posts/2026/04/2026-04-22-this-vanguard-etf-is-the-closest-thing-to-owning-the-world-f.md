The Vanguard Total World Stock ETF (VT) is gaining traction among investors as a compelling alternative to the popular Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (VOO) and Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI). With a portfolio of approximately 10,000 stocks from around 40 countries, VT offers a 60/40 mix of U.S. and international equities, making it a cost-effective option for global diversification with an expense ratio of just 0.06%.

This shift towards international equities is particularly relevant as they have outperformed the S&P 500 by nearly 3% over the past year. A slowing U.S. economy and attractive valuations overseas are prompting investors to reassess their allocations. The diversification benefits of VT are underscored by its exposure to sectors less dominated by technology, which can help mitigate risk during periods of U.S. market volatility.

For portfolio managers and financial analysts, VT represents a strategic opportunity to enhance diversification and potentially improve returns in a changing economic landscape.
