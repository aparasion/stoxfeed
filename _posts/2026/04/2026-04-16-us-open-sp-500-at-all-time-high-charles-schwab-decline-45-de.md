US equities showed a slight pullback on Thursday, with the S&P 500 slipping about 0.1% after reaching a historic close above 7,000 the previous day. The Nasdaq and Dow also fell around 0.2%, as the recent rally in technology stocks, particularly the “Magnificent Seven,” began to lose momentum. This shift comes amid a backdrop of geopolitical tensions and mixed macroeconomic signals, prompting investors to reassess their positions.

The market's current sentiment remains strong, but the cooling in tech stocks raises questions about sustainability. Earnings reports from major companies like Taiwan Semiconductor and PepsiCo exceeded expectations, while Charles Schwab's mixed results led to a 4% drop in its shares. The ongoing earnings season, coupled with initial jobless claims indicating labor market resilience, is crucial for determining the next market direction, especially with Netflix's upcoming results acting as a potential catalyst.

As the market transitions from momentum-driven gains to a more selective phase, professionals should closely monitor earnings surprises and macroeconomic developments, as these will likely dictate volatility and overall market sentiment in the near term.
