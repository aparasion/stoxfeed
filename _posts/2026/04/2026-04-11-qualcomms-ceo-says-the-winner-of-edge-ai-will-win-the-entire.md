Qualcomm (QCOM) CEO Cristiano Amon has emphasized the importance of edge AI in recent discussions at major tech events, arguing that the future of artificial intelligence will be defined by local processing on devices rather than centralized data centers. This shift towards edge computing promises lower latency and enhanced privacy, positioning Qualcomm at the forefront with its new Snapdragon X2 processors designed for on-device AI capabilities.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Analysts project that Qualcomm could capture 20% to 25% of the Windows PC market by 2027, bolstered by a robust automotive pipeline worth $45 billion. With fiscal Q1 2026 revenues in automotive and IoT hitting record levels and a forward P/E ratio of around 12—below many AI-focused peers—Qualcomm appears undervalued despite its strong growth trajectory.

Investors should note that Qualcomm's evolving role as an edge AI infrastructure leader may not yet be fully recognized by the market. As the company diversifies beyond smartphones, there’s potential for a substantial revaluation, making QCOM an intriguing buy for those looking to capitalize on the AI revolution.
