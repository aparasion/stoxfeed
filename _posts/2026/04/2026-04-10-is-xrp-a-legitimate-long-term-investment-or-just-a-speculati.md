XRP has seen a significant market cap of $82 billion, supported by Ripple's aggressive acquisition strategy, which totaled over $2.4 billion in 2025. Despite these developments, XRP's price remains at $1.35, a striking 63% decline from its all-time high in July 2025. This raises questions about whether XRP is a sound long-term investment or merely a speculative asset. Ripple's acquisitions, including a $1.2 billion purchase of prime brokerage Hidden Road, enhance its financial services platform, positioning XRP as a more legitimate investment compared to other altcoins.

The XRP Ledger (XRPL) is now home to approximately $458 million in tokenized real-world assets, a significant increase from just $5 million at the beginning of 2025. This growth underscores the platform's potential, yet the disconnect between XRP's price and the increasing on-chain capital suggests that short-term trading may not yield favorable results. 

Investors should consider that while Ripple's infrastructure offers substantial long-term value, the immediate price fluctuations are often influenced by broader macroeconomic trends rather than XRP's specific fundamentals.
