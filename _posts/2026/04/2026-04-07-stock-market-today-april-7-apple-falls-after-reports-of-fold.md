Apple (AAPL) shares fell 2.07% to close at $253.50 on Tuesday, driven by reports of engineering challenges related to its anticipated foldable iPhone and concerns over slowing growth in its App Store segment. Trading volume surged to 61.1 million shares, nearly 30% above the three-month average, indicating heightened investor activity amid these developments. 

The drop in Apple’s stock comes as analysts weigh the implications of potential shipment delays for the foldable device, set to debut in September. While some investors are apprehensive about the impact on the company’s services growth—previously up 14% in fiscal Q1—others remain optimistic about the stock's long-term potential. The upcoming fiscal Q2 results on April 30 will be critical for assessing these trends and could influence market sentiment significantly.

Investors should closely monitor Apple's earnings report for insights into service segment performance and any updates on the foldable iPhone, as these factors could shape stock valuation and market positioning in the consumer electronics sector.
