Nike (NKE.US) reported fiscal Q3 2026 results that exceeded market expectations, initially boosting shares by 3% in after-hours trading. However, the stock ultimately fell nearly 10% to $47 as investors digested mixed signals from the earnings report. Key highlights included earnings per share of $0.35, surpassing estimates of $0.30, and revenue of $11.3 billion, slightly above the consensus. Despite improvements in inventory management, gross margins were pressured by higher tariffs and rising operating costs.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. While North America showed resilience with a 3% revenue increase, overall sales remain under pressure, particularly in Greater China, where revenue is expected to decline by 20%. Analysts are concerned that the anticipated recovery is taking longer than previously expected, which could extend the period of pressure on Nike’s results and dampen investor confidence.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Nike is making strides in stabilizing its business, the path to recovery appears uneven and prolonged, particularly in crucial markets. This uncertainty may continue to weigh on the stock and investor sentiment in the near term.
