A renewed feud between Star Xu, founder of OKX, and Changpeng Zhao (CZ), founder of Binance, has emerged, ignited by the release of Zhao's memoir. Xu has accused Zhao of being a "habitual liar" regarding a decade-old contract dispute from Zhao's time at OKCoin, where he faced allegations of misleading conduct. The clash not only revisits these historical grievances but also delves into personal matters, with Xu questioning Zhao's marital disclosures amidst ongoing scrutiny of Binance's operations.

This dispute carries significant implications for the cryptocurrency market, as it underscores the ongoing tensions between major exchanges, which can influence investor sentiment and market stability. The public nature of their conflict, including a $1 billion wager proposed by Zhao, could exacerbate volatility in crypto assets, particularly if it distracts from broader market trends or regulatory developments.

Market professionals should monitor this feud closely, as reputational damage or operational disruptions stemming from such high-profile disputes can lead to shifts in trading behavior and investor confidence in both platforms.
