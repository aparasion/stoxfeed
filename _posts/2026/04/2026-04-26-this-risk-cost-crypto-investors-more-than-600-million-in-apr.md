In April, the cryptocurrency sector faced significant turmoil as protocols suffered over $606 million in hacks, marking the worst theft month since the Bybit breach in February 2025. Two major attacks, attributed to North Korea’s Lazarus Group, targeted projects within the Solana and Ethereum ecosystems, leading to a dramatic loss of trust and capital in decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols. Following these breaches, Aave experienced an exodus of over $8.4 billion in deposits within 48 hours, contributing to a broader decline in total value locked across DeFi.

The implications for the financial markets are profound, as investor confidence in DeFi wanes amid rising skepticism about its viability as an investment vehicle. Ethereum and Solana, while not compromised at the chain level, saw substantial outflows, with Ethereum alone losing $1.6 billion on April 24. This trend highlights the fragility of trust in the space, which could lead to further price declines.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to prioritize security by keeping assets in custodial accounts or cold wallets rather than in DeFi protocols. While the current environment may appear bleak, historical patterns suggest that price dips following hacks can represent buying opportunities for resilient assets like Ethereum and Solana.
