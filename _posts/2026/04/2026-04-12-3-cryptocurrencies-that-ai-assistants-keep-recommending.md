As artificial intelligence (AI) tools gain traction in investment circles, many Americans are turning to them for cryptocurrency advice. While AI can analyze vast data sets, it also risks providing inaccurate recommendations based on potentially unreliable sources. Notably, leading AI assistants like ChatGPT and Grok suggest that Bitcoin and Ethereum should comprise 50% to 70% of a crypto portfolio, highlighting their dominance in the market and institutional adoption.

This trend underscores a growing reliance on AI for investment decisions, particularly in the volatile crypto space. Bitcoin's reputation as "digital gold" and Ethereum's role in decentralized finance (DeFi) position them as safer bets amid a landscape where only half of the top ten cryptocurrencies from five years ago remain relevant. The upcoming Ethereum upgrades aim to enhance scalability and usability, further solidifying its market position.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach AI-generated investment advice with caution. While these tools can provide initial insights, thorough personal research and risk assessment remain essential in navigating the complexities of the cryptocurrency market.
