Apple shares dropped 4% on Tuesday following reports of engineering setbacks related to its upcoming foldable iPhone, which was initially slated for a September 2026 launch. Sources indicate that both Apple and its supply chain are under pressure to meet deadlines, but current solutions are insufficient to address the challenges. This situation raises concerns about potential delays in a product that is expected to be a significant addition to Apple's lineup.

The implications for the financial markets are notable, as iPhones account for over half of Apple’s revenue, which reached $143.8 billion in Q1 of fiscal 2026. Delays in the foldable iPhone could hinder Apple's ability to capture market share against competitors like Samsung, which has already established a foothold in the foldable segment since 2019. 

Market professionals should closely monitor Apple’s progress in resolving these engineering issues, as any further delays could impact revenue forecasts and investor sentiment in the tech sector.
