Brent crude prices have surged back above $100 per barrel, rebounding sharply with a 7.6% increase as market sentiment shifts following Donald Trump's recent address on the ongoing conflict in Iran. His failure to present clear exit strategies and a hawkish tone have dampened earlier optimism sparked by Iran's willingness to engage in peace talks. The OIL contract is now testing a key resistance level at $110, while technical indicators suggest the market is nearing overbought territory.

This resurgence in oil prices is critical as it reflects heightened geopolitical tensions and the potential for sustained volatility in energy markets. Trump's announcement of continued military operations and threats against Iranian infrastructure have raised concerns about supply disruptions, further complicating the outlook for global oil supplies. Analysts are wary of the implications for U.S. fuel prices, which are already at record highs.

Market professionals should monitor the resistance level at $110 closely, as a sustained breach could lead to further price escalation, while any signs of de-escalation in the conflict may provide a counterbalance to current bullish momentum.
