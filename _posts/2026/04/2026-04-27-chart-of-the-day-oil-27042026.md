Brent crude prices opened the week on a positive note, trading at $101.56 per barrel, marking a 1.81% increase. The price has consistently moved above key moving averages, indicating a sustained uptrend. The Relative Strength Index (RSI) suggests moderate upward momentum, with no immediate overbought conditions. Geopolitical tensions surrounding the Strait of Hormuz remain a significant factor influencing oil prices, as Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi's recent discussions with Oman hint at potential de-escalation.

The market's reaction to these developments is crucial, especially with WTI prices retreating from earlier highs. Traders should remain vigilant, as control over the Strait of Hormuz is a critical leverage point for Iran, complicating any expectations for a smooth resolution. Additionally, upcoming decisions from central banks like the Fed, ECB, and BoE, along with key macroeconomic data releases, could further impact oil market volatility.

In summary, market professionals should prepare for potential price spikes in oil as geopolitical developments unfold, particularly from the Middle East, alongside critical economic indicators this week.
