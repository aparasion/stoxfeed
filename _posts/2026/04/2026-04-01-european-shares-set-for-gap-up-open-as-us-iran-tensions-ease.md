European stocks are poised for a strong opening as tensions in the Middle East show signs of de-escalation. U.S. President Donald Trump announced that the military campaign against Iran could conclude within two to three weeks, claiming significant objectives have been met. His remarks suggest that control of the Strait of Hormuz, a vital oil shipping lane, may soon be handed back to other nations, potentially stabilizing oil supply concerns that have plagued markets.

The implications for financial markets are significant. As investors reacted positively to the news, U.S. equities surged, with the Nasdaq Composite rising 3.8% and the S&P 500 gaining 2.9%. Brent crude prices also climbed above $105 a barrel, reflecting ongoing supply risks despite the potential for reduced military engagement. Analysts caution that even if conflict eases, infrastructure damage may keep supply tight in the near term.

Market professionals should remain vigilant, as geopolitical developments continue to influence energy prices and equity performance. The situation underscores the interconnectedness of global markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to oil supply and geopolitical stability.
