Annaly Capital (NLY) has demonstrated solid total return performance since its IPO, notably outperforming the S&P 500, largely due to its substantial 12.8% dividend yield. However, potential investors should recognize that Annaly operates as a mortgage REIT, managing a portfolio of mortgage-backed securities rather than traditional real estate. This distinction is crucial, as it indicates that Annaly's focus is on total return rather than providing a consistent dividend stream.

Investors should be cautious; Annaly's dividends are highly volatile, with historical patterns showing significant fluctuations that align closely with its stock price. For those relying on dividends for income, Annaly may not be the best fit, as its dividend reliability is questionable. While recent increases might suggest a positive trend, the long-term outlook for stable income remains uncertain.

The key takeaway is that Annaly Capital is suited for investors prioritizing total returns over income reliability. Those looking to reinvest dividends may find value, but income-focused investors should approach with caution.
