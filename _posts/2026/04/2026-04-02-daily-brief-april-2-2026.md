### Market Brief for April 2, 2026

#### What Happened Today
U.S. stock markets experienced significant volatility, with major indices declining sharply following President Trump's address regarding escalating military actions in Iran. The S&P 500 fell by 1.13%, while the Nasdaq-100 dropped 1.53%, reflecting investor anxiety over geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices, which surged past $100 per barrel. Energy stocks were notable movers, with Chevron and other oil-related equities benefiting from the price spike. The tech sector faced pressure, particularly AI-related stocks, as the Nasdaq entered correction territory, down over 10% from its peak. In contrast, companies like Nebius Group and Intuitive Machines saw positive momentum, driven by growth in the AI and space sectors.

#### Expectations for the Next Session
Market participants are closely monitoring upcoming economic indicators, including the March jobs report, which is expected to show a slight rebound in nonfarm payrolls. Geopolitical tensions remain a key concern, particularly the ongoing conflict in Iran, which could further impact oil prices and inflation expectations. Investor sentiment is currently bearish, with many anticipating continued market volatility as the situation unfolds. Additionally, the potential for further declines in tech stocks may present buying opportunities for value investors looking to capitalize on lower prices amid the broader market sell-off.
