MRA Advisory Group has significantly reduced its stake in the Direxion NASDAQ-100 Equal Weighted Index Shares (QQQE), selling 52,132 shares valued at approximately $5.35 million in the first quarter of 2026. This move has decreased the position's value by $5.61 million, reflecting both the sale and market price changes. Following this transaction, QQQE now represents 2.89% of MRA's total assets under management.

This reduction in holdings comes amidst a broader market trend where the QQQE has underperformed the S&P 500 by 3.8 percentage points over the past year, despite a 33.1% increase in its share price. The equal-weighted strategy of QQQE aims to provide balanced exposure to the Nasdaq-100, mitigating the influence of large-cap stocks, which have dominated recent market gains. MRA's rebalancing likely reflects a strategic shift rather than a negative outlook on the ETF's fundamentals.

For market professionals, this transaction underscores the importance of portfolio diversification and the potential implications of concentrated gains in mega-cap stocks. Investors may need to assess their strategies based on market dynamics and individual stock performance within the Nasdaq-100.
