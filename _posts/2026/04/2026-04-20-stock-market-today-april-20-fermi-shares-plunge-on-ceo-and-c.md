Fermi (NASDAQ:FRMI), an AI-focused private power developer, saw its stock plummet 17.56% to $5.40 on Monday following the abrupt departures of its CEO and CFO. This leadership shake-up coincides with the announcement of a new strategic initiative dubbed “Fermi 2.0.” Trading volume surged to 61 million shares, significantly surpassing the three-month average of 9 million, indicating heightened investor concern.

The decline reflects broader investor uncertainty about Fermi's future, especially as the company struggles to secure clients for its ambitious Project Matador data center in Texas. This project, intended to be the largest energy and data campus in the U.S., has faced delays due to a lack of major customers. Despite the turmoil, Stifel analysts maintain a “buy” rating, suggesting that a new CEO could improve negotiations with potential clients.

Investors should closely monitor the appointment of Fermi’s new CEO, as this decision could significantly impact the company's trajectory and stock performance moving forward.
