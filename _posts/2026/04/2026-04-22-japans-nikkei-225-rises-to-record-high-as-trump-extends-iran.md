Japan's Nikkei 225 surged to a record high of 59,691 on Wednesday, buoyed by positive trade data that showed a surplus of 667 billion yen for March. This growth comes amid a backdrop of geopolitical tensions, as President Trump announced an extension of the U.S. ceasefire with Iran, despite ongoing uncertainties regarding negotiations and the Middle East conflict. While Japan's export performance shines, other Asia-Pacific markets faced declines, with South Korea's Kospi down 1.02% and Australia's S&P/ASX 200 falling by 0.98%.

The divergence in market performance highlights the impact of regional geopolitical risks on investor sentiment. The uncertainty surrounding the Iranian negotiations has led to volatility in oil prices, with West Texas Intermediate and Brent crude both retreating slightly. Additionally, the upcoming Bank of Japan policy meeting adds another layer of complexity for investors.

Market professionals should monitor the implications of these geopolitical developments on commodity prices and regional equities, particularly as the situation in the Middle East evolves and trade data continues to influence market dynamics.
