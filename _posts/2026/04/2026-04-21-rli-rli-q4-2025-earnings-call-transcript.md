RLI Corp. reported strong fourth-quarter results, with operating earnings per share rising to $0.94, up from $0.52 year-over-year, driven by improved underwriting performance and lower storm activity. The company achieved a combined ratio of 82.6, significantly better than the previous year's 94.4, and marked its 30th consecutive year of underwriting profitability, a rare feat in the insurance industry. Net investment income also grew by 9% in the quarter, contributing to a 33% increase in book value per share for the year.

These results underscore RLI's disciplined approach in a competitive market, particularly in the property segment, where premium growth faced challenges but was supported by favorable prior-year loss emergence. The company’s ability to maintain profitability amid declining premiums in certain lines, such as catastrophe-exposed property, highlights its strategic focus on risk-adjusted returns and operational efficiency.

For market professionals, RLI’s performance signals resilience in a volatile environment, emphasizing the importance of disciplined underwriting and strategic investment in technology to enhance operational efficiency. The firm’s commitment to maintaining double-digit rate increases in auto liability suggests ongoing opportunities for growth, particularly as market conditions evolve.
