As market volatility looms, potential retirees are grappling with the risk of a stock market crash just before they exit the workforce. A proactive approach to retirement planning is essential, especially for those on the brink of retirement. The author outlines a three-part strategy to mitigate the impact of a downturn, emphasizing the importance of flexibility and preparedness.

First, delaying retirement can provide additional time to recover from market losses, allowing individuals to maintain their income streams longer. Second, adjusting spending habits can help preserve retirement savings during turbulent times, with a willingness to embrace a more frugal lifestyle. Lastly, maintaining a robust cash reserve—ideally covering two to four years of living expenses—can prevent retirees from having to liquidate investments at a loss during market downturns.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the importance of having a contingency plan for retirement. By anticipating potential market challenges, investors can better safeguard their financial futures and navigate the complexities of transitioning into retirement.
