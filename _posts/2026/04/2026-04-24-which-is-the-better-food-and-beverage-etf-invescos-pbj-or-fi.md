The Invesco Food & Beverage ETF (PBJ) and First Trust Nasdaq Food & Beverage ETF (FTXG) offer targeted exposure to the U.S. food and beverage sector, but they cater to different investor priorities. PBJ has historically outperformed with lower volatility, while FTXG provides a higher yield and slightly lower expense ratio, making it appealing for income-focused investors.

Both ETFs consist of 31 stocks, with PBJ incorporating a mix of Consumer Defensive, Cyclical, and Basic Materials, while FTXG focuses predominantly on Consumer Defensive stocks. Recent performance highlights PBJ's superior one-year return and lower maximum drawdown, indicating stronger capital appreciation potential compared to FTXG's focus on stable passive income.

Investors must weigh their priorities: choose PBJ for growth and reduced volatility or FTXG for income generation through higher dividends. Both funds serve as defensive plays in a portfolio, but their differing strategies necessitate careful consideration based on individual investment goals.
