The recent U.S. strikes on Kharg Island, Iran's primary oil export terminal, alongside Iran's retaliatory actions against Saudi petrochemical facilities, signal an escalation of conflict that directly threatens the oil and gas sector. Iran's Revolutionary Guard has declared an end to restraint, raising the specter of broader international conflict and direct threats to U.S. and allied infrastructure, including military bases and energy assets.

This escalating situation is already impacting financial markets, with Brent and WTI crude prices climbing as traders react to increased risks in the Gulf region. Concurrently, U.S. equity futures are declining, reflecting a shift toward risk-off sentiment among investors. The Strait of Hormuz remains a critical flashpoint, with potential disruptions to global energy supplies further heightening market anxiety.

As diplomatic efforts falter, with Iran rejecting a ceasefire proposal, market professionals should brace for potential energy shocks. The EU and Japan are already taking steps to secure oil supplies, underscoring the urgency of the situation and its implications for global economic stability.
