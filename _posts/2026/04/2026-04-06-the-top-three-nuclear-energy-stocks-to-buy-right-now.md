Nuclear energy is experiencing a significant revival, with 75 reactors currently under construction and another 120 planned globally, according to the World Nuclear Association. The U.S. Department of Energy aims to triple nuclear power production by mid-century, positioning nuclear energy at the intersection of rising power demands from artificial intelligence, green energy initiatives, and geopolitical instability in energy markets.

Investors should take note of three key players in this sector: Cameco (CCJ), Constellation Energy (CEG), and BWX Technologies (BWXT). Cameco, the world’s second-largest uranium producer, reported an 11% revenue increase in 2025, driven by its high-grade uranium mines and a strong profit margin of 16.93%. Constellation, the largest nuclear power provider in the U.S., is expanding its fleet to meet AI power needs and has seen its revenue grow by 8.34%. BWX is pioneering small modular reactors, with an 18% revenue increase in 2025, showcasing its innovative approach to nuclear technology.

As the nuclear renaissance unfolds, these companies represent compelling investment opportunities, each playing a critical role in the evolving energy landscape. Their growth trajectories and strategic initiatives make them worth considering for long-term portfolio positioning.
