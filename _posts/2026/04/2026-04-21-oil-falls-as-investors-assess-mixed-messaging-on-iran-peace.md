Oil prices fell on Tuesday amid escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran, as uncertainty looms over the second round of peace talks. Reports indicate that U.S. Vice President JD Vance will lead the delegation to Pakistan, while Iranian officials express reluctance to negotiate, emphasizing their readiness to take military action. This backdrop has contributed to a 1.51% drop in West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures, settling at $88.26 per barrel, while Brent crude futures decreased by 0.68% to $94.87.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly as Rystad Energy highlights that disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz could lead to a major revision of oil price forecasts. If prices breach the $100 mark, it could unlock an additional 2.1 million barrels per day of supply from South America, positioning the region as a critical player in global oil dynamics.

Market professionals should monitor these developments closely, as the interplay between geopolitical tensions and oil supply could create volatility and impact energy sector investments in the near term.
