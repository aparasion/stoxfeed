Sanctioned cryptocurrency exchange Grinex has suspended trading after a significant cyber attack resulted in the theft of over 1 billion Russian rubles (approximately $13.7 million). The exchange, linked to Russia’s crypto ecosystem and accused of facilitating sanctions evasion, reported that the attack shows signs of involvement from foreign intelligence agencies, prompting a criminal complaint to law enforcement. Grinex had previously been viewed as a successor to the similarly sanctioned Garantex exchange.

This incident highlights the vulnerabilities within the crypto sector, particularly for exchanges operating in politically sensitive environments. The attack not only disrupts Grinex's operations but also raises concerns about the security of other platforms within the region, such as TokenSpot, which may have been targeted by the same entity. The potential for further sanctions-related scrutiny could impact investor confidence and trading volumes in the broader cryptocurrency market.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments surrounding Grinex and similar exchanges, as ongoing security threats could lead to increased regulatory actions and volatility in cryptocurrency valuations.
