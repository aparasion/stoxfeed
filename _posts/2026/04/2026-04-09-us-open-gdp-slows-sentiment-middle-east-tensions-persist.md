Wall Street is navigating a complex landscape as mixed economic signals emerge amid geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and persistently high oil prices. February data revealed a 0.5% month-over-month rise in U.S. consumer spending, while core PCE inflation held steady at 0.4%. However, personal income dipped by 0.1%, and initial jobless claims exceeded forecasts, hinting at a cooling labor market. The fourth-quarter GDP growth was revised down to 0.5%, indicating a slowdown despite ongoing consumer support.

These developments are influencing investor sentiment, as rising geopolitical uncertainty, particularly surrounding Iran, dampens risk appetite. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq are slightly down, reflecting caution among investors balancing solid yet slowing macroeconomic indicators against a backdrop of potential regional escalation. Elevated oil prices continue to exert inflationary pressures, complicating the Federal Reserve's interest rate decisions.

As the market digests these factors, the upcoming economic releases will be critical in shaping expectations for monetary policy and guiding investor strategies.
