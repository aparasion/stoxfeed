The UK’s FTSE 100 index showed resilience on Friday, climbing 0.33% to 10,638.69 as gains in financial, mining, and select industrial stocks offset early losses. The market remains cautious, with investors closely monitoring geopolitical developments in the Middle East and upcoming U.S.-Iran diplomatic talks. Notably, Convatec Group led the rally with a 4.5% increase, while Kingfisher and Coca-Cola HBC also posted solid gains.

This uptick in the FTSE 100 reflects a broader trend where investors are selectively optimistic despite geopolitical uncertainties. The performance of key sectors, particularly financials and industrials, indicates a potential recovery phase, with several stocks like Burberry Group and Barclays seeing gains between 1.4% and 3.6%. However, losses in energy and defense sectors, including BAE Systems and Compass Group, highlight the market's mixed sentiment.

Market professionals should watch for further developments in the Middle East, as diplomatic outcomes could significantly influence investor sentiment and sector performance in the coming days.
