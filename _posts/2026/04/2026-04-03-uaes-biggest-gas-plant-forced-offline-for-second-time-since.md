WTI crude oil prices surged to $111.29 following significant disruptions in the Middle East, particularly at the Habshan gas facilities in the UAE, which were temporarily shut down due to a fire caused by an attack. Operated by ADNOC, Habshan is a critical gas processing site with a capacity of 6.1 billion standard cubic feet per day. This incident marks the second operational suspension at Habshan since the onset of the ongoing conflict, raising concerns about regional energy stability.

The implications for the energy sector are profound, as disruptions in major processing facilities can lead to supply constraints and increased volatility in oil prices. Additionally, the targeting of oil infrastructure, such as the recent drone attack on Kuwait's Mina Al-Ahmadi refinery, underscores the heightened geopolitical risks in the region, which could further strain supply chains and impact global oil markets.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Middle East, as ongoing attacks could lead to sustained price increases and influence trading strategies in the energy sector.
