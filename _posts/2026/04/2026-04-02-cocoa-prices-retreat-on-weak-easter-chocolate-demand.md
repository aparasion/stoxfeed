Cocoa prices faced significant pressure on Thursday, with May ICE NY cocoa closing down 2.99% and London cocoa down 0.45%. The decline is primarily attributed to concerns over weak chocolate demand, with early estimates indicating a potential 5% drop in Easter chocolate sales compared to last year. Additionally, rising ICE cocoa inventories, which reached a 1.5-year high, are contributing to the bearish sentiment in the market.

The current dynamics in the cocoa market reflect broader trends affecting supply and demand. While West African drought conditions have raised concerns about future production, high inventories and weak consumer demand are overshadowing these factors. Notably, major chocolate producer Barry Callebaut reported a 22% decline in cocoa sales volume, signaling challenges in the sector. The recent cuts in cocoa prices paid to farmers in Ghana and the Ivory Coast further complicate the outlook.

Market professionals should monitor the evolving supply-demand balance closely, as any shifts could impact cocoa prices significantly. The potential for short-covering rallies exists, particularly given the excessive short positions held by funds in London cocoa.
