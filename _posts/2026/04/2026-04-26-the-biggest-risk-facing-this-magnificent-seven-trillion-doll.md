Apple Inc. (AAPL) continues to dominate the tech landscape, but investors should remain vigilant about the long-term risks associated with its flagship product, the iPhone. Despite a staggering 931% increase in stock value over the past decade, the company's reliance on the iPhone for 59.3% of its revenue raises concerns, particularly as emerging technologies like artificial intelligence (AI) could disrupt its competitive position. 

The potential for an AI-native device to capture consumer interest poses a significant threat. While Apple has a robust ecosystem with over 2.5 billion active devices and an incoming CEO with strong hardware expertise, the market is evolving rapidly. Companies like OpenAI are also poised to introduce new devices that could challenge Apple's dominance. 

Investors should keep a close eye on developments in AI hardware, as these innovations could reshape consumer behavior and market dynamics. However, there’s no immediate cause for alarm; Apple’s established market presence and distribution capabilities provide a strong buffer against potential disruptions.
