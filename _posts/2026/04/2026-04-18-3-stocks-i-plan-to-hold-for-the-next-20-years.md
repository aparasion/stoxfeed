In a nod to Warren Buffett's investment philosophy, one analyst identifies three stocks poised for long-term growth: AbbVie, Amazon, and Enbridge. Each of these companies has demonstrated resilience and adaptability, making them prime candidates for a 20-year holding period. AbbVie, despite facing patent expirations, has successfully transitioned from its flagship product, Humira, to new revenue generators like Rinvoq and Skyrizi, while maintaining a strong dividend history. 

Amazon's diverse business model extends beyond e-commerce into cloud services and AI, creating a robust ecosystem that keeps consumers engaged. Its continuous expansion into new markets further solidifies its competitive edge. Meanwhile, Enbridge's extensive pipeline network and status as North America's leading natural gas utility position it well to benefit from sustained demand for fossil fuels, complemented by a reliable dividend growth record.

For investors, these selections highlight the importance of companies with strong fundamentals and adaptability in navigating market changes, making them worthy of consideration for long-term portfolios.
