Figma (FIG) experienced significant volatility in March 2026, suffering a 28.1% decline amid broader market pressures. This downturn comes after a 30.6% drop in January and a 13.4% rebound in February, highlighting the stock's roller coaster ride. The decline can be attributed to a combination of factors, including investor skepticism towards richly valued software stocks, concerns over AI competition, and a general slowdown in enterprise spending on traditional software-as-a-service (SaaS) tools.

The software sector is currently facing headwinds as companies reassess their reliance on traditional SaaS in light of advancing AI technologies. Figma's core collaborative design software is particularly vulnerable to this shift, especially with competitors like Adobe introducing overlapping features. Despite these challenges, Figma has achieved positive cash flow and maintains a strong balance sheet, allowing for strategic investments during this transitional phase.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Figma's stock remains volatile and richly valued at approximately 13 times sales, its long-term potential hinges on successfully navigating the AI landscape. Investors may need to adopt a patient approach, as the company's strong execution history could yield significant rewards by 2030 if it adapts effectively.
