President Donald Trump has dismissed a proposed 45-day ceasefire aimed at resolving tensions with Iran, which would have preceded a looming deadline for Tehran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. A White House official confirmed that Trump has not endorsed the ceasefire, despite ongoing discussions involving the U.S., Iran, and regional mediators. Instead, Trump issued a stark ultimatum via social media, threatening military strikes on Iranian infrastructure if the Strait remains closed by Tuesday night.

This situation holds significant implications for financial markets, particularly in sectors sensitive to geopolitical tensions, such as energy and defense. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments, and any escalation in conflict could lead to supply disruptions, impacting oil prices and related equities. Market participants should be vigilant as developments unfold, especially given the potential for volatility in energy markets.

Investors should prepare for heightened market fluctuations as the deadline approaches, particularly in oil and defense stocks, which may react sharply to any announcements from the White House or developments in the conflict.
