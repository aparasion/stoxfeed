Micron Technology (MU) experienced a sharp decline in March, with shares plummeting up to 18.1% despite reporting record-breaking fiscal Q2 results. The semiconductor company posted a staggering revenue of $23.9 billion, a 196% year-over-year increase, and adjusted earnings per share soared to $12.20, driven by robust demand for memory chips used in AI processing. However, this impressive performance was overshadowed by Alphabet's announcement of a new compression algorithm that could significantly reduce the memory required for AI applications, potentially impacting Micron’s NAND processor sales.

The implications for the financial markets are multifaceted. While Micron's recent results exceeded analysts' expectations, the introduction of Google's algorithm raises concerns about future demand for its memory products, particularly those generating 21% of revenue. This development could disrupt the current momentum in Micron's stock, which had gained 239% in 2025 prior to the announcement.

Investors should remain cautious and avoid hasty decisions. The long-term effects of the algorithm could lead to increased AI adoption and, paradoxically, a resurgence in demand for Micron's memory chips as efficiency drives consumption.
