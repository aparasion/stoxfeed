The S&P 500 index has faced a rocky start in 2026, down 4% as of April 1, amid rising interest rates, geopolitical tensions, and overall market uncertainty. This downturn follows a robust 18% total return in 2025, highlighting the volatility that often accompanies stock market investing. Long-term investors are encouraged to maintain composure, as historical data shows the S&P has delivered a remarkable 277% total return over the past decade, despite experiencing significant drawdowns in previous years.

In this environment, savvy investors are advised to adopt a long-term perspective and consider deploying capital while prices are lower. The Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (NYSEMKT: VOO), with its minimal expense ratio of 0.03%, presents a compelling buying opportunity. Additionally, stocks like Alphabet and Meta Platforms, which have seen declines of 5.5% and 13% respectively this year, may offer attractive entry points for those focused on long-term growth, particularly in the AI sector.

Investors should view current market volatility as a chance to capitalize on discounted assets, reinforcing the adage to "be greedy when others are fearful."
