Intel (INTC) shares jumped 2.31% in after-hours trading following a strong first-quarter earnings report that exceeded Wall Street expectations. The semiconductor giant reported a 7% year-over-year revenue increase to $13.6 billion, significantly surpassing its prior guidance. Notably, Intel's data center and AI revenue soared 22% to $5.1 billion, indicating a robust acceleration in its most critical growth area, as the company positions itself to capitalize on the expanding AI infrastructure market.

This earnings report underscores a pivotal shift in the narrative surrounding CPUs, which are regaining importance in AI applications alongside GPUs. CEO Lip-Bu Tan emphasized that as AI workloads evolve, CPUs will play a crucial role in managing data and system coordination. This trend is evident in Intel's collaborations with major players like Nvidia and Google, suggesting that Intel's CPU business is well-positioned to benefit from the AI boom.

While the results are promising, risks remain, particularly with ongoing losses in Intel's foundry business. Investors should weigh these factors carefully, as the stock's recent surge may already reflect optimism about the company's turnaround and the CPU's renewed relevance in AI.
