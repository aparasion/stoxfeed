The ongoing war in Iran is shaping up to be a pivotal factor for global markets in 2026, with the S&P 500 currently up over 4% as investors remain optimistic amid U.S. President Trump's comments on potential peace talks. However, the more significant market driver may be the Federal Reserve's direction under Jerome Powell, whose term ends on May 15. The recent dropping of a criminal investigation into Powell could facilitate the confirmation of Trump's nominee, Kevin Warsh, but uncertainty remains about Powell's future role.

As the Fed's leadership could influence market stability, investors are advised to prepare for potential volatility. If Powell departs, it could raise concerns about the Fed's independence, with significant implications for market confidence. Conversely, a smooth transition may allow the S&P 500 to reach new heights.

To mitigate risk amid this uncertainty, investors should consider trimming positions in high-priced stocks and reallocating to more stable investments, such as dividend stocks, to enhance portfolio resilience against market fluctuations.
