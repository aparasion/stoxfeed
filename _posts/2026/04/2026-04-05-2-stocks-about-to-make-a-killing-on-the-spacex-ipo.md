SpaceX is poised to launch one of the largest IPOs in history, seeking a valuation of up to $2 trillion as it prepares to go public after merging with Elon Musk's AI venture, xAI. The company's last recorded valuation was $1.25 trillion, and it aims to raise as much as $75 billion in this offering, which would set a record for IPO capital raised. This monumental event not only stands to significantly enrich investors like Musk but also has implications for major players in the tech sector.

Alphabet, which holds a 7% stake in SpaceX, could see its investment soar to approximately $140 billion if the company achieves its target valuation. This windfall would provide Alphabet with much-needed liquidity to support its ambitious AI initiatives. Meanwhile, Nvidia, a key supplier for SpaceX, stands to benefit from increased chip purchases as the company expands its operations and integrates xAI, potentially fueling further demand for Nvidia's technology.

The upcoming IPO presents a critical opportunity for investors to reassess their portfolios, particularly with Alphabet and Nvidia positioned to gain from SpaceX's success.
