Avis Budget Group (CAR) has experienced extreme volatility reminiscent of the GameStop saga, with shares skyrocketing from under $100 to nearly $850 before plummeting 38% in a single day. As of Thursday, CAR was trading around $417, with options volume surging to over 200,000 contracts and implied volatility hitting 235%, far exceeding the S&P 500's 20%. This dramatic price movement has prompted speculation about brokers adjusting margin requirements, with reports indicating that traders holding concentrated positions must now post 100% margin.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly for traders and portfolio managers. The sharp rise and subsequent fall in CAR's stock highlights the risks associated with low float stocks controlled by a few shareholders, which can lead to erratic price movements and increased trading costs. The situation may also prompt a reevaluation of risk management strategies among brokers and traders alike.

Market professionals should closely monitor CAR's trading patterns and the broader implications of such volatility on margin requirements and liquidity in similar stocks.
