Crude oil and gasoline prices surged on Monday, with May WTI crude closing at a four-week high, driven by escalating tensions in Iran. President Trump's warning of potential military action if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed has heightened market volatility, as the waterway is crucial for global oil supply. The U.S. military's preparations for strikes against Iranian energy targets further contributed to the bullish sentiment in the oil market.

The implications for financial markets are significant. The ongoing conflict has already led to a 6% production cut among Persian Gulf oil producers due to storage capacity constraints. Additionally, Saudi Aramco's record price increase for oil to Asia signals strong demand amidst supply disruptions. While OPEC+ plans to increase output, the geopolitical landscape complicates these intentions, potentially leading to tighter supplies and sustained higher prices.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Middle East, as continued hostilities could further constrain oil supply and drive prices higher, impacting energy stocks and broader market sentiment.
