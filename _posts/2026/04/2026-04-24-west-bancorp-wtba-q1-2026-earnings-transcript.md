West Bancorporation reported a robust first quarter for 2026, with net income soaring 35% year-over-year to $10.6 million, driven by a 17% increase in net interest income. The bank's net interest margin improved by 12 basis points from the previous quarter, reflecting effective cost management and a decline in deposit costs. Importantly, credit quality remains strong, with zero loans past due over 30 days and a watch list that has decreased to just 1.4% of total loans, primarily linked to the struggling trucking sector.

This performance signals a positive outlook for West Bancorporation amidst a competitive banking landscape. The bank's ability to maintain flat loan balances while growing deposits highlights its strategic focus on relationship-building and disciplined underwriting. Management anticipates continued loan growth as refinancing activity stabilizes, and they plan to allocate maturing investment cash flows to support this expansion.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is West Bancorporation's strong credit quality and improving margins, suggesting resilience in a challenging economic environment. This positions the bank favorably for future growth, particularly if loan demand rebounds as anticipated.
