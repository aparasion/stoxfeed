The U.K. Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) has unveiled new crypto regulations that could significantly impact firms in the sector, particularly those handling client assets. The proposed "cryptoasset perimeter" guidance, effective by October 2027, classifies any firm holding client crypto assets for over 24 hours as a regulated custodian, necessitating a full safeguarding license. This change could catch many software providers off guard, as it expands the definition of custody to include platforms that previously operated outside regulatory scrutiny.

The implications for the crypto market are substantial. Firms like validators and stablecoin issuers will face stricter rules, including the loss of tech-only exemptions when offering added-value features. The FCA's focus on "shadow custody" means that even the ability to theoretically override client authority will classify a firm as a custodian, which could lead to increased compliance costs and operational adjustments for many players in the space.

Market participants should be aware of the five-month application window starting September 30, 2026, to secure authorization under the new regime. Missing this deadline could result in fines or operational suspensions, underscoring the urgency for firms to reassess their compliance strategies in light of these evolving regulations.
