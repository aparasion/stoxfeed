Joby Aviation (NYSE: JOBY) is on the verge of launching its electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, aiming to begin commercial flights by 2026, starting in Dubai and expanding to the U.S. This ambitious plan positions Joby as a potential pioneer in the flying taxi market, but significant hurdles remain. The company is currently navigating the FAA certification process, having nearly completed stage four of five. However, the path to approval is fraught with uncertainty, as any technical setbacks or new safety requirements could delay timelines.

Investors should closely monitor three critical factors over the next 12 to 18 months: FAA approval, real demand for air taxis, and the ability to scale production. Joby’s partnerships with firms like Blade Air Mobility and Uber could facilitate market entry, but the true test will be whether consumers embrace this new mode of transport. Additionally, scaling production efficiently will be crucial; without it, demand may not translate into a sustainable business model.

As Joby approaches this pivotal phase, 2026 will be a year of reckoning. Success hinges on proving operational reliability and consumer interest, making it essential for investors to track developments in these areas closely.
