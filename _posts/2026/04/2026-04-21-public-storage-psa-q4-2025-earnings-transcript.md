Public Storage (PSA) has reported a strong fourth quarter and full-year performance, with Core FFO per share reaching $4.26 for the quarter and $16.97 for the year, aligning with the high end of guidance. However, the company faced challenges, including a slight decline in same-store revenue and NOI, attributed to increased expenses and external pressures, particularly in Los Angeles. Despite these headwinds, non-same-store NOI grew by 20% year-over-year, bolstering overall financial performance.

The financial outlook for 2026 indicates a cautious approach, with guidance for Core FFO set between $16.35 and $17.00, reflecting anticipated negative same-store NOI and revenue trends. Public Storage is actively addressing these challenges through strategic leadership changes and a focus on digital integration, aiming to enhance operational efficiency and customer engagement. The company’s liquidity position remains robust, with $1.8 billion available, supporting its capital allocation strategy.

Market professionals should note that while Public Storage is navigating significant regulatory and operational challenges, its strong leadership transition and emphasis on technological advancements could position it well for long-term growth amidst a shifting industry landscape.
