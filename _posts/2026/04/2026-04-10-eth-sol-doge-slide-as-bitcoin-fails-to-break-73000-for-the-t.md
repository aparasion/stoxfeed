Bitcoin has struggled to break above $73,000 for the third time since the recent ceasefire in the Iran conflict, keeping the cryptocurrency within a tight range between $70,000 and $73,000. Despite a notable weekly gain of 7.9%, the repeated rejection at this resistance level signals caution among traders. Analysts suggest that Bitcoin needs to clear $75,000 and consolidate above $74,000 to confirm a bullish trend, while Ethereum and other major altcoins remain range-bound, reflecting broader market apprehension.

The ongoing geopolitical tensions and volatility in oil prices are contributing to this cautious sentiment. While Bitcoin's performance has been strong relative to the previous weeks, the inability to breach critical resistance levels has left many wondering if a genuine bullish phase is on the horizon. Notably, altcoins like Algorand and Aptos have seen declines, indicating a potential rotation in trader sentiment rather than new capital inflows.

Market professionals should monitor Bitcoin's ability to break through the $73,000 barrier, as a successful rally could reignite bullish momentum across the crypto space. Conversely, any escalation in geopolitical tensions could lead to a pullback toward the $68,000 to $70,000 range, highlighting the delicate balance in the current market environment.
