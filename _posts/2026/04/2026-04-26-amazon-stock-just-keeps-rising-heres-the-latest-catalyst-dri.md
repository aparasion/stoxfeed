Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN) shares have surged over 25% in the past month, driven by a significant partnership with Meta Platforms (NASDAQ: META). Meta will utilize Amazon Web Services' Graviton5 chips for its AI workloads, marking a notable expansion in their long-standing collaboration. This development underscores Amazon's successful custom silicon strategy, which is gaining traction as demand for efficient AI solutions grows.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. Amazon's aggressive investments in AI and custom chips are beginning to pay off, as evidenced by its rapidly increasing AI revenue run rate, projected to exceed $15 billion by Q1 2026. Additionally, Anthropic's commitment of over $100 billion to AWS technologies further validates Amazon's strategy, positioning it as a key player in the AI landscape. However, the stock's current price-to-earnings ratio of 37 suggests that much of this growth may already be priced in.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach Amazon shares with caution. While the recent momentum is promising, the high valuation and significant capital expenditures expected in 2026 warrant a more conservative investment strategy, potentially favoring a "hold" position over new buys.
