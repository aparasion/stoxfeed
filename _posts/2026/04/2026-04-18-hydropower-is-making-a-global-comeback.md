Governments are increasingly turning to hydropower as a vital component in diversifying their energy portfolios amid rising fossil fuel prices driven by geopolitical tensions. While solar and wind power often dominate discussions, the International Energy Agency (IEA) highlights hydropower as the "forgotten giant" of electricity generation, contributing approximately 14% of global electricity production—comparable to solar and wind combined. With around 60% of hydro resources in emerging economies untapped, there is significant potential for growth in this sector.

The ongoing shift towards renewable energy sources is crucial for enhancing energy security and reducing reliance on volatile fossil fuels. Hydropower's flexibility allows for rapid adjustments in electricity generation, making it an efficient alternative during periods of low solar and wind output. Additionally, pumped-storage hydropower (PSH) systems can provide long-duration energy storage, further stabilizing energy supply.

As governments prioritize energy diversification, investing in hydropower could not only bolster energy access but also stimulate economic growth. Market professionals should monitor developments in this sector, as increased investment in hydropower could reshape the energy landscape and offer new opportunities for growth and stability.
