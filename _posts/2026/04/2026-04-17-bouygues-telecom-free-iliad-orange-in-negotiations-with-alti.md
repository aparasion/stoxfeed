Bouygues Telecom, Free-iliad Group, and Orange have initiated negotiations to acquire SFR from Altice France, with a proposed enterprise value of €20.35 billion. The consortium's ownership distribution is set at approximately 42% for Bouygues, 31% for Free-iliad, and 27% for Orange. The exclusivity period granted by Altice extends until May 15, 2026, allowing the parties to finalize the deal's terms.

This acquisition is significant for the telecommunications sector, as it consolidates major players in the French market, potentially reshaping competitive dynamics. Bouygues is slated to take over the B2B operations, while the B2C segment will be divided among the three companies. The implications for market share and pricing strategies could influence stock performance, particularly for Orange, which saw a 1.57% increase in share price to €18.10 at last close.

Market professionals should monitor the progress of this negotiation closely, as it may lead to shifts in market positioning and competitive strategies within the telecom sector.
