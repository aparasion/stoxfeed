Oil prices are poised for a significant weekly gain, with Brent crude holding steady at $105 per barrel, despite renewed diplomatic hopes between the U.S. and Iran that are capping further upside potential. The market is reacting to Iran's Foreign Minister's anticipated travel to Pakistan, signaling that dialogue remains a possibility, which has kept Brent from breaking the $110 barrier this week. Meanwhile, West Texas Intermediate (WTI) is trading about $10 below this benchmark as geopolitical tensions continue to influence pricing.

This week's developments have broader implications for the oil market, particularly as the EU's sanctions against Russia and the restart of Ukrainian crude flows through the Druzhba pipeline add complexity to supply dynamics. Additionally, BP's ongoing labor issues at its Whiting refinery and the strategic petroleum releases from the U.S. government further complicate the landscape, impacting refining margins and overall supply availability.

Market professionals should note that while prices are rising, geopolitical factors and ongoing negotiations could lead to volatility. Traders may want to monitor developments in U.S.-Iran relations closely, as any breakthrough could significantly influence oil supply and pricing in the near term.
