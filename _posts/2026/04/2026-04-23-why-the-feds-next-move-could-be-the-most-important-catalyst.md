Oil prices have surged in recent months amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in Iran, pushing the national average gas price to $4.02 per gallon. This spike in energy costs is contributing to rising inflation, which the Bureau of Labor Statistics recently reported at 3.3% — the highest in nearly two years. As production costs for oil-dependent goods like plastics and fertilizers increase, the ripple effect is felt across various sectors, heightening concerns about economic stability.

The Federal Reserve is currently in a difficult position, balancing the need to control inflation with the risk of stifling economic growth. Fed Chair Jerome Powell has opted for a "wait and see" approach, refraining from raising interest rates for now. However, prolonged inflation could compel the Fed to act, which historically tends to dampen stock market performance. The S&P 500 has rallied over 12% since Powell's remarks, but uncertainty surrounding the conflict in the Middle East looms large.

Investors should remain vigilant, as the interplay between inflation and interest rates could significantly influence market dynamics in the coming months. A long-term perspective will be crucial as the situation evolves.
