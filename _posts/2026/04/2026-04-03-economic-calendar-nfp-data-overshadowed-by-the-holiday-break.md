Trading activity is expected to slow significantly as markets approach the long Easter weekend, with most exchanges halting operations today and Monday, except for the U.S. This pause comes just ahead of the release of critical March Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) data at 2:30 p.m. ET, which could introduce volatility into the session as investors position themselves in anticipation of the report.

The NFP data is a key economic indicator that can influence market sentiment and stock performance, particularly in sectors sensitive to labor market conditions. Traders will be closely monitoring the figures for insights into economic strength and potential implications for Federal Reserve policy, which could impact interest rates and overall market direction.

As markets brace for the holiday break, the upcoming NFP release may serve as a pivotal moment for traders looking to recalibrate their strategies. The data could either reinforce or challenge recent market trends, making it essential for professionals to stay alert to its implications.
