Oil prices are poised for volatility as negotiations between Iran and the US unfold, significantly impacting shipping operations through the Strait of Hormuz. Currently, over 800 tankers are stranded in the Strait, and the resolution of these talks is crucial for reducing shipping risks and stabilizing oil prices. A sustained drop in oil prices hinges on the movement of these vessels in the coming days.

In the broader market, European stocks are experiencing a notable surge, with the Eurostoxx index rising 5% and the FTSE 100 up 3%. However, oil majors are lagging due to the sharp decline in oil prices, which is dampening momentum in energy stocks. The market is reacting to the potential for a ceasefire in the Middle East, leading to a 3% surge in US indices as investor sentiment shifts toward optimism.

Market professionals should closely monitor the developments in US-Iran negotiations, as their outcome could have significant implications for oil prices and overall market sentiment.
