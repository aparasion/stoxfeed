Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) and Arm Holdings are emerging as key players in the rapidly evolving landscape of agentic artificial intelligence (AI), a sector poised to reshape the chip market. As AI transitions from content creation to executing tasks, the demand for high-performance CPUs is expected to surge, with AMD leading the way through its innovative Venice architecture designed for agentic AI applications. Arm is also making a significant pivot by developing its own data center CPUs, aiming to capture a 15% share of the projected $100 billion CPU market.

This shift towards agentic AI is not only a technological evolution but also a potential catalyst for substantial market growth. Companies like Salesforce are positioning themselves as essential data orchestrators for AI agents, enhancing their value proposition in a market that has yet to fully recognize the implications of these advancements. Similarly, ServiceNow and UiPath are set to benefit from the orchestration layer required to manage these AI agents effectively.

For market professionals, the rise of agentic AI presents a compelling investment opportunity, particularly in AMD and Arm, as well as in companies like Salesforce, ServiceNow, and UiPath, which are strategically aligned with this trend.
