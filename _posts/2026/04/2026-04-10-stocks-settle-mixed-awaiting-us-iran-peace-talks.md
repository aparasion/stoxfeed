The S&P 500 Index closed down 0.11% on Friday, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 0.56%. In contrast, the Nasdaq 100 managed a slight gain of 0.14%, reaching a six-week high. The mixed performance followed easing inflation concerns, with U.S. consumer prices rising less than expected, and optimism surrounding diplomatic negotiations between the U.S. and Iran. However, the S&P 500's decline was fueled by weakness in software stocks, particularly amid fears of AI disruption, and a significant drop in consumer sentiment to a record low.

The pressure on stocks was compounded by geopolitical tensions, particularly reports of U.S. warships being reloaded for potential strikes on Iran, which overshadowed positive inflation data. Notably, software and cybersecurity stocks struggled, with significant losses for companies like ServiceNow and Cloudflare, while chipmakers like Broadcom and AMD saw gains, reflecting a divergence in sector performance.

Market professionals should monitor the evolving geopolitical landscape and its impact on energy prices, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz, as well as the ongoing concerns regarding AI's influence on tech stocks. The mixed signals from inflation data and consumer sentiment could also shape market expectations ahead of upcoming Federal Reserve meetings.
