The evolution of crypto custody is reshaping institutional finance, moving beyond mere asset storage to emphasize real-time connectivity and mobility across fragmented markets. Paul Frost-Smith, CEO of Komainu, highlights that as institutional adoption of crypto accelerates, the focus shifts to efficiently managing and mobilizing assets. With over $200 billion now under professional custody, inefficiencies in siloed infrastructures are increasingly impacting trading, liquidity, and risk management.

This transition is critical for financial markets, as the ability to quickly move and manage assets can significantly enhance capital efficiency and operational agility. Institutions that can integrate their systems to allow for real-time asset movement will gain a competitive edge, enabling better liquidity and faster execution. The convergence of crypto with traditional finance is no longer speculative; it’s about optimizing balance sheets and improving settlement processes.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of prioritizing integrated infrastructure over traditional custody solutions. As the landscape evolves, firms that can effectively connect their digital asset operations will be better positioned to capitalize on emerging opportunities while mitigating risks associated with fragmented systems.
