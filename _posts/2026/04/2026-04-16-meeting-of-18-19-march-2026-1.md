The European Central Bank (ECB) recently held a monetary policy meeting amid heightened market volatility following the outbreak of war in the Middle East. The conflict has triggered a surge in energy prices, with Brent crude oil surpassing $100 per barrel, leading to significant sell-offs in equity and bond markets. This geopolitical shock has shifted market expectations, with investors now anticipating rate hikes of approximately 40 basis points by the end of 2026, reversing earlier expectations of a rate cut.

The war's impact on inflation is particularly pronounced, with near-term inflation compensation rising sharply in the euro area. The ECB's projections now reflect an average headline inflation of 2.6% for 2026, up from previous estimates, driven by escalating energy costs. The euro area's vulnerability as a net energy importer has further complicated the economic outlook, leading to a depreciation of the euro against the US dollar.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the increased uncertainty surrounding monetary policy and inflation expectations, which could lead to further volatility in asset prices. The ECB's response to these developments will be critical in shaping market dynamics in the coming months.
