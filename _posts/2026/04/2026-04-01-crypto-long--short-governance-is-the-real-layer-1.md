This week’s Crypto Long & Short newsletter highlights the urgent need for hybrid governance structures as cryptocurrency and traditional finance (TradFi) converge. Nilmini Rubin emphasizes that recent events, such as the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank, have exposed vulnerabilities where failures in traditional finance can directly impact digital assets. This necessitates a rethinking of governance frameworks to ensure accountability and resilience in the face of crises.

The newsletter also discusses how financial institutions must adapt their anti-money laundering (AML) strategies to address the unique risks posed by cryptocurrencies. Meredith Fitzpatrick points out that the immutable and pseudonymous nature of blockchain transactions requires a shift from traditional risk assessment models to dynamic monitoring of on-chain activities. This evolution is crucial as institutions increasingly engage with digital assets.

The key takeaway for market professionals is that effective governance and risk management will be critical as the crypto landscape matures. Institutions that prioritize these aspects will be better positioned to navigate the complexities of this rapidly evolving market.
