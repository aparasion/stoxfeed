The recent Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) minutes reveal growing concerns about inflation, potentially jeopardizing the current bull market on Wall Street. Despite a strong performance from the S&P 500 and other indices over the past seven years, the FOMC's acknowledgment of persistent inflation risks—exacerbated by the ongoing Iran war—signals a shift in the central bank's outlook. With inflation expected to hover around 3.6% in April, the anticipated rate cuts in 2026 may not materialize, leaving investors on edge.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Rising crude oil prices due to the Iran conflict have already increased costs for consumers and businesses alike, complicating the Fed's efforts to stabilize prices. As the FOMC grapples with these inflationary pressures, the prospect of interest rate hikes looms larger, which could dampen market enthusiasm and lead to increased volatility.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is clear: the landscape has shifted, and reliance on future rate cuts may be misplaced. Investors should brace for potential rate increases, which could reshape market dynamics and affect stock valuations across sectors.
