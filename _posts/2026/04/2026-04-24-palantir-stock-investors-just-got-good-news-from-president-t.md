Palantir Technologies (PLTR) has seen its stock decline 30% from its peak, driven by valuation concerns and a broader market shift towards safer assets amid geopolitical tensions. Despite this downturn, the company has secured significant government contracts, including a $1 billion agreement with the Department of Homeland Security and a $300 million deal with the USDA, indicating robust growth in its government business, which constitutes over half of its revenue.

The expansion into new government sectors, such as the FAA, highlights Palantir's ability to diversify beyond its traditional defense and intelligence contracts. Analysts remain optimistic about the company’s potential, with Morgan Stanley noting its unique software architecture and strong financial performance — a 70% revenue increase in the last quarter — positioning it favorably in the enterprise software market. The consensus suggests a median target price of $200 per share, representing a 37% upside.

Investors should consider entering a position in Palantir, but they must remain cautious of its high valuation, which could lead to volatility in response to any negative news.
