Nvidia (NVDA) continues to dominate the AI landscape, leveraging its unparalleled graphics processing units (GPUs) to secure a leading position in AI-accelerated data centers. The company's market capitalization has skyrocketed from $360 billion at the end of 2022 to over $4 trillion, driven by explosive sales and profit growth. However, despite its strong performance, Nvidia faces potential challenges that could lead to a significant decline in its stock value.

Several factors could trigger a downturn for Nvidia, including the historical tendency for technology stocks to experience bubble bursts. While adoption of AI technology is strong, optimization for business applications may take years. Additionally, competitors are developing cheaper AI chips, which could erode Nvidia's market share and pricing power. Trade tensions with China further complicate the outlook, as they limit Nvidia's sales potential in a crucial market.

Market professionals should be aware of the risks surrounding Nvidia, particularly given its high valuation metrics. If historical trends hold, the stock could see a drastic decline, potentially falling to $100 before 2026.
