Oil prices surged above $110 per barrel amid escalating geopolitical tensions, particularly following new threats from former President Donald Trump against Iranian infrastructure. This development comes as investors remain focused on the oil market, especially with the potential for resumed trade through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical shipping route that has been largely disrupted due to ongoing conflicts. Asian equities experienced a modest rebound, buoyed by improved sentiment from Wall Street, though trading activity was limited due to holiday closures across major markets.

The latest U.S. labor market data showed stronger-than-expected nonfarm payroll growth, with 178,000 jobs added in March, easing concerns about a potential economic slowdown. However, the mixed performance of U.S. index futures and the rise in oil prices indicate that market participants are weighing the implications of strong labor data against heightened geopolitical risks. 

As markets prepare for the upcoming week, the interplay between oil prices and economic indicators will be crucial. Traders should monitor how these factors influence market sentiment and sector performance, particularly in energy and financials, once U.S. markets reopen.
