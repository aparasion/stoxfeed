Investors faced significant turbulence in March and the first quarter of 2026, with the S&P 500 experiencing a 5% decline for the month and a 4.6% drop for the quarter. This volatility stemmed from concerns over AI-driven growth sustainability, the ongoing war in Iran, and the broader economic landscape, which saw the VIX spike as investor sentiment turned cautious. Despite a three-year bull market that saw the index rise 78%, recent uncertainties prompted a shift in market dynamics.

Historically, the second quarter has been favorable for the S&P 500, with gains in five of the last six years. Factors such as potential clarity regarding U.S. military involvement in Iran and the enduring strength of tech stocks, particularly in AI and cloud services, suggest a possible rebound. Companies like Nvidia and Amazon have shown resilience, indicating that growth opportunities remain.

As we move into Q2, the prevailing sentiment is cautiously optimistic. Long-term investors may find that, despite recent setbacks, the S&P 500's historical recovery patterns could present significant opportunities for portfolio growth.
