Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz have escalated as at least nine oil tankers navigated the critical sea lane this week amid ongoing U.S.-Iran hostilities. Notably, the VLCC RHN, capable of carrying 2 million barrels of oil, entered the strait on Wednesday, while another VLCC, Alicia, crossed on Tuesday. However, tanker transits remain drastically low—90% below pre-war levels—due to the heightened risk of Iranian attacks, despite a ceasefire agreement reached on April 7.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, as the Strait of Hormuz is a crucial conduit for global oil supply, accounting for approximately 20% of world crude shipments before the conflict. The International Energy Agency has highlighted that restoring traffic through this strait is essential for alleviating pressure on energy supplies and stabilizing prices, which have been volatile due to the ongoing disruptions.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as any shifts in tanker traffic could directly influence oil prices and broader market sentiment.
