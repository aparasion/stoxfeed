Tensions in the Middle East escalated as the U.S. intercepted three Iranian oil tankers outside the Strait of Hormuz, marking a significant expansion of the ongoing tanker war. This development has implications for global oil supply, with Brent crude prices surging back above $100 a barrel, impacting stock performance across Asia and futures in Europe and the U.S. The geopolitical instability is likely to hinder peace talks, exacerbating concerns over sustained high oil prices and their effects on market indices.

In corporate news, Tesla reported Q1 earnings that exceeded expectations, with revenue hitting $22.38 billion. However, a substantial increase in capital expenditures to $25 billion raised concerns among investors, causing Tesla's stock to reverse initial gains and close down 1%. Despite a 16% year-over-year increase in subscriptions for its driverless technology, the company continues to face challenges in its core EV business, which is struggling against intensified competition.

The key takeaway for market professionals is that geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices could create a volatile environment for equities, while Tesla's increased capex and ongoing challenges in its automotive segment may hinder its ability to regain momentum against its peers in the tech sector.
