Jewett-Cameron Trading Company Ltd. (JCTC) reported a significant decline in revenue for fiscal 2025, with total sales dropping to $41.3 million from $47.1 million in the previous year. The company faced challenges across multiple segments, notably in lumber and pet products, largely attributed to newly imposed tariffs and excess inventory. The metal fencing segment remained the only bright spot, maintaining flat sales despite the adverse conditions, underscoring its status as the best margin-producing category.

The financial implications are stark, with gross profit margins falling to 15.1% from 18.8% year-over-year, and a net loss of $4.1 million compared to a profit last year. The company is actively restructuring, including a 27% reduction in headcount and a strategic pivot towards its metal fencing products, while also working to monetize non-core assets to improve liquidity. 

A key takeaway for market professionals is the ongoing pressure from tariffs and inventory management challenges, which may hinder short-term profitability. However, the focus on core competencies and cost reduction initiatives could position Jewett-Cameron for recovery as market conditions stabilize.
