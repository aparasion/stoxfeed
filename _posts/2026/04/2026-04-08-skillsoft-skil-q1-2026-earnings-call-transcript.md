Skillsoft Corp. (SKIL) reported mixed first-quarter results, revealing a 2.8% year-over-year revenue decline to $124.2 million, driven by a 15.7% drop in Global Knowledge revenue. However, the company achieved a significant turnaround in adjusted net income, posting $2.5 million compared to a loss last year, alongside a notable increase in free cash flow to $26.2 million. Despite a challenging macro environment affecting discretionary spending, particularly in the U.S. public sector, Skillsoft's Talent Development Solutions (TDS) segment continued to grow, marking three consecutive quarters of revenue growth.

The company's leadership emphasized ongoing cost reduction initiatives and a strategic focus on AI product expansion, including the launch of the CAISY AI-powered coach. Looking ahead, Skillsoft reiterated its fiscal 2026 guidance, projecting revenue between $530 million and $545 million, contingent on macroeconomic stabilization. 

Market professionals should note the potential for improved performance in the latter half of the fiscal year, as management expects ongoing investments in product innovation and go-to-market strategies to yield positive results.
