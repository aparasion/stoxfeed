XPO Logistics (XPO) reported a strong fourth quarter, with company-wide revenue of $1.9 billion, down 1% year-over-year, primarily due to a 23% drop in LTL fuel surcharge revenue linked to diesel prices. However, the LTL segment saw a 2% increase in revenue excluding fuel, and adjusted EBITDA rose 15% to $303 million, reflecting effective cost management and operational efficiencies. Notably, net income from continuing operations surged 31% to $76 million, while adjusted diluted EPS increased 16% to $0.89.

The results underscore XPO's resilience in a challenging freight market, driven by a strategic shift towards high-margin local customers, which now represent a growing portion of revenue. The company also reported a significant reduction in purchase transportation expenses and improved operating ratios, indicating enhanced profitability. Furthermore, management anticipates continued yield growth and margin expansion in 2025, supported by ongoing network investments and operational improvements.

For market professionals, XPO's ability to maintain margin expansion despite revenue challenges signals a robust operational framework poised for growth. The company's focus on local customer acquisition and efficiency improvements positions it well for potential market recovery, making it a stock to watch in the upcoming quarters.
