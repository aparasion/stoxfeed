Oil prices surged nearly 3% on Thursday, with Brent crude surpassing $97 per barrel, following allegations from Iran of ceasefire violations. This development has led to a mild selloff in European stocks, as traders react to the heightened geopolitical tensions and the ongoing closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical shipping route. With only three vessels passing through the strait recently and around 800 tankers waiting, market sentiment is increasingly cautious ahead of weekend peace talks.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, particularly as we approach Q1 earnings season. Companies like Delta Airlines are already feeling the pinch from rising fuel costs, projecting a $2 billion hit due to the conflict. This situation may limit the usual positive earnings surprises and could lead to widespread profit downgrades, dampening investor enthusiasm for stocks amid an uncertain macroeconomic backdrop.

Market professionals should closely monitor the outcome of the upcoming peace talks, as any setbacks could exacerbate economic disruptions and further impact corporate earnings forecasts. The interplay between oil prices and stock performance will remain a critical focus in the coming weeks.
