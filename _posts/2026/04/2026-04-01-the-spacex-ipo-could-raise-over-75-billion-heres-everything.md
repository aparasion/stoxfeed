SpaceX is set to make waves in the financial markets with its highly anticipated IPO, potentially launching as soon as June. The company is reportedly filing its prospectus this week, aiming to raise over $75 billion, which would mark the largest IPO in history with a valuation projected between $1.5 trillion and $1.75 trillion. This staggering figure is based on an estimated $16 billion in revenue for 2025, suggesting a valuation multiple exceeding 100 times revenue.

The implications for the market are significant, as SpaceX's entry could shift investor focus and capital towards the aerospace sector, impacting competitors like Rocket Lab and AST SpaceMobile. Moreover, CEO Elon Musk's potential plans for dual-class shares could limit public influence on corporate decisions, raising questions about governance. With SpaceX's ambitious projects, including space-based data centers and AI initiatives, investor sentiment may be driven more by hype than fundamentals.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is to approach the SpaceX IPO with caution. While the excitement is palpable, the valuation appears speculative, and long-term viability hinges on the successful execution of its ambitious plans.
