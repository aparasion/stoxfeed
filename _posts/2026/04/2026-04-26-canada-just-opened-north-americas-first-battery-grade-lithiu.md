China's grip on the global lithium supply chain continues to tighten, controlling half of the market and presenting significant challenges for Western manufacturers. As the demand for lithium-ion batteries surges, particularly in the electric vehicle (EV) sector, this concentration creates vulnerabilities for countries reliant on imports. The current energy crisis, exacerbated by geopolitical tensions, has intensified the race for lithium, making 2026 a pivotal year for production expansion.

In a notable development, Canada is taking steps to challenge China's near-monopoly on lithium refining. Mangrove Lithium has launched North America's first commercial-scale electrochemical lithium refining facility in British Columbia, capable of producing 1,000 tonnes of battery-grade lithium annually. This facility represents a critical move towards establishing a domestic supply chain for lithium, supported by the Canadian government’s focus on energy security and job creation.

The emergence of domestic lithium production in Canada could alleviate supply chain pressures and enhance energy independence, but it also raises environmental concerns associated with lithium extraction. As nations strive for self-sufficiency in critical minerals, the balance between sustainable practices and resource development will be crucial.
