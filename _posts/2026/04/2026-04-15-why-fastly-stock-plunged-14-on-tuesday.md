Fastly (FSLY) shares plummeted 14% on Tuesday following contrasting analyst reports that left investors divided. Craig-Hallum downgraded the stock from "buy" to "hold," citing rising hardware costs and setting a price target of $24, closely aligning with Monday's close of $24.37. Conversely, Evercore initiated coverage with an "outperform" rating and a $32 target, highlighting strong demand for Fastly's content delivery and edge computing services, bolstered by AI capabilities.

The market's reaction underscores the challenges Fastly faces as it navigates its turnaround, with a notable 22% year-over-year revenue growth overshadowed by negative earnings. The stock, despite its recent decline, remains up 282% year-over-year, reflecting investor optimism about future potential. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the divergence in analyst sentiment, which could signal volatility ahead. Investors should weigh the implications of Fastly's financial health against its growth prospects in the AI-driven edge computing landscape.
