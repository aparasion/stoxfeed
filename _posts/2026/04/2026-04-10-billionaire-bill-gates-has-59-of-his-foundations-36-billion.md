Bill Gates' philanthropic trust, primarily focused on global health and poverty alleviation, has amassed a significant stock portfolio valued at approximately $36 billion. Notably, the trust's top holdings exclude Microsoft and instead concentrate on three major companies: Berkshire Hathaway, WM (formerly Waste Management), and Canadian National Railway. This strategic shift highlights Gates' preference for value stocks, with Berkshire alone accounting for 25.4% of the portfolio.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Berkshire Hathaway's recent performance has been under pressure, particularly following Warren Buffett's resignation announcement, yet the company remains robust with substantial liquid assets. Meanwhile, WM is poised for growth with strong free cash flow projections, despite its high earnings multiple. Canadian National Railway is navigating tariff challenges but is expected to benefit from improved cash flow and share buybacks, making it a potentially attractive investment at its current valuation.

For market professionals, the Gates Foundation's investment strategy underscores the importance of value-oriented stocks in uncertain economic times. Monitoring these holdings could provide insights into broader market trends and opportunities in the sectors they dominate.
