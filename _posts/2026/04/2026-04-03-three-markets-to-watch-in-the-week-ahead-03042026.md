Geopolitical tensions are escalating as President Donald Trump threatens a significant bombing campaign in Iran, raising concerns about the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz and its potential impact on global oil supplies. This situation has intensified market volatility, with crude oil prices firmly above $100, prompting investors to reassess risk exposure in the face of potential supply disruptions.

As Wall Street prepares for a critical week of macroeconomic data, including the March CPI report, investors are particularly focused on how surging fuel prices may influence inflation and economic growth. The S&P 500, currently hovering about 6% below its all-time highs, faces pressure from both geopolitical uncertainties and upcoming earnings reports, especially in the tech sector where high operational costs could lead to disappointing results.

Market professionals should closely monitor the interplay between geopolitical developments and economic indicators. A failure to restore stability in the Strait of Hormuz could exacerbate energy crises, while a strong CPI report may further complicate the Federal Reserve's monetary policy, influencing both currency and equity markets significantly.
