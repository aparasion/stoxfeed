USA Rare Earth (USAR +0.44%) is making strides in its rare-earth value chain, bolstered by potential federal support, but investor enthusiasm may be waning. The company recently announced an investment in French processor Carester, aiming to counter China's dominance in the sector. However, despite initial optimism that drove shares higher in early 2026, concerns about execution and funding risks have led some analysts to shift their outlook from cautiously bullish to bearish.

The crux of the issue lies in the nonbinding $1.6 billion funding proposal from the U.S. Department of Commerce, which remains pending full approval. With congressional scrutiny emerging over potential conflicts of interest, the path to securing this capital is uncertain. If federal funding falls through, USA Rare Earth may face significant challenges in financing its operations, potentially leading to greater equity dilution and jeopardizing its ambitious sales and earnings projections.

Given these risks, market professionals may find it prudent to remain on the sidelines regarding USA Rare Earth. Instead, consider diversifying into growth ETFs that include exposure to rare-earth ventures and other speculative growth opportunities.
