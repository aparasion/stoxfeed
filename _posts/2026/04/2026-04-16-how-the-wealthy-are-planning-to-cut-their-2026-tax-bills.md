Wealthy Americans are adapting to a shifting tax landscape following the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, which has made many tax cuts permanent and alleviated fears of a looming 2025 deadline. As tax professionals emphasize the importance of strategic planning, high-net-worth investors are focusing on five key strategies, including long-short tax-loss harvesting to minimize capital gains and leveraging bonus depreciation for business investments.

With the S&P 500 gaining over 75% since the start of 2023, investors are increasingly aware of the need to manage capital gains effectively. Long-short tax-loss harvesting allows for offsetting gains while maintaining a neutral portfolio, making it a popular choice among high earners. Additionally, the renewal of bonus depreciation incentivizes businesses to invest in qualifying assets, further impacting capital allocation decisions.

A crucial takeaway for market professionals is the growing trend of changing domiciles to mitigate state taxes, as several states propose new taxes on high earners. This shift could influence investment strategies and asset allocation as wealthy individuals seek more favorable tax environments.
