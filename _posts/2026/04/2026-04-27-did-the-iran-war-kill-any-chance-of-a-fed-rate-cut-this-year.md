The stock market opened the week at record highs, driven by a robust performance in AI stocks, as the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite continue to thrive. Investors are now turning their attention to upcoming corporate earnings from key "Magnificent Seven" companies and the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meeting, where the focus will be on interest rate decisions amidst geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict in Iran.

The Fed's decision, expected to maintain the current Fed Funds Rate of 3.5%-3.75%, will be closely scrutinized for any signals regarding future rate cuts. The Iran war's influence on energy prices is significant, with the latest CPI report indicating rising costs, which could pressure the Fed's monetary policy. As uncertainty looms over the conflict and its economic implications, the likelihood of immediate rate cuts appears low, especially with Jerome Powell's potential departure and the Senate's consideration of Kevin Warsh as his successor.

Investors should brace for volatility as the FOMC meeting approaches, particularly regarding any insights on how geopolitical events may shape monetary policy. The evolving situation in Iran could keep the Fed on hold, complicating the outlook for rate adjustments this year.
