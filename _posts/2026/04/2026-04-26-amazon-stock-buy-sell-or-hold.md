Amazon (AMZN) shares have surged nearly 25% in the past month, bringing the year-to-date gain to approximately 14%. This rally comes ahead of the company’s first-quarter earnings report scheduled for April 29, where investors will scrutinize whether Amazon's robust business momentum justifies its current valuation near a 52-week high. Notably, Amazon Web Services (AWS) has been a key driver, showing a 24% year-over-year sales increase in the fourth quarter, contributing significantly to the company's overall earnings.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial, as AWS accounted for 57% of Amazon's operating income despite only representing 18% of net sales. However, the company is facing a hefty capital expenditure plan of $200 billion by 2026, primarily for AI and cloud infrastructure, which may pressure profitability in the near term. Management's guidance suggests modest growth in operating income, with expectations of only 3.3% year-over-year growth.

For market professionals, Amazon's current valuation at a price-to-earnings ratio of 37 suggests caution. While the business fundamentals remain strong, the stock appears more suited for a "hold" strategy rather than a buy, given the already priced-in optimism and significant future investments.
