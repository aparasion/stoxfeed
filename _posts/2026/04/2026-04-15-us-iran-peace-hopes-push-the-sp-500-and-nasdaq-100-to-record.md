The S&P 500 Index closed up 0.80% on Wednesday, reaching a new all-time high, alongside a 1.40% gain in the Nasdaq 100. This rally was fueled by optimism surrounding a potential ceasefire extension between the U.S. and Iran, which could ease geopolitical tensions. In contrast, the Dow Jones Industrial Average dipped 0.15%, weighed down by concerns over U.S. metals tariffs impacting industrial stocks.

The mixed economic data released on Wednesday contributed to market dynamics, with the Empire manufacturing survey showing stronger-than-expected growth, while the NAHB housing market index fell to a seven-month low. Earnings season is underway, with Q1 S&P 500 earnings projected to rise by 12% year-over-year, although excluding the tech sector, growth is expected to be a modest 3%. This divergence highlights sector-specific challenges amidst broader market optimism.

Market professionals should note the ongoing volatility in industrial stocks due to tariff concerns, while tech and software companies continue to attract investor interest, signaling a potential shift in sector leadership as earnings reports unfold.
