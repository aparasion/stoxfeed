Bitcoin remains constrained below $75,000 despite a recent positive trend, as U.S. stocks, particularly the Nasdaq and S&P 500, soar to new record highs. The Nasdaq extended its winning streak to 11 sessions, gaining 1.6% and closing above 24,000, while the S&P 500 rose 0.8% to surpass 7,000. Despite the broader risk-on sentiment benefiting crypto-related equities—like Coinbase and Robinhood—bitcoin's attempts to break through the $75,000 threshold have repeatedly stalled, with traders eyeing $72,000 as a critical support level.

The divergence between the strong performance of equities and bitcoin's struggle highlights a cautious sentiment in the crypto market. Traders suggest that maintaining levels above $72,000 is essential for sustaining a bullish breakout narrative; otherwise, bitcoin may revert to a low-volatility consolidation phase. 

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of monitoring bitcoin's price action relative to equities, as a sustained hold above $72,000 could signal renewed momentum, while a drop could lead to further consolidation.
