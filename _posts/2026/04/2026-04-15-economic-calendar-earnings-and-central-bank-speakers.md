The Q1 2026 earnings season kicks off today with key reports from ASML, Bank of America, and Morgan Stanley, all scheduled before the market opens. This marks a critical moment for investors as they evaluate corporate performance against a backdrop of shifting economic indicators. 

In addition to earnings, market participants will be closely monitoring U.S. Producer Price Index (PPI) data, which came in weaker than expected, potentially influencing monetary policy discussions. Central bank officials are also set to deliver remarks that could provide further insight into future interest rate decisions. Meanwhile, CPI readings from several European countries, including France and Poland, will add to the global economic narrative.

For traders and portfolio managers, today's developments underscore the importance of aligning investment strategies with evolving macroeconomic conditions. The weaker PPI could signal a more dovish stance from the Federal Reserve, impacting sector performance and market sentiment.
