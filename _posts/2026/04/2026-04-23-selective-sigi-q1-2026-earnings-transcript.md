In a strong start to 2026, the company reported a 12% operating return on equity (ROE) and solid earnings per share (EPS) figures, with fully diluted EPS at $1.58 and non-GAAP operating EPS at $1.69. The GAAP combined ratio stood at 98.3%, reflecting 6.2 points of catastrophe losses, while the underlying combined ratio improved to 92.1%. Management emphasized a disciplined approach to underwriting, prioritizing margins over top-line growth amid competitive pressures.

The results indicate a mixed performance across segments, with total premiums written declining 1% year-over-year. However, the excess and surplus lines saw a 1% increase, and renewal pure price increases in general liability and commercial auto approached 10% and 12%, respectively. The company’s strategic investments in AI tools for claims and risk management are enhancing operational efficiency, which is crucial in a challenging market environment.

Market professionals should note the reaffirmed guidance for a GAAP combined ratio between 96.5% and 97.5% for the year, indicating management's confidence in maintaining profitability despite ongoing industry challenges. This disciplined approach could position the company favorably for long-term growth, even as it navigates a competitive landscape.
