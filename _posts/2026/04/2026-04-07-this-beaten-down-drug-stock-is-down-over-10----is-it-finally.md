Abivax (NASDAQ: ABVX) is drawing attention amid recent market volatility, as its shares have dipped 11% since January, despite impressive gains last year. The France-based biotech is focused on developing obefazimod, a promising treatment for ulcerative colitis (UC) that operates through a novel mechanism aimed at regulating inflammation without the immunosuppressive effects common in existing therapies. Investors are particularly keen on the upcoming results from a maintenance study, expected in Q2, which will be pivotal in assessing obefazimod's long-term efficacy.

The stock's current valuation of approximately €8.15 billion ($9.41 billion) reflects optimism surrounding obefazimod's potential, but this high market cap also underscores the risks inherent in investing in a clinical-stage biotech with no revenue. A positive outcome from the maintenance study could drive shares significantly higher, while disappointing results may lead to a sharp decline.

For investors with a higher risk tolerance, now may be an opportune time to consider a position in Abivax, while those seeking stability might prefer more established biotech options.
