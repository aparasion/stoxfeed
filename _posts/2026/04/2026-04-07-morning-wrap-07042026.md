Tensions are escalating as President Trump sets a midnight deadline for Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz, threatening to destroy the country’s energy infrastructure if it does not comply. While Iran has dismissed the ultimatum as "arrogant rhetoric," U.S. officials interpret this as part of ongoing negotiations, suggesting a potential delay in military action if dialogue continues to progress.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Oil prices are responding to the uncertainty, with WTI climbing to over $115 per barrel, reflecting fears of supply disruptions in the critical Strait of Hormuz. U.S. stock index futures are also dipping, with the S&P 500 down about 0.5%, as investors brace for potential volatility stemming from geopolitical tensions.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments surrounding the Iran deadline, as any escalation could lead to further spikes in oil prices, impacting energy stocks and broader market sentiment.
