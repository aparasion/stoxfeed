Investors are increasingly anxious amid rising trade tensions, inflation, and the potential impact of artificial intelligence on the labor market. In this climate of uncertainty, defensive stocks like Procter & Gamble (PG) emerge as a refuge, offering stability and consistent income. With a remarkable history of 136 consecutive years of dividend payments and 70 years of uninterrupted increases, Procter & Gamble exemplifies the resilience of consumer staples, making it a compelling choice for risk-averse investors.

While the broader market has outperformed Procter & Gamble over the past decade, the company’s steady revenue growth—projected at a compound annual rate of 3% through fiscal 2028—highlights its ability to weather economic fluctuations. Unlike more volatile sectors, Procter & Gamble's essential products mitigate the risks associated with technological disruption, providing a safety net for shareholders.

For market professionals, Procter & Gamble represents a strategic investment that offers minimal downside risk in turbulent times, reinforcing the importance of incorporating defensive stocks into diversified portfolios.
