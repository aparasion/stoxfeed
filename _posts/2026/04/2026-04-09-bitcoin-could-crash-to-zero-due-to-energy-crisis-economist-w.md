A prominent economist has issued a stark warning that Bitcoin's value could plummet to zero amid escalating energy crises. This alarming prediction highlights the cryptocurrency's heavy reliance on energy-intensive mining processes, which could become unsustainable as energy costs rise and regulatory pressures mount.

The implications for financial markets are significant, especially as Bitcoin's volatility can impact broader cryptocurrency markets and investor sentiment. A potential collapse in Bitcoin's value could trigger sell-offs across digital assets, affecting related sectors such as blockchain technology and fintech. Furthermore, this scenario raises questions about the long-term viability of cryptocurrencies as an asset class, particularly in an environment increasingly focused on sustainability.

Market professionals should closely monitor energy trends and regulatory developments, as these factors could influence not only Bitcoin but also the broader cryptocurrency landscape. Understanding the interplay between energy costs and digital asset valuations will be crucial for navigating potential market shifts.
