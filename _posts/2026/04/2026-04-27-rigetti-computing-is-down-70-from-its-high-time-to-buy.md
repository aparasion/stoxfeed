Rigetti Computing (RGTI) has experienced significant volatility, with its stock soaring 275% last fall before losing all gains by February. The company’s unique approach to quantum computing, utilizing 36-qubit chiplets to form a 108-qubit system, has garnered attention for its high reliability and speed. However, Rigetti faces financial challenges, including a 34% revenue decline in 2025, attributed to delays in product launches and contract issues, resulting in a substantial loss of $216 million.

Despite these hurdles, analysts project a robust 219% revenue growth this year and a 103% increase by 2027, which may rekindle investor interest. However, with a price-to-sales ratio exceeding 730 and no earnings to justify valuation, the stock remains speculative. Investors considering Rigetti should approach with caution, viewing any potential purchases as high-risk speculative plays until the company demonstrates improved financial stability and profitability prospects.
