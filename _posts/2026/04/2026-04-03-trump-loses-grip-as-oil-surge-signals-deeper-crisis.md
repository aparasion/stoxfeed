Escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran have reignited bullish momentum in oil markets, with West Texas Intermediate (WTI) prices surging past $140 per barrel for the first time in years. This late-week price spike comes as President Trump’s efforts to stabilize the market falter, amid fears of ongoing conflict and the potential for disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. Meanwhile, OPEC+ is considering a production hike of 206,000 barrels per day to address tightening supply conditions.

This resurgence in oil prices could have significant implications for energy stocks and related sectors, as rising crude prices typically lead to increased revenues for oil producers. Additionally, the geopolitical climate may prompt further volatility in energy markets, particularly if strikes on Middle Eastern infrastructure continue. The situation is compounded by China's directive to maintain refinery operations, despite profitability concerns, which could affect global supply dynamics.

Market professionals should monitor these developments closely, as the interplay between geopolitical tensions and production decisions will likely shape energy market trajectories in the coming weeks.
