European stocks are set to open higher as optimism grows over potential diplomatic talks between the U.S. and Iran. President Trump revealed that Tehran has expressed interest in negotiating a deal, alleviating fears of further disruptions to energy supplies. This development comes amid ongoing discussions, with Turkey reportedly mediating between the two nations, and could lead to an extension of the current ceasefire to allow for more negotiations.

The market implications are significant, particularly for energy stocks, as Brent crude prices fell nearly 2% to below $98 a barrel in response to the easing tensions. Meanwhile, U.S. stocks rallied sharply on renewed hopes for a resolution, with the Nasdaq Composite rising 1.2% and the S&P 500 gaining 1%. This positive sentiment is crucial as the earnings season unfolds, potentially offsetting concerns stemming from a naval blockade announcement and disappointing housing data.

Investors should monitor developments in U.S.-Iran negotiations closely, as successful diplomacy could stabilize energy markets and influence broader market trends.
