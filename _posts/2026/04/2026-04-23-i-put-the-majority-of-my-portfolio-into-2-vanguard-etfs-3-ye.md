A personal investment strategy shift has led to a notable endorsement of the Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF (VTI) and Vanguard Total International Stock ETF (VXUS) as effective long-term portfolio anchors. The author transitioned from a diverse mix of ETFs to focusing on these two low-cost options, emphasizing simplicity and expense efficiency. This decision has yielded satisfactory returns, particularly as VTI rebounded from the 2022 bear market, despite lagging behind the S&P 500 due to its mid- and small-cap exposure.

The financial markets have seen a resurgence in international stocks, with VXUS gaining traction over the past year and a half, which could signal a shift in investor sentiment towards global equities. However, the author acknowledges the underweighting of technology exposure in both ETFs, which has affected overall performance relative to the S&P 500, particularly during tech-driven market rallies.

For market professionals, this case highlights the benefits of a low-cost, diversified approach while underscoring the importance of sector allocation in performance outcomes. As international equities gain momentum, maintaining a balanced exposure could be a strategic consideration moving forward.
