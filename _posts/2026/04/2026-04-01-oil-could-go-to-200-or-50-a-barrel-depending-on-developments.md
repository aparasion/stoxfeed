Energy Transfer (ET) stands out as a compelling investment in the current volatile oil market, where prices could swing dramatically due to geopolitical tensions. With the potential for oil to soar above $200 a barrel amid escalating conflict, or plummet to $50 with a peace deal, Energy Transfer is positioned to thrive in either scenario. The company primarily operates on a fee-based model, benefiting from increased volumes as global energy supplies face disruption.

The midstream energy firm is likely to see heightened activity from its pipeline system as U.S. producers ramp up drilling and the Strategic Petroleum Reserve releases more oil to mitigate supply shortfalls. Additionally, Energy Transfer's ongoing projects in natural gas pipelines promise to bolster cash flow, providing a buffer against falling crude prices. With 90% of its earnings insulated from oil price fluctuations, the company maintains a strong distribution yield of 6.3%.

For market professionals, Energy Transfer represents a strategic play that balances risk and reward, making it a top choice for energy investments this month.
