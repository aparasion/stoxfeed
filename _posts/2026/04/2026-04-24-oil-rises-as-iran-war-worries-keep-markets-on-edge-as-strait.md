Oil prices surged on Friday amid escalating tensions in the Middle East, with Brent crude climbing over 1.25% to $105.38 per barrel and U.S. West Texas Intermediate futures rising 1.14% to $96.96 per barrel. The ongoing conflict has led to naval blockades in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint through which approximately 20 million barrels of oil are transported daily. Despite a truce extension between Israel and Lebanon, both the U.S. and Iran are seizing ships, heightening concerns about energy supply disruptions.

The implications for the financial markets are significant. Analysts from the Commonwealth Bank of Australia noted that prolonged closure of the Strait could escalate economic costs, potentially forcing one side to concede. Fatih Birol, head of the International Energy Agency, described the situation as the largest energy security threat in history, with a loss of 13 million barrels per day impacting global supply chains.

Market professionals should closely monitor developments in the region, as any escalation could lead to further volatility in oil prices and broader market implications, particularly for energy stocks and commodities.
