SoFi Technologies (SOFI) is facing scrutiny after Muddy Waters Research released a short-seller report accusing the company of financial engineering and questionable accounting practices. Following a strong performance earlier this year, SoFi's stock has taken a hit, and investors are now bracing for the upcoming Q1 2026 earnings release on April 29. The report claims SoFi's personal loan charge-off rate is significantly understated at 2.89%, suggesting a "real" rate of 6.1%, which raises concerns about the company's financial health.

This situation is critical for investors as it could impact SoFi's growth trajectory and profitability expectations. The report alleges that inflated earnings, particularly a 90% overstatement in 2025 EBITDA, could mislead stakeholders about the company's true performance. As SoFi continues to attract new customers, the market will be closely watching how management addresses these allegations and the implications for future earnings.

The key takeaway for investors is to monitor SoFi's personal charge-off rate and any developments regarding the allegations, as these factors could significantly influence stock performance and investor confidence moving forward.
