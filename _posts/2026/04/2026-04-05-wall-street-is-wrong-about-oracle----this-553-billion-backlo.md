Oracle (NYSE: ORCL) is facing a significant market downturn, with its stock plummeting over 50% in the past six months, despite reporting a robust fiscal third-quarter earnings that revealed a staggering $553 billion backlog. This backlog, primarily tied to the construction of data centers for artificial intelligence (AI) infrastructure, indicates strong demand for Oracle's services as it positions itself as a critical provider for AI technology.

The company's backlog surged 325% year over year, suggesting that Oracle is well-placed to capitalize on the growing need for AI infrastructure. Management's optimistic revenue growth forecast for FY27 and beyond contrasts sharply with Wall Street's pessimistic outlook, which may not fully reflect Oracle's potential in the AI sector. However, the company’s rising debt—up nearly 50% in less than a year—raises concerns about its financial health, especially if customer demand falters.

For aggressive investors, Oracle's current stock price may represent a buying opportunity, particularly for those who believe in the transformative power of AI. The company’s strategic focus on AI infrastructure could yield significant returns if it successfully navigates its financial challenges.
