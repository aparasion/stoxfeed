Pakistan is facilitating crucial negotiations between Iranian and U.S. officials aimed at solidifying a two-week ceasefire in the ongoing conflict that has seen heightened tensions around the Strait of Hormuz. The Iranian delegation, led by parliamentary speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, arrived in Islamabad as both sides prepare to address critical issues, including Iran's blockade of shipping traffic through this vital oil passage. Ghalibaf has issued an ultimatum, stating that talks cannot proceed until Israel halts its attacks on Lebanon and the U.S. releases Iran's frozen assets.

The outcome of these negotiations is pivotal not only for regional stability but also for global oil markets, as the Strait of Hormuz is responsible for transporting 20% of the world's crude oil. Any disruption in shipping through this chokepoint could lead to significant price volatility and supply chain concerns in the energy sector.

Market professionals should monitor the developments closely, as a successful resolution could ease tensions and restore oil flow, while continued hostilities may lead to increased prices and heightened geopolitical risk.
