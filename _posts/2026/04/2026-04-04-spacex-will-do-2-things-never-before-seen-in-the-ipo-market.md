SpaceX is accelerating its plans for an initial public offering (IPO), with prediction markets estimating over a 50% chance of completion before July. This IPO could be historic, potentially raising between $50 billion and $75 billion, and valuing the company at up to $1.8 trillion, positioning it among a select group of companies with market caps exceeding $1 trillion. Unlike traditional IPOs, SpaceX's offering will also feature a significant allocation for retail investors, with Elon Musk reportedly considering a 30% share for them—three times the typical amount.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. SpaceX's IPO could trigger a wave of large offerings, especially as other tech giants like Anthropic and OpenAI eye similar moves. With SpaceX's unique positioning in the space sector and its ownership of xAI, the excitement surrounding its market debut is likely to draw considerable investor interest, particularly from retail channels.

Investors should be mindful of the potential for volatility post-IPO, as initial hype may lead to price fluctuations once lock-up provisions expire. Patience could yield better entry points for those looking to capitalize on SpaceX’s long-term growth trajectory.
