Last week marked a historic moment for Wall Street, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite reaching record-closing highs on April 17, fueled by a significant geopolitical shift regarding the Iran conflict. The Nasdaq's 13-day rally underscores a broader recovery for major indexes, reversing corrections from late March. The catalyst was a ceasefire agreement that allowed Iran to reopen the crucial Strait of Hormuz, a key shipping lane for global oil, which had been disrupted since U.S. military actions began in February.

This development has implications for the financial markets, particularly in the energy sector. The reopening of the Strait led to a nearly 11% drop in West Texas Intermediate crude oil prices, easing inflationary pressures that had surged during the conflict. However, while the immediate outlook seems positive for consumer spending, the underlying inflationary trends remain concerning. The Federal Reserve's inflation forecasts suggest that pressures will persist, complicating the market's response to the recent rally.

Market professionals should remain cautious; despite the positive sentiment surrounding the ceasefire, inflationary pressures and high valuations could pose significant risks. With the Federal Reserve unlikely to cut interest rates in the near term, the historically expensive stock market may face challenges ahead, particularly if inflation continues to rise.
