ServiceNow (NOW) is facing significant pressure, currently trading 56% below its recent highs amid growing concerns that artificial intelligence (AI) could undermine the competitive advantages of established software companies. Despite these fears, 91% of analysts covering the stock maintain a buy rating, with a median price target suggesting a potential 76% upside. This optimism is rooted in ServiceNow's robust SaaS business model and its extensive proprietary data.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. ServiceNow's platform, which streamlines workflow management across enterprises, could be vulnerable to AI's ability to automate many of the tasks it currently manages. However, the company is proactively integrating AI into its offerings and shifting to a usage-based billing model, which could mitigate risks associated with declining pricing power. Analysts project a 24% annual earnings growth over the next three to five years, positioning the stock as a potentially attractive investment at less than 25 times projected 2026 earnings.

In summary, while there are valid concerns about AI's impact, ServiceNow's current valuation offers a compelling entry point for investors, particularly if the company can navigate the evolving landscape effectively.
