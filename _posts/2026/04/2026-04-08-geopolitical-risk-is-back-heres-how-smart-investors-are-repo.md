On February 28, a U.S. and Israeli attack on Iran jeopardized about 20% of the global oil supply, raising concerns over delayed shipments and the potential for a severe energy shock. This geopolitical event has underscored the importance of managing risk in investment portfolios, especially as a global recession looms. Institutional investors are now reassessing their strategies, with a focus on diversifying holdings to mitigate inflation risks and prepare for market volatility.

Goldman Sachs is advocating for a shift away from growth-heavy portfolios, urging investors to incorporate hard assets like gold ETFs, inflation-protected Treasuries, and infrastructure stocks that generate stable cash flows. Meanwhile, Wells Fargo is emphasizing energy stocks and commodities as direct beneficiaries of the current conflict, while Morgan Stanley recommends increasing allocations to defense and aerospace sectors due to anticipated government spending.

The key takeaway for market professionals is the necessity of broadening investment horizons. Diversification and strategic asset selection can provide overlapping benefits, helping portfolios withstand inflationary pressures and economic uncertainty.
