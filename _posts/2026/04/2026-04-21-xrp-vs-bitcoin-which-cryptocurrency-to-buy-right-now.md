Bitcoin's status as a safe asset is under scrutiny as its correlation with mainstream risk assets, like technology stocks, increases. Year-to-date, Bitcoin has dropped approximately 14%, reacting negatively to geopolitical tensions, particularly the war in Iran. Despite this, its institutional adoption is on the rise, with hedge funds and asset management firms increasingly holding Bitcoin, which may help stabilize its price over time.

In contrast, XRP, while lacking Bitcoin's brand recognition, is the fourth-largest cryptocurrency with a market cap of $88 billion. Its future hinges on expanding its ecosystem and mainstream adoption, aided by recent regulatory developments that facilitate the creation of XRP-based funds. Major financial institutions, including Goldman Sachs, have begun investing in these funds, indicating growing institutional interest.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Bitcoin may offer a more stable investment due to its historical recovery patterns, XRP presents higher growth potential as institutional adoption increases. Investors should weigh these factors carefully, especially in the current volatile market environment.
