U.S. President Donald Trump's recent address emphasized a forthcoming military escalation against Iran, stating the U.S. will "hit them extremely hard" over the next few weeks. This announcement triggered immediate market reactions, particularly in Asia, where major indices such as South Korea's Kospi dropped 4.37%. U.S. stock futures also fell over 1%, while Treasury yields rose, indicating a sell-off in the bond market.

The implications for financial markets are significant. Oil prices surged, with Brent crude rising 5.37% to $106.59 per barrel, as analysts warn that continued conflict could disrupt energy supplies in the Gulf. The U.S. dollar strengthened against major currencies, reflecting increased demand for safe-haven assets amid geopolitical tensions. 

Market professionals should remain vigilant, as the potential for prolonged conflict in the region could lead to sustained volatility in energy prices and broader market sentiment. The situation underscores the interconnectedness of geopolitical events and market dynamics.
