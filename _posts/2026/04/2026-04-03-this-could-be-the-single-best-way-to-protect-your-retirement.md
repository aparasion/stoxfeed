Stock market volatility poses a significant challenge for retirees who rely on their portfolios for income. A downturn can force them to sell assets at a loss, jeopardizing their long-term financial stability. To mitigate this risk, financial experts recommend establishing a solid cash buffer before entering retirement, ensuring that retirees can weather market fluctuations without depleting their investment accounts.

Having a cash cushion—ideally covering two years' worth of expenses—allows retirees to maintain their lifestyle during downturns while waiting for the market to recover. This strategy protects against the need to liquidate investments in a declining market, preserving the portfolio's value for future needs. While cash may yield lower returns than stocks, it provides peace of mind and financial security.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of advising clients to prioritize liquidity in retirement planning. A well-structured cash reserve can enhance portfolio resilience and ensure that retirees can navigate market volatility without compromising their financial goals.
