Oracle's Dubai office sustained damage from falling debris linked to Iranian missile interceptions, highlighting escalating tensions in the region. While no injuries were reported, the incident underscores a broader threat, as Iran's Revolutionary Guard has declared 18 U.S. tech companies, including major players like Nvidia, Apple, and Microsoft, as potential targets for retaliation against U.S. and Israeli actions in Iran.

This development raises significant concerns for the tech sector, particularly for companies with operations in the Middle East. The risk of targeted attacks on tech infrastructure, including data centers and cloud services, could disrupt operations and impact earnings. Analysts suggest that this trend may lead to increased volatility in tech stocks, as investors reassess the geopolitical risks associated with their holdings.

Market professionals should closely monitor the situation, as the potential for further escalations could not only affect stock performance but also reshape investment strategies in the tech sector.
