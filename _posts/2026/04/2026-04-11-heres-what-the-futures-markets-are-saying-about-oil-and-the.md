Oil futures are signaling significant investor concern over the ongoing conflict in the Persian Gulf, particularly its impact on the crucial Strait of Hormuz, through which 34% of global crude oil trade flows. Currently, the market is in backwardation, indicating a near-term supply scarcity as spot prices exceed future prices. This suggests that traders are anticipating a temporary disruption in oil supply, which could benefit energy stocks like Chevron (CVX).

Despite the optimistic outlook reflected in futures pricing, the conflict remains unresolved, and the logistics of reopening the strait, including shipping insurance and infrastructure damage, pose ongoing challenges. However, the futures market's belief in a resolution may also suggest that energy equities could see upward momentum as the situation stabilizes. 

For market professionals, this presents a compelling opportunity to consider increasing allocations to energy stocks, as the current market dynamics indicate potential for recovery and growth in the sector amidst geopolitical tensions.
