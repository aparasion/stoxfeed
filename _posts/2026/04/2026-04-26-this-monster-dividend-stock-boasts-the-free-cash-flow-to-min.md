Verizon Communications (VZ) is positioning itself as a compelling option for dividend-focused investors, boasting a robust 6.12% dividend yield that significantly outpaces the S&P 500. Despite its reputation as a defensive stock, Verizon's consistent dividend growth—evidenced by a 19-year streak of payout increases—highlights its potential as a wealth-building asset, particularly in a market increasingly dominated by growth stocks.

This dividend appeal is underpinned by Verizon's strong free cash flow, which reached $20.1 billion in 2025, with projections of at least $21.5 billion for 2026. Such financial stability allows the company to allocate 60% of its cash flow to dividends while also planning for share buybacks. This combination of yield and growth makes Verizon an attractive consideration for investors looking to enhance their portfolios with reliable income.

As Verizon prepares to report its first-quarter results on April 27, market professionals should watch for insights on cash flow and debt reduction, which could further solidify its status as a dependable dividend stock.
