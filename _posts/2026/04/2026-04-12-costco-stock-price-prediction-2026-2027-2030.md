Costco Wholesale Corp. (NASDAQ: COST) is drawing bullish projections from analysts, with price targets reaching as high as $1,732 by 2030. The stock has gained 21% year-to-date, reflecting strong consumer reliance on its wholesale offerings amid ongoing tariff concerns. With a market cap of $457.86 billion and a trailing P/E ratio of 52.78, Costco maintains a consensus rating of Buy among 29 out of 38 analysts, despite elevated valuations compared to peers like Walmart and BJ’s Wholesale Club.

The company's robust membership growth, with renewal rates near 93% and a 7% year-over-year increase, underpins its solid financial standing. However, the looming threat of tariffs poses a risk to the retail sector, potentially impacting Costco's pricing power and sales. While the stock's PEG ratio suggests it may be overvalued, Costco's strategic investments in warehouses and technology could enhance long-term growth if managed effectively.

Investors should weigh Costco's historical resilience against the backdrop of tariff uncertainties. If the company navigates these challenges successfully, it could present a compelling buying opportunity, capitalizing on its loyal customer base and operational adaptability.
