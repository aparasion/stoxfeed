The announcement of a two-week ceasefire between the US and Iran has triggered significant market movements, with stocks surging, bond yields declining sharply, and Brent crude oil prices plummeting nearly 13%. This development includes a halt to US and Israeli strikes on Iran in exchange for the reopening of the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz, a key artery for global oil and gas supplies.

The immediate impact on the financial markets is pronounced, particularly in the energy sector. The steep drop in oil prices reflects a market reassessment of supply dynamics, potentially easing inflationary pressures that have plagued economies. With Brent crude experiencing a two-standard deviation move, traders are recalibrating their positions in anticipation of a more stable supply environment.

Market professionals should closely monitor how this ceasefire influences commodity prices and broader market sentiment, as shifts in oil supply dynamics could have lasting implications for inflation and monetary policy strategies moving forward.
