A temporary ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran has triggered a relief rally across global markets, easing tensions that had escalated into military threats. The ceasefire, facilitated by Pakistan, comes just before a critical deadline set by President Trump and has led to a drop in oil prices below $100 per barrel, though they remain significantly elevated compared to pre-war levels.

The implications for financial markets are substantial. While the ceasefire has sparked optimism, experts caution that the underlying trust deficit between the U.S. and Iran complicates the path to lasting peace. Investors are particularly focused on the potential for a more durable resolution, as the conflict has already disrupted commodity markets, including critical supplies like helium for semiconductor production. A failure to maintain the ceasefire could lead to renewed volatility in energy prices and broader market instability.

The key takeaway for market professionals is to remain vigilant; while the ceasefire may provide short-term relief, the fragility of the agreement and its dependence on both parties' compliance could lead to renewed tensions and sustained high commodity prices in the near term.
