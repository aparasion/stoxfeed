The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) is set to dominate the IPO landscape in 2025, hosting seven of the ten largest initial public offerings, while also paving the way for digital currency listings. This strong positioning underscores the NYSE's commitment to fostering innovation and attracting capital, which could significantly impact market dynamics as more companies seek to go public.

The S&P 500 experienced a notable rebound this week, gaining 3% and breaking a five-week losing streak, largely driven by geopolitical developments surrounding the U.S.-Iran conflict. President Trump's comments about a potential resolution provided a catalyst for the rally, which was further supported by quarter-end rebalancing and positional adjustments. However, uncertainty remains, particularly regarding the Strait of Hormuz, as futures experienced volatility following mixed signals from the administration.

Looking ahead, the upcoming earnings reports from major retail and consumer companies, alongside critical economic data such as the March CPI, will be pivotal for market sentiment. Traders should prepare for potential volatility as these developments unfold, particularly with the geopolitical backdrop influencing investor confidence.
