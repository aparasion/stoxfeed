Cava Group (CAVA +2.60%) continues to attract investor interest with its ambitious growth strategy, aiming to expand from 439 locations to over 1,000 by 2032. However, recent financial results reveal troubling signs, including a sharp slowdown in same-store sales growth to just 0.5% in Q4 2025 and declining restaurant-level margins, which fell from 25% in fiscal 2024 to 21.4% by year-end. Rising costs for food, labor, and the impact of digital sales commissions are straining profitability, raising concerns about the sustainability of its current valuation.

Despite these challenges, Cava's strong balance sheet—with nearly $300 million in net cash—positions the company to fund its expansion. However, with the stock trading at approximately 185 times expected earnings, the market's expectations are high. Analysts are watching closely for signs of recovery in customer traffic and stabilization in margins, which will be critical to maintaining investor confidence.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is that while Cava's growth narrative remains compelling, the pressure on margins and slowing sales growth could signal a need for caution in the stock's valuation trajectory.
