The South Korean stock market has gained momentum over the past three sessions, rallying more than 260 points or 4.8%, with the KOSPI closing at 5,494.78 on Tuesday. Despite this upward trend, the index may face a pause as global markets await clarity on geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly regarding U.S.-Iran relations. The mixed performance in U.S. markets—where the Dow fell slightly while the NASDAQ and S&P 500 saw minor gains—adds to the uncertainty.

Sector performance in South Korea was mixed; technology stocks like Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix posted notable gains, while financial stocks such as Shinhan Financial and KB Financial struggled. This divergence highlights the ongoing volatility in the market, influenced by external factors like crude oil prices, which have risen amid geopolitical tensions.

As South Korea prepares to release its current account figures, market participants should closely monitor how these developments might impact investor sentiment and sector performance in the coming days.
