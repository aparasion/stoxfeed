Apple (NASDAQ: AAPL) continues to rely heavily on its flagship iPhone, which accounted for nearly 60% of its revenue in Q1 2026, growing 23.4% year-over-year. However, this dependence poses a long-term risk, particularly if a competitor introduces a breakthrough device that reshapes consumer interaction with the internet. As AI technology evolves, the potential for new hardware that could disrupt Apple's dominance is a concern worth monitoring.

Despite these risks, Apple's robust distribution network, with over 2.5 billion active devices, and a powerful ecosystem provide a significant competitive advantage. The recent leadership change to hardware-focused CEO John Ternus may further enhance Apple's product strategy, reinforcing its market position. While the threat of disruption exists, the company's established infrastructure and innovation capabilities suggest it remains in a strong position.

Investors should remain vigilant about emerging AI-driven hardware but can take comfort in Apple's resilience and market leadership. As new devices are unveiled, including potential offerings from OpenAI, the landscape may shift, but immediate panic appears unwarranted.
