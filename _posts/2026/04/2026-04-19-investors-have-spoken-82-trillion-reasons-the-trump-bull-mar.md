The stock market's performance under President Donald Trump has been notable, with the Dow Jones, S&P 500, and Nasdaq Composite achieving substantial gains of 57%, 70%, and 142%, respectively, during his first term. However, despite these impressive statistics and a similar trajectory for his second term until recently, investor sentiment is shifting as uncertainty rises due to geopolitical tensions and a historically high valuation of equities.

Currently, nearly $8.2 trillion is parked in money market funds, an all-time high that signals investor skepticism about the sustainability of the Trump bull market. This influx occurs even as interest rates decline, suggesting that investors are prioritizing capital preservation amid concerns over rising oil prices and inflationary pressures linked to the ongoing Iran conflict. The S&P 500's Shiller Price-to-Earnings Ratio is also at its second-highest level in history, raising alarms about potential overvaluation.

Market professionals should note that the persistent capital inflows into money market funds, coupled with geopolitical risks and high equity valuations, could indicate a precarious position for the current bull market. This environment warrants caution as it may foreshadow a significant market correction.
