Crude oil prices experienced a sharp increase on Sunday, with West Texas Intermediate futures climbing approximately 7% to $89.74 per barrel and Brent crude rising nearly 5.8% to $95.59. This surge follows heightened tensions between the U.S. and Iran, sparked by recent attacks on commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz. The U.S. Navy's seizure of an Iranian container ship and President Trump's threats against Iran have escalated the situation, leading to concerns over potential disruptions in oil supply from this critical shipping lane.

The implications for the financial markets are significant, as rising oil prices can impact inflation rates, consumer spending, and overall economic growth. With the ceasefire agreement between the U.S. and Iran set to expire this week, market participants are closely monitoring developments that could further influence oil supply dynamics and prices. 

Professionals should prepare for volatility in energy markets, as any escalation in conflict could lead to sustained price increases and affect sectors reliant on oil, such as transportation and manufacturing.
