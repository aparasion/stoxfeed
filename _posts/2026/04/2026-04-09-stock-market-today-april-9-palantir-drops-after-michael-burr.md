Palantir Technologies (PLTR) saw its stock plunge 7.30% to $130.49 on Thursday, following critical remarks from investor Michael Burry regarding the company’s competitive landscape, particularly the rise of Anthropic. Burry's concerns center on Palantir's high valuation and the threat posed by Anthropic, which has rapidly increased its annual recurring revenue from $9 billion to $30 billion, suggesting a shift in market preference toward more accessible AI solutions. 

The trading volume for Palantir surged to 90.8 million shares, significantly above its three-month average, indicating heightened investor anxiety about the company’s ability to maintain its growth trajectory amid increasing competition. Meanwhile, broader market indices like the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite saw modest gains, but software peers such as Microsoft and Oracle also faced declines, reflecting sector-wide scrutiny of AI valuations.

For investors, this development underscores the importance of monitoring competitive pressures and growth sustainability in the tech sector. Palantir’s ability to justify its valuation will be critical, particularly as market sentiment shifts in response to emerging competitors.
