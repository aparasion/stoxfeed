Microsoft (MSFT) shares surged over 4% on Wednesday, continuing a robust rally that has seen the stock rebound from significant lows earlier this year. After a steep decline of more than 30% from its 52-week high, driven by fears of AI disruption and rising capital expenditures, the stock appears to have found support, gaining approximately 15% since late March as investors capitalize on discounted prices.

This uptick is particularly noteworthy as a recent KeyBanc survey revealed that 85% of IT executives plan to increase their spending on Microsoft's Azure cloud platform, signaling strong demand for its services. Additionally, Microsoft’s acquisition of compute capacity in a Norwegian data center, originally intended for OpenAI, could alleviate previous capacity constraints and bolster Azure’s growth trajectory.

For market professionals, the implication is clear: Microsoft’s strategic investments and growing customer commitment to Azure may position the stock for further upside, making it a key player to watch in the tech sector.
