Shares of Meta Platforms (NASDAQ: META) are currently priced around $675, reflecting a valuation of 29 times earnings as the company embarks on a significant investment cycle in artificial intelligence (AI). Despite this seemingly high valuation, Meta's fourth-quarter revenue surged 24% year-over-year to $59.9 billion, driven by a robust increase in daily active users, which reached 3.58 billion. The company's optimistic first-quarter guidance, projecting revenue between $53.5 billion and $56.5 billion, suggests a potential 30% year-over-year growth, even accounting for currency benefits.

Meta's ambitious plans for AI come with substantial capital expenditures, projected to rise from $72.2 billion in 2025 to between $115 billion and $135 billion in 2026. While this could pressure free cash flow and earnings, management's commitment to maintaining financial discipline, including workforce reductions, indicates a strategic approach to balancing growth and costs. 

Investors should consider that while Meta's growth outlook is compelling, the significant capital investments and associated risks warrant cautious positioning. The potential for annualized returns of around 13% over the next five years makes the stock attractive, but careful sizing of positions is advised given the uncertainties surrounding AI investments and regulatory challenges.
