Wall Street is poised for a volatile week as key geopolitical tensions, high expectations for tech earnings, and the upcoming Federal Reserve meeting converge. The situation in Iran, where potential de-escalation efforts are underway, could significantly influence commodity prices and overall market sentiment. Meanwhile, major tech companies, including Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, and Google, are set to report earnings, with investors eager to see if growth aligns with the artificial intelligence narrative that has driven recent market enthusiasm.

This week’s earnings reports are critical, as the technology sector is expected to deliver robust profit growth, outpacing the broader market despite substantial AI-related investments. However, even solid results may not suffice if they fall short of elevated expectations, potentially leading to sharp valuation corrections. Additionally, the Federal Reserve's stance on monetary policy will be closely scrutinized, as any shifts could impact interest-sensitive sectors like technology.

In this context, market professionals should prepare for heightened volatility as three independent forces—geopolitical risks, earnings season, and Fed policy—interact, creating an environment where quick reactions to new information will be essential.
