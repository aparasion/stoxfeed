In a volatile market, two industrial stocks have emerged as attractive investment opportunities: Rocket Lab (RKLB) and Fluor Corp. (FLR). Rocket Lab, a key player in the space sector, reported a full-year revenue of $602 million and 21 successful launches, with projections indicating revenue could reach $880 million by 2026. The company’s contract backlog has surged to $1.85 billion, bolstered by significant contracts with the Space Development Agency, and its stock has skyrocketed 265% over the past year.

Conversely, Fluor is capitalizing on increased spending in AI infrastructure, defense, and energy, boasting a backlog of $25.5 billion, with 81% of contracts now reimbursable, mitigating risk. The company is also engaging in a $1.4 billion share repurchase program, which could enhance shareholder value. Despite a 20% year-to-date stock increase, Fluor's low trailing P/E ratio suggests it may be undervalued.

Both companies exemplify resilience amid market uncertainty, making them compelling options for investors seeking quality amid chaos.
