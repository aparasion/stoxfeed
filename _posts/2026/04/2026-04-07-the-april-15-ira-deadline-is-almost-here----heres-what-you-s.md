April 15 marks Tax Day, a crucial date for financial professionals to remember, especially regarding Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs). As the deadline approaches, individuals have the opportunity to open new IRAs or make contributions to existing ones for the tax year 2025. Notably, contributions must be made by this date to avoid penalties, even if one files for a tax extension.

For tax year 2025, the contribution limits are $7,000 for those under 50 and $8,000 for those 50 and older. These limits will increase for tax year 2026, emphasizing the importance of timely contributions. Financial professionals should advise clients to double-check their contribution amounts and ensure they are not exceeding limits, as excess contributions can incur a 6% excise tax penalty annually.

The key takeaway is that with the deadline looming, there is a significant opportunity for tax-efficient retirement savings. Encouraging clients to act now can enhance their financial strategies and mitigate potential penalties.
