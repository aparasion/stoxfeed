The nuclear renaissance is gaining momentum, with the U.S. Department of Energy targeting a tripling of nuclear output by 2050. Notably, Microsoft is collaborating with Constellation Energy to revive the Three Mile Island plant, while the development of small modular reactors (SMRs) is emerging as a key innovation. These compact reactors promise to address energy needs for data centers, although no SMRs are currently operational. Oklo, a notable player in this space, saw its stock surge 704% last year but faces challenges due to its lack of revenue and significant operating losses.

In contrast, BWX Technologies is also advancing SMR technology but brings a more established revenue stream and profitability to the table. With a projected revenue growth of 18% in 2025, BWX's experience in naval nuclear reactors positions it as a safer investment compared to Oklo, which relies heavily on government grants and investor funding.

For market professionals, BWX Technologies presents a compelling opportunity within the SMR sector, combining innovation with financial stability, making it a more prudent choice amid the evolving nuclear landscape.
