European stocks are set to open lower as escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran raise concerns over the stability of a fragile ceasefire. The U.K.'s FTSE 100 is projected to drop 0.34%, while Germany's DAX is expected to decline by 1.1%. The situation intensified over the weekend when a U.S. Navy destroyer disabled an Iranian-flagged cargo ship, further straining relations and complicating upcoming negotiations.

The potential for renewed conflict in the Gulf could have significant implications for energy markets and global trade, particularly given the Strait of Hormuz's critical role in oil transportation. The recent blockade by the U.S. and Iran's response to it may lead to increased volatility in oil prices, which could spill over into broader market sentiment. This comes on the heels of a strong week for U.S. equities, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq reaching record highs.

Market professionals should brace for potential volatility in European stocks and energy prices as geopolitical tensions evolve, particularly with the ceasefire set to expire this week.
