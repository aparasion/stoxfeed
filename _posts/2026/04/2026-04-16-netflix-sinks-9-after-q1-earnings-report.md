Netflix shares fell over 9% in after-hours trading despite the company reporting strong Q1 2026 earnings, including an EPS of $1.23 and revenue of $12.25 billion, both surpassing market expectations. However, investors reacted negatively to the company’s cautious forward guidance, which projected lower-than-expected earnings and revenue for the upcoming quarter. Notably, Reed Hastings, co-founder and chairman, announced his departure from the board, adding to the uncertainty.

The market's focus on Netflix's guidance rather than its solid performance underscores a shift in investor sentiment, emphasizing future growth potential over past results. While the company achieved a record level of user engagement and saw a significant increase in free cash flow, the projections for Q2 and full-year metrics fell short of consensus estimates. This divergence may signal concerns about Netflix's ability to sustain its growth trajectory amid increasing competition and rising content costs.

For market professionals, the key takeaway is the importance of guidance in shaping stock performance; Netflix's cautious outlook may lead to further volatility. Investors should monitor the stock’s performance relative to the 200-day EMA support level, as a breach could indicate a downtrend following a recent rebound.
