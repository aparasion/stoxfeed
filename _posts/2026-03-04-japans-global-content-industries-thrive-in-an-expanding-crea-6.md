Stanford University's Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) is a leading institution dedicated to interdisciplinary research and policy dialogue on critical issues facing Asia and its global interactions. The center aims to educate future experts in Asian affairs while bridging the gap between research and practical application through various publications and public engagements.

APARC provides valuable policy-relevant insights to decision-makers in the U.S. and Asia, enhancing the public's understanding of Asian issues. Recent publications from APARC scholars include works on America's trade policy in the Asia-Pacific, China's rise as a global power, and the U.S.-Southeast Asia partnership.

In a recent briefing, former diplomat Rahm Emanuel discussed the current political climate in America, the implications for U.S. alliances in the Indo-Pacific, and lessons learned from Japan. This dialogue underscores the center's commitment to addressing contemporary challenges in Asia and fostering international collaboration.
