Localization is evolving, with leaders from Notion and SurveyMonkey emphasizing a shift from viewing localization as merely an execution layer to a strategic function that drives business outcomes. In a recent webinar, Kevin O’Donnell of Global10x highlighted how localization teams can gain influence by aligning their efforts with broader business metrics rather than traditional operational metrics, which often lead to diminishing returns.

This reframing is crucial as companies increasingly leverage AI and aim for rapid global expansion. By focusing on metrics like revenue growth and user engagement, localization teams can demonstrate their value as growth partners rather than cost centers. The discussion underscored the importance of early involvement in product development cycles, allowing localization teams to shape decisions that enhance customer experiences across diverse markets.

For localization professionals, the key takeaway is to prioritize clarity over volume, engage proactively, and tailor quality efforts to context. This strategic mindset will position localization teams as essential players in achieving business success. For more insights, I recommend checking out the full discussion in the original article.
