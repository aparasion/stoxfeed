XTM International has appointed Lorcan Malone as its new Chief Executive Officer, succeeding Ian Evans, who led the company through a significant growth phase marked by strategic acquisitions and advancements in AI product innovation. Malone brings over 20 years of experience in the technology sector, having previously held leadership roles at companies like Sitecore and Boxever, and is expected to drive further value for enterprise and government customers.

Under Malone's leadership, XTM aims to enhance its AI-driven localization technology, leveraging recent acquisitions such as Transifex and TXTOmedia. This strategic move positions XTM to capitalize on the evolving localization market, allowing organizations to use localization as a key driver for global growth and competitive advantage. Malone emphasized the company's commitment to customer-centric strategies, product excellence, and deep integration of its expanded capabilities.

XTM currently supports over 1,300 customers and 880+ languages, streamlining localization processes to help enterprises efficiently deliver multilingual products. For more details on this leadership change and its implications for XTM, read the full article.
