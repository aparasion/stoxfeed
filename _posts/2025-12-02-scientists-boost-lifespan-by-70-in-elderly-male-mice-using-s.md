A recent study from the University at Buffalo indicates that cannabis-infused beverages may significantly reduce alcohol consumption among users. Survey results revealed that participants who consumed these beverages cut their weekly alcohol intake by approximately 50% and reported a decrease in binge drinking episodes. Notably, nearly two-thirds of respondents indicated they either reduced or completely stopped drinking alcohol after integrating cannabis beverages into their routines.

This finding is particularly relevant to the longevity and healthspan fields, as excessive alcohol consumption is linked to various health issues, including liver disease and cognitive decline. By offering an alternative that may mitigate alcohol use, cannabis beverages could play a role in promoting healthier lifestyles and potentially enhancing longevity.

For professionals in aging biology and healthspan research, this study underscores the importance of exploring non-traditional interventions for substance use reduction.
