A recent article highlights the distinctions between using a static glossary and a dynamic term base in localization workflows, emphasizing when each is most effective. Glossaries can suffice for simple terminology needs, particularly in single-language projects with low volume, where a single team manages naming decisions and enforcement is not critical. However, as projects scale and involve multiple languages and contributors, the need for a term base becomes essential to maintain consistency and prevent miscommunication across teams.

The article underscores the operational advantages of a term base, particularly in environments where terminology mistakes can lead to significant costs, such as rework or customer confusion. By integrating terminology checks into existing workflows, teams can proactively address potential issues, especially as AI-generated content increases the volume and complexity of outputs.

For localization professionals, the takeaway is clear: transitioning from a glossary to a term base can enhance quality and efficiency, particularly in multi-team and multi-language scenarios.
