Stanford University's Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center (APARC) serves as a vital hub for interdisciplinary research and policy dialogue focused on contemporary Asia. The center aims to address critical challenges in the region and enhance understanding of Asian affairs among U.S. and Asian decision-makers. Through its publications, public engagement, and international collaborations, APARC educates future experts in Asian studies.

Recent publications from APARC scholars include works that analyze America's evolving trade policy in the Asia-Pacific, China's rise as a global power, and the dynamics of U.S.-Southeast Asia relations. Notably, former diplomat Rahm Emanuel participated in an APARC briefing discussing the current political landscape in America and its implications for U.S. alliances in the Indo-Pacific, drawing lessons from Japan's experiences.

APARC's efforts not only contribute to academic discourse but also aim to influence policy decisions, making it a significant player in shaping the future of U.S.-Asia relations. For more details, readers are encouraged to explore the full article.
