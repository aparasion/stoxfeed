Treasury yields declined on Wednesday as investors positioned themselves ahead of the Federal Reserve's interest rate decision. The 10-year yield fell to 4.175%, while the 30-year bond yield dropped to 4.824%. The 2-year note yield also decreased to 3.659%, reflecting market anticipation that the Fed will maintain rates in the current range of 3.5% to 3.75%.

This yield movement is significant as it indicates market sentiment regarding the Fed's monetary policy amid rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions. With the Fed's upcoming Summary of Economic Projections expected to provide insights into future rate cuts, traders are keenly focused on any signals from Fed Chair Jerome Powell regarding the potential impact of oil prices on policy decisions.
