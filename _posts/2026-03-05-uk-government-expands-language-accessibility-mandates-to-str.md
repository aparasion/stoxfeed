A significant shift in the UK entertainment landscape is set to occur with the implementation of the Media Act of 2024, mandating that streaming services adhere to the same accessibility standards as traditional broadcasters. Starting February 24, 2026, major platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Disney+ will be required to provide subtitles, audio descriptions, and signing for the 18 million individuals with hearing impairments and the 350,000 with visual disabilities in the UK.

UK Media Minister Ian Murray emphasized the importance of inclusivity as audiences increasingly prefer on-demand content over live television. The new regulations will be enforced by the Office of Communications (Ofcom), which will oversee compliance and can impose fines of up to GBP 250,000 or 5% of qualifying revenue for violations. Streaming services will have four years to meet the requirements, with interim targets set for two years, and Ofcom will conduct public consultations to refine the accessibility code.
