Bitcoin has surged past $75,000, reaching a peak of $75,800, driven largely by the unwinding of bearish put-option hedges. This significant rally has not only broken through a long-standing resistance zone but has also propelled the broader crypto market, with the CoinDesk 20 Index rising by 5%. Traders are closing short positions initiated during February's sell-off, contributing to the bullish momentum.

The implications for the financial markets are substantial. As traders unwind their bearish bets, market makers are forced to buy Bitcoin to rebalance their exposure, further supporting price increases. Notably, major altcoins like ether and XRP have also experienced strong gains, reflecting the overall positive sentiment in the crypto space. However, the rally has been primarily driven by hedge unwinds rather than aggressive bullish positioning, indicating a cautious market environment.
